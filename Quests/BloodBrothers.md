# Side Quest: The Ties That Bleed
**Type:** Optional Side Quest / Monster of the Week  
**Location:** Apache Junction, Arizona  
**Chronicle:** Valley by Night (1994)  
**Status:** Design – In Progress  

---

## Quest Overview

This is a **three-part side quest** designed to introduce the Blood Brothers as an *unnatural, coordinated threat* that **cannot be solved by the PC acting alone**. The quest reinforces core themes of *Valley by Night*: leverage over strength, alliances over heroics, and the danger of misjudging Sabbat-created horrors.

This Blood Brother cell is **not** the Guadalupe Sabbat pack and operates independently as a roaming or semi-contained anomaly on the edge of the Valley.

---

## PART I — RUMORS (Slow Burn Foreshadowing)

**Goal:** Establish unease, pattern recognition, and fear without naming the threat.

### Location Tone: Apache Junction
- Edge-of-the-Valley desert town
- Sparse greenery, harsh sodium lighting
- Large, desolate trailer park complexes
- Long sightlines, little privacy
- People notice strange behavior quickly—and stop talking about it

### Rumor Tiers

#### Tier 1 — Unease
- “People keep moving out of the same trailer row.”
- “Dogs won’t go near the south lot.”
- “You hear shouting at night, but it’s the same voice from different places.”
- “Sheriff’s been out there twice. No reports.”

#### Tier 2 — Pattern
- “Three guys. Always together.”
- “I saw them in two places at once.”
- “Hit one guy—another screamed like he got stabbed.”
- “Three trailers. Same layout. Same lights. Same time.”

#### Tier 3 — Fear
- “I shot one. He didn’t drop.”
- “They bleed together.”
- “They grab you from two sides at once.”
- “They don’t hunt like predators. They hunt like machinery.”

#### Tier 4 — Suppression
- Contacts refuse to talk further
- False explanations surface (“Anarchs,” “junkies”)
- A source goes missing briefly and returns shaken but silent
- Clear indication someone powerful wants this quiet

#### Tier 5 — Truth Without Context
- “They were made. Not embraced.”
- “You don’t kill them. You separate them.”
- “Fire, noise, distance.”
- “If they stop moving together, they panic.”

**Design Note:**  
No rumor explicitly names “Blood Brothers” or “Sabbat.”

---

## PART II — SOLO INVESTIGATION (The Warning)

**Goal:** Allow the PC to investigate alone while clearly signaling that solo action is insufficient.

### Structure
- PC follows rumors to Apache Junction
- Surveillance, questioning locals, or light intrusion is possible
- Escalation is subtle at first, then abrupt

### Key Signals to the Player
- Targets reposition with impossible coordination
- The PC is watched from multiple angles simultaneously
- Minor confrontations end inconclusively:
  - Damage has no clear effect
  - Threats are mirrored, not reacted to
- Environmental cues:
  - Lights flick on in sequence
  - Footsteps echo incorrectly
  - Voices overlap unnaturally

### Mandatory Outcome
- The PC **must disengage**, escape, or be forcibly separated from the threat
- Survival is possible
- Victory is not

**Narrative Lesson:**  
> “This isn’t about stats or bravery. This is about leverage.”

---

## PART III — THE SHOWDOWN (Allies Required)

**Goal:** Resolve the threat by breaking the Blood Brother cell, not overpowering it.

### Required Design Constraint
The encounter is **not winnable solo**.  
Success requires **outside NPC assistance** (specific NPCs TBD).

NPC roles to be filled later:
- Information / Analysis
- Physical disruption / containment
- Environmental control / denial

### Core Tactical Truth
- The Blood Brothers’ power collapses when:
  - Separated by distance
  - Denied sensory awareness of one another
  - Overloaded by environmental chaos

### Possible Outcomes
- One Brother destroyed → cell destabilized
- Cell driven off → future consequences
- Partial containment → political fallout

No clean victory.

## CRITICAL DESIGN NOTE — SANGUINUS IMPLEMENTATION

⚠ **IMPORTANT**

Blood Brothers use **Sanguinus differently than standard Discipline models**.

- In the Blood Brothers clanbook, Sanguinus powers function as:
  - **Cell-level expressions**, not individual spell lists
- Multiple Sanguinus effects may be available simultaneously **when the cell is intact**
- Powers such as:
  - Brother’s Blood
  - Gestalt
  - Octopod
  - Walk of Caine  
  are **situational expressions of shared vitae**, not discrete “known powers” in the usual sense

### Action Item
Before final implementation:
- Decide how to model Sanguinus as:
  - A shared-cell mechanic
  - Not individual dot-based power stacking
- Ensure combat, agent logic, and UI systems do not misinterpret these powers

This quest **depends** on Sanguinus behaving as an *alien, unfair mechanic* until the cell is disrupted.
