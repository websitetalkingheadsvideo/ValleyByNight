# 🛠 REED HOUSE — STYLE GUIDE

## 📍 Location Identity

- **Type:** Private haven / emergency fallback site
- **Era:** Mid-1990s
- **Owner:** Butch Reed (Anarch affiliation)
- **Setting:** Working-class neighborhood near an auto shop in Phoenix, AZ

## 🎭 Emotional Tone

### Core Feelings

- Paranoia
- Watchfulness
- Subdued hostility

### Initial Reaction

> "This place doesn't want company."

### Intended Atmosphere

Utilitarian, oppressive, survival-oriented

## 🔒 Behavioral Rules

- Never assume you're alone.
- Do not make sudden moves (the dog reacts first).
- This is not a place for guests — get in, get out.
- The house itself discourages rest or comfort.

## 🎨 Visual Language

### Motifs & Themes

- **Dominant Motif:** Decay and utility
- **Design Philosophy:** Functional > aesthetic
- **Style:** Stark, stripped-down mid-'90s realism

### Color Palette

- **Muted, dusty earth tones:** beige, ochre, concrete gray
- **Accents:** Rust red, oil black, oxidized metal

### Lighting

- Shadow-heavy
- Practical (lamps, single bulbs)
- No decorative fixtures

### Materials

- Chain-link fencing
- Exposed concrete and unfinished drywall
- Reinforced steel doors and locks
- Worn wood (furniture, floorboards)
- Analog technology (CRT TVs, rotary phones, old wiring)

### Never Include

- Bright or saturated colors
- Soft textures (plush, velvet, etc.)
- Modern digital appliances
- Decorative or cozy design elements

## 🧭 Movement & Layout

### Flow Rules

#### Entry Points

- Front door (visible, direct)
- Back door (less used, more concealed)

#### Circulation

- Linear and guided (fast, clear routes)
- No wandering or lounging zones

#### Sight Lines

- Living room commands views of all key spaces
- Ghoul Rottweiler can cross entire house without obstruction

## 🧛 Supernatural Layer

### Ghoul Dog (Security)

- Rottweiler, undead and alert
- Patrols both interior and fenced exterior
- Not just a threat — it's surveillance
- No one enters unseen; nothing goes unnoticed

### Masquerade Maintenance

- All supernatural features (dog, reinforced interiors) disguised as mundane
- Neighborhood reads it as "paranoid loner" not vampire hideout

## 🏗 Production Guidelines

### Usage Type

- Repeated haven location
- Ideal for tension, ambush, secrecy scenes
- Not suited for downtime, romance, or comfort arcs

### Visual Development Notes

- Use shadows and occlusion for tension
- Keep spaces claustrophobic and airless
- Avoid any cinematic softness — emphasize grit
