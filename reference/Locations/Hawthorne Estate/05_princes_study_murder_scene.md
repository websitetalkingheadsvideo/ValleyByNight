# Prince's Study — Murder Scene Version

## Cinematic Description
The study looks different the moment the door swings open. The warm amber lamplight that once felt controlled now seems sickly, distorted by the presence of death. The air is still—too still—as though the room itself recoils from what happened here.

A wide, ashen **scatter of gray-white dust** lies across the floor in the center of the room, fanned out like someone collapsed forward before disintegrating. Some parts of the ash are fine, powdered; others form small clumps where the Prince’s clothing or jewelry resisted the fire for a fraction of a second longer. A faint scorch pattern radiates outward, subtle but visible under the lamplight.

The desk chair behind the mahogany desk is overturned, resting on its side like it was kicked away or knocked aside in a final, violent motion. One of the carefully stacked papers is crumpled, lying several feet away as though caught in the blast of the Prince’s final moment.

The heavy curtains over the reinforced window stir slightly—just enough to suggest someone brushed against them recently. A whisper of displaced dust lingers in the air, illuminated by moonlight sneaking through a narrow gap in the velvet.

On the desk’s surface, a glass paperweight has cracked down the center. A pen lies on the floor. The room’s perfect order is broken, but only just. The violence was sudden, final, and efficient.

Under the desk, the shadows look ordinary at first glance. But a deeper scrutiny—heightened senses, a moment of focus—reveals a tension in the darkness. The assassin remains there, hidden beneath layers of Obfuscate, curled tightly in a pocket of shadow, waiting for the moment to flee or strike if discovered.

The Prince is gone. Only ash remains. The silence feels heavy enough to crush breath.
