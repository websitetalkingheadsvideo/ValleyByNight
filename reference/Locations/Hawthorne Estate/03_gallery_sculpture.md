# Sculpture Gallery (Right Wing)

## Cinematic Description
You turn into the gallery on the right, and immediately the atmosphere shifts. The Main Salon’s warm openness tightens into something more intimate, more curated. Here, the lighting dims to a soft corridor glow, punctuated by focused amber spotlights that fall in perfect circles on the floor.

Each spotlight reveals a sculpture.

Marble. Bronze. Glass. Some abstract, some anatomical, some so flawlessly carved that the shadows across their surfaces feel like breath. Their silent forms stretch down the length of the room, each one isolated in its own pool of warm light, surrounded by darkness thick enough to swallow detail.

Dust motes drift lazily through the beams. A towering bronze with elongated limbs stands beside a delicate marble dancer frozen mid-twist. A modern glass monolith fractures the warm lighting into thin bands of teal and gold.

The gallery is silent. But the sculptures feel like they’re watching you.

## GB Description
This gallery is a long, narrow room used to showcase rotating works of art. Tonight’s exhibition features sculpture-only pieces arranged evenly along the walls and centerline.

**Layout & Lighting**
- Dim overall, focused spotlights  
- Wooden or stone-paneled walls  
- Museum-like atmosphere  

**Sculptures Include**
- Marble figures  
- Bronze castings  
- Abstract shapes  
- Modern glass works  

A quiet space where Kindred mingle in hushed tones.
