# 🪟 Dunlap Apartments — Back Window Late-Night Hook

*(Malkavian-Adjacent, Plot-Seeding Encounter)*

## Core Truth

This is not about being watched.

This is about seeing a place you weren't meant to notice yet.

## Trigger Conditions (very permissive)

- Player is at Dunlap Apartments
- Late-night state (pre-dawn)
- Player chooses:
  - Look out back window
  - Wait
  - Kill time (passive)

No Auspex required for something to happen.  
Perception only changes what happens.

## What the Back Window Overlooks

The park beyond the fence — mundane, neglected, ignored.

This matters because:

- It's normal
- It's public
- It's where supernatural things hide best

## Perception Tiers (This is the Important Part)

### 👁️ Tier 0 — Nothing Happens (Most Players)

**They see:**

- A dark park
- Faint movement that resolves into nothing
- Maybe fireflies, maybe not

The scene ends quietly.

**Hidden effect:**  
A flag is set: "Player has seen the park at night."

They don't know this matters.

---

### 👁️ Tier 1 — "That's Odd"

*(Alertness, Awareness, mild supernatural sensitivity)*

**They notice:**

- The park doesn't feel empty
- Sound carries wrong
- A shape where there shouldn't be one — gone when focused on

**This produces:**

- Unease
- A lingering thought
- No explanation

**Badge pressure:**

- `Unnatural Awareness`
- `Something Off About This Place`

---

### 👁️ Tier 2 — The Glimmer

*(Auspex, Malkavian intuition, prior exposure)*

Now the park changes — but only briefly.

**They may see:**

- Moss that glows faintly
- A circle of stones that wasn't there
- Insects moving in impossible patterns
- The sense of music without sound

**Crucially:**  
It never lasts long enough to prove.

**Badge pressure:**

- `Touched the Unseen`
- `Thin Places`

---

### 👁️ Tier 3 — Recognition (Rare, Dangerous)

*(High Auspex, Malkavian clan logic, repeated exposure)*

This is where the game gets quiet.

Nothing changes visually.

**Instead:**

The player realizes the park is a node

- Not active
- Not hostile
- But waiting

**Later consequences might include:**

- An NPC referencing the park casually
- A dream involving fences or circles
- A rumor that makes no sense until it does

**Badge pressure:**

- `Saw the Shape of It`
- `Places That Remember`

## Why This Works So Well

### 1. It rewards inaction

The player didn't go out.  
They didn't investigate.  
They just looked.

That reinforces your late-night philosophy perfectly.

### 2. It fits Malkavian logic

- Not madness
- Not jumpscares
- Just perception arriving early

Other clans might miss it entirely.  
Malkavians notice without knowing why.

### 3. It scales across the whole game

This park can later become:

- A changeling contact point
- A contested supernatural space
- A rumor nexus
- A moral test

And players who never looked out the window?  
They'll walk into it blind.

## Design Rule (DO NOT BREAK THIS)

Never explain the park immediately.

- No codex entry.
- No quest marker.
- No follow-up unless the world brings it back.

The power of this moment is:

> **"You weren't supposed to see that yet."**
