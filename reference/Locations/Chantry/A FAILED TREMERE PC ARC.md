# A Failed Tremere PC Arc
*(Early → Mid → Contained)*

This is a PC who:

- Is competent
- Is not openly treasonous
- But does not fit cleanly into the Chantry's expectations

They are not punished.  
They are sorted.

## Act I — Accounted For (Same Beginning)

The opening is identical.

### Chantry Posture

> "We know who you are. Do not embarrass us."

### Living Situation

- Assigned quarters
- Temporary tone
- No explanation

### Access

- **Library:** none
- **Lab:** supervised assistance only
- **Grounds:** functional movement

The PC assumes:

> "This is normal."

They're right — so far.

## Act II — Useful But Awkward (The Divergence)

This is where the arc splits.

The PC may:

- Ask the wrong questions
- Focus on theory over application
- Show curiosity without discretion
- Avoid social rituals
- Push too hard, too early — or not at all

Nothing dramatic.

Just off.

### Living Situation

- Quarters do not improve
- Still serviceable
- Still temporary-feeling

No one comments on it.

### Access Changes (Or Lack Thereof)

**Library:**

- Still request-only
- Requests take longer
- Certain topics are quietly redirected

**Laboratory:**

- Still assisting
- Never trusted alone
- Work is compartmentalized

**Grounds:**

- Allowed, but watched
- Conversations stop when the PC approaches
- No one assumes authority

The PC is present, but never central.

### Social Signals (Subtle, But Consistent)

- Elders stop soliciting opinions
- Praise is procedural, not personal
- The PC is described as:
  - "Capable"
  - "Reliable"
  - "Better suited to support work"

This is the warning sign most players miss.

### What the PC Thinks is Happening

> "I'm stalled."

**What's actually happening:**

> "You are being classified."

## Act III — Contained and Stable (The Failure State)

This is the end state — and it can last decades.

The Chantry has decided:

- You are not dangerous.
- You are not essential.
- You are predictable.

That sounds merciful.

**It isn't.**

### Living Situation

- Quarters are reassigned — smaller, more permanent
- Personalization discouraged
- No expectation of change

This is where you sleep because it's easiest.

### Access State

**Library:**

- Narrow, practical texts
- No theoretical works
- No forbidden sections

**Laboratory:**

- None
- Or repetitive, menial tasks
- Never research leadership

**Grounds:**

- Movement allowed
- Presence unnoticed
- The PC becomes part of the furniture

This is the quiet death of ambition.

### Social Reality

- Other Tremere forget to mention the PC
- New apprentices don't know their name
- Elders speak past them

The PC is not disliked.

**They are irrelevant.**

## The Realization Moment (Worse Than Failure)

It happens when the PC notices:

- No one checks on them
- No one worries about their absence
- No one references their work

They are not being watched because:

> There's nothing to watch.

## Why This is a Failure State, Not a Neutral One

In Vampire terms:

- Power stagnates
- Influence calcifies
- Knowledge dries up
- Escape becomes harder, not easier

Leaving the Chantry at this point:

- Raises fewer alarms
- But provides no allies
- No leverage
- No safety net

They have been allowed to fade.

## The Horror of This Arc

The failed Tremere is not punished.  
They are sorted into safety.

Which means:

- No growth
- No risk
- No notice

**For a Tremere, that's spiritual death.**
