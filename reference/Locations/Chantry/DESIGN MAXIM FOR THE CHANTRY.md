# Design Maxim for the Chantry

> **Write this somewhere permanent:**
>
> Other havens decide where you belong.  
> The Chantry decides how much of yourself you're allowed to use.
>
> That's why it's different.  
> That's why it's dangerous.  
> That's why it must not behave like the others.

## The Chantry as an Access Lattice (Not a Haven)

For a Tremere PC, the Chantry is always present in some form.  
What changes is what parts of it open to them, and why.

The question is never:

> "Do you live here?"

It's always:

> "How much of this place is allowed to acknowledge you?"

## The Three Axes of Chantry Access

Each of these is evaluated independently.  
That's important — it allows asymmetry, paranoia, and punishment without exile.

### 📚 1. Library Access

**Knowledge is the soft leash.**

**Possible states:**

- **None** – "You are not cleared to study."
- **Supervised** – texts provided on request
- **Sectioned** – access to limited shelves
- **Independent** – free study, logged
- **Entrusted** – restricted or dangerous texts

**What this really measures:**

- Trust in discretion
- Risk of independent theory
- Willingness to let the PC ask the wrong questions

**Examples:**

- A PC with high usefulness but low obedience might:
  - Have wide access — but always logged
- A PC with loyalty but low standing might:
  - Be fed knowledge slowly, deliberately

### ⚗️ 2. Laboratory Access

**Power is the hard leash.**

**Possible states:**

- **None** – no unsanctioned experimentation
- **Assisting Only** – another's work, never alone
- **Shared Space** – access during assigned hours
- **Independent Bench** – personal workspace
- **Sanctioned Research** – projects with backing

**What this really measures:**

- Fear of innovation
- Fear of unsupervised success
- Fear of irreversible mistakes

**Important:**  
A PC can be trusted socially and still denied a lab.

Because labs create outcomes, not just ideas.

### 🌿 3. Grounds / Physical Space

**Belonging masquerading as privilege.**

**Possible states:**

- **Restricted** – quarters + corridors only
- **Functional** – transit allowed, no lingering
- **Accepted** – allowed to be seen, not to host
- **Integrated** – movement without question
- **Representative** – others assume you belong

**This axis controls:**

- Who sees the PC
- Who assumes authority
- Who feels entitled to approach them

This is how the Chantry controls social gravity.

## Why These Axes Must Remain Separate

Because separation lets you do things like:

**Give a PC:**

- Full library access
- No lab access
- Restricted grounds

→ *"We trust your mind, not your ambition."*

**Or:**

- Lab access
- No library
- Full grounds

→ *"You are useful, not independent."*

**Or the truly terrifying one:**

- Everything

→ *"You are now indispensable."*

**Indispensable is not a reward.**  
**It's a trap.**

## How This Interacts with the Haven Agent

This is the key distinction:

- **Haven Agent** → "Where are you allowed to sleep?"
- **Chantry Access Agent** → "How much of us are you allowed to touch?"

They should share signals (obedience, discretion, liability), but they should never collapse into one another.

A Tremere PC can:

- Sleep in the Chantry
- And still be profoundly excluded

That's on-theme.

## Player Experience (Important)

**The PC should:**

- Feel access changes before they understand them
- Notice doors opening or closing
- Be invited or not invited without explanation
- Realize later that every privilege carried a debt

**They should never see:**

- "Library Access: 3/5"
- "Lab Trust +1"

**They should feel:**

> "They're letting me do this now. Why?"
