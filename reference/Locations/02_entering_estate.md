# Entering the Hawthorn Estate

## Cinematic Description
The heavy front doors open with a soft, controlled sigh—as if the mansion has been holding its breath. Warm desert amber light spills outward, cutting a thin wedge into the moonlit dark behind you. Crossing the threshold feels like stepping into another world, one curated for elegance and power.

Inside is the Main Salon, wider than expected—a grand space with ballroom proportions. The ceiling rises high overhead, supported by dark wood beams that disappear into shadow. The polished floor reflects the light like still water, broken only by the long central carpet runner leading deeper inside.

Along both sides, art and sculpture rest in pools of soft gold illumination: long canvases in ornate frames, marble busts, figures of silver and glass. Their shadows spill across the walls like silent observers. The air is cool—cooler than Arizona should ever be at night—and faintly scented with aged books, sandalwood, and something floral from another century.

A broad staircase dominates the far end of the room, rising in a slow curve to a second-story balcony that overlooks the entire salon. Gilded railing. Velvet runner. The kind of staircase designed for entrances, not exits. Your footsteps echo faintly—too faintly—as if the house absorbs sound.

The room is empty. But you don't feel alone.

## GB Description
Beyond the front doors lies the Main Salon, a large and open room designed for gatherings and formal court. The interior is significantly wider than it appears from the outside, approaching ballroom scale.

**Key Features**
- High ceiling with exposed beams  
- Polished floor with central rug  
- Decorated walls with paintings and mirrors  
- Warm, soft lighting  
- Sculptures positioned along the sides  

A major feature is the wide central staircase on the far wall, rising to a second-floor overlook. Multiple hallways branch from this space.
