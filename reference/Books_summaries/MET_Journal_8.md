# MET Journal 8

The document is the eighth and final issue of the Mind's Eye Theatre Journal, a quarterly publication focused on enhancing live-action roleplaying experiences within the World of Darkness universe.

## Introduction: The Final Issue

- This is the last issue of the MET Journal that will be available for the foreseeable future.
- As White Wolf ramps up and fine-tunes future Mind's Eye Theatre releases, the Journal has become less necessary.
- Resources that went into the Journal will now go into making Laws books better, bigger, and more inclusive.
- The Mind's Eye Theatre line will emerge stronger for the change.

## The Current Thinking: Reader Feedback

This section compiles reader feedback about the Journal throughout its run, including both praise and criticism:

### Common Criticisms

- **Too Much Filler**: Some information is very useful, while some feels like just filler.
- **Incomplete Information**: Useful sections would benefit from more complete information (e.g., Bastet section needed more tribes, more complete Pumonca powers).
- **Lore System Issues**: The Lore system "screws anyone who wants to come into a game knowing more" - spending three minutes with characters gives more info than any level of Lores.
- **Too Much Fiction**: Some readers don't want fiction in a rules-focused publication.
- **Too Much Changeling and Wraith**: Most people play Vampire or Werewolf, but Journal focused heavily on less-played games.
- **Pricing Concerns**: Some found it too expensive relative to content.
- **Timing Issues**: Quarterly publication wasn't frequent enough for some needs.

### Common Praise

- **Sense of Humor**: The Journal poked fun at itself and the product.
- **No Advertisements**: Advertisement-free publication focused on MET rules.
- **Inexpensive**: Good value for the content provided.
- **Useful Clarifications**: Rules clarifications and FAQs were valuable.
- **Additional Rules**: Rules for minor bloodlines and updates to MET gameworld were welcome.
- **Metaplot Information**: Articles detailing history and exposing metaplot information were wonderful for LARPers who don't follow tabletop.

### Reader Suggestions

- More FAQs and official updates
- More articles on chronicle creation, storytelling techniques, legal aspects
- More timely publication schedule
- More focus on commonly-played games (Vampire, Werewolf)
- Official errata beyond FAQ level
- Less fiction, more solid material

## Purpose and Value

This final issue serves as:
- A reflection on the Journal's contributions to the MET community
- A compilation of reader feedback and suggestions
- Guidance for the future of MET gaming
- A farewell to a publication that filled gaps in the LARP environment
- A transition point as resources shift to core rulebooks

## Legacy

The Journal filled important gaps in the MET LARP environment for eight issues, providing:
- Rules for new character types
- Original storylines and plot hooks
- Guides for game management
- Rules updates and clarifications
- Community advice and social guidance
- Setting material for less common game types

While the Journal is ending, its contributions will continue to influence future MET publications, and the community it served will continue to grow and evolve.

This final issue acknowledges both the Journal's successes and its limitations, providing honest reflection on what worked, what didn't, and what the future holds for Mind's Eye Theatre.
