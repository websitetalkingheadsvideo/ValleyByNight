# 🗣️ **Deadguy Speaks — Community Perception & Responsibility**
Richard Dansky’s editorial is unusually serious for the column. Written in the aftermath of the Columbine shooting, it examines why LARPers become easy scapegoats during moral panics.  
Key points include:

- **Mainstream misunderstanding**: Many people don’t “get” LARPing and may view it as threatening or occult.
- **Responsibility of players and STs**: Rather than adopting a persecuted attitude, the community should focus on **education, transparency, and courtesy**.
- **Practical advice**:
  - Explain the hobby to parents, teachers, and authorities.
  - Notify police and venue owners before games.
  - Encourage players to patronize venues and avoid disruptive behavior.
  - Engage in **community outreach** (blood drives, charity events) to build goodwill.
- **Core message**: Fair or not, LARPers must actively manage their public image to keep games safe and sustainable.

# 🎒 **Newbie Boot Camp — A First‑Timer’s Guide to LARP**
James Stewart offers a detailed walkthrough of what new players experience and how to survive their first game.

### Highlights:
- **LARP ≠ tabletop**: MET is driven by character interaction, not a single central plot.
- **Character design**: New players should create **social characters** with clear goals.
- **In‑game navigation**:
  - Introduce yourself boldly.
  - Learn gesture systems for powers.
  - Ask for rules explanations when needed.
- **Social dynamics**: Cliques exist; newcomers must push themselves to engage.
- **Advice for veterans**: Be welcoming, avoid overwhelming newbies with rules, and help them integrate.

# 📣 **Pronouncements from On High — Metaplot & Continuity**
This column addresses a major concern: **Do MET games have to follow White Wolf’s evolving tabletop metaplot?**

### The answer: **No.**
- Storytellers are free to ignore, adapt, or fully embrace metaplot developments.
- If you diverge from canon, communicate clearly with players.
- If you adopt metaplot changes, you can:
  - Drop players directly into major events.
  - Let consequences ripple into the chronicle.
  - Introduce changes gradually or indirectly.

The emphasis is on **ST autonomy** and **narrative coherence**.
