# Blood Sacrifice - The Thaumaturgy Companion

This supplement for Vampire: The Masquerade explores non-Hermetic traditions of blood magic practiced by Kindred outside the Tremere clan. The book presents four major blood magic traditions: Akhu (Egyptian Setite Sorcery), Dur-An-Ki (Middle Eastern Assamite Sorcery), Sadhana (Indian blood magic), and Wanga (Afro-Caribbean blood magic). Each tradition has its own philosophy, methods, paths, and rituals rooted in specific cultural and religious contexts.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book expands blood magic beyond the Hermetic Thaumaturgy of the Tremere.
- It presents four distinct non-Western blood magic traditions.
- Each tradition is deeply intertwined with religious and cultural beliefs.
- Provides new paths, rituals, and systems for each tradition.
- Explores the challenges of learning cross-cultural blood magic.

## Introduction: In Vitae Veritas

The book opens with a fictional prelude involving a Lasombra who encounters non-Hermetic blood magic, setting the tone for the exploration of diverse magical traditions.

## Chapter One: Akhu - The Divine Image

### Heka and the Mummies

Akhu is the Egyptian tradition of blood magic, preserved primarily by the Followers of Set. The book discusses the relationship between Akhu and the magic of Egyptian Mummies (Hekau), noting similarities despite thousands of years of separate development.

### Magical Theory and Practice

**Images and Imitations**: Egyptian magic makes great use of images - statues, paintings, and figurines. An idol carried the power of the god it represented. Spells could bring models to life or affect real objects through sympathetic magic.

**Words and Names**: Writing was intrinsically magical to the Egyptians. Hieroglyphics carried power. Naming a thing gave power over it - the secret True Name gave total power.

**The Multi-Part Soul**: The Egyptians believed the body carried multiple spiritual forces:
- **Ka**: The astral double, vehicle for consciousness
- **Ba**: The breath, life force (vampires steal this as blood)
- **Ab**: The heart, source of good or evil thoughts, record of deeds
- **Haibit**: The shadow, self-destructive evil aspect (some equate with the Beast)
- **Sekhem**: Spiritual vitality and magical power
- **Ren**: The true name, essence of identity
- **Khu**: The shining, aura visible to spiritual perceptions

### The Afterlife

The Egyptians believed in two realms of the dead:
- **Amenti (Western Lands)**: Received souls found worthy by Osiris
- **Duat**: The dark Underworld, a grim place of torment for the unworthy

### Blasphemy Shrines

Setite sorcerers must perform rites of desecration to work magic - they reverse the conditions that send souls to Amenti by mutilating cadavers, breaking bones, and defiling grave goods. This pulls magical power from Amenti to fuel their rituals.

### The Book of Going Forth by Night

Set's book gives full instructions for Opening the Gate and other rituals. The book can be written in hieroglyphics on papyrus, or in modern languages in printed form.

### Tools and Techniques

- **Amulets**: Magical objects that continuously protect (sa or meket)
- **Magic Rods**: Various rods used to trace circles and direct magic
- **Funerary Paraphernalia**: Canopic jars, sarcophagi, mummification materials
- **Stelae**: Stone tablets carved with spells
- **Figurines**: Icons of gods used in rituals
- **Sand**: Used for spiritual cleanliness and exorcism
- **The Circle**: Drawn to hold forces in or out

### Notable Gods

Extensive list of Egyptian gods and their roles in Akhu, including Osiris, Ra, Isis, Horus, Set, Thoth, Ptah, Heka, Khnum, Anubis, Amun, and many others.

### Game Mechanics

- Most Akhu path powers do not require expending vitae - the lector priest draws upon the blasphemy shrine's power instead.
- Different paths may use different rolls (Attribute + Ability) rather than Willpower.
- All rituals use Intelligence + Occult rolls.
- Difficulty usually equals the power or ritual's level (3-9).
- Requires at least one dot of Occult and one dot of Linguistics (Egyptian).

### Akhu Paths

- **Divine Hand**: Sympathetic magic affecting objects and people through models
- **Death of Duat**: Conjures attributes of the Egyptian netherworld to inflict mental and hypnotic effects
- **Ushabti**: Brings figurines to life as servants
- **Setite Alchemy**: Creates magical salves, unguents, and potions (Milk of Set)

## Chapter Two: Dur-An-Ki - The Demon Haunted World

### Babylonian Magic

Dur-An-Ki (Sumerian: "Master of Heaven and Earth") is the Middle Eastern tradition of blood magic, derived from Mesopotamian, Persian, Jewish, Islamic, and other mortal religious and magical traditions.

### The Way of Wind

A path focused on air and wind manipulation, allowing control over weather, flight, and atmospheric phenomena.

### Fusion of Traditions

How Dur-An-Ki incorporates elements from multiple Middle Eastern cultures and religions.

### Assamite Rituals

Unique rituals practiced by Assamite sorcerers, including:
- **Gift of Mithra's Bull**: Ritual of strength and power
- **Rite of Marduk Slain and Risen**: Resurrection and renewal magic
- **From Marduk's Throat**: Voice and command magic

### New Knowledge

How Dur-An-Ki practitioners acquire and share knowledge, and the barriers to learning this tradition.

## Chapter Three: Sadhana - Meditation Upon Blood

### Indian Blood Magic

Sadhana (Sanskrit: "Attaining") is the Indian tradition of blood magic, chiefly from Hindu roots. Practitioners are called Sadhus (Achievers).

### The Path of Meditation

Sadhana focuses on meditation, spiritual discipline, and connection to the divine. It requires understanding of Hindu philosophy and religious concepts.

### Learning Sadhana

- Generally taught only to fellow undead brahmins.
- Requires understanding of Hindu scriptures and meditation practices.
- Students must devote themselves to the religious aspects, not just the magical techniques.

### Sadhana Paths and Rituals

Unique paths and rituals rooted in Hindu mythology and philosophy, requiring both magical knowledge and spiritual understanding.

## Chapter Four: Wanga - Legacy of the Caribbean

### Afro-Caribbean Blood Magic

Wanga is a blood magic tradition inspired by Voodoo and other African and Afro-Caribbean religions and magical beliefs. Practitioners are called Wangateurs (some call themselves houngans, bokors, or aymberos).

### The Loa and Spirits

Wanga involves working with powerful spirits (loa, saints, mystères) worshipped in Voodoo, Santeria, and other Afro-Caribbean religions.

### Religious Requirements

Students of Wanga generally must devote themselves to one of the Afro-Caribbean religions. The magic cannot be separated from the religious context.

### Wanga Paths and Rituals

Unique paths and rituals that require understanding of Voodoo, Santeria, or related traditions, and often involve working with spirits and loa.

## Appendix: Mysterious Ways

### The Challenge from Beyond

As Western vampires encounter their counterparts in and from other lands, they encounter unfamiliar blood magic. The Tremere now face a minor but growing challenge to their long-assumed monopoly on Thaumaturgy.

### Reasons to Research

The book encourages players and Storytellers to research real-world folklore and mythology to inspire new paths, rituals, characters, and stories. Libraries and bookstores hold treasuries of lore that can inspire game material.

### Learning Times

Estimated learning times for rituals:
- Level One: One week
- Level Two: One month
- Level Three: One season (three months)
- Level Four: Two seasons
- Level Five: One year

Under optimal conditions (devoting every waking moment to study), characters can cut these times in half.

### Creating New Paths

Rules for creating entirely new paths:
- Requires minimum Occult 5 and the path's Trait at 5
- Needs a laboratory
- Each level requires extended Intelligence + Occult rolls (difficulty 5)
- Each roll represents a full year of research
- Character expends five blood points per level
- Rolling a botch means losing all accumulated successes

### Creating New Rituals

Rules for creating new rituals:
- Character needs Occult higher than the ritual level
- Must research by means appropriate to the tradition
- Extended action roll: Intelligence + Occult (difficulty = ritual's level, max 10)
- Must accumulate three times the ritual's level in successes
- Each increment takes time based on the ritual's level
- Character must expend two blood points per level

### Blood Magic Errata

Corrections to previously published material, including the Level Five power for Setite Sorcery path "The Snake Inside" - Temperance.

## Key Themes

- **Cultural Barriers**: Each blood magic tradition is deeply rooted in specific cultures, religions, and worldviews. Learning cross-cultural magic requires understanding and accepting those beliefs.
- **Religion and Magic**: Unlike Hermetic Thaumaturgy, these traditions cannot be separated from their religious contexts. A student cannot say "Nevermind the sermon, just teach me how to cast the spell."
- **The Tremere Challenge**: The destruction of the Tremere antitribu and upheavals in both sects have broken the Tremere monopoly on blood magic, creating new opportunities and challenges.
- **Global Expansion**: As the world becomes smaller, Western vampires encounter non-Western blood magic, and the Tremere seek to master these foreign arts.
- **Tradition vs. Innovation**: The tension between preserving traditional forms and adapting to new circumstances.

## Purpose and Value

Blood Sacrifice - The Thaumaturgy Companion serves as:

- An expansion of blood magic beyond Hermetic Thaumaturgy.
- A guide to four distinct non-Western blood magic traditions.
- Rules for learning and using cross-cultural blood magic.
- Information on the cultural and religious contexts of different magical traditions.
- A resource for creating new paths and rituals inspired by real-world folklore.

This book is essential for anyone wanting to explore blood magic beyond the Tremere, understand the diversity of magical traditions in the World of Darkness, or incorporate non-Western cultures and religions into their chronicles. It provides both mechanical systems and cultural context for these rich and complex magical traditions.
