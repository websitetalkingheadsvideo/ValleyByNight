# Clanbook: Brujah (Revised)

This supplement for Vampire: The Masquerade provides comprehensive information on Clan Brujah, the "Learned Clan" known for their passion, rage, and role as agents of change throughout Kindred history. The book explores the clan's history from the philosopher-kings of Biblical times to the revolutionaries and rabble-rousers of the modern nights, revealing both their noble past and their current reputation as hot-headed troublemakers.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book provides detailed information on Clan Brujah for players and Storytellers.
- It explores the clan's history as both philosophers and warriors.
- Examines the division between the clan's noble past and its current reputation.
- Provides character creation options, new Disciplines, and information on Brujah culture.

## Introduction: A Night Like Any Other

The book opens with a story of a mortal's Embrace into the Brujah clan, illustrating the clan's methods and philosophy. The Embrace is presented as a test of will and desire for change, with the sire pushing the potential childe to prove they truly want more from unlife.

## Chapter One: Millennia of Conflict

### The Story of the Brujah

The story of the Brujah reads like a shadow account of human history. Every major conflict in the World of Darkness has likely had a Brujah on one side or the other. The Brujah have agitated, argued for change, effected it, or been left to lick their wounds in the aftermath of their failed revolutions.

### The Second City

As far back as anyone claims to remember, the history of the Brujah is fraught with controversy. Most Brujah believe that their legendary progenitor was himself a rebel. Of the Third Generation, Brujah may have incited the parricide of the Second Generation. Many believe that the other members of the Third Generation considered Brujah a troublemaker.

### Brujah and the Wine Seller

One common story tells of Brujah tormenting and finally killing a wine seller in the Second City. This story illustrates how the Kindred of the Second City abused mortals with impunity. If this tale is true, it answers questions about why the other members of the Third Generation mistrusted Brujah.

### Troile

A figure surrounded in as much mystery as Brujah himself, Troile represents for many Brujah what has become the modern embodiment of the clan. Sources discussing Troile vary greatly, even on the matter of the childe's gender. By all modern belief, Troile committed the Amaranth upon his sire, which is one of the most popular reasons accepted for the start of the kinslayer wars that ultimately destroyed the Second City.

### The Division of Blood

One of the most pervasive Brujah rumors concerns the different lineages spawned by Brujah and Troile. The general consensus is that the clan truly descends from Troile, who committed diablerie upon Brujah. The Curse of Troile - the clan's propensity for rage - may be one of the more backhanded blights Caine attributed to his childer.

### The Modern Brujah

From the proud philosopher-kings of Biblical times to the prophets and revolutionaries of the Final Nights, the Brujah have always embodied a current zeitgeist. If the Brujah are raging fiends, it is because that is what the world now demands of them.

## Chapter Two: Unlife Among the Brujah

### Raves

Brujah raves are manic parties undertaken with reckless abandon. Whether gala affairs or raucous, secret garage parties, raves are places where Brujah can convene, put their ideologies behind them and let their hair down. The typical rave offers no end of hedonistic excess - dancing, thunderous music, drug- and alcohol-addled vessels, kine to flirt with or bully, and perhaps a good-natured brawl.

### Prestige Rants

Prestige rants are gatherings where Brujah achievements are recognized and the assembled Brujah can have a mean-spirited laugh at the follies of their peers. These affairs often require an invitation, and to decline risks even more public mockery than simply attending and taking your licks.

### Violence

Brawls between Brujah tend to be affectionate or competitive, as with mortal siblings. It's just a bit of friendly roughhousing, or the odd bit of alpha-Lick assertion. This typically goes over the heads of other Kindred, however, who either assume the worst and flee or join in as if unlives were at stake.

### The Antitribu

The Brujah antitribu arose as did many of the other anti-clans, as a result of sire-childe grievances that culminated in the Anarch Revolt and the formation of the Sabbat. As members of the Sabbat, the Brujah antitribu don't typically follow the Traditions as a code. The single greatest difference between Brujah of the Camarilla and the Sabbat looks to be the fire of rebellion and change.

### The Traditions

As much as any Brujah desires change and challenge, only the most die-hard anarchist would dare suggest dispensing with the Traditions. The Brujah have to be very careful, however, as these rants tend to require an invitation, and to decline risks even more public mockery.

### The Masquerade

Ask any Brujah about the Ventrue and you probably work him into a lather. It's not entirely undeserved - over the course of centuries, the Ventrue have placed themselves center stage in almost every rotten double-dealing that's involved the Kindred. The Brujah warrant a little more respect because dignitas demands it, but few mistake the nastiness that lies behind a patronizing smile.

## Key Themes

- **Agents of Change**: The Brujah have always been at the forefront of revolution and change.
- **The Fall from Grace**: The clan's history shows a fall from philosopher-kings to rabble-rousers.
- **Passion and Rage**: The clan's curse drives them to extremes of emotion and action.
- **The Division**: The tension between the clan's noble past and its current reputation.
- **Rebellion**: The Brujah's role in the Anarch Revolt and their relationship with authority.
- **The Learned Clan**: Despite their reputation, the Brujah were once known for their scholarship and philosophy.

## Purpose and Value

Clanbook: Brujah (Revised) serves as:
- Comprehensive information on Clan Brujah
- The history of the clan from its noble beginnings to its current state
- Exploration of the clan's dual nature as both philosophers and warriors
- Character creation options for Brujah characters
- New Disciplines and powers
- Information on Brujah culture, including raves and prestige rants
- Notable members and roleplaying guidance

This book is essential for anyone wanting to play or understand Brujah characters, providing the complete history of the "Learned Clan" and exploring how they evolved from philosopher-kings to the passionate revolutionaries and rabble-rousers of the modern nights.
