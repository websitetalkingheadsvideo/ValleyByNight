# Storyteller's Guide

The document is a comprehensive guide for Storytellers running Mind's Eye Theatre Vampire: The Masquerade chronicles, providing advanced rules, rare bloodlines, blood magic paths, conspiracies, and storytelling advice.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book is for Storytellers, Assistant Storytellers, and Narrators who entertain notions of becoming Storytellers.
- It is part translation from the Vampire Storytellers Handbook and part collection of things Storytellers are likely to find helpful and exciting.
- It's not about being given a Bible of Storytelling or a mandate on how to run your game - think of it as Storytellers hanging out, talking about this thing they love and spend so much free time on.
- The book includes new blood magic paths from Blood Magic: Secrets of Thaumaturgy.

## Introduction: The Storyteller's Creed

The book opens with "The Storyteller's Creed" - a mission statement and mantra for Storytellers:

- I am the Storyteller. I am here to tell stories.
- People come to me to have a good time, and I will do my best to give it to them.
- Without the story, there is no game. A good story is my goal and my guide.
- I will recognize that I can't please everyone, no matter what I do. When in doubt, I will favor the story.
- I will be fair and balanced in my judgments. I will not play favorites or punish without cause.
- I will do what is best for the game - I will deny the bad character concept, the unreasonable request, whining and temper tantrums.
- I will have the guts to do what is necessary, especially when it's unpleasant.
- I will do my best to be open to the players' needs and understanding of their concerns.
- If I find myself not having fun, I will give myself a break rather than let the game or players suffer.

## Chapter Two: The Thousand Eyes

An introduction to some of the most disturbing denizens of the World of Darkness - rare and dangerous bloodlines that have almost vanished:

### The Baali

- Even monsters have their bogeymen - to their erstwhile cousins, the Baali are the devils under the bed.
- Legend claims an Antediluvian dallied with a singing slave-boy who turned against the Third Generation, wrought foul pacts with forces between shadows, and gathered followers.
- Once thought eradicated by the Inquisition, the Baali have come into awareness of modern Cainites.
- Never numerous, their ranks have dwindled further in recent nights.
- They are not cackling minions of evil, but sages and academics intent on discovering knowledge few others have the courage to pursue.
- **Disciplines**: Daimoinon, Obfuscate, Presence
- **Advantage**: All Baali gain a free Occult Ability. Neonate Demons may also choose one bonus trait in University or Occult Influence.
- **Disadvantage**: Baali are particularly affected by acts of faith. When confronting a mortal with True Faith, all Baali must risk twice the normal number of Traits. If struck by a holy symbol wielded by one of True Faith, the Baali takes twice the damage.

### The Nagaraja

- Reviled for their unsavory practices by all but the most charitable of Kindred.
- Their ranks have eroded to the point of insignificance - less than 20 (some say no more than a dozen) remain.
- Most tales put their origins somewhere in the Middle East.
- They are necromancers on par with (or greater than) the Giovanni.
- Not only the blood of their victims is needed to sate their hunger but the flesh as well.
- **Disciplines**: Auspex, Dominate, Necromancy
- **Advantage**: All Nagaraja receive a free Subterfuge Ability. Each may choose one Medicine, Occult or University Influence trait at no cost.
- **Disadvantage**: Not only must a Nagaraja drink blood, she must also eat of her prey's flesh. Without fresh meat, a Nagaraja's body wastes away. Nagaraja do not have retractable fangs - instead, their mouths are full of sharp teeth like a piranha's.

### The True Brujah

- Every family has its secrets - the True Brujah have borne one such secret for centuries.
- Sagacious and erudite, the Elois are the scholars the Brujah often aspire to be.
- They claim the founder of their blood was slain by its childe ages ago, and that those who claim the name Brujah are no more than the bastard childer of that ancient diablerist.
- Cold and imperturbable, the Elois pursue ancient lore with serpentlike detachment.
- Their Blood seems to deaden the spirit as it preserves the flesh, immutable as stone.
- **Disciplines**: Potence, Presence, Temporis
- **Advantage**: Each True Brujah receives one Academics Ability at no cost and may choose an additional Ability in any one Lore for no cost.
- **Disadvantage**: The blood of the Elois smothers their emotions. Elois wishing to increase their Morality or Virtue traits must pay double the standard experience point costs. On all Conscience and Conviction challenges, True Brujah must bid two extra Traits or automatically lose the bid.

## Chapter Three: Blood Magic

The new blood magic paths and rituals from Blood Magic: Secrets of Thaumaturgy, including:

### Path of the Blood's Curse

- Developed by rogue Pander and Caitiff thaumaturges.
- May only be learned by Kindred of 10th generation or weaker.
- Powers only work on vampires of equal or better generation.
- **Basic**: Ravages Of The Beast, Weight Of The Sun
- **Intermediate**: Abated Tooth, Treacherous Bonds
- **Advanced**: The Withering Of Ages

### Path of Curses

- A natural extension of human spite, developed early in the history of Thaumaturgy.
- The accursed usually know exactly who is making their unlives miserable.
- **Basic**: Stigma, Malady
- **Intermediate**: Pariah, Corrupt Body
- **Advanced**: Fall From Grace

## Purpose and Value

This guide serves to:

- Provide advanced material for experienced Storytellers
- Introduce rare and dangerous elements that can shake up chronicles
- Offer new blood magic paths and advanced Disciplines
- Provide practical advice for running better games
- Help Storytellers handle difficult situations

The Storyteller's Guide is essential for anyone wanting to run advanced or complex Mind's Eye Theatre chronicles, providing tools, antagonists, and advice for creating memorable and challenging games while maintaining safety and fun for all players.
