# MET Apocalypse

This book is the core rulebook for Mind's Eye Theatre Werewolf: The Apocalypse, providing all the essential rules, systems, and information needed to play and run live-action roleplaying games as the Garou (werewolves) in the World of Darkness. The book presents the Garou as defenders of Gaia against the Wyrm, a corrupting force seeking to destroy all of creation, in a world where the Apocalypse is imminent.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book contains all the information needed to start playing Mind's Eye Theatre Werewolf: The Apocalypse.
- It is based on the tabletop game Werewolf: The Apocalypse, adapted for live-action play.
- Provides elegantly simple rules designed to be easy to play and easier to start.
- Focuses on the Garou's war against the Wyrm and their role as defenders of Gaia.

## Chapter One: Introduction

### What is Mind's Eye Theatre?

Mind's Eye Theatre is a live-action roleplaying game where players physically act out their characters rather than sitting around a table. Players become both actors and screenwriters as stories unfold in real time. The game emphasizes storytelling and improvisational theatre, with Narrators serving as mediators and storytellers.

### The Art of Storytelling

The book emphasizes storytelling as a social activity that brings communities together. Live-action storytelling is presented as a step up from tabletop gaming, giving players control over their characters and freedom to guide themselves through another world.

### The Narrator

The Narrator is the foundation of stories in The Apocalypse. Responsibilities include:
- Detailing the world that players live in
- Settling disputes when they arise
- Playing the roles of all characters the players meet
- Hosting the game and keeping the story going
- Ensuring player safety and rule adherence

It is advised to have one Narrator for every six or seven players.

### The Character

Characters in The Apocalypse represent the majority of characters involved in the story and control the outcome of the game. Creating a character is easy and takes only a few moments, requiring only a few abilities to define a basic character. However, players must flesh out and breathe life into the character, defining personality and determining role in the story.

### Elegantly Simple

The game was designed to be simple to play. The rules are intended to be easy to understand and use, with the story being more important than the rules. Rules are meant as guidelines rather than absolute laws.

### The Rules of Safety

Critical safety rules that must always be followed:
- **Don't Touch!** Never actually carry out any sort of physical contest.
- **No Stunts!** Don't get physical - never climb, jump, run, leap or swing during a game.
- **No Weapons!** Fake and real weapons of any sort are absolutely forbidden.
- **No Drugs or Drinking** - impaired players are a danger to others and the game.
- **It's Only a Game** - don't take things too seriously.
- **Be Mindful of Others** - not everyone around you is playing the game.
- **Have Fun** - the goal is to tell stories, not to achieve superiority.

## Chapter Two: Setting

### A World of Darkness

The world of The Apocalypse is similar to our own but grittier, more corrupt, and more tainted. This is expressed through Gothic-Punk aesthetics - combining the sinister, looming shadows of Gothic art with the raw rage, random violence, and rebellion of the Punk Movement.

### The Garou

Werewolves in The Apocalypse are not the creatures of movie screens but creatures of far-reaching mythic status. They are both heroes and monsters, a combination of wolves and men. A Garou is born either as a wolf or a human, and learns to take on other forms after maturing. The crucial fact of whether the Garou's mother was a wolf or a human determines their "breed."

### The Silver Record: A History of the Garou

The Garou's sacred writings describe how their race emerged from the raw earth and were given gifts by Luna. The Record speaks of when the Garou were given:
- The power of shapechanging
- The strength of Rage
- The insight of Gnosis
- The freedom of spirit-walking

These were the birth-gifts given by the Four Winds.

### The Garou's Birth-Gifts

A Garou can control the shape he wears, choosing from a spectrum ranging from human to wolf. The most powerful form is the **Crinos form**, the half-wolf, half-man shape where they are towering monsters and deadly warriors.

Garou are creatures of **Rage** and **Gnosis**. Their Rage enables them to be deadly warriors and move faster than the eye can see. Their Gnosis allows them to tap into the spiritual wealth of Gaia and use spiritual magic. Garou are beings of two worlds: the Earth and the spirit world, called the **Umbra**.

### The Sacred Ways of the Garou

Over the centuries, spirits have given wisdom and knowledge of special powers to the Garou. These powers and secrets are shared among members of a Garou's breed and auspice, further defining a Garou's purpose and role within a tribe.

### The Tribes

The Record speaks of fifteen groupings of Garou that originally formed. Among the first were:
- **Silver Fangs** - rulers and warrior-heroes
- **Shadow Lords** - beta-wolves, schemers and politicians
- **Black Furies** - alpha females who followed their own path
- **Stargazers** - mystical, philosophical Garou who watched the skies

Other tribes include the Fianna, Get of Fenris, Silent Striders, Uktena, Wendigo, Croatan, Bunyip, White Howlers, Glass Walkers, Bone Gnawers, Red Talons, and Children of Gaia.

Now only thirteen tribes remain after the loss of the Bunyip, Croatan, and White Howlers (who became the Black Spiral Dancers).

### The War for Gaia

The Garou are at war with the Wyrm in defense of Gaia. Many believe these are the last days of that war, that the prophecies of the Apocalypse are coming true. This threat influences all of the Garou's actions. Garou tend to fluctuate between two extremes:
- **Frenzy** - totally consumed with hatred of the Wyrm and all corruption
- **Harano** - totally overwhelmed by the imbalance of the fight and become utterly depressed

### The Wyrm

The Wyrm is a fundamental force in the universe, seen by some as the power of corruption and oblivion. It seeks to devour all of Gaia. The Wyrm used to be the balancing factor in the Triat, maintaining balance between the Weaver (patterned reality and order) and the Wyld (chaos, magic and pure creative energy). However, the Weaver went mad, and the Wyrm became corrupted and now seeks to consume everything.

The Wyrm manifests itself in many forms - some physical, although the actual Wyrm resides in a metaphorical Pattern Web. The spirit army of the Wyrm consists of corrupted Incarna, Bane soldiers, and Wyrm Elementals.

## Key Themes

- **The War Against the Wyrm**: The Garou's eternal struggle to defend Gaia from corruption and destruction.
- **The Imminent Apocalypse**: The sense that these are the last days and the final battle is approaching.
- **Balance Between Extremes**: The tension between frenzy and Harano, between action and despair.
- **Tribal Unity and Division**: The need for tribes to work together despite their differences.
- **The Veil**: The necessity of hiding the Garou's existence from humanity.
- **Spiritual Connection**: The Garou's relationship with Gaia, spirits, and the Umbra.

## Purpose and Value

MET Apocalypse serves as:
- The complete rulebook for playing Werewolf: The Apocalypse in live-action format
- A guide to the Garou's world, culture, and war against the Wyrm
- Information on character creation, rules, and systems for MET Werewolf
- Advice for both players and Narrators on running successful games

This book is essential for anyone wanting to play or run live-action Werewolf: The Apocalypse games, providing all the tools needed to tell stories of the Garou's struggle against the Wyrm in the final nights before the Apocalypse.
