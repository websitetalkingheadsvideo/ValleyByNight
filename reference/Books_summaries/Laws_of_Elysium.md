# Laws of Elysium

The document is a comprehensive guide to playing elder characters in Mind's Eye Theatre Vampire: The Masquerade, providing everything needed to play ancient, powerful vampires who have survived for centuries.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book outlines elders for The Masquerade, what a player should be ready for, and what a Storyteller needs.
- From rules to character creation to storytelling, there is plenty here to use and abuse.
- **Not recommended for mere children of the night** - elder characters require more thought, time, and maturity than average vampires.

## Introduction: What It Means to Be an Elder

### Definition

- An elder is simply defined as a vampire who has managed to survive for at least 250 years.
- Such a short definition does not, however, cover the many requirements the elders demand before one is allowed to sit at their table.
- It takes strength, resources, and cunning to arrive at the coveted title of "elder" - age is no longer the sole prerequisite.

### The Elder's Place in Kindred Society

- The Camarilla was founded on the will of the elders, and even now they continue as its strongest proponents and leaders.
- Elders make up the upper ranks of the Inner Circle and Justicars, and often some of the most powerful princes.
- Yet they are also the favored pawns of the Methuselahs in their unending Jyhad.
- In the Sabbat, the elders are the bishops and paladins, feared by the younger ranks.
- Elders are well aware that the Anarch Revolt was started because of elders who overstepped their bounds.

### The Impact of Elders

- An elder's appearance can easily throw a chronicle into a tizzy, even if she doesn't directly affect the action.
- Why is she here? What does she want? Does her presence bode good or ill?
- Her age and experience will draw many younger vampires into her sphere, hoping for favorable attention or assistance.
- She will be affected by her extremely long unlife, often unable to adapt to new technology, bearing long grudges and hatreds.
- The only emotions left to her often become hate, rage, and bitterness.

## Just Visiting: A Cautionary Tale

The book opens with a narrative vignette about Alaric Montfleury, an ancient elder visiting a modern city. The story illustrates:

- How an elder's mere presence can disrupt an entire city's power structure
- The casual way elders can demonstrate their superiority
- How elders can manipulate situations without direct action
- The paranoia and speculation that follows an elder's arrival
- The way elders view modern Kindred as children playing at being vampires

## Character Creation

### Warning

- This is a quick-and-dirty guide to elder creation.
- Assumes familiarity with the Mind's Eye Theatre character creation process.
- Elder characters are not meant for new, inexperienced, or immature players.

### Steps of Character Creation

**Step One: Inspiration - Who are you?**
- Choose your clan
- Choose your Nature and Demeanor
- Consider when your character was Embraced
- Think about what she was before her change and afterward
- Consider three events that marked turning points in her unlife

**Step Two: Attributes - What are your basic capabilities?**
- Unlike regular vampires, elders' categories fall as 10 Primary, eight Secondary, and six Tertiary (instead of 7/5/3)
- Choose Traits that reflect centuries of experience

**Step Three: Advantages - What do you know?**
- **Abilities**: Elder characters begin with eight Abilities (instead of five)
- **Disciplines**: Elders may begin with six Disciplines (instead of three)
- **Influences**: Six makes a good starting point. The Influences listed are from level six and up

**Step Four: Last Touches - Fill in the details**
- **Generation**: Elders generally are of the eighth generation, although Pretenders attempt to presume eldership at ninth
- **Status**: Most elders have at least two Status Traits: Acknowledged and Respected
- **Willpower and Blood Traits**: See Laws of the Night for starting numbers based on generation
- **Beast/Path Traits**: Elder characters begin with two Beast/Path Traits

**Step Five: Spark of Life - Narrative descriptions**
- Background story covering centuries of unlife
- When was your character Embraced, and what did she do before then?
- What events in history has she witnessed, and how did they affect her?

### The Name Game

- When creating an elder character, put thought into her name
- Names like "Slash," "Blade," "Desire" are fine for neonates, but inappropriate for elders
- Consider: How old is your character? What is her heritage? Where is she from?
- Baby name books and telephone books are excellent resources

## Disciplines and Rituals

The book provides detailed information on elder and master-level Disciplines:

### Auspex Master Powers
- **Clairvoyance**: See and hear events without Psychic Projection
- **Soul Scan**: Discover the "location" of anyone you know

### Celerity Master Powers
- **Quickness**: Disappear in an incredible burst of speed (four extra challenges)
- **Velocity**: Move faster than most conscious minds can think (five extra challenges)

### Chimerstry Powers
- **Fata Morgana**: Create illusions visible to one person
- **Pseudo Blindness**: No longer affected by Obfuscate or Chimerstry below Advanced
- **Mass Reality**: Cause whole groups to experience Horrid Reality

### Dementation Master Powers
- **Kindred Spirits**: Force victim to become identical to you in many respects
- **Mind of a Killer**: Alter the mind, making her a cold-blooded killer

### Dominate Master Powers
- **Obedience**: Use all Dominate Disciplines without eye contact
- **Rationale**: Those you Dominate are convinced their actions are entirely of their own design

### Fortitude Master Powers
- **Imperviousness**: Negate nonaggravated wounds with Simple Tests
- **Invulnerability**: Negate aggravated wounds with Simple Tests

### Other Advanced Disciplines
- **Melpominee**: Daughters of Cacophony powers
- **Mytherceria**: Kiasyd powers
- Various other clan-specific and rare Disciplines at master level

## Playing an Elder

### The Challenges

- Elders are centuries old and require more thought and time than average vampires
- Their mere presence can unbalance a chronicle if thrown in without thought
- Immature players can run roughshod over the story and other players simply by dint of enhanced abilities
- Elders must roleplay the weight of centuries, not just use powerful abilities

### Roleplaying Considerations

- Think about who your character was before she was Embraced
- Consider how unlife has changed her over the years
- Many attitudes may have crystallized when she was Embraced
- Think about what she was before her change and afterward
- Consider friendships and interests maintained for all these years
- Think of three events that marked turning points in her unlife

### The Elder's Perspective

- Elders have watched civilizations rise and fall
- They measure their lifespans by eras of human history
- They often cannot adapt to new technology
- They bear long grudges and hatreds
- The only emotions left often become hate, rage, and bitterness
- They view modern Kindred as children

## Storytelling for Elders

### Integrating Elders into Chronicles

- Elders should not be thrown into chronicles without thought
- Their presence should have purpose and impact
- Consider how their arrival affects existing power structures
- Think about what they want and why they're there
- Use elders to shake up stagnant chronicles

### Creating Challenges for Elders

- Elders are powerful, but not invincible
- Create challenges appropriate to their power level
- Use other elders, Methuselahs, or unique threats
- Remember that power brings responsibility and enemies
- Elders have long memories and many enemies

### Managing Elder Players

- Ensure elder players understand the responsibility
- Monitor for power-gaming or disruptive behavior
- Help players roleplay the weight of centuries
- Balance elder power with appropriate challenges
- Ensure all players have meaningful experiences

## The Only Rules That Matter

Behave yourself so everyone can enjoy the game:

1. **It's Only a Game**: Don't take things too seriously
2. **No Touching**: Never actually have physical contact with another player
3. **No Stunts**: Never climb, jump, run, leap or swing from anything
4. **No Weapons**: Fake or real weapons of any sort are forbidden
5. **No Drugs or Drinking**: Drugs and alcohol impair performance
6. **Be Mindful of Others**: Remember non-players may be watching
7. **The Rules Are Flexible**: Feel free to adjust rules for better games

## Purpose and Value

This book serves to:

- Prepare both Storytellers and would-be players for the challenges of elder characters
- Provide rules for creating and playing elder characters
- Offer advanced Disciplines and powers
- Guide Storytellers in integrating elders into chronicles
- Help players understand what it means to play centuries-old vampires
- Ensure elder characters enhance rather than disrupt games

Laws of Elysium is essential for anyone wanting to play or run elder characters in Mind's Eye Theatre, providing comprehensive information about creating, playing, and storytelling with ancient vampires while emphasizing responsible play and maintaining game balance.
