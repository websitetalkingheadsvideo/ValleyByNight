# Clanbook: Gangrel (Revised)

This supplement for Vampire: The Masquerade provides comprehensive information on Clan Gangrel, the "Wild Ones" who have recently departed from the Camarilla. The book explores the clan's history, their relationship with the wilderness, their independence, and the aftermath of their departure from the sect. The book examines how Gangrel navigate a world where they are no longer part of the Camarilla but are not necessarily enemies either.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book provides detailed information on Clan Gangrel for players and Storytellers.
- It explores the clan's recent departure from the Camarilla and its consequences.
- Examines the clan's relationship with the wilderness and their independent nature.
- Provides character creation options, new Disciplines, and information on Gangrel culture.

## Introduction: Aftermath of Independence

The book opens with a story illustrating the consequences of the Gangrel's departure from the Camarilla. A Gangrel who left the sect finds himself facing hostility from Camarilla Kindred, including a former clanmate who has been blood bound to serve a prince. The story demonstrates the complex situation facing independent Gangrel in the modern nights.

## Chapter One: Stone, Steppe, and Sea - A History of the Gangrel Clan

### The Origins

The Gangrel trace their lineage to Ennoia, one of the Third Generation. The clan has always been associated with the wilderness, with animals, and with independence. Unlike many other clans, the Gangrel have never been fully integrated into Kindred society, preferring to maintain their autonomy.

### The Wild Ones

The Gangrel have always been the "Wild Ones" - Kindred who maintain their connection to the natural world and resist the constraints of civilization. They are known for their shapeshifting abilities through Protean, their connection to animals, and their fierce independence.

### The Departure

In recent nights, the Gangrel have officially departed from the Camarilla. This decision was made by the clan's leadership, led by Xaviar, who felt that the Camarilla's restrictions and politics were incompatible with the Gangrel way of life. The departure has left many Gangrel in a difficult position - they are no longer part of the Camarilla, but they are not necessarily enemies either.

### The Aftermath

The departure has created tension between Gangrel and Camarilla Kindred. Some princes view independent Gangrel with suspicion, while others are more tolerant. Some Gangrel have been blood bound to serve Camarilla princes, creating further division within the clan.

## Chapter Two: Inside the Gangrel

### Clan Culture

The Gangrel maintain a loose, informal organization. They value independence, self-reliance, and connection to the natural world. The clan has no formal hierarchy, though elders are respected for their experience and wisdom.

### The Wilderness

Gangrel are deeply connected to the wilderness. They understand animals, can communicate with them, and often prefer the company of beasts to that of other Kindred. This connection to nature sets them apart from other clans.

### Independence

The Gangrel's independent nature means they rarely form lasting alliances or organizations. They come together when necessary but prefer to operate alone or in small groups. This independence has served them well but also makes them vulnerable.

### Relationships with Other Clans

The Gangrel have complex relationships with other clans:
- They respect the Nosferatu for their survival skills
- They have a long-standing rivalry with the Ventrue
- They share some common ground with the Brujah
- They are often at odds with the Tremere

## Key Themes

- **Independence**: The Gangrel's fierce independence and resistance to authority.
- **The Wilderness**: The clan's deep connection to the natural world and animals.
- **The Departure**: The consequences of leaving the Camarilla and navigating a world where they are no longer part of the sect.
- **Survival**: The Gangrel's focus on survival and self-reliance.
- **The Beast**: The clan's acceptance and mastery of their bestial nature.
- **Loneliness**: The price of independence - the Gangrel often find themselves alone.
