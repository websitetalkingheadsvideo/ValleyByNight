# Clanbook: Ravnos (Revised)

This supplement for Vampire: The Masquerade provides comprehensive information on Clan Ravnos, the "Deceivers" known for their trickery, illusion, and nomadic lifestyle. The book explores the clan's history, their role as wanderers and con artists, their relationship with the Romani people, and the recent events that have devastated the clan. The book examines how Ravnos navigate a world where they are often distrusted but also needed for their unique skills.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book provides detailed information on Clan Ravnos for players and Storytellers.
- It explores the clan's history as wanderers and tricksters.
- Examines the clan's relationship with the Romani and their nomadic culture.
- Provides character creation options, new Disciplines, and information on Ravnos culture.

## Introduction: The Devil's Due

The book opens with a story of a mortal's Embrace into the Ravnos clan, illustrating the clan's methods and the transformation from mortal to Kindred. The story follows an archaeologist who becomes involved with a Ravnos smuggler, leading to her Embrace and introduction to the clan's world of deception and illusion.

## Chapter One: Agreed Upon Lies

### The History of Deception

The Ravnos trace their lineage to Ravana, one of the Third Generation. The clan has always been associated with trickery, illusion, and deception. They are wanderers who have never fully integrated into Kindred society, preferring to maintain their independence and mobility.

### The Romani Connection

The Ravnos have a long-standing association with the Romani people, though the exact nature of this relationship is complex and often misunderstood. Many Ravnos are of Romani descent, and the clan's culture reflects Romani traditions of travel, storytelling, and survival through wit and deception.

### The Nomadic Life

The Ravnos are wanderers by nature. They rarely settle in one place for long, preferring to travel and experience new places and people. This nomadic lifestyle has shaped their culture and their approach to unlife.

### The Art of Being Ravnos

Being Ravnos means mastering the art of deception, illusion, and survival. Ravnos learn to read people, to manipulate situations, and to always have an escape plan. They are masters of misdirection and illusion, using their powers to survive in a world that often distrusts them.

## Chapter Two: On the Art of Being Ravnos

### Clan Culture

The Ravnos have no formal organization or hierarchy. They are too independent and nomadic for such structures. Instead, they form loose networks based on family ties, shared experiences, or temporary alliances.

### The Path of Paradox

Many Ravnos follow the Path of Paradox, a philosophy that embraces contradiction and change. This path reflects the Ravnos' nature as tricksters and wanderers who see truth in contradiction and meaning in change.

### Relationships with Other Clans

The Ravnos have complex relationships with other clans:
- They are often distrusted for their trickery and deception
- Some clans find them useful for their skills in illusion and misdirection
- They are sometimes dismissed as harmless wanderers
- Their nomadic nature makes them difficult to control or predict

## Chapter Three: Orphans in Gehenna's Wake

### Character Creation

Guidance on creating Ravnos characters:

### Notable Members

Profiles of significant Ravnos, including their achievements, methods, and roles in Kindred society.

## Key Themes

- **Deception**: The clan's mastery of illusion, trickery, and misdirection.
- **The Nomadic Life**: The Ravnos' preference for travel and their resistance to settling down.
- **The Romani Connection**: The clan's relationship with the Romani people and their shared culture.
- **Survival**: The Ravnos' focus on survival through wit, deception, and adaptability.
- **Independence**: The clan's fierce independence and resistance to authority.
- **Change**: The Ravnos' embrace of change and contradiction.
