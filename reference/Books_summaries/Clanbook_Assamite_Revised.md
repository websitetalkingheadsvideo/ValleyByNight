# Clanbook: Assamite (Revised)

This supplement for Vampire: The Masquerade provides comprehensive information on the Children of Haqim, commonly known as the Assamites. The book explores the clan's history, organization, culture, and the recent schism that has divided the clan between those seeking Camarilla membership and those remaining loyal to the ancient ways of Alamut. The book reveals that much of what other Kindred believe about the Assamites is based on lies and misconceptions.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book provides detailed information on the Assamite clan for players and Storytellers.
- It reveals the true history of the Children of Haqim, contradicting many Camarilla-held beliefs.
- Explores the recent schism within the clan following ur-Shulgi's awakening.
- Provides character creation options, new Disciplines, and information on the clan's three castes.

## Introduction: From the Treaty of Tyre

The book opens with excerpts from the Treaty of Tyre, the 1496 agreement that bound the Assamites to restrictions including the Tremere curse that made Kindred blood poisonous to them. This historical document sets the stage for understanding the clan's relationship with the Camarilla.

## Chapter One: How Quickly We Forgot

### The True History

The chapter reveals that everything other Kindred know about the Assamites is a lie. The clan was not always a band of bloodthirsty assassins. According to their own history, Haqim was asked by the other Antediluvians to create judges to maintain order among the Kindred. The Children of Haqim were originally meant to be impartial arbiters and defenders of justice.

### Haqim and the First Nights

Haqim was a warrior-turned-scholar who was Embraced and became known as the Hunter - one who sought Truth above all else. When his siblings came to him asking for judges, he reluctantly agreed, creating a second family of judges. However, his two families (the scholars and the judges) could not get along, and Haqim eventually left, never to return.

### The Second City

After the destruction of the First City, Haqim wandered and eventually settled in the Second City. He maintained neutrality during conflicts, insulating himself and his childer from the wars between other Antediluvians. This neutrality characterized Haqim's relations with Caine and the other members of the Third Generation.

### The Fall

Over time, the judges took leadership of the house, and the clan's role as judges evolved into something darker. The Children of Haqim began to judge and punish other Kindred, often through violence. The lie that they were simple assassins for hire became so ingrained that even the Children themselves began to believe it.

### The Modern Schism

With the coming of the Final Nights, the Assamite clan is in a state of flux. Recent developments have shattered preconceived notions about what Assamites are. As much as a third of the Assamite population has petitioned for membership in, or at least a truce with, the Camarilla, while another third seems to have redoubled its efforts to destroy every vampire from the face of the planet.

## Chapter Two: Prayers to Broken Stone

### Clan Structure

Although organizing vampires is like herding predatory beasts, the Assamites maintained for millennia a hierarchy unsurpassed by any clan save the Tremere. This structure has broken down under the strain of recent events, but both of the clan's major factions maintain enough of it that it is still worthy of examination.

### The Three Castes

The Assamites are organized into three castes:
- **Warriors**: The fighters and assassins of the clan, led by the Caliph
- **Viziers**: The scholars and diplomats, led by the Vizier (also known as the Amr)
- **Sorcerers**: The blood magicians, led by the Amr

### Haqim

The clan hierarchy begins with Haqim, the Assamite Antediluvian. Unlike many clan founders, Haqim's name and even appearance are well-documented. His last recorded appearance was in A.D. 121 in the British Isles, though the clan catalogues at least one "Haqim sighting" a decade. Many active members of his brood claim personal acquaintanceship with Haqim - al-Ashrad and ur-Shulgi among them.

### The Eldest

The office of Eldest began in the Second City as an unofficial honor. When Haqim left for his frequent journeys, he would place one of his childer in charge. After the Children of Haqim founded Alamut, the position became formal. The last Eldest was Jamal, who held the Black Throne from 1494 to 1998. When ur-Shulgi arose, Jamal was destroyed for refusing to renounce Allah.

### The Du'at

The du'at council is traditionally the assembly of the heads of the three Assamite castes: the Caliph of the warriors, the Vizier of the viziers, and the Amr of the sorcerers. With the destruction of one member and the subsequent departure of the other two, it is unlikely that a du'at will be re-formed in the foreseeable future.

### The Caliph

The warrior caste's leader is known as the Caliph. The Caliph is responsible for the defense of the Children as a whole and guides the warrior caste in its defense of the clan. Currently, the position of Caliph is open following ur-Shulgi's destruction of the previous holder.

### The Vizier

The leader of the vizier caste goes by the title of Vizier. The Vizier is responsible for maintaining the clan's knowledge, history, and diplomatic relations. Al-Ashrad, the former Amr and now Eldest-in-Exile, seeks to restructure his followers into an organization more palatable to the Camarilla.

### The Amr

The leader of the sorcerer caste is known as the Amr. The Amr guides the sorcerers in their study of blood magic and their role as demon-hunters. The sorcerers were originally created by Haqim to hunt demons, directly defying Saulot's protestations.

### Methods of Control

The elders of the Assamite clan use several methods to keep their underlings connected and focused:
- **Blood Magic**: Rapid long-distance communication through sorcery
- **Sense of Superiority**: Most Assamites know Haqim's Laws and the basics of their line's history
- **"Us or Them" Mentality**: Many Assamites have a strong sense of clan loyalty

## Chapter Three: Sons and Daughters of Haqim

This chapter provides information on creating Assamite characters, including:
- Character concepts for each caste
- New Disciplines and powers
- Notable members of the clan
- Roleplaying hints for different types of Assamites

### Character Creation

Guidance on creating Assamite characters:
- Choosing a caste (warrior, vizier, or sorcerer)
- Character concepts appropriate to each caste
- How the schism affects character creation
- Relationships with other clans

### Notable Members

Profiles of significant Assamites, including:
- **Al-Ashrad**: Former Amr, now Eldest-in-Exile, leader of the schismatics
- **Ur-Shulgi**: Ancient sorcerer who awakened and destroyed Jamal, leader of the loyalists
- **Mata Hari**: Famous vizier spy
- **Tegyrius**: Ancient vizier and former Vizier
- **Nar-Sheptha**: Notable sorcerer

## Key Themes

- **The Lie**: Everything other Kindred know about the Assamites is based on deception.
- **The Schism**: The clan is divided between those seeking Camarilla membership and those remaining loyal to ur-Shulgi.
- **The Three Castes**: Warriors, viziers, and sorcerers each have distinct roles and cultures.
- **Judgment and Justice**: The clan's original purpose was to judge and maintain order among Kindred.
- **The Treaty of Tyre**: The 1496 agreement that bound the clan and led to the Tremere curse.
- **Haqim's Legacy**: The clan struggles with the memory and absence of their founder.

## Purpose and Value

Clanbook: Assamite (Revised) serves as:
- Comprehensive information on the Children of Haqim
- The true history of the clan, contradicting Camarilla beliefs
- Information on the recent schism and its implications
- Character creation options for all three castes
- New Disciplines and powers for Assamite characters
- Notable members and roleplaying guidance

This book is essential for anyone wanting to play or understand Assamite characters, providing the true history of the Children of Haqim and exploring the clan's current state of upheaval as it struggles with its identity and future in the Final Nights.
