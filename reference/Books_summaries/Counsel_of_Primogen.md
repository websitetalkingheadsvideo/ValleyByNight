# Counsel of Primogen

This supplement for Vampire: The Masquerade explores the role and power of the primogen council - the council of elders that advises and assists the prince in managing a Camarilla city. The book provides detailed information on how primogen councils function, the political dynamics within them, advice for playing primogen characters, and guidance for Storytellers on incorporating primogen politics into their chronicles.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book focuses exclusively on the primogen council and its members.
- It provides both player and Storyteller perspectives on primogen politics.
- Explores the history, structure, and dynamics of primogen councils.
- Includes advice for characters who are primogen or aspire to become primogen.
- Provides Storyteller guidance on running primogen-focused chronicles.

## Introduction: Hallowed Halls of Power

The book opens with a satirical description of primogen as "the true movers of the Camarilla" who "direct Kindred affairs from on high" with "puppets twitching and dancing on endless invisible strings." This is immediately undercut by the admission that "as long as the gullible neonates on the street believe crap like this, they'll stay firmly on the top of the heap."

### What This Book Is

- A guide to primogen councils and their members.
- Advice for players and Storytellers.
- Information on primogen politics, power, and responsibilities.
- Examples of primogen councils and individual primogen.

### What This Book Isn't

- Not a complete guide to Camarilla politics (see Guide to the Camarilla).
- Not a substitute for understanding the basic structure of Kindred society.
- Not a guarantee that your primogen character will be all-powerful.

## Chapter One: Hallowed Halls of Power

### A Brief Look Back

The history of primogen councils:

- **Early Days**: Individual princes ruled domains with their broods. Some cities were ruled by single lords, others by multiple elders competing for supremacy.
- **Tremere Exception**: The Tremere proved the primary exception, placing even their smallest territories under group rather than individual authority due to their system of numerical symbolism and the fact that alone a Tremere lord was little more than a large target.
- **Formalization**: The Warlocks' example likely convinced the Founders and early princes that an organized, semi-formal council of advisors might prove prudent in maintaining a domain.

### Tribal Cultures

Early elder coteries were largely informal affairs - elders participated for their own purposes and only rarely formalized their relations through treaties. Some individual princes, faced with the combined might of a coterie of elders, assembled similar groups as allies and advisors.

### Coteries or Covens

The evolution from informal elder gatherings to formal primogen councils, and how different cities developed different structures based on local needs and power dynamics.

### The Whip

The role of the primogen council whip - the member responsible for maintaining discipline and ensuring attendance at council meetings.

### An Important Distinction

The difference between being a primogen and being an elder - not all elders are primogen, and primogen status must be earned and maintained.

### Other Elders

How primogen interact with other elders in the city who are not on the council, and the political implications of these relationships.

## Chapter Two: Around the Table

### On Your Marks

Some Storytellers might stage contests for primogen positions. Even if player characters are of insufficient age or power to be competing themselves, they may be involved as agents, pawns, or impediments to the contestants.

### Accidental Office

How some Kindred become primogen almost by accident - through default, necessity, or being the only available candidate.

### A Bigger Picture

The relationship between primogen and higher authorities:
- **Justicars**: Have close to absolute power and can insist that a given primogen relinquish his position.
- **Archons**: When speaking with their patron's voice, have similar authority.
- **The Prince**: Cannot always remove a primogen if the primogen is able to maintain his position despite the prince's wishes.

### Outside Interests

How primogen balance their council duties with their personal interests, clan responsibilities, and other obligations.

### You May Not Sit

The process of removing a primogen from office and the various methods used:
- **Filibuster**: One primogen can raise a fine old stink about being bullied, potentially costing the prince his unlife.
- **Outvoting**: When voting via show of hands, it's easy to maneuver a simple outvote.
- **Systematic Opposition**: If a primogen is consistently outvoted night after night, the lack of results may bring the eventuality of the clan removing the target or the target departing of his own accord.

### Resource Wrangling

How primogen compete for and manage resources within the city, and the political implications of resource allocation.

### That Old Gang-up of Mine

How primogen form alliances and work together (or against each other) to achieve their goals.

### Views from the Gallery

How primogen are perceived by other Kindred in the city, and the importance of maintaining appearances.

### Clan Perspectives

How different clans approach primogen service:

- **Toreador**: Often serve as harpies, using their social skills to maintain influence.
- **Tremere**: Approach primogen service with their characteristic organization and hierarchy.
- **Ventrue**: See primogen as a stepping stone to greater power.
- **Brujah**: Often chafe against the structure but serve when necessary.
- **Nosferatu**: Use their information networks to maintain influence.
- **Malkavian**: Unpredictable, making them either excellent or terrible primogen.
- **Gangrel**: Rarely serve as primogen due to their independent nature.

### Horse Trading

The art of negotiation and deal-making within the primogen council, and how favors and boons are exchanged.

### The Harpy

The relationship between primogen and harpies - primogen often desire harpy status for the additional social power it provides.

### Archons

How primogen interact with archons and justicars, and the political implications of these relationships.

## Chapter Three: Shaking the Foundation

### Do Not Let Others Become Envious

The importance of not flaunting power or resources, as those who others envy become targets for destruction.

### Master the Spectacle

The Roman principle of "bread and circuses" - providing sustenance and entertainment to keep the masses docile. The power to make statements on a large scale creates mystique and lends an aura of strength.

### Violence in Politics

When and how primogen resort to violence, and the political consequences of such actions.

### Wear Council Under a Weak Prince

How primogen councils function when the prince is weak or ineffective, and the opportunities and dangers this presents.

### La Sombra Antitribu

The possibility of Lasombra antitribu serving as primogen in Camarilla cities, and the political implications.

### So Does It Exist or Not?

The question of whether Cathayans (Kuei-jin) could serve as primogen, and the challenges this would present.

### The Giovanni

How primogen interact with the Giovanni, and the delicate balance required when dealing with the necromancers.

### The Really Weird Stuff

Unusual situations that primogen might encounter, from bizarre clan politics to supernatural threats.

## Chapter Four: Who Holds There In S

### Important Childe

The role of primogen's childer in maintaining and extending their influence.

### The Driven Politician

Character archetypes for primogen characters:
- The power-hungry manipulator
- The reluctant servant
- The clan loyalist
- The independent operator
- The reformer

### Rank and File

How primogen interact with the general Kindred population of the city, and the importance of maintaining connections.

### Disciplines

How primogen use their Disciplines to maintain power and influence, with emphasis on social and mental Disciplines.

## Chapter Five: Storytelling

Guidance for Storytellers on incorporating primogen politics into chronicles:

- **Creating Primogen Councils**: How to design interesting and functional primogen councils for your city.
- **Primogen as Antagonists**: Using primogen as obstacles or enemies for player characters.
- **Primogen as Allies**: How primogen can support player characters.
- **Political Intrigue**: Running games focused on primogen politics.
- **Power Struggles**: Handling conflicts within primogen councils.
- **Player Primogen**: Supporting players who want to play primogen characters.

## Key Themes

- **Power and Responsibility**: Primogen have significant power but also bear heavy responsibilities to their clans and the city.
- **Politics and Manipulation**: Primogen politics are complex, requiring careful maneuvering and strategic thinking.
- **Appearances Matter**: How primogen are perceived is often more important than reality.
- **The Prince's Shadow**: Primogen operate in the prince's shadow, supporting or undermining as circumstances require.
- **Clan Loyalty vs. City Duty**: The tension between serving one's clan and serving the city as a whole.

## Purpose and Value

Counsel of Primogen serves as:

- A comprehensive guide to primogen councils and their members.
- Advice for players wanting to play primogen characters or understand primogen politics.
- Guidance for Storytellers on incorporating primogen into their chronicles.
- Information on the political dynamics of Camarilla cities.
- Examples and case studies of primogen councils and individual primogen.

This book is essential for anyone wanting to understand the upper echelons of Camarilla politics, play elder characters involved in city governance, or run chronicles focused on political intrigue and power struggles within Kindred society.
