# Liber des Goules (The Book of Ghouls)

The document is a comprehensive guide to ghouls in the World of Darkness, providing detailed information about creating, playing, and storytelling with ghoul characters in Mind's Eye Theatre.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book outlines and describes what is expected of a ghoul in a live-action setting, and details what a player should expect from the World of Darkness.
- It provides rules, roleplaying hooks, and ideas for newcomers and long-time players of The Masquerade.
- Storytellers will find story ideas and clarified rules to use as they see fit.
- The book aims to show that ghouls are much more than slaves or convenient juice bags.

## Introduction: The Nature of Ghouls

### What Are Ghouls?

- Ghouls are mortals who have ingested the potent blood of vampires, though not always of their own free will.
- This powerful elixir carries the curse of Caine's damnation and infects every creature that consumes it.
- Most notable are human beings, but animals of all kinds can become ghouls.
- Ghouls incorporate the best and worst of both worlds - they can stay out in the midday sun, taste immortality, and take on supernatural Disciplines, but they must often pay with unnatural love.

### The Importance of Ghouls

- Vampires build their communities on the backs of their ghouls.
- Ghouls are the bridge between the worlds of Kindred and kine.
- Without ghouls, vampires would be prisoners to the rising and setting of the sun, and mostly helpless to affect the sunlit world.
- The Camarilla's strides to preserve the Masquerade would falter or fail without ghouls.
- The Sabbat relies heavily on ghouls, as evidenced by the Revenant families.
- Even lowly Anarchs need ghouls for their very survival.

### The Blood Bond

- Ghouls are always faced with the Blood Bond and the possibility of mistreatment through it.
- Being Blood Bound does not make a ghoul stupid - ghouls often cope with the Bond better than vampires.
- A ghoul is not going to appreciate being ignored, struck, or emotionally abused, and will react as a spurned lover would.
- Jealousy and resentment can lead a ghoul to question her Bond, eventually causing a true break from her Regnant.
- Some ghouls, when faced with nothing but abuse, strike back and become vampire hunters, bent on the annihilation of their former masters.

### The Ghoul's Dual Nature

- With the exception of Revenant families, a ghoul is still very much a normal human.
- The majority of ghouls may still live with loved ones, hold day jobs, pay bills, go to college, and so on.
- Apart from their addiction to vitae, ghouls function like everybody else.
- The difference lies in the ghoul's longevity and access to vampiric Disciplines.
- These differences set a ghoul apart from kine society, both physically and socially, and are largely responsible for even the most stable ghoul's eventual alienation from normal life.

## A Cautionary Tale: Hideaway

The book opens with a narrative vignette about Lanaugha, a vampire who is staked and imprisoned by her ghoul Jace. The story illustrates:

- The dangers of taking ghouls for granted
- How the Blood Bond can lead to dangerous obsessions
- The consequences of being too kind or too trusting
- How a spurned ghoul can become a deadly enemy
- The importance of maintaining proper boundaries in the Regnant-ghoul relationship

## Ghoul Types

The book details various types of ghouls:

### Standard Ghouls

- Mortals who have consumed vampire blood
- Bound to their Regnants through the Blood Bond
- Can learn Disciplines from their Regnants
- Extended lifespan but not immortality
- Still vulnerable to normal human dangers

### Revenants

- Very different from other ghouls, having been ghouls since their conception
- Revenant families have had vampire blood in their veins for generations
- Brought up separate from the rest of humanity
- Most are unable to exist with vampires or humans
- Represent a unique subculture within the World of Darkness

### Animal Ghouls

- Animals of all kinds can become ghouls
- Serve various purposes for their Regnants
- Can be more loyal and less complicated than human ghouls
- Still subject to the Blood Bond
- Appendix provides detailed rules for animal ghouls

## Character Creation

The book provides comprehensive rules for creating ghoul characters:

### Basic Creation Rules

- Ghouls are created differently from vampires
- They retain their human nature while gaining supernatural benefits
- Character creation reflects the ghoul's dual nature
- Rules for starting Disciplines, Abilities, and Backgrounds
- Special considerations for Revenant characters

### Disciplines

- Ghouls can learn Disciplines from their Regnants
- They are limited in how many Disciplines they can learn
- Some Disciplines are easier for ghouls to learn than others
- Rules for ghouls learning Disciplines independently

### Backgrounds

- Ghouls have access to different Backgrounds than vampires
- Some Backgrounds reflect their connection to their Regnants
- Others reflect their place in mortal society
- Rules for ghouls maintaining mortal connections

## Roleplaying and Storytelling

### Playing Ghouls

- Ghouls are perfect characters for new players who know little about Mind's Eye Theatre
- The opportunity to learn while playing is invaluable
- The very nature of "new ghoul" roles explains any "out-of-character" awkwardness or ignorance
- Experienced players will find ghouls to have a capacity for multilevel play
- Ghouls can interact with more denizens of the World of Darkness than just vampires

### Storytelling with Ghouls

- Storytellers can use ghouls as antagonists
- Ghouls can perpetuate storylines
- They can puncture vampiric arrogance
- They can continue game action during daylight hours
- Ghouls make excellent bodyguards, servants, envoys, and guardians

### Story Hooks

- The spurned ghoul seeking revenge
- The ghoul caught between Regnant and mortal loved ones
- The ghoul who questions the Blood Bond
- The ghoul who becomes a hunter
- The ghoul who discovers their Regnant's secrets
- The ambitious ghoul seeking power

## The Ghoul's Role in Kindred Society

### Servants and Retainers

- Ghouls fulfill several important roles, all fertile ground for Mind's Eye Theatre plots
- By playing the roles of servants, ghouls are privy to all sorts of dirty little secrets
- They make excellent bodyguards with their command of physical Disciplines
- Vampires who scoff at the abilities of "lowly" ghouls tempt fate more than they know

### Envoys to the Mortal World

- Ghouls serve as envoys to and guardians against the mortal world
- This is one of their most important roles, as they tend to their Regnants' daytime business
- An efficient ghoul in this position can make her Regnant's unlife much easier
- An incompetent one can lead hunters right to the haven door

### Political Tools

- Ghouls enable vampires to extend their influence to the sunlit hours
- Courts of law, businesses, and elections are all daytime operations
- If a vampire wants to affect something in the mortal world while the sun is high, he probably has to use a ghoul
- Considering the variety of ways vampires interact with the world during daytime hours, that leaves an awful lot for ghouls to do

## Safety and Responsibility

The book emphasizes:

- The most important thing is the story
- Rules are flexible to create a story-driven game
- If a chronicle is being dictated by rules instead of ideas, there are definite problems
- Storytellers should feel free to disregard what they disagree with and go wild with what they like
- The only limitations to a story are the ones you set

## Key Themes

- **Dual Nature**: Ghouls exist between the worlds of Kindred and kine
- **The Blood Bond**: Both a blessing and a curse
- **Power Dynamics**: The complex relationship between Regnant and ghoul
- **Alienation**: How becoming a ghoul changes one's relationship with normal life
- **Danger**: The risks of taking ghouls for granted
- **Opportunity**: Ghouls as perfect characters for new and experienced players alike

## Purpose and Value

This book serves to:

- Broaden the perspectives of both Storytellers and players
- Flesh out a vital part of the World of Darkness that has been largely taken for granted
- Show that ghouls are much more than slaves or convenient juice bags
- Provide easy-to-follow rules and suggestions on how to create and play ghouls
- Give Storytellers story ideas and tools for using ghouls effectively
- Demonstrate the importance of ghouls to Kindred society

Liber des Goules is essential for anyone wanting to play or run ghoul characters in Mind's Eye Theatre, providing comprehensive information about ghouls' place in the World of Darkness, their relationships with vampires, and their unique role in Kindred society.
