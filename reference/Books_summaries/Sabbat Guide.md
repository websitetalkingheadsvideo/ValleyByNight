# Sabbat Guide

The document is a comprehensive guide to the Sabbat, the most brutal and militant sect in Vampire: The Masquerade, providing detailed information about the sect's philosophy, history, structure, clans, disciplines, and gameplay mechanics for Mind's Eye Theatre.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book details the innermost workings of the Sabbat, from their rituals to their unique Disciplines, from revenant families to the workings of the antitribu.
- It is not a stand-alone volume - players need Laws of the Night for basic rules, and may reference The Camarilla Guide for elder Discipline levels.
- The book emphasizes that players are not actual vampires and must maintain boundaries between game and reality.

## The Nature of the Sabbat

### Core Philosophy

- The Sabbat is described as an apocalyptic blood cult, a coven of death-obsessed leeches, crusaders fighting an endless civil war, and a hodgepodge of blood-drinking psychotics.
- For the Sabbat, being a vampire means being a weapon in the Great Jyhad - an unending holy war against the Antediluvians and their servants in the Camarilla.
- The sect believes the world belongs to them, not to the ancient evil that sleeps beneath the land.
- Sabbat vampires understand they are damned creatures of the Beast, and rather than rail against their natures, they flaunt their monstrosity.

### Internal Contradictions

- The Sabbat is simultaneously righteous and venal, spiritual and base, patriotic and selfish.
- Hypocrisy plays a great role - the sect has a strong, consistent philosophy that individual corruption often pushes aside.
- The sect is racked with corruption, festering from within, yet denying its own collapse with unmatched passion.

### Modern Nights

- The Sabbat has matured and become a force to be reckoned with, claiming or reclaiming cities long held by the Camarilla.
- The sect is considerably younger than the Camarilla, able to adopt modern technology with speed that terrifies its counterpart.
- The Sabbat exercises great influence over Third World nations and squalid cities, while being more careful in modernized parts of the world.

## History of the Sabbat

### Origins

- The sect's history prior to the Convention of Thorns is shrouded in mystery, with conflicting theories about its origin.
- The Sabbat came into being after the Anarch Revolt, when rebellious neonates of Clans Lasombra and Tzimisce destroyed their Antediluvians.
- After innumerable bloody skirmishes, the anarchs gained ground, and young vampires turned to open revolt.

### The Convention of Thorns (1493)

- Hardestadt and other elders formed the Camarilla and called for all "Kindred" to parley.
- The Convention of Thorns promised to restore order, offering forgiveness to most anarchs in exchange for returning to elder rule.
- Clan Assamite suffered the brunt of punishment - they were cursed to be unable to drink freely of other Kindred's vitae.
- A war party of anarchs and rogue Assamites rampaged through Thorns, leaving it burning - the sect that would become the Sabbat committed itself that night.

### Expansion and Development

- With the discovery of the New World, the Sabbat saw a new empire and flourished while the Camarilla huddled in Europe.
- Lasombra and Tzimisce fell to warring amongst themselves for rapidly depleting resources.
- The Sabbat established strongholds in Canada and Mexico, effectively blocking in the Camarilla.
- The Code of Milan was documented in 1933, a collection of tenets composing Sabbat ideology.

## Key Documents

### The Convention of Thorns

The original treaty that ended the Anarch Revolt, establishing the Camarilla and indirectly creating the Sabbat through those who rejected it.

### The Purchase Pact (1803)

An agreement that suspended all grievances between Sabbat, declaring that any Sabbat making open war on another for personal gain would be declared forsaken.

### The Code of Milan (1933)

The fundamental code of Sabbat behavior, including:
- Unity in support of the regent
- Service to leaders who serve the regent's will
- Observance of auctoritas ritae
- Keeping word of honor
- Treating peers fairly
- Putting sect good before personal needs
- Protection from enemies
- Protection of Sabbat territory
- Freedom as fundamental principle
- Use of Monomacy to settle disputes
- Support of the Black Hand

## Geographic Distribution

### North America

- Most successful staging ground for the Sabbat
- Dominance in Miami, Washington DC, Baltimore, Philadelphia, Atlantic City, Detroit, Mexico City, and Montreal
- Recent losses in New York City after a Camarilla coup
- Cities like Atlanta, Richmond, Boston, and Raleigh-Durham recently fallen or in contention

### South America

- Limited sway due to feudal, balkanized vampiric domains
- Major cities are prime targets but considered secondary due to economics
- Neutral attitudes make it a perfect middle ground for meetings with other sects

### Europe

- Prime ground for subtle warfare
- Limited influence, primarily in Spain and Italy where Lasombra hold traditional sway
- Many old Sabbat from the Anarch Revolt still give counsel
- Eastern Europe almost a separate continent, with hoary Tzimisce ruling as feudal overlords

### Africa and Middle East

- Fierce rivalry between Followers of Set and Serpents of the Light
- Middle East is a fortification for the Black Hand
- Shifting religious allegiances make fertile ground for recruitment

### Australia

- Marginally independent territory, frustrating for the Sabbat
- Rising crime and political unrest suggest growing Sabbat presence
- Major cities like Sydney and Melbourne remain remarkably free of Sabbat influence

### Asia

- Remains shrouded in mystery
- Native vampires have little interest in contact
- All attempts to gain ground have failed miserably
- Asia is a deathtrap for the Sabbat

## Structure of the Sword of Caine

### Hierarchy

The Sabbat observes an organized hierarchy, though leadership is capricious and the structure is only as strong as its weakest member.

### The Regent

- Supposedly coordinates the master plan of the sect
- Supported by a consistory of other powerful vampires
- Holds little sway over the sect as a whole
- Must be able to back up demands with power

### The Cardinals

- Handle sect interests over large geographical regions
- Archbishops report directly to them
- Coordinate Sabbat in their own cities

### The Prisci

- Advisors, sages, and chamberlains to the Sabbat
- Make up the majority of the consistory
- Offer unique insight to other members, particularly the regent
- Title carries no temporal power

### The Archbishops

- Handle administration of Cainite affairs in individual cities
- Bear responsibility for all vampires underneath them
- Must balance keeping the city hospitable to Sabbat while preventing it from becoming a wasteland

### The Bishops

- Lead Sabbat in cities without archbishops
- Typically a council of three to five, depending on city size
- Maintain Sabbat influence and lead spiritual growth

### The Ducti

- Leaders of individual packs
- Attend to operational matters of packmates
- Work closely with pack priests
- Act as liaisons to bishops and archbishops

### The Priests

- Charged with spiritual growth of packmates
- Many hail from Clan Tzimisce, but any clan may become a priest
- Conduct all of the pack's ritae
- Become pack leader pro tem if ductus is eliminated

### The Templars

- Bodyguards appointed by bishops or greater Sabbat
- Also known as paladins
- Great honor conveying favor of sect leader and endorsing fighting skills

### The True Sabbat

- Most populous group of Sabbat
- Members who have proven themselves and earned the privilege of continued existence
- Once a Cainite receives her Creation Rite, she is considered True Sabbat

## Clans and Antitribu

### Core Sabbat Clans

- **Lasombra**: One of the two founding clans, maintain numerical superiority
- **Tzimisce**: The other founding clan, still maintain numerical superiority

### The Antitribu

- "Anti-clans" who have turned away from their parent clans
- Most have no love for their parent clans and few harbor illusions of reconciliation
- Reasons for clan schisms often lost to centuries of ill will
- Include: Assamite Antitribu, Brujah Antitribu, Gangrel Antitribu, Malkavian Antitribu, Nosferatu Antitribu, Ravnos Antitribu, Salubri Antitribu, Serpents of the Light, Toreador Antitribu, Ventrue Antitribu

## Factions of the Sabbat

### The Black Hand

- A sect within a sect, a unique group distinct from all other Sabbat
- Conceived as a special militia, a military force at the disposal of Sabbat leaders
- Specializes in warfare, from intrigue and intelligence to assassination to physical combat
- Draws membership from Assamite and Gangrel antitribu, plus militant vampires
- Members bear a permanent, mystical sigil on the palms of their right hands
- Membership lasts until Final Death

### The Sabbat Inquisition

- Political faction charged with rooting out heretics and infernalists
- Possesses power to depose bishops, archbishops, pack priests, and ducti
- Recently doubled in size from 15 to 30 members
- All members are True Sabbat who have proven their devotion

## Revenant Families

The book details several revenant families who serve the Sabbat:

### Bratovitch

- Rural revenants who serve Tzimisce
- Known for their loyalty and service

### Grimaldi

- Revenant family with specific characteristics and roles

### Obertus

- Revenant family with scholarly or monastic traditions

### Oprichniki

- Revenant family associated with Tzimisce

### Zantosa

- Revenant family known for their decadence and experimentation
- Set standards others try to imitate
- Living on the edge carries risks
- Many pursue Path of Cathari
- Disciplines: Auspex, Presence, Vicissitude
- Weakness: Cannot resist interesting temptations

### Szlachta and Vohzd

- Specialized ghouls created by Tzimisce
- **Szlachta**: Guardian ghouls serving as soldiers, bodyguards, and sentries
  - Created through Vicissitude modifications
  - Features include spikes, armored plates, enhanced senses, organic weapons
  - Require Mental and Physical Challenges to create
  - Not suitable as player characters
- **Vohzd**: War ghouls from an earlier age
  - Team projects requiring at least 15 regular ghouls
  - Disassembled and reassembled into final form
  - Rarely made in modern nights due to practical difficulties

## Ritae (Rituals)

The Sabbat practices numerous rituals (ritae) that bind the sect together:

- **Auctoritas Ritae**: The authority rituals that define Sabbat structure
- **Ignoblis Ritae**: Common rituals practiced by packs
- Pack-specific rituals created by priests
- Rituals for various purposes: creation, war, celebration, punishment

## Character Creation

- Sabbat characters receive 4 Discipline points instead of 3
- Sabbat characters receive no Background points (reflecting the sect's emphasis on pack over individual resources)
- Characters must choose a Path of Enlightenment instead of Humanity
- Pack creation and Vaulderie (blood bond breaking ritual) are essential

## Gameplay and Storytelling

### Running Sabbat Chronicles

- Emphasizes the sect's brutal nature while maintaining game safety
- Focus on pack dynamics and sect politics
- Balance between freedom and structure
- Handling the sect's internal contradictions

### Themes

- The Great Jyhad against Antediluvians
- Freedom versus structure
- Individual versus sect
- Monstrosity and acceptance of the Beast
- War against the Camarilla
- Internal politics and corruption

## Modern Developments

- The sect has scored astounding coups and become a legitimate contender
- Technology adoption gives the Sabbat advantages
- Recent territorial gains and losses
- Growing influence in certain regions
- Challenges from organized hunters and other threats

## Safety and Responsibility

The book includes strong warnings about:
- Maintaining boundaries between game and reality
- Not confusing character actions with player actions
- Respecting the "No Touching" rule
- Understanding that intense roleplay doesn't excuse inappropriate behavior
- The importance of the Masquerade even for Sabbat (though they scorn the term)

This guide is essential for anyone wanting to play or run Sabbat characters in Mind's Eye Theatre, providing comprehensive information about the sect's philosophy, structure, history, and mechanics while emphasizing responsible play and maintaining healthy gaming environments.
