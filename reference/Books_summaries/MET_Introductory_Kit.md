# MET Introductory Kit

This booklet is a simplified version of the live-action rules for Vampire: The Masquerade, designed as an introduction to Mind's Eye Theatre. It provides just the highlights of the vampire's world but enough information to start experimenting with play. The kit serves as a gateway to the full Laws of the Night rulebook, giving new players a taste of what live-action roleplaying offers.

## Purpose and Scope

- This is a simplified introduction to Mind's Eye Theatre Vampire: The Masquerade.
- It gives just the highlights but enough to start playing.
- Designed for players who have never played Masquerade or LARP before.
- Serves as a stepping stone to the full Laws of the Night rulebook.

## Welcome to Mind's Eye Theatre

Mind's Eye Theatre is a roleplaying game based on Vampire: The Masquerade where you play the part of a vampire, much as if you were acting in a movie. This game is probably unlike anything you have played before - it doesn't have cards, dice, or a playing board. It's far more like the make-believe of childhood or "How to Host a Murder" than traditional games.

Playing Mind's Eye Theatre is like being in a movie. You and your friends are the characters, but there is no script. You don't sit around a table describing your actions - you get up and do them. The "director" of this movie is the Storyteller, who creates the stage and the minor characters.

## Characters

When you play this game, you take on another persona, a character - in this case, a vampire. Your character can be from any walk of life, age, creed, race or sex. The only limit is your imagination. During the game, you speak and act as your character, unless you're resolving a rules dispute or talking to the Storyteller.

Creating a character for Masquerade is easy, and it takes just a few minutes. Only a few things are necessary to define the basic capabilities of a character. However, a character should be more than just numbers - characters have pasts, likes and dislikes, goals and dreams, all the intangible things that make a person complete.

## The Storyteller

The Storyteller is the one who creates the world that the players move through. She creates a skeletal framework of setting and plot, then turns the characters loose to put flesh on its bones. She acts as an impartial judge when the rules are questioned, describes scenes that can't be staged, and plays the parts of antagonists or other people with whom the characters interact. A Storyteller's word is law in the game.

## Elegantly Simple

This game was designed to be easy to play and easier to start. Character creation takes only a few minutes. The basic rules are simple, and they cover most of the encounters a new player will enter. Even very new players who have never played Masquerade or LARP before will find that this game takes little effort to pick up.

## The Only Rules That Matter

These are the most important and immutable rules of MET, the ones that keep your game and your players safe:

### No Touching... No Stunts...

That means none whatsoever. You never actually strike or grapple with another player. It's far too easy for things to get out of hand in the heat of the moment. Save the stunts for your imagination.

### No Weapons...

No matter how careful you are. Whether it's to prevent someone from skewering themselves, or to make sure the police don't think you're a threat, weapons of any sort are forbidden. Even fake or toy weapons are not allowed. Use item cards instead.

### No Drugs or Drinking

Drinking and drugs do not inspire peak performance, and players who are so impaired are a threat to other players and the game. It's one thing to play a character who is drunk or stoned, but another thing entirely to actually come to a game under the influence.

### It's Only a Game

If a character dies, if a rival gets the upper hand, it's just a game. You and the rest of the players are doing this for fun. Taking things too seriously, or taking character issues into real life, will only spoil everyone's enjoyment. Leave the game behind when the session's over.

### Be Mindful of Others

Not everyone around you is playing the game. You want to ensure that your game and your players are welcomed. Frightening people and getting the local law enforcement called on you is not the way to do it. Behave yourself and don't get carried away so everyone can continue to have fun.

### Have Fun

Not "Win." Not "Go out and conquer everyone else." Just have fun, because in MET, it's not about how the game ends, but what happens along the way.

## The World of Darkness

On the surface, the World of Darkness is not too much different from our own. People are born, grow up, work and die every day. Plants grow, as do skyscrapers. The same newspapers are sold on the street corner, and television is the same vast wasteland. Below the surface, however, is a much darker element, one that is fed by the violence and despair which the monsters need to fuel themselves. The monsters in the World of Darkness are all too real. This is the world that the vampires call home.

## What is a Vampire?

Vampires have been fixtures in myth and legend since time immemorial. The vampires of the World of Darkness are sentient, with some faint similarities to their mortal selves, but as different from mortals and myth as night from day.

Key facts about vampires:
- **Vampires are immortal** - While it is possible to kill a vampire, he will not age or die of natural causes. He does not need water, food or air. For all intents and purposes, he is a corpse.
- **Vampires require the blood of the living to survive** - Blood preserves their bodies and allows vampires to perform amazing supernatural feats. A vampire may close any wounds she makes with her fangs by simply licking the wounds.
- **Anyone who dies from a vampire's bite will become a vampire** - This is false. It takes a very special process, known as the Embrace, to create a vampire.
- **Vampires are burned by sunlight** - True. Vampires must avoid sunlight or risk death.
- **Vampires are repelled by garlic and running water** - False. Myths and nothing more.
- **A cross or other holy symbol can burn or repel a vampire** - Generally untrue. It is the faith of the person who wields the symbol that the vampire must fear.
- **Vampires will die from a stake through the heart** - While they cannot die from such wounds, vampires are immobilized by wooden stakes until the item is removed.

## The Embrace

The Embrace is the process by which a new vampire is sired, or created. It is almost never given lightly. To Embrace a new vampire, the sire drains her chosen victim of blood to the point of death, then places a small amount of her own vampiric blood in the victim's mouth. The new childe reawakens as one of the living dead, suffering a ravenous Hunger and learning to use the powers granted by his blood.

## Origins of the Vampires

Vampires believe themselves descended from Caine, the first murderer. Caine was cursed by God with vampirism and exiled from his people. During his wanderings, he encountered Lilith, who taught him many secrets - most particularly, how to use the blood in his veins to work magical power, and how to create others like him.

Caine created three childer, who then created 13 vampires. These 13 became known as Antediluvians, and their childer, created in their images, inherited their magical gifts and curses. Thus were formed the clans.

## The Dark Ages

The clans spread across the world, sowing discord in their wake. Though each successive generation was weaker than the last, they made up for it in numbers. The clans established themselves in various regions, each developing their own culture and traditions.

## Key Themes

- **Live-Action Roleplaying**: The emphasis on physical acting and improvisation rather than tabletop mechanics.
- **Storytelling Over Rules**: The story is more important than the rules.
- **Safety First**: Critical safety rules that must always be followed.
- **Introduction to the World**: A simplified gateway to the full Vampire: The Masquerade experience.

## Purpose and Value

MET Introductory Kit serves as:
- A simplified introduction to Mind's Eye Theatre for new players
- A stepping stone to the full Laws of the Night rulebook
- Basic information about vampires and the World of Darkness
- Essential safety rules and gameplay concepts

This booklet is perfect for players who want to try live-action roleplaying for the first time, providing just enough information to start playing while encouraging them to seek out the full rulebook when they're ready to dive deeper into the world of Vampire: The Masquerade.
