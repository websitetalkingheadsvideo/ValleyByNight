# Mind's Eye Theatre - Camarilla Guide

This supplement for Mind's Eye Theatre Vampire: The Masquerade expands upon Laws of the Night, focusing specifically on the Camarilla sect and its seven founding clans (plus the recently departed Gangrel). The book provides advanced character creation options, superior Disciplines, detailed information on Camarilla politics and society, and extensive rules for building and managing a Camarilla city.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book is not a substitute for Laws of the Night - players still need that core rulebook.
- It assumes familiarity with the basic rules of Mind's Eye Theatre.
- Focuses exclusively on Camarilla clans and their erstwhile sibling Clan Gangrel.
- Provides rules for the mysterious Gargoyles, new Traits for character creation, and everything a Storyteller needs to add depth to her city.

## Chapter One: Introduction

### Welcome to the Final Nights

The book opens with a discussion of the Camarilla in the modern era - a sect under strain. The change in the Malkavians has brought consternation, the departure of the Gangrel shook the sect to its foundations, and strongholds are assaulted by Assamites, Sabbat, and mysterious Cathayans. Despite these challenges, the Camarilla stands as it has for nearly six centuries.

### History: Nights Gone By

The Camarilla's history is traced from its origins:

- **1381**: English peasant rebellion draws attention of young Kindred, sparking the beginnings of rebellion.
- **Early 1400s**: Young Brujah Tyler assaults elder Ventrue Hardestadt, inspiring childer to rise against sires across Europe.
- **1435**: Hardestadt gathers elders in convocation, proposing an arrangement to deal with the anarch movement - the Founders lay groundwork for the next five centuries.
- **1493**: Convention of Thorns - anarchs accept terms for surrender, Camarilla comes into its own. Many anarchs reject the treaty and flee to Scandinavia, eventually forming the Sabbat.
- **New World**: Many ancillae pack up and go west to carve out domains. War between Camarilla and Sabbat becomes a crucible in the Americas.
- **Last Century**: World Wars, technological advancement, and the rise of the anarch movement on the West Coast. The Gangrel depart the Camarilla.

### The Traditions

The six Traditions that form the bulwark of Camarilla society:

1. **The Masquerade**: Thou shalt not reveal thy true nature to those not of the Blood.
2. **The Domain**: Thy domain is thy concern. All others owe thee respect while in it.
3. **The Progeny**: Thou shalt sire another only with permission of thine elder.
4. **The Accounting**: Those thou create are thine own childer. Their sins are thine to endure.
5. **Hospitality**: Honor one another's domain. Present thyself when coming to a foreign city.
6. **Destruction**: Thou art forbidden to destroy another of thy kind. Only the eldest may call the blood hunt.

## Chapter Two: Character Creation

### New Archetypes

Additional Nature/Demeanor options for Camarilla characters:
- **Idealist**: Utterly committed to some purpose greater than your own wants and needs.
- **Soldier**: Given orders and takes pride in accomplishing them well.
- **Scientist**: Diligent rational examination can reveal all the secrets of the universe.
- **Dabbler**: The world is full of new things to learn and you want to sample all of them.

### Abilities

Specialized Abilities common in Camarilla society:

- **Hunting**: Skill for gaining nightly sustenance without breaking the Masquerade. Gives retest when attempting to refresh Blood Pool.
- **Psychology**: Working knowledge of psychology in theory and practice. Can determine a subject's Nature, Derangements, or motivations with sufficient time and conversation.

### Merits and Flaws

Extensive list of new Merits and Flaws organized by category:

**Physical Merits**: Bruiser, Friendly Face
**Physical Flaws**: Dulled Bite, Glowing Eyes, Permanent Fangs
**Mental Merits**: Coldly Logical
**Mental Flaws**: Thirst for Innocence, Guilt-Wracked
**Supernatural Merits**: Bright Aura, Healing Touch, Inoffensive to Animals
**Supernatural Flaws**: Cold Breeze, Beacon of the Unholy, Bound, Death Sight, Lord of the Flies

### Master Level Influences

Detailed charts showing the power granted by Influences 6-10 in various realms:
- **Bureaucracy**: Control government machinery, from public works to rebuilding cities.
- **Church**: Influence religious institutions, from identifying True Faith to excommunicating clergy.
- **Finance**: Control stock markets, corporations, and economic trends.
- **Health**: Control medical care, blood supply, and disease samples.
- **High Society**: Grant or destroy Fame, control fashion trends.
- **Industry**: Control heavy industry, unions, and labor movements.
- **Legal**: Manipulate the justice system from investigations to rewriting laws.
- **Media**: Control news stories, create scandals, black out information.
- **Occult**: Access magical items, learn truthful lore, found cults.
- **Police**: Control precincts, appropriate evidence, call SWAT raids.
- **Political**: Enact legislation, get candidates into office, engineer scandals.
- **Street**: Control downtown areas, locate weaponry, start riots.
- **Transportation**: Control who enters or leaves cities, cause transportation disasters.
- **University**: Falsify degrees, control research, discredit academics.

## Chapter Three: Disciplines

### Superior Disciplines

Rules for Disciplines beyond the basic five levels, available to elders of suitably low generation:

- **Experience Costs**: Elder (12 points, Generation 8), Master (15 points, Generation 7), Ascendant (18 points, Generation 6), Methuselah (21 points, Generation 5).
- **Learning Time**: Mastering even Elder level powers may take a decade or two; Methuselah level can take an entire century.
- **Creative Insanity**: Once an elder has learned the five basic powers, she may start creating her own personalized Disciplines.

## The Gargoyles

Complete rules for playing Gargoyles:

### History

Created by the Tremere through magical experimentation on captive Tzimisce, Gangrel, and Nosferatu. For nearly a thousand years they faithfully served their Tremere masters, but in recent nights some have slipped their leashes and become free.

### The Split

- **Slaves**: Still follow the orders of their Tremere masters.
- **Freemen**: No longer serve the Tremere but are often without places to roost.

### Character Creation

- **Disciplines**: Fortitude, Potence, Visceratika, Flight
- **Advantages**: All Gargoyles automatically begin with first level of Flight and free level of Awareness.
- **Disadvantages**: Hideous appearance (cannot possess Appearance-related Social Traits without special powers), suffer Negative Traits Repugnant or Bestial when true forms are apparent, possess Negative Trait Submissive x2 when resisting Dominate or mind control.

### Roleplaying Hints

Like the stone they embody, Gargoyles possess great patience and stoicism. Free Gargoyles won their liberty only after years of careful planning and are not going to risk that freedom on hasty action.

## Key Themes

- **Elder Power**: The book emphasizes the vast gulf between elders and neonates in terms of power and influence.
- **Political Intrigue**: Camarilla society is built on manipulation, alliances, and careful positioning.
- **The Masquerade**: The central principle that keeps Kindred safe from mortal discovery.
- **Tradition and Change**: The tension between ancient traditions and modern challenges.

## Purpose and Value

The Camarilla Guide serves as:

- An essential expansion for players and Storytellers running Camarilla-focused games.
- A sourcebook for advanced character options and superior Disciplines.
- A guide to Camarilla politics, society, and city management.
- Complete rules for playing Gargoyles.
- Detailed information on Master Level Influences and their effects.

This book is essential for anyone wanting to explore the depths of Camarilla society, play elder characters, or understand the complex political structures that govern the largest sect of vampires in the World of Darkness.
