## The Current Thinking: Rules Clarifications

This section addresses frequently asked questions about the revised edition of Laws of the Night:

### Virtue System Questions

- **Virtue Tests**: Stimuli under the vampire's control don't cause Virtue Tests. Players can call for Virtue Tests if they think their character would be affected.
- **Virtue Trait Loss**: Storytellers may strip Virtue Traits for particularly severe events, though this is not common. Characters who lose all Traits in a Virtue suffer effects automatically.
- **Compressed Scale**: Alternative system where all Blood and Willpower Traits are halved, with corresponding adjustments to expenditures and limits.

### Blood Trait System

- **Mortals**: Have 10 Blood Traits under normal system (5 under compressed scale). Each one removed causes a health level of lethal damage.
- **Ghouls**: Have normal Blood Traits like humans, but replace one human Blood Trait with a Trait of ghoul blood to become ghouls.
- **Blood Trait Expenditures**: Halved and rounded up in compressed scale system.

### Discipline Comparisons

Relative strengths/speeds/endurance levels:
- **Celerity**: Basic (outrun a bike), Intermediate (outrun a horse), Advanced (outrun a car)
- **Fortitude**: Basic (take a punch), Intermediate (take a baseball bat), Advanced (take a blowtorch)
- **Potence**: Basic (throw a large television), Intermediate (throw a refrigerator), Advanced (throw a small car)

### Obfuscate Clarifications

- **Vanish from Mind's Eye**: Always risks detection when interacting with surroundings. Alternative system allows expending a Mental Trait per full sentence while Obfuscated.
- **Breaking Obfuscate with Auspex**: Get a two-Trait bonus for each power class that exceeds opponent's. Cannot be used for bidding, but can be used for resolving ties and calculating overbids.

### Thaumaturgy Limits

- Thaumaturgy's strength lies in flexibility. Storytellers can increase or waive limits on expenditures if needed, but should be careful about consequences.
- Recommended upper limit of five Traits on various limited expenditures.
- Cauldron of Blood, with its aggravated damage, can be extremely dangerous if misused.

### Other Rules Clarifications

- **Thanatosis Power**: Should affect Mental Disciplines, not Physical ones. Withering the head causes Negative Traits Oblivious and Witless, rendering vampires unable to use any Disciplines except Celerity, Fortitude, and Potence.
- **Summon Power**: Dissipates at dawn at victim's location. Cannot place someone in dangerous situation. Summoning breaks if there's no safe way to reach summoner.
- **Ability Retests**: Each Discipline should get retest from only one Ability. Storytellers can specify different Abilities but this gets complex.
- **Caitiff**: Pick three Disciplines as primary Disciplines (should come from "common eight"). Advance at "clan costs." Cannot begin play with Status, generally of very weak Generation.
- **Cross-Game Compatibility**: Compressed scales work fine across games. Full scales require accommodation with other games.

## Purpose and Value

The Journal serves as a bridge between official publications, providing:
- Material that couldn't fit in core rulebooks
- Rules clarifications and FAQs
- Practical guidance for Storytellers and players
- Setting material for less common game types
- Community-driven content and advice

This issue particularly emphasizes rules clarification and system refinement, recognizing that the revised edition raised many questions that needed official answers to maintain game consistency and fairness.
