# Prince's Primer

This supplement for Vampire: The Masquerade presents a collection of essays, treatises, and advice on becoming and remaining a prince - the supreme authority within a Camarilla domain. The book includes perspectives from princes, anarchs, and other political figures, providing both practical advice and satirical commentary on the nature of power in Kindred society.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book is a resource for understanding princedom in the Camarilla.
- It contains thoughts from princes, anarchs, and others on how to rule a city successfully.
- Includes the infamous "A Manifesto on Becoming Prince."
- Features Conclave reports, Tremere treatises, anarch memos, and more.
- Provides both serious advice and satirical commentary.

## Brief Introduction

The book opens with a note that it contains "the thoughts on ruling of a half dozen of the finest political minds the Kindred have to offer" - though the content suggests this may be somewhat tongue-in-cheek. The payment for these secrets is the reader's success, which means "some traitor I knew five centuries gone has seen his last sunset."

## How To Use This Book

The book is organized as a collection of documents from various sources, each providing different perspectives on princedom. Readers are advised to consider the source and context of each piece of advice.

## Leadership Cost Analysis

A discussion of the various costs - in resources, time, blood, and political capital - required to become and remain a prince.

Technology has made it possible for anyone to access information. Computers provide a leading edge in the modern age of cable television, personal telephones, and computer networks. It's even more important to secure a leading edge in information.

Controlling the media is crucial for maintaining the Masquerade and shaping public perception. The book discusses various methods of media manipulation and their effectiveness.

## On the Leading of the Clans

Advice on managing the various clans within a domain:

### Brujah

- If you oppose authority, then you are their brother.
- Lead them but do not rule over them - they will chafe against any authority.
- Never bully them, but if someone else has, make a show of striking back.
- Give them the tools of the fight: guns, blood, feeding grounds, political protection.

### Gangrel

- The biggest mistake is to trespass in their domain.
- They are territorial and straightforward.
- Give them what they want (often things like staying out of city parks or shutting down logging companies).
- They will not pay attention to you by virtue of many years of betrayal.

### Malkavians

- There is absolutely no discernible pattern of behavior.
- Some make excellent allies, some make terrible enemies, some make a good meal.
- Find out what each one wants, then give it to them. Or don't.
- There is nothing you can expect from a Malkavian.

### Nosferatu

- Do not show that you are offended by their appearance.
- Approach them as equals and treat them as you would any other Kindred.
- They control all the information in the city.
- They will want favors, not material goods, and the price will be high.

### Toreador

- Entering dangerous territory when dealing with this clan.
- Split between Artistes and Poseurs.
- Praise them, go to their parties, appeal to their vanity.
- Do not act rudely toward a Toreador unless doing it deliberately.

### Tremere

- Dealing with any Tremere is an invitation to trouble.
- Arrogant and vicious, they have a tendency to use and manipulate others.
- Very dangerous adversaries but even more dangerous as allies.
- Find out where the Chantry is - the real one.

### Ventrue

- They will have more money, more status, more power, more influence, and more contacts than you do.
- Every Ventrue secretly wants to be the prince.
- The only thing you can offer them is the opportunity to achieve more power.
- They will never side with a non-Ventrue.

## Other Clans

Brief advice on dealing with Assamites, Followers of Set, Giovanni, and Ravnos.

## The Sects

### The Sabbat

- If you have ever considered joining the Sabbat, make your choice now.
- The Sabbat is not much different from the Camarilla at its core.
- No matter how much an elder hates and fears you, he will hate and fear the Sabbat more.
- Once the Sabbat have established a foothold in your city, you are doomed.

### The Inconnu

- They are all powerful and are not to be trifled with.
- They consider concerns like yours to be beneath their notice.
- If you avoid them, they will avoid you.

## Other Dwellers In Darkness

### Lupines

- They rarely venture into cities.
- Avoid provoking them.
- Find out what areas they have claimed and avoid them at all costs.
- If you have contacts among the Gangrel, use them to curry favors.

### Mages

- Assume that dealing with a mage will be like dealing with a Tremere, only worse.
- Ignore the magi and they will most likely ignore you.
- If you cannot ignore a mage, strike hard, strike fast, and strike to kill.

### Wraiths

- They make superb spies.
- The trick is making contact with them.
- They all have concerns on earth which they're pathetically eager to have you protect.
- Everything you say will get back to the Giovanni eventually.

### Hunters

- Quietly get out of their way.
- If you can, carefully send them after your enemies.
- If you cannot avoid a hunter, Embrace the hunter, blood bond him, and then send him off to kill the people who sent him.

## Making Your Move

The crucial moment when you decide to take the princedom for yourself:

- **Take Power**: Power that is given by others can be taken away by others. Power that you attain and take for yourself can only be lost by you.
- **No Appointments, No Votes, No Revolutionary Councils**: Just take power.
- **Total and Absolute Loyalty**: There can be no dissent within your ranks.
- **No Mercy**: Show no mercy. The moment you do, you are admitting weakness.
- **Stir Up Troubles**: Use mortal troubles as cover for what you are doing.
- **Cut Lines of Communication**: Take down the prince's advisors.
- **Kill Him Quickly**: Put out of your head all melodramatic notions of trials and sunrises. Kill him quickly and scatter his ashes.

## Rules and Regulations

### Report from the St. Louis Conclave

A formal report from a Conclave called by Prince Richard of Clan Ventrue, providing official recommendations on the role of the prince in the modern era.

### On the Position of the Prince Within His Domain

Discussion of the prince's role, rights, and responsibilities, including:
- The prince as dominant political factor
- Rights and privileges
- The Traditions and their interpretation
- Recommendations from the Conclave

### The Traditions

Detailed discussion of each of the Six Traditions and how princes should interpret and enforce them:

1. **The Masquerade**: Should be open to even stricter interpretation than before.
2. **The Domain**: The prince has leeway to set domain as long as he possesses the power to exert his authority.
3. **The Progeny**: Must establish a firm policy, as any deviation will bring allegations of favoritism.
4. **The Accounting**: Responsibility and accountability must be the hallmarks of Kindred society.
5. **Hospitality**: Must remain sacrosanct - without knowledge, a prince has no power.
6. **Destruction**: A Blood Hunt must never be called lightly.

### Advisories on Enemies of the Camarilla

Official recommendations on dealing with:
- **The Sabbat**: Any incursion must be halted before it begins.
- **Anarchs**: Make excellent cannon fodder.
- **Assamites**: Powerful allies and dangerous enemies.
- **Followers of Set**: Are to be destroyed when practical.
- **Giovanni**: A prince is advised to work with the Giovanni presence.
- **Ravnos**: Treat all individual Ravnos as if they were potentially in the employ of the Sabbat.
- **Lupines**: The best way to combat them should they pose a threat remains the same as always.

## Princes of Note

Profiles of notable princes:

### Siegfried, Prince of Vancouver

- Originally a Visigoth chieftain.
- Founded and protected Vancouver.
- Did not declare himself prince until the 1940s.
- Maintains peace with the Lupines.
- His rule is recognized neither by the Camarilla nor the Sabbat.

### Dr. Alexander Meuser, Prince of Jerusalem

- Embraced during the Zionist movement.
- Instrumental in the creation of the State of Israel.
- Portrays himself as a diplomat rather than a ruler.
- Faces incredible violence from various factions.

### Robert Pedder, Prince of Hong Kong

- Known as the Tai Pan.
- Shaped the growth of Hong Kong throughout its history.
- Faces the loss of his domain when Hong Kong reverts to China in 1997.
- Front runner to become the next Ventrue Justicar.

### His Eminence and Benevolent Grace Lord Magnus, Rex Mundi

- The most famous (and possibly fictional) prince.
- Believed to be the brainchild of a coterie of Malkavian and Toreador Harpies.
- Appears to criticize and ridicule harsh, unpopular, or unwise princes.
- Has acquired mythic status in Kindred society.

### Christian, Brujah Elder

- An outspoken enemy of the very concept of princedom.
- Author of "A Manifesto on Becoming Prince."
- Currently finds himself the prince of a respectably sized city.
- Ironically now in the same position he has criticized so harshly.

## A Manifesto on Becoming Prince

The infamous anarch manifesto providing satirical and serious advice on seizing power, including discussions of:
- The nature of power in Kindred society
- The importance of controlling resources
- The necessity of absolute loyalty
- The art of the coup
- Maintaining power once achieved

## Key Themes

- **Power Through Control**: Princes must control scarce resources to maintain power.
- **No Mercy**: Showing mercy is admitting weakness.
- **Information is Power**: Controlling information is crucial to maintaining authority.
- **Appearances Matter**: How you are perceived is infinitely more important than how you truly are.
- **The Beast Rules**: Every Kindred looks out into the night and sees only scarcity.
- **Revolution and Betrayal**: The art of taking power requires careful planning and ruthless execution.

## Purpose and Value

Prince's Primer serves as:

- A collection of perspectives on princedom from various sources.
- Both serious advice and satirical commentary on Kindred politics.
- Examples of notable princes and their methods.
- Official Conclave recommendations on the role of the prince.
- The infamous "Manifesto on Becoming Prince."

This book is essential for anyone wanting to understand the nature of power in Camarilla society, play characters who aspire to princedom, or run chronicles focused on political intrigue and power struggles. It provides both practical advice and cautionary tales about the costs and consequences of seeking ultimate power in the World of Darkness.
