# Minds Eye Theatre - Pickering Lythe

This book presents a haunted Edwardian setting and several ready-to-run stories for Mind's Eye Theatre Vampire: The Masquerade. The book focuses on Pickering Lythe, a location that serves as the backdrop for multiple interconnected stories involving vampires, mystery, and horror.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book provides a ready-to-run setting and stories for MET Vampire: The Masquerade.
- It focuses on Pickering Lythe as a location for chronicles.
- Contains multiple interconnected stories that can be run separately or together.
- Provides Storyteller resources including NPCs, plot hooks, and scene breakdowns.

## Chapter One: Introduction

### What is Pickering Lythe?

An introduction to the setting and how to use the book, explaining the haunted Edwardian atmosphere and the types of stories that can be told in this location.

### How to Use this Book

Guidance for Storytellers on how to incorporate the setting and stories into their chronicles, including how to adapt the material for different groups and play styles.

## Chapter Two: The Festival of Lights

A story set during the Wordsworth Festival of Lights in Pickering Lythe:
- **Pickering Lythe: Wordsworth Festival of Lights**: The setting and atmosphere
- **Dramatis Personae: The Nobility of Pickering Lythe**: Key NPCs involved in the story
- **The Outsiders**: Characters from outside the local area
- **As the Night Goes On: Scene Breakdowns and Planned Events**: How to run the story
- **Sinister Seeds**: Plot hooks and complications

## Chapter Three: The Terror of Dis Pater

A horror story for vampires:
- **The Terror of Dis Pater: A Horror Story for Vampires**: The main story
- **Dramatis Personae: The Faces of Death**: NPCs involved in the horror story
- **As the Night Goes On: The Stages of Terror**: How the horror unfolds
- **Sinister Seeds**: Additional plot elements

## Chapter Four: Richard III's Revenge

A story involving historical artifacts and the Crouchbacks:
- **The Crouchbacks: Wholesalers of History**: Information on the NPCs
- **Dramatis Personae: The Bargain Hunters**: Characters seeking historical items
- **The Items for Sale**: Artifacts and objects central to the story
- **Richard III's Revenge: The Much Anticipated Sequel**: The main story
- **Sinister Seeds**: Plot complications

## Chapter Five: Black Box Shorts

Short, self-contained stories that can be run quickly:
- **How to Use this Chapter**: Guidance on running short stories
- **Who's Got the Ball?**: A short story
- **Buttons, Buttons**: Another short story
- **Love Remembered**: A story about memory and loss
- **Another Player in the Game**: A story about new arrivals
- **Court Games**: Stories involving vampire politics

## Chapter Six: Player Aides

Resources for players, including:
- **How to Use this Chapter**: Explanation of player resources
- **Permission to Reproduce**: Information on copying materials
- **Combat Cheat Sheet**: Quick reference for combat rules
- **Boon Tickets**: Physical props for tracking boons
- **Status Tickets**: Physical props for tracking status

## Appendix: Printable Character Sheets

Reproducible character sheets for use in games.

## Key Themes

- **Haunted Edwardian Setting**: The book emphasizes a gothic, historical atmosphere.
- **Interconnected Stories**: Multiple stories that can be run separately or together.
- **Mystery and Horror**: Stories focus on uncovering secrets and facing horrors.
- **Vampire Politics**: Court intrigue and political maneuvering among vampires.
- **Historical Elements**: Stories incorporate historical artifacts and figures.

## Purpose and Value

MET Pickering Lythe serves as:
- A ready-to-run setting for MET Vampire: The Masquerade chronicles
- Multiple interconnected stories that can be adapted for different groups
- Storyteller resources including NPCs, plot hooks, and scene breakdowns
- Player aids and resources for running games
- A haunted Edwardian setting with gothic atmosphere

This book is useful for Storytellers who want ready-to-run stories and a detailed setting for their MET Vampire: The Masquerade games, providing everything needed to run multiple interconnected stories in the Pickering Lythe location.
