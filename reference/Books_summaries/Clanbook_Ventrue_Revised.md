# Clanbook: Ventrue (Revised)

This supplement for Vampire: The Masquerade provides comprehensive information on Clan Ventrue, the "Blue Bloods" who have assumed the prestigious but demanding role of leaders throughout Kindred history. The book explores the clan's history, organization, and the question of whether their leadership is truly for the good of all Kindred or merely an empty ruse that excuses their excess and greed.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book provides detailed information on Clan Ventrue for players and Storytellers.
- It explores the clan's role as leaders and their organization.
- Examines the question of whether Ventrue leadership is genuine service or self-serving manipulation.
- Provides character creation options, new Disciplines, and information on Ventrue society.

## Introduction: To Fail and Succeed

The book opens with a story illustrating Ventrue methods of influence and control, showing how they use Dominate, Presence, and political manipulation to achieve their goals. The story demonstrates the clan's approach to leadership and the lengths they will go to maintain control.

## Chapter One: The Kings of the Kindred

### The History of Leadership

According to their own oral history and annals, the Ventrue have Embraced only those "worthy" of the honor. Nobles, religious leaders, and great military men have all found their way into Clan Ventrue over the centuries. The Ventrue have high standards for admittance into their ultra-exclusive club of undead.

### Gender and Ethnicity

While gender plays no noticeable role in a Kindred once Embraced, it has traditionally mattered a great deal before that fateful night. Women did not command the kind of influence that men had in Western society for not just centuries, but millennia. The number of men far outweighs the number of women, especially among the older Kindred - even in the modern nights. Clan Ventrue is very much an aged and malevolent "boy's club."

### The Embrace

The Ventrue want those accustomed to wielding influence and authority, those for whom command is second nature. They want men for the most part, though exceptions arise. The clan is very much an aged and malevolent "boy's club," though modern nights see more diversity.

## Chapter Two: The King is Dead, Long Live the King

### Clan Organization

Of late, many have compared the Ventrue clan's organization to a corporation. While this simile seems appropriate, it is largely inaccurate. The clan's current hierarchical arrangement predates the modern corporation by many centuries, and it is rather a product of the clan's classical roots.

### The Roman Model

The system borrows heavily from the Roman Republic's political institutions. Although the democratic elements of that system were stripped away long ago, the overall structure and some of the names remain. This system runs parallel to the Camarilla's organization.

### The Directorate (The Ephorate)

Most young Ventrue have heard about the Directorate, the shadowy body of 12 or so elder Ventrue that has tremendous sway over the clan. Older Ventrue still remember the older name, the Ephorate, by which the council was known for many centuries. The classical name comes from the ruling council of ancient Sparta.

### Powers of the Ephorate

The ephors establish precedent in matters of "clan policy." They manage several clanwide endeavors and clan holdings. Ventrue have vast monetary reserves spread through banks and secret caches around the world. They own vast amounts of real estate, hold stock in every major corporation, and hold controlling interests in more than a few of them.

The ephors also choose the clan's representative to the Camarilla Inner Circle. They set policy for the clan as a whole. Perhaps the Ephorate's most important power is its role in arbitrating intra-clan disputes.

### The Elders (Strategoi)

Clan Ventrue has many venerable members who do not sit on the Board of Directors. Some are princes, others primogen, but all have garnered great respect within the clan. The strategoi are the honorary "generals" of Clan Ventrue, as their name suggests. They carry out the policies that the secretive ephors hand down.

### Clan Titles

Ventrue love titles and offices, although most would never admit to such emotional attachment. The Ventrue have made distinct two levels of title: local and clanwide. In each case, the highest-ranking Ventrue have the greatest discretion over promoting and demoting others through the ranks.

### Advancement

Increasing esteem within the clan takes more than just ability. The elders, particularly the local and international Directorates, approve who advances when. Personal feelings, alliances, jealousies, contacts, bribes, favors, and a host of other purely political factors come into play.

## Key Themes

- **Noblesse Oblige**: The idea that leadership comes with responsibility, though whether this is genuine or a ruse is questioned.
- **The Blue Bloods**: The clan's reputation as the aristocratic leaders of Kindred society.
- **Organization and Hierarchy**: The complex system of titles, offices, and advancement within the clan.
- **Influence and Control**: The Ventrue's methods of maintaining power through economic, political, and social means.
- **The Directorate**: The shadowy council that guides the clan's policies and direction.
- **Dignitas**: The concept of honor and status that drives Ventrue behavior.
