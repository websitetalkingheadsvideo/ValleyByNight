# The Long Night

The document is a narrative excerpt from a role-playing game setting, specifically focusing on the dark and mysterious world of vampires in the "Vampire: The Dark Ages" game.

## Overview of "Vampire: The Dark Ages"

This text presents "Vampire: The Dark Ages" for Mind's Eye Theatre, detailing its setting, gameplay, and character creation. It emphasizes the importance of storytelling and safety in live-action roleplaying.

## Gameplay and Safety Rules

- The Long Night is a live-action roleplaying game set in the Dark Ages.
- Players must adhere to safety rules, including no physical contact and no weapons.
- Emphasis on fun and storytelling over competition.

## Character Creation and Roleplaying

- Players create unique vampire characters with distinct personalities and backgrounds.
- Characters should be vivid and expressive, contributing to the overall story.
- The Storyteller guides the narrative while players make decisions for their characters.

## Setting and Historical Context

- The game is set in 1197, during a time of societal change and the looming Inquisition.
- Vampires exist alongside various supernatural beliefs held by mortals.
- The narrative explores themes of power, competition, and the search for salvation among vampires.

## Generations of Vampires and Their Hierarchy

- The Antediluvians are the legendary forefathers of vampire clans, believed to be engaged in a silent war known as the Jyhad.
- Methuselahs, from the fourth and fifth generations, serve as tools for the Antediluvians, with few remaining active.
- Elders from the sixth and seventh generations hold significant power, while ancillae from the eighth and ninth generations are influential but rarely claim titles.
- Neonates, from the tenth and eleventh generations, are modern vampires, while those from the twelfth and thirteenth generations are often disregarded.

## Clan Influence on Power Dynamics

- Vampires are divided into 13 clans, with power derived from age, generation, and clan lineage.
- A Ventrue ancilla in a Ventrue city holds more respect than a Tremere of the same age in the same location.

## Sects and Their Political Roles

- Various sects exist within Kindred society, influencing power dynamics across age, generation, and clan.
- The Inconnu, Furores, Autarkis, and Promethians represent different factions with unique goals and societal roles.

## Positive Physical Traits Overview

Positive physical traits enhance a character's physical abilities, resilience, and combat effectiveness. These traits are beneficial in various physical challenges and combat scenarios.

- **Eah Melding**: Useful for resisting damage while injured.
- **Stalwart**: Provides strength against overwhelming odds.
- **Steady**: Ensures controlled weapon attacks and piloting.
- **Tenacious**: Allows prolonging physical confrontations.
- **Tireless**: Enhances stamina for endurance challenges.
- **Tough**: Useful when wounded or winded.
- **Vigorous**: Aids in defensive combat and athletic challenges.
- **Wiry**: Supports acrobatic movements and grappling.

## Negative Physical Traits Overview

Negative physical traits hinder a character's physical capabilities and resilience. These traits can lead to disadvantages in physical confrontations and challenges.

- **Clumsy**: Lacks coordination and balance.
- **Cowardly**: Prioritizes self-preservation over confrontation.
- **Decrepit**: Moves slowly and tires easily.
- **Delicate**: Easily damaged by physical harm.
- **Docile**: Lacks persistence in physical confrontations.
- **Flabby**: Underdeveloped muscles limit strength application.
- **Lame**: Disabled in one or more limbs.
- **Lethargic**: Exhibits a serious lack of energy.
- **Puny**: Overall weak physical presence.
- **Sickly**: Responds poorly to physical stress.

## Positive Social Traits Overview

Positive social traits enhance a character's ability to interact, persuade, and lead others effectively. These traits are crucial in social challenges and leadership situations.

- **Alluring**: Inspires desire and attraction in others.
- **Charismatic**: Motivates and inspires followers.
- **Charming**: Persuades and convinces others easily.
- **Compassionate**: Shows care for the weak and downtrodden.
- **Dignified**: Carries oneself honorably and attractively.
- **Diplomatic**: Navigates social situations tactfully.
- **Eloquent**: Speaks convincingly and interestingly.
- **Friendly**: Easily fits in and is likable.
- **Intimidating**: Commands respect and fear from others.

## Negative Social Traits Overview

Negative social traits impede a character's social interactions and can lead to unfavorable perceptions. These traits can create barriers in social challenges and relationships.

- **Bestial**: Appears inhuman and feral.
- **Callous**: Unfeeling and insensitive to others.
- **Condescending**: Displays contempt for others.
- **Dull**: Boring and uninteresting in conversation.
- **Naive**: Lacks sophistication and maturity.
- **Obnoxious**: Annoying in speech or action.
- **Repugnant**: Disgusting appearance to others.
- **Shy**: Timid and socially hesitant.
- **Tactless**: Inappropriate in social situations.
- **Untrustworthy**: Perceived as unreliable.

## Positive Mental Traits Overview

Positive mental traits enhance a character's cognitive abilities, awareness, and emotional resilience. These traits are beneficial in mental challenges and defensive situations.

- **Alert**: Reacts quickly to danger.
- **Attentive**: Notices extraordinary occurrences.
- **Calm**: Maintains self-control under disturbance.
- **Clever**: Resourceful and quick-witted.
- **Creative**: Generates original ideas and solutions.
- **Cunning**: Ingenious and crafty in actions.
- **Dedicated**: Fully committed to beliefs.
- **Determined**: Unwavering in mental endeavors.
- **Discerning**: Picks out details and subtleties.

## Negative Mental Traits Overview

Negative mental traits hinder a character's cognitive functions and emotional stability. These traits can lead to challenges in mental confrontations and decision-making.

- **Forgetful**: Struggles to remember important information.
- **Gullible**: Easily deceived or fooled.
- **Impatient**: Intolerant of delays and restless.
- **Oblivious**: Unaware of surroundings and events.
- **Predictable**: Lacks originality in actions.
- **Shortsighted**: Fails to see beyond the superficial.
- **Submissive**: Lacks assertiveness and backbone.
- **Violent**: Prone to uncontrollable rage.
- **Witless**: Slow to process information.

## Followers of Set and Their Beliefs

- Followers of Set embrace a moral code from Set himself, valuing corruption, pain, and sin.
- They believe true happiness comes from succumbing to one's darkest desires and weaknesses.

## The Road of Typhon

- The Road of Typhon is akin to a religion, focusing on the worship of entities through blood sacrifices.
- Followers reject ideals of purity, believing enlightenment is found in corruption and decay.

## Derangements and Their Impact

- All vampires have Derangements that can activate under stress, controlling their actions.
- Examples include Amnesia, Angelism, and Paranoia, each with specific triggers and effects.

## Merits and Flaws in Character Creation

- Merits enhance character traits, while Flaws provide negative traits for balance.
- Players can customize characters with various Merits and Flaws, impacting gameplay and interactions.

## Lasombra Clan Overview

- Lasombra are known for their ability to become invisible in reflective surfaces, marking them as supernatural.
- Suggested moral paths include the Road of Heaven, Road of Chivalry, and Road of Humanity.

## Malkavian Clan Overview

- Malkavians are feared and ridiculed for their madness, which they believe grants them insight.
- They can ignore Status Traits in social challenges once per evening, but must choose a permanent Derangement at character creation.

## Nosferatu Clan Overview

- Nosferatu are outcasts due to their grotesque appearance and smell, leading them to master information and espionage.
- They cannot initiate social challenges while visible, except in intimidation scenarios.

## Ravnos Clan Overview

- Ravnos are nomadic tricksters known for thievery and deception, often accepted in cities to avoid mass visits from their clan.
- Each Ravnos must indulge in their chosen crime at least once per session, leading to distrust among other Kindred.

## Toreador Clan Overview

- Toreador are passionate about beauty and art, often becoming entranced by captivating subjects.
- They can harvest vitae from their herd but risk distraction from their surroundings.

## Tremere Clan Overview

- Tremere are former mages who became vampires, facing distrust from other clans due to their origins.
- They have a hierarchical structure, with lower-ranking members receiving training and knowledge from superiors.

## Tzimisce Clan Overview

- Tzimisce are known for their cruelty and territorial nature, often ruling their domains with an iron fist.
- They receive a free Finance or Politics Influence Trait but risk gaining Derangements from excessive use of their Discipline, Vicissitude.

## Ventrue Clan Overview

- Ventrue are dedicated to rulership and order, often associated with wealth and resources.
- Each member has a specific blood preference, which can complicate feeding.

## Laibon Bloodline Overview

- Laibon are solitary hunters and shamans from Africa, known for their magical abilities to control their Beasts.
- They consume blood more quickly than other vampires, requiring careful management of feeding.

## Lhiannan Bloodline Overview

- Lhiannan are remnants of pagan traditions, often hunted by mortals and Kindred alike.
- They are at a disadvantage against Faith magic due to their pagan nature.

## Lamia Bloodline Overview

- Lamia are created by Cappadocians and serve as bodyguards, worshipping the Dark Mother Lilith.
- They carry a plague that can infect those they feed upon, leading to restrictions in their movement.

## Salubri Clan Overview

- Salubri are healers and teachers, now hunted by the Tremere after the death of their founder.
- They can attain Golconda more easily than others but face challenges in gaining shelter due to their status.

## Shadow Discipline and Physical Traits

- Shadows possess three Physical Traits, customizable by characters.
- Additional Blood Traits can enhance shadow tendrils with more Physical Traits.

## Ogham Discipline and Its Powers

Unique to Lhiannan, Ogham allows inscribing runic glyphs for various effects.

- **Three powers**: Consecrate the Grove, Crimson Woad, and Moon and Sun, each with specific effects and costs.

## Potence Discipline and Supernatural Strength

- Potence grants enhanced physical strength, allowing for incredible feats.
- Basic, Intermediate, and Advanced levels provide abilities like Prowess, Might, Vigor, and Puissance.

## Presence Discipline and Charisma

- Presence enhances charisma, affecting emotions rather than logic.
- Powers include Majesty, which prevents aggression against the user.

## Protean Discipline and Transformation

- Protean allows transformation into nonhuman forms, like claws or mist.
- Basic, Intermediate, and Advanced levels provide various transformation abilities.

## Quietis Discipline and Secrecy

- Quietis focuses on silence and stealth, ideal for assassins.
- Powers include Silence of Death and Blood Sweat, affecting targets' abilities.

## Serpentis Discipline and Set's Powers

- Serpentis is unique to Followers of Set, featuring powers like Eyes of the Serpent.
- Abilities include paralyzing gazes and transforming into a serpent.

## Thaumaturgy Discipline and Blood Manipulation

- Clan Tremere's secret Discipline, allowing manipulation of blood for various effects.
- Powers include Blood Mastery and Theft of Vitae, with advanced rituals for greater effects.

## Combat and Challenge System Overview

- Challenges involve bidding Traits and resolving outcomes through Rock-Paper-Scissors.
- Multi-party challenges and surprise rules add complexity to combat scenarios.

## Weapon Examples in Dark Medieval World

- Various weapons are categorized for ease of play, including knives, clubs, and swords.
- Each weapon has bonus traits, negative traits, and concealability options, affecting gameplay.

## Armor and Protection Mechanics

- **Three types of armor**: leather (absorbs 1 Health Level), chain (absorbs 2, heavy), and plate (absorbs 3, heavy and clumsy).
- Shields are indestructible and can absorb damage if the attacker fails a Simple Test.

## Frenzy and Its Management

- Frenzy types include Rage, Control, and Terror, triggered by specific situations.
- Players can stave off frenzy with Willpower Traits or attempt to end it through challenges.

## Blood Oath and Its Implications

- Blood Oath binds a Thrall to a Regnant through repeated blood consumption.
- Breaking a Blood Oath requires significant Willpower and can lead to permanent Status loss.

## True Faith as a Defense Mechanism

- True Faith allows mortals to repel vampires through belief in a higher power.
- Engaging in meditation can restore Willpower Traits, enhancing the character's abilities.

## Importance of Session Reports

- Journals of events from each session help track changes in the chronicle.
- Reports from Narrators and players should be informal and confidential.
- Consider making reports mandatory or tie experience rewards to their submission.

## Balancing Player Satisfaction

- Players enjoy character advancement and story engagement.
- Provide opportunities for characters to improve their status without overwhelming power.
- Allow for character setbacks to fuel motivation and revenge plots.

## Setting the Scene for Storytelling

- The setting significantly impacts the story's mood and feel.
- Choose locations based on the story's needs, considering logistics and atmosphere.
- Use decorations, music, and lighting to enhance the immersive experience.

## Effective Use of Props in Gameplay

- Props should enhance the story without overshadowing character interactions.
- General props can provide clues or insights into characters.
- Personal props help players embody their characters, enhancing the medieval atmosphere.
