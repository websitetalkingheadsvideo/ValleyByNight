# Laws of the Night

The document is the core rulebook for Mind's Eye Theatre Vampire: The Masquerade, providing all the essential rules, systems, and information needed to play and run live-action roleplaying games in the World of Darkness.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book contains all the information needed to start playing and telling stories in Mind's Eye Theatre.
- It is based on the tabletop creation Vampire: The Masquerade, updated for live-action play.
- This edition has been created to update the rules and correct bugs that have appeared through continuous play.
- It provides elegantly simple rules designed to be easy to play and easier to start.

## Introduction: Stories Around the Fire

The book opens with a discussion of storytelling as the oldest form of entertainment, tracing the evolution from oral tradition through books, radio, and television to roleplaying games and live-action roleplaying (LARP). Mind's Eye Theatre is described as a return to the oldest form of storytelling - interactive, participatory, and collaborative.

### What is Mind's Eye Theatre?

- Unlike traditional games, it doesn't have cards, dice, or a playing board.
- It's far more concerned with the stories to be told along the way than "winning."
- It's like being in a movie - you and your friends are the characters, but there is no script.
- Most scenes are played out in real-time - an hour in the make-believe world is 60 minutes long.
- Players always remain in character during the game unless there is a rules dispute.

### Characters

- When you play Masquerade, you take on another persona, most likely a vampire.
- Your character can be almost anything - from any walk of life, age, creed, race, or sex.
- You decide what your character says or does, where she goes, and what choices she makes.
- Characters are the heart and soul of the story - they have pasts, likes and dislikes, goals and dreams.

### The Storyteller

- The Storyteller creates the world that the players move through.
- She creates a skeletal framework of setting and plot, then turns the characters loose to put flesh on its bones.
- She acts as an impartial judge when rules are questioned, describes scenes that can't be staged, and plays antagonists.
- The Storyteller is usually assisted by Narrators.

## The Rules Thou Shalt Never Break

These are the most important and immutable rules of MET, the ones that keep your game and your players safe:

1. **No Touching. No Stunts.** - None whatsoever. Save the stunts for your imagination.
2. **No Weapons** - Fake or real weapons of any sort are forbidden. Use item cards instead.
3. **No Drugs or Drinking** - Players impaired by drugs and alcohol are a threat to other players and the game.
4. **It's Only a Game** - Don't take things too seriously. Leave the game behind when the session's over.
5. **Be Mindful of Others** - Not everyone around you is playing the game. Be considerate of non-players.
6. **Do What Works For You** - The "Golden Rule" - feel free to adjust rules for better games.
7. **Have Fun** - Not "Win." Not "Go out and conquer everyone else." Just have fun.

## The World of Darkness

- On the surface, the World of Darkness is not too much different from our own.
- Below the surface, however, is a much darker element, fed by violence and despair.
- **Gothic-Punk** describes the attitude - Gothic for the sinister, looming shadow; Punk for the rebellion against power.
- The world is a place of mystery, best left alone for safety's sake.

### The Kindred

- Vampires are immortal, sentient creatures with faint similarities to their mortal selves.
- They require the blood of the living to survive.
- They are burned by sunlight and must avoid it or risk death.
- They are nocturnal creatures, finding it difficult to stay awake during the day.
- The Embrace is the process by which a new vampire is created - almost never given lightly.

### The Camarilla

- Dates back to the years of the Inquisition.
- The Masquerade is both guiding principle and ironclad policy.
- Claims all vampires to be under its aegis.
- Six clans hold full membership: Brujah, Malkavian, Nosferatu, Toreador, Tremere, and Ventrue.
- The Inner Circle consists of one representative for each clan.
- Justicars are appointed by the Inner Circle to be judge, jury, and executioner.
- Archons are chosen by justicars to act in their name.

### The Sabbat

- Spawned by the Inquisition and the Anarch Revolt.
- Claims two clans: Lasombra and Tzimisce, plus antitribu.
- Organizes itself in a parody of the Catholic Church.
- Freedom and loyalty are the guiding principles.
- Looks ahead to the nights of Gehenna, determined to survive by any means necessary.

### The Traditions

The inviolate rules of Kindred existence:

1. **The Masquerade**: Thou shalt not reveal thy true nature to those not of the Blood.
2. **The Domain**: Thy domain is thy concern. All others owe thee respect while in it.
3. **The Progeny**: Thou shalt sire another only with permission of thine elder.
4. **The Accounting**: Those thou create are thine own childer. Their sins are thine to endure.
5. **Hospitality**: Honor one another's domain. Present thyself when coming to a foreign city.
6. **Destruction**: Thou art forbidden to destroy another of thy kind. Only the eldest may call the blood hunt.

### The Generation Spread

- **Second Generation**: Directly sired by Caine - little is known.
- **Third Generation**: The Antediluvians - founders of the clans.
- **Fourth and Fifth Generations**: Methuselahs - millennia old and almost as powerful.
- **Sixth, Seventh, and Eighth Generations**: Elders - most visible players of the Jyhad.
- **Ninth and Tenth Generations**: Ancillae - walk a dangerous line.
- **11th, 12th, and 13th Generations**: Neonates and young ancillae - most MET characters.
- **14th and 15th Generations**: Thin-blooded - weak blood, may herald Gehenna.

## Chapter Three: Character Creation and Traits

Everything needed to create a character:

### Steps of Character Creation

1. **Inspiration**: Choose clan, Nature, and Demeanor
2. **Attributes**: Prioritize Attributes (7/5/3 for regular vampires, 10/8/6 for elders)
3. **Advantages**: Choose Abilities (5 for regular, 8 for elders), Disciplines (3 for regular, 6 for elders), and Influences
4. **Last Touches**: Assign Blood Traits, Willpower Traits, Virtue Traits, Status Traits, choose Negative Traits
5. **Spark of Life**: Narrative descriptions and Extra Traits

### Attributes

- **Physical**: Strength, Dexterity, Stamina
- **Social**: Charisma, Manipulation, Appearance
- **Mental**: Perception, Intelligence, Wits

### Abilities

- **Talents**: Alertness, Athletics, Awareness, Brawl, Dodge, Empathy, Expression, Intimidation, Leadership, Streetwise, Subterfuge
- **Skills**: Animal Ken, Crafts, Drive, Etiquette, Firearms, Melee, Performance, Security, Stealth, Survival
- **Knowledges**: Academics, Computer, Finance, Investigation, Law, Linguistics, Medicine, Occult, Politics, Science

### Disciplines

The mysterious powers granted on the Embrace, including:
- Animalism, Auspex, Celerity, Chimerstry, Dementation, Dominate, Fortitude
- Melpominee, Necromancy, Obeah, Obfuscate, Obtenebration, Potence, Presence
- Protean, Quietus, Serpentis, Thanatosis, Thaumaturgy, Vicissitude

### Backgrounds

- **Allies**: Mortal confederates and aides
- **Contacts**: Sources of reliable information
- **Fame**: Outstanding reputation in mortal society
- **Generation**: Potency of one's blood (Storyteller approval required)
- **Herd**: Mortal followers who provide easy access to blood
- **Influence**: Sway over institutions of mortal society
- **Mentor**: An older or more experienced Cainite instructor
- **Resources**: Material wealth and access to ready cash
- **Retainers**: Loyal servants who oversee projects and property

### Blood Traits

- Each vampire has a certain number of Blood Traits, or vitae.
- Blood can be used for healing, powering Disciplines, augmenting Physical Traits, creating ghouls, and appearing human.
- Starting Blood Traits are determined by generation.
- Vampires become hungry at five or fewer Blood Traits, ravenous at two or fewer.

### Willpower Traits

- Measure the strength of resolve and sense of self.
- Can be spent to refresh lost Traits, gain retests, enter challenges without appropriate Abilities, control frenzy, ignore wound penalties, suppress derangements.
- Starting Willpower is determined by generation.

### Virtue Traits

Three categories:
- **Conscience/Conviction**: Adherence to chosen morality
- **Self-Control/Instinct**: Ability to guide or resist frenzy
- **Courage**: Battle against Rötschreck (fear of fire/sunlight)

### Morality Traits

- Show how closely you adhere to your chosen codes of ethics.
- Starting Morality equals the average of Conscience/Conviction and Self-Control/Instinct Traits.
- Low Morality indicates closeness to losing control to the Beast forever.

### Negative Traits

Represent weaknesses or shortcomings:
- **Physical**: Clumsy, Cowardly, Decrepit, Delicate, Docile, Flabby, Lame, Lethargic, Puny, Sickly
- **Social**: Bestial, Callous, Condescending, Dull, Feral, Naïve, Obnoxious, Repugnant, Shy, Tactless, Untrustworthy
- **Mental**: Forgetful, Gullible, Ignorant, Impatient, Oblivious, Predictable, Shortsighted, Submissive, Violent, Witless

### Derangements

Mental instabilities or neurotic behaviors. Taking a derangement counts as two Negative Traits.

### Flaws

Specific deficiencies or drawbacks, rated from one to seven Traits. A character may total up to seven Traits of Flaws.

### Free Traits

Each character gets five Free Traits to spend on any area of development. Additional Free Traits are earned from Negative Traits, Flaws, or Derangements.

## Chapter Four: Disciplines

Detailed descriptions of the mysterious powers granted on the Embrace:

### Basic Disciplines

- **Animalism**: Communion with and control over natural animals
- **Auspex**: Incredible sensory acuity and psychic sensitivity
- **Celerity**: Superhuman speed
- **Chimerstry**: Crafting illusions and hallucinations (Ravnos)
- **Dementation**: Catalyzing madness (Malkavian)
- **Dominate**: Control of minds through piercing gaze
- **Fortitude**: Resilience against forces that normally injure vampires
- **Melpominee**: Voice manipulation (Daughters of Cacophony)
- **Necromancy**: Commerce with and power over the dead
- **Obeah**: Shelter and heal the injured (Salubri)
- **Obfuscate**: Concealment through tricking minds
- **Obtenebration**: Manipulation of tangible darkness (Lasombra)
- **Potence**: Incredible strength
- **Presence**: Unnatural charisma and ability to sway emotions
- **Protean**: Shapeshifting and wilderness survival (Gangrel)
- **Quietus**: Manipulating blood to bring silent death (Assamites)
- **Serpentis**: Reptilian transformation (Setites)
- **Thanatosis**: Control of death and decay (Samedi)
- **Thaumaturgy**: Tremere blood magic
- **Vicissitude**: Shaping flesh and bone like clay (Tzimisce)

Each Discipline includes detailed descriptions of Basic, Intermediate, Advanced, Master, and sometimes Elder and Methuselah level powers.

## Chapter Five: Rules, Systems and Drama

The basic rules needed for play:

### Challenges

- **Physical Challenges**: Tests of strength, speed, dexterity
- **Social Challenges**: Tests of charisma, manipulation, persuasion
- **Mental Challenges**: Tests of intelligence, perception, wits
- **Static Challenges**: Tests against a fixed difficulty

### Combat

- Rock-Paper-Scissors based resolution
- Damage types: Bashing, Lethal, Aggravated
- Staking rules
- Healing rules

### Health Levels

- Healthy
- Bruised
- Hurt
- Injured
- Wounded
- Mauled
- Crippled
- Incapacitated

### Frenzy

- **Frenzy**: Rage-induced loss of control
- **Rötschreck**: Fear-induced flight from fire or sunlight
- Controlled by Self-Control/Instinct Virtue Tests

### The Blood Bond

- Created by drinking another vampire's blood three times
- Creates powerful emotional attachment
- Can be broken only through special means (Vaulderie for Sabbat)

### Derangements

Mental instabilities that can develop from low Humanity or traumatic experiences.

### Healing

- Bashing damage heals quickly
- Lethal damage requires blood expenditure
- Aggravated damage requires significant blood expenditure and time

## Key Themes

- **Story First**: The game is about telling stories, not winning
- **Elegant Simplicity**: Rules are designed to be easy to learn and use
- **Character Focus**: Characters are the heart and soul of the story
- **Safety**: Rules exist to keep players safe and games enjoyable
- **Flexibility**: The Golden Rule - adjust rules to make your game better
- **Collaboration**: Storyteller and players work together to create stories

## Purpose and Value

Laws of the Night serves as:

- The essential rulebook for Mind's Eye Theatre Vampire: The Masquerade
- A complete guide to character creation and gameplay
- A reference for all Disciplines, clans, and systems
- A guide for Storytellers on running games
- An introduction to the World of Darkness setting

This book is the foundation for all Mind's Eye Theatre Vampire play, providing everything needed to create characters, understand the rules, and tell stories in the World of Darkness while emphasizing safety, fun, and collaborative storytelling.
