# Clanbook: Followers of Set (Revised)

This supplement for Vampire: The Masquerade provides comprehensive information on the Followers of Set, the "Children of the God of Storms" known for their religious devotion, corruption, and mastery of Serpentis. The book explores the clan's history, their various faiths and sects, their role as corruptors and tempters, and their belief that they are descended from a god. The book examines how Setites navigate a world that fears and hates them but is also drawn to their power.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book provides detailed information on the Followers of Set for players and Storytellers.
- It explores the clan's history and their belief in their divine origin.
- Examines the various Setite faiths and sects, including the Children of Damballah and the Tlacique.
- Provides character creation options, new Disciplines, and information on Setite culture.

## Introduction: The Healing Power of Religion

The book opens with a story of a mortal's corruption and eventual Embrace into the Followers of Set, illustrating the clan's methods of temptation and conversion. A drug-addicted woman is tested and eventually Embraced, becoming a servant of Set. The story demonstrates the Setites' approach to finding and converting mortals, and their use of religion and temptation as tools.

## Chapter One: A History of Lies

### The Origins

The Followers of Set trace their lineage to Set, who they believe was a god who became a vampire. According to their beliefs, Set was the Egyptian god of storms, chaos, and the desert, who was betrayed and cast down. The Setites believe that Set will return one day to reclaim his power.

### The God of Storms

Set was the Egyptian god of storms, chaos, and the desert. The Setites believe that their founder was this god, who was betrayed by his brother Osiris and cast down. They await his return and work to prepare the world for his coming.

### The Various Faiths

The Followers of Set are not a monolithic group. They are divided into various faiths and sects, each with its own interpretation of Set's teachings:
- **The Children of Damballah**: Setites who follow voodoo traditions
- **The Tlacique**: Bloodthirsty Setites who practice human sacrifice
- **Traditional Setites**: Those who follow the original Egyptian faith

### The Role of Corruption

The Setites see themselves as agents of Set's will, working to corrupt and tempt others. They believe that by spreading corruption and temptation, they prepare the world for Set's return. This mission gives them purpose and justifies their actions.

## Chapter Two: After Such Knowledge

### Clan Culture

The Setites are bound together not only by blood but by ties of faith. Their priests have access to secrets that would break the minds of lesser vampires. Even the youngest Setite childe has passed through the Gates of Revelation, experiencing the truth of Set's power.

### The Temples

Setite temples are centers of worship, corruption, and power. They serve as havens, meeting places, and repositories of religious knowledge. Each temple is dedicated to Set and serves as a focal point for the clan's activities.

### Serpentis

Serpentis is the Setites' unique Discipline, allowing them to transform into serpents, control serpents, and perform other snake-related powers. This Discipline reflects their connection to Set, the serpent god.

### Corruption and Temptation

The Setites are masters of corruption and temptation. They know how to find people's weaknesses and exploit them. They use drugs, sex, power, and other temptations to corrupt mortals and Kindred alike.

### Relationships with Other Clans

The Setites have complex relationships with other clans:
- They are feared and hated for their corruption and methods
- They are sometimes needed for their knowledge and power
- They are distrusted because of their religious fanaticism
- They maintain their independence from both Camarilla and Sabbat

## Chapter Three: Broken Images

Profiles of significant Setites, including their achievements, methods, and roles in Kindred society.

## Key Themes

- **Divine Origin**: The Setites' belief that they are descended from a god.
- **Corruption**: The clan's role as agents of corruption and temptation.
- **Faith**: The various Setite faiths and their religious devotion.
- **The Return**: The Setites' belief that Set will return one day.
- **Temptation**: The clan's mastery of finding and exploiting weaknesses.
- **Serpentis**: The Setites' unique Discipline and their connection to serpents.
