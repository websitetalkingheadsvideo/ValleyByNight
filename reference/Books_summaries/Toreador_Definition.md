# Toreador Definition

## Overview

**Toreador** (also known as "The Degenerates" or "The Artistes") are one of the seven founding clans of the Camarilla, tracing their lineage to Arikel, one of the Third Generation. They are defined by their deep appreciation of beauty, art, and aesthetics, which shapes every aspect of their unlife.

## Core Characteristics

### Art and Beauty

The Toreador are fundamentally defined by their appreciation of art and beauty in all its forms. They are drawn to beautiful things—whether works of art, beautiful people, or beautiful experiences. This appreciation is not merely superficial; it is a driving force that influences their choices, relationships, and place in Kindred society.

### The Division: Artistes vs. Poseurs

The clan is divided between two groups:
- **Artistes**: True artists who create and produce art
- **Poseurs**: Those who merely appreciate or pretend to be artists

This division creates ongoing tension within the clan, as Artistes often look down on Poseurs, while Poseurs may resent the Artistes' natural talent. This internal conflict shapes Toreador politics and social dynamics.

## Role in Camarilla Society

### Harpies

The Toreador are the most prevalent clan to serve as **Harpies**—the court position responsible for tracking status and recording boons within Camarilla domains. Toreador harpies use their social skills, connections, and appreciation of beauty to maintain influence and shape the direction of the Camarilla. They often sweep support from multiple clans in their domains, positioning their clan as a dominating force within the sect.

### Social Influence

Toreador frequently use their influences and sway to move the Camarilla in directions they see fit, often influencing political races as a result. Their high clan status makes ascending to positions of influence easier, even for younger members of the clan.

### Primogen Service

Toreador often serve as primogen, using their social skills to maintain influence within the Camarilla's political structure.

### The Grand Harpy Rumor

There are persistent rumors about **The Grand Harpy**—a matriarchal figure that all other harpies allegedly follow when making decisions. According to these rumors, the Grand Harpy was appointed by the Inner Circle with jurisdiction over every Camarilla domain. While no actual evidence has been found for this position, speculation suggests that if such a position exists, the current Grand Harpy is rumored to be a **Toreador who resides in New York**. This rumor reflects the Toreador's dominance in harpy positions and their reputation for wielding social influence across the Camarilla.

## Appearance and Style

Toreador are known for their focus on **high fashion** and aesthetics. They emphasize appearance, style, and presentation, though they are advised to avoid over-the-top competition that might undermine their sophisticated image.

## Behavioral Characteristics

### Entranced by Beauty

Toreador are passionate about beauty and art, often becoming **entranced by captivating subjects**. This can be both a strength and a vulnerability, as their appreciation of beauty can distract them from their surroundings. When harvesting vitae from their herd, they risk becoming so absorbed in the beauty of the moment that they lose awareness of potential threats or important events around them.

### Social Interaction

When dealing with Toreador:
- **Praise them** - They respond well to flattery and appreciation of their aesthetic sensibilities
- **Go to their parties** - Social gatherings are important to Toreador culture and influence
- **Appeal to their vanity** - Their self-image and presentation matter greatly to them
- **Do not act rudely** - Unless doing so deliberately as a calculated move, rudeness toward a Toreador can have serious social consequences

Dealing with Toreador can be "entering dangerous territory" due to their social influence and the potential for offense if their aesthetic sensibilities are not respected.

## Historical Context

The Toreador have always been associated with beauty, art, and aesthetics since their founding. They are one of the founding clans of the Camarilla and play an important role in maintaining and shaping Camarilla society. Their relationship with art and beauty is both their greatest strength and their greatest weakness, as their appreciation can lead them to become lost in beauty or vulnerable to manipulation through aesthetic appeal.

## Key Themes

- **Beauty**: Appreciation of beauty in all its forms, often to the point of becoming entranced
- **Art**: Relationship with art as both creators and appreciators
- **Aesthetics**: Focus on appearance, style, and presentation
- **Social Influence**: Use of social skills and connections to maintain power
- **Camarilla Politics**: Active role in shaping and directing the sect
- **Vanity**: Strong need for praise, recognition, and appreciation
- **Distraction**: Risk of becoming so absorbed in beauty that they lose awareness of their surroundings

## Summary

The Toreador are the aesthetes and artists of the Camarilla, defined by their appreciation of beauty and their role as social influencers. They are the most common harpies, using their social skills and connections to maintain significant influence within Kindred society, while their internal division between true artists and poseurs creates ongoing tension within the clan.

Their passion for beauty can be both their greatest strength and their greatest weakness—they become entranced by captivating subjects and risk distraction from their surroundings, even when feeding. When dealing with Toreador, one must be careful to respect their aesthetic sensibilities, praise their accomplishments, and avoid rudeness unless it is a deliberate political move. Their dominance in harpy positions and rumored influence in positions like the Grand Harpy reflect their ability to shape Camarilla society through social means rather than brute force.
