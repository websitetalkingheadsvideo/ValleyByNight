# Anarch Guide (MET)

The document is a comprehensive guide to the Anarch Movement in Mind's Eye Theatre Vampire: The Masquerade, providing detailed information about anarch philosophy, history, structure, character creation, Disciplines, and gameplay mechanics.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book is the concluding volume in the series outlining the major vampiric societies in the modern World of Darkness.
- It details the Anarch Movement - Kindred of all kinds who follow the banner of the Anarch Revolt.
- It provides everything needed to play anarch characters, from fiery rabble-rousers to passionate intellectuals to zealous warriors and cunning infiltrators.
- **Important Disclaimer**: This book includes strong warnings about responsible play, especially regarding sensitive topics in post-9/11 society. Players are encouraged to use code words (like the "Bunny-Hop Rule") when playing in public venues to avoid alarming non-players.

## Prelude: Sacrifices

The book opens with a narrative vignette illustrating anarch politics, showing how anarchs operate, the conflicts they face, and the sacrifices they make for their cause. The story demonstrates:

- The tension between different anarch factions
- The relationship between anarchs and Camarilla princes
- The strategic thinking required for anarch survival
- The willingness to sacrifice for the cause

## Introduction and Disclaimer

### Safety and Responsibility

The book includes extensive warnings about:

- This is a game about fictional characters in a fictional setting - not a terrorist handbook
- Players must not break stuff, steal things, hurt people, or commit antisocial acts "in character"
- Players must be mindful of discussing potentially menacing activities in public
- The "Bunny-Hop Rule" - substitute potentially menacing words with harmless ones when playing in public
- Remember "The Only Rules That Matter" from Laws of the Night
- Play hard, but play responsibly

## Chapter One: History in Ashes

### The Climate of the Revolt

- The 14th century was one of the worst periods to be a Cainite in Europe
- The Inquisition, invading armies, and mortal diseases threatened vampires
- Many elders chose solitude and self-preservation over protecting their childer
- Neonates took their cue from mortal revolts to create their own destiny

### The Spanish Uprising

- The Society of Leopold destroyed hundreds of Cainites across Europe
- Elders used their childer as cannon fodder, leading to great animosity
- Patricia Tyler, Embraced by Robin Leeland, led a coterie against Hardestadt
- Spanish Brujah neonates erupted in frenzy against their elders
- The revolutionaries began using the term "anarch" to describe themselves

### The Keepers

- Gratiano de Veronese, a Lasombra elder, secretly aided the Anarch Movement
- He helped organize the anarchs and spread their message to Italian neonates
- In 1483, Gratiano led an attack on the Lasombra Antediluvian's haven
- The anarchs succeeded in diablerizing the Antediluvian
- Gratiano declared that Lasombra would choose leaders based on merit

### The Bond Breakers

- Tzimisce neonates Velya and Lugoj discovered the koldunic ritual to break blood bonds
- This ritual would later become the Vaulderie
- Tzimisce neonates rose up against their sires
- Lugoj led an attack on the Tzimisce Antediluvian and diablerized it

### The Revolt Spreads

- The arrival of Tzimisce anarchs with the Vaulderie secret spread the revolt beyond Spain and Italy
- Neonates and ancillae of every clan flocked to the anarch cause
- Clan boundaries were often forgotten in the battle to destroy local elders

### The Church Strikes Back

- Both sides became sloppy about hiding the conflict from mortals
- Pope Innocent VIII made witches the official concern of the Inquisition in 1484
- The renewed Society of Leopold destroyed scores of vampires
- More anarchs suffered because elders were better at hiding

### The Founding of the Camarilla

- Hardestadt (or someone using his name) advocated forming a large organization
- In 1486, leaders of major European clans met in Vienna
- This was the beginning of the Camarilla and the beginning of the end for the Anarch Revolt
- The formation of the Camarilla removed the anarchs' advantage

### The Convention of Thorns (1493)

- Leaders from both sides met in the Abbey of the Sacred Crown
- The Convention was less like a peace agreement than terms for surrender
- Most anarchs accepted it, particularly the Brujah
- The Assamites declined and were cursed by the Tremere
- Lasombra and Tzimisce vehemently disagreed and left to form the Sabbat

### Revolution's Ashes

- Although the revolt failed in its stated goal, it succeeded in transforming Kindred society
- Elders learned to fear their childer
- Anarchs learned that even Antediluvians could be destroyed
- The concept of libertas (free will) was developed
- The revolt led to the creation of both the Camarilla and the Sabbat

### The Anarchs Forward

- For nearly three centuries, the visible anarch presence in Europe all but disappeared
- The French Revolution saw a resurgence of anarch activity
- Modern anarchs continue the struggle
- The Movement has evolved and adapted to new circumstances

## Chapter Two: Taking Up the Fight

### Character Creation for Anarchs

- Anarch characters follow the same basic creation process as other vampires
- Some unique considerations for anarch backgrounds and motivations
- Emphasis on why the character joined the Movement
- Consideration of the character's role in the anarch community

### Anarch Disciplines

Anarchs have access to all standard Disciplines, plus:

#### Combination Disciplines

Unique powers developed by anarchs through innovative use of their Disciplines:

- **Aspect of Beast** (Animalism + Dominate): Causes victim's inner bestial nature to manifest physically
- **Badger's Hide** (Fortitude + Protean): Makes skin leathery and tough, repels impaling attacks
- **Call Upon the Blood** (Animalism + Auspex): Sense the Beast in other vampires, locate Kindred and ghouls
- **Chaos Fold** (Dementation + Dominate): "Fold" a latent Derangement into a target's psyche
- **Give 'Em Hell** (Fortitude/Potence + Presence): Rally troops to perform greater feats of valor
- And others...

All combination Disciplines require:
- Prerequisite Disciplines at appropriate levels
- Experience Trait costs
- In-character teachers
- Storyteller approval

### Anarch Thaumaturgy

- Anarchs with Thaumaturgy face unique challenges
- Lack of magical resources and teachers makes advancement slow
- Many eschew offensive powers in favor of defensive rituals
- New rituals unique to anarchs have been developed
- Anarchs are generally limited to common paths due to Tremere persecution
- "Death before capture" code among anarch Thaumaturgy wielders

## Chapter Three: The Rebel Unlife

### Political Impulses

The book details various ideological spectrums within the Movement:

#### The Loyal Opposition

- **Sympathizers**: Camarilla loyalists who support the sect in theory but have reservations about elder abuses
- **Reformers**: Loyalists who support the Camarilla in theory but object to its structure in practice
- **Constructionists**: Ancillae who have lost faith in the Camarilla and wish to construct a viable alternative

#### The Disloyal Opposition

- **Radicals**: Espouse beliefs far outside the accepted norm, respect only individual achievement
- **Militants**: Focus on liberating their home areas through direct action
- **Nihilists**: Seek to bring chaos and disorder, often through violence

### Anarch Organization

- **Barons**: Leaders of anarch territories
- **Emissaries**: Representatives who negotiate with other sects
- **Firebrands**: Rabble-rousers and speakers
- **Moles**: Infiltrators within other sects
- **Bruisers**: Warriors and fighters
- **Quartermasters**: Supply and logistics coordinators

## Chapter Four: Radical Thinking

### Motivation

- Nobody comes to the Anarch Movement by accident
- Characters need clear motivations and goals
- Consider mortal background and post-Embrace events
- Think about what makes the character angry or resentful about Kindred society

### Past Imperfect

- Start with mortal life - were there circumstances that predisposed the character to anarch beliefs?
- Consider involvement in mortal reformist or dissident groups
- Think about the pivotal event that led to joining the Movement

### The Department of Misperception

- Realize that other Kindred will make assumptions about anarchs
- Camarilla views anarchs as whining brats or subversive traitors
- Sabbat views anarchs as undisciplined rabble
- Consider how to project the desired image

### Dressing the Part

- Consider clothing trends from the period of Embrace
- Think about how you want to be perceived
- Different roles require different styles:
  - Firebrands: Durable but flashy clothes
  - Emissaries: Well-groomed and personable
  - Moles: Whatever styles are accepted in the society they're blending into
  - Bruisers: Jeans and T-shirts, denim or leather jackets
  - Barons: Clean but not too formal

### Props

- Firebrands might carry political or philosophical works
- Glasses suggest sophistication
- Notebooks or PDAs for those with information to track
- Satchels for those who need to pick up stakes quickly
- Cell phones or pagers for staying in touch

### Say That Like You Mean It

- Different anarchs express themselves in different ways
- Think about how your character speaks and moves
- Consider situations you're likely to be in
- Firebrands don't tiptoe; emissaries are diplomatic
