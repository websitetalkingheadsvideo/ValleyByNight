# Dark Epics

The document is a guide for running large-scale live-action roleplaying games, specifically within the context of the World of Darkness universe, focusing on logistics, character interaction, and storytelling dynamics.

## Understanding the Shift from Tabletop to Live-Action Roleplaying

- Transition from tabletop games to live-action changes player dynamics significantly.
- In live-action, players interact directly with numerous Storyteller characters, enhancing immersion.

## The Importance of Game Size and Structure

- Chronicles can range from small (7 players) to large (over 75 players), affecting gameplay.
- Larger games require more organization, administrative support, and can dilute personal character interactions.

## Managing Large Chronicles and Networks

- Large games necessitate a structured approach to maintain coherence and player engagement.
- Networks of chronicles can enhance storytelling but require communication and compromise among Storytellers.

## Challenges and Benefits of Convention Games

- Convention games attract hundreds of players, presenting logistical challenges for Storytellers.
- These events focus on singular plots, allowing players to explore different characters and scenarios.

## Variations in Chronicle Types and Structures

- Chronicles can be isolated, online, or spin-offs, each with unique organizational needs.
- Growth can lead to new games or networks, adapting to player interests and demographics.

## Comprehensive Character Portfolio and Roleplaying Guidelines

- A strong character portfolio enhances player experience and roleplaying.
- Convention games are typically one-shots, while chronicles can be ongoing with detailed roleplaying.

## Managing Long-Running Chronicles

- Chronicles can evolve over time, reflecting player contributions and changing themes.
- A stable chronicle has established rules, communication networks, and protocols for new players.

## Theme and Mood in Storytelling

- Themes may shift based on player actions and character motivations.
- A predominant theme can change as plots resolve, requiring new themes to be introduced.

## Character Creation and Setting Considerations

- Character creation in large games must consider existing demographics and genre conventions.
- Balancing character types is crucial to maintain the integrity of the game world.

## Logistics and Venue Selection for Large Games

- Finding suitable venues requires consideration of space, accessibility, and costs.
- A large game site should accommodate multiple rooms and provide necessary amenities.

## Communication and Information Management

- Effective communication is vital for keeping players informed and managing game logistics.
- Utilizing websites and email lists can streamline information dissemination among players.

## Handling Character Interactions at Conventions

- Be prepared for diverse character backgrounds and histories that may clash.
- Inform players about any significant deviations from the World of Darkness metaplot before gameplay.

## Team Coordination for Events

- Establish clear roles and responsibilities among Storytellers and Narrators before the event.
- Communicate with convention staff about expectations and emergency procedures.

## Front Desk Operations

- Encourage early arrival for players to handle logistics before gameplay begins.
- Distribute character sheets, tokens, and house rules to players upon arrival.

## Managing New and Late Players

- Provide extra attention to new players to help them understand the game.
- Have a plan for integrating latecomers smoothly into ongoing gameplay.

## Mentoring and Integration of New Players

- Identify experienced players to mentor newcomers, helping them acclimate to the game.
- Assign Setting Storytellers to assist new players in understanding the game's history and dynamics.

## Character Creation and Approval Process

- Define game demographics and limits on rare character types early on.
- Complete character approvals promptly to enhance player engagement and satisfaction.

## Learning Lore and Its Requirements

- Characters must learn Lore levels from multiple teachers.
- The number of teachers required equals the Lore level being learned.
- Learning from tomes counts as only one level, regardless of the source.

## Ability Specializations and Their Benefits

- Specializations enhance skills within an Ability, providing a one-Trait bonus.
- Only one specialization per Ability is allowed, and it must be specific.
- Specializations do not stack; only one bonus Trait applies at a time.

## Backgrounds and Their Functions

- Backgrounds reflect a character's ties and history, with a maximum of five levels.
- Allies provide assistance and specialized talents, while Contacts offer information.
- Fame extends influence over a larger area, while Resources provide financial support.

## Influence and Its Strategic Use

- Influence allows characters to manipulate various societal aspects, from politics to media.
- Actions like Attack, Block, and Trace can be performed using Influence Traits.
- Each Influence type has specific costs and effects, impacting gameplay significantly.

## Managing Influence and Its Limitations

- Influence can only affect similar categories and is limited by character Traits.
- Actions like Growth and Watch are resolved monthly, ensuring fair play.
- Conceal and Stealth can protect Influence from being traced or followed.

## Techniques for Managing Mass Combat

- The Narrator must balance scene pace with player engagement.
- Consider character safety and player preferences for scene structure.
- Quick, narrative scenes may be preferred for less tension, while detailed, by-the-book scenes are better for high-stakes situations.

## Playing It Conservatively in Combat

- Maintain focus and keep the scene moving to avoid distractions.
- Make fair rules calls and encourage players to discuss objections after the game.
- Control the flow of combat by managing turns and actions collectively.

## The Narrative Approach to Combat

- Encourage cooperative play by prioritizing story outcomes over strict rules.
- Gather player goals and limits to negotiate outcomes that satisfy everyone.
- If conflicts arise, revert to a conservative approach to ensure fairness.

## Managing Innocent Bystanders in Combat

- Clearly define participants and spectators at the scene's start.
- Keep bystanders informed to enhance their experience without disrupting the scene.
- Limit new participants from joining mid-combat to maintain narrative integrity.

## Interactions Between Supernatural Creatures

- Vampires and wraiths experience heightened emotional states, with wraiths testing for Angst in vampire-heavy scenes.
- Lifesight allows wraiths to perceive vampires' auras, but is ineffective against vampires with Obfuscate.
- Powers like Presence and Dominate do not affect wraiths across the Shroud, while Obtenebration can induce Angst in wraiths.

## Changeling and Vampire Dynamics

- Vampires embody Banality and loss, with Malkavians being exceptions due to their low Banality and Dreaming affinity.
- Garou interact more with fae than vampires, highlighting their similarities and differences.
- Fae Arts are limited by the Banality of other creatures, with vampires averaging a Banality of 8.

## Experience Trait Management

- Experience Trait awards should be standardized across chronicles to maintain balance.
- A graduated cap system can regulate character power growth over time.
- Automatic awards may be used for chronicles with infrequent sessions to control character development.
