### Kindred
**From "The Kindred" (Laws of the Night):**
- Vampires are immortal, sentient creatures with faint similarities to their mortal selves
- They require the blood of the living to survive
- They are burned by sunlight and must avoid it or risk death
- They are nocturnal creatures, finding it difficult to stay awake during the day
- The Embrace is the process by which a new vampire is created - almost never given lightly

## Sects and Organizations

### Camarilla
**From "The Camarilla" (Laws of the Night):**
- Dates back to the years of the Inquisition
- The Masquerade is both guiding principle and ironclad policy
- Claims all vampires to be under its aegis
- Six clans hold full membership: Brujah, Malkavian, Nosferatu, Toreador, Tremere, and Ventrue
- The Inner Circle consists of one representative for each clan
- Justicars are appointed by the Inner Circle to be judge, jury, and executioner
- Archons are chosen by justicars to act in their name

**From "The Founding of the Camarilla" (Anarch Guide):**
- The formation of the Camarilla as a response to the Inquisition and Anarch Revolt

**From "At Home with the Camarilla" (MET Journals):**
- Life and politics within Camarilla domains

**From "Camarilla Society" (Clanbook Toreador):**
- The Toreador are one of the founding clans of the Camarilla and play an important role in Camarilla society

### Sabbat
**From "The Sabbat" (Laws of the Night):**
- Spawned by the Inquisition and the Anarch Revolt
- Claims two clans: Lasombra and Tzimisce, plus antitribu
- Organizes itself in a parody of the Catholic Church
- Freedom and loyalty are the guiding principles
- Looks ahead to the nights of Gehenna, determined to survive by any means necessary

**From "Sabbat Guide":**
- Comprehensive guide to the Sabbat sect, its history, structure, and clans

**From "The Nature of the Sabbat" (Sabbat Guide):**
- The fundamental principles and organization of the Sabbat

**From "The True Sabbat" (Sabbat Guide):**
- The core beliefs and practices that define true Sabbat membership

**From "Advisories on Enemies of the Camarilla" (Prince's Primer):**
- **The Sabbat**: Any incursion must be halted before it begins

## Concepts and Systems

### Masquerade
**From "The Masquerade" (Clanbook Brujah Revised):**

**From "The Traditions" (Prince's Primer):**

- The structure and rules governing Elysium spaces

**From "Tips for Working Elysium" (A Harpy's Primer):**

### Domain
**From "The Domain" (Prince's Primer - The Traditions):**
- The prince has leeway to set domain as long as he possesses the power to exert his authority

**From "On the Position of the Prince Within His Domain" (Prince's Primer):**
- The prince's rights and responsibilities within their domain

### Praxis
**From "A Harpy History" (A Harpy's Primer):**
- Praxis refers to the authority and power to rule over an area
- The prestation system was designed to ensure that only the strongest could claim praxis over an area the Camarilla wanted
- Eliminating confusion and weak or compromised individuals from claiming praxis was a key goal
- With Camarilla vampires, social strength is far superior to physical strength when it comes to maintaining praxis

### Boon
**From "So You Are a Harpy" (A Harpy's Primer):**
- The harpy position tracks status and records boons
- Boons are our money, the value we hold within our society
- You don't have to keep a log. All you need to do is arbitrate between the two kindred if they cannot come to a mutual agreement on payment

### Prestation
**From "A Harpy History" (A Harpy's Primer):**
- The prestation system was created before the official founding of the Camarilla, and was used as the basis for determining support for rulership in an area
- Vampires in each domain who were in support of a status system would have evaluated other kindred within their domain and ranked them, passing that information up their social ladder
- The higher a vampire was in the rankings, the more privilege and power they were given at the formation
- It was a covert way to ensure that strong leaders were in place at the beginning, eliminating confusion and weak or compromised individuals from claiming praxis over an area the Camarilla wanted
- The prestation system was designed first and foremost as a protection against weak leaders within the sect
- The position of harpy grew organically from the need for organization, accountability, and measurable authority over social structures, which is why harpies are appointed by acceptance in a domain rather than being voted upon

### Ghoul
**From "Liber des Goules":**
- Comprehensive guide to ghouls and their role in Kindred society

**From "The Ghoul's Dual Nature" (Liber des Goules):**
- With the exception of Revenant families, a ghoul is still very much a normal human
- The majority of ghouls may still live with loved ones, hold day jobs, pay bills, go to college, and so on
- Apart from their addiction to vitae, ghouls function like everybody else
- The difference lies in the ghoul's longevity and access to vampiric Disciplines
- These differences set a ghoul apart from kine society, both physically and socially

- Various types of ghouls and their characteristics

**From "The Ghoul's Role in Kindred Society" (Liber des Goules):**
- How ghouls function within vampire society

## The Embrace and Relationships

### Embrace
**From "The Embrace" (Clanbook Ventrue Revised):**
- The process of creating a new vampire

**From "The Embrace" (MET Introductory Kit):**
- The process by which a new vampire is created

**From "The Kindred" (Laws of the Night):**
- The Embrace is the process by which a new vampire is created - almost never given lightly

### Childe
**From "Important Childe" (Counsel of Primogen):**
- The relationship between sires and their childer in primogen politics

### Sire
**From "Important Childe" (Counsel of Primogen):**
- The vampire who created another vampire through the Embrace
- The relationship between sires and their childer affects primogen politics
- Sires have authority and responsibility over their childer

### Blood Bond
**From "The Blood Bond" (Laws of the Night):**
- The supernatural connection created when a mortal consumes vampire blood (vitae)
- Creates a powerful emotional and psychological attachment to the vampire who provided the blood

**From "The Blood Bond" (Clanbook Tremere Revised):**
- The Tremere's relationship with the Blood Bond and how they use it

**From "The Blood Bond" (Liber des Goules):**
- The relationship between ghouls and their Regnants (the vampires who provide them with vitae)
- Jealousy and resentment can lead a ghoul to question her Bond, eventually causing a true break from her Regnant
- Some ghouls, when faced with nothing but abuse, strike back and become vampire hunters, bent on the annihilation of their former masters
