# MET Journal 5

The document is the fifth issue of the Mind's Eye Theatre Journal, a quarterly publication focused on enhancing live-action roleplaying experiences within the World of Darkness universe.

## Introduction to Mind's Eye Theatre Journal

- The Journal is a quarterly publication dedicated to filling gaps in the White Wolf LARP environment.
- It provides rules for new character types, original storylines, guides for game management, and rules updates not found in other Mind's Eye releases.
- This issue (first of 2000) presents material on Wraith: The Great War, the Bunyip for Laws of the Wyld West, and vampire Vikings known as the einherjar.

## Deadguy Speaks: Romance in LARP

Richard Dansky's column addresses the complex issue of romantic entanglements in live-action roleplaying games.

### Gender Balance in LARP

- MET games average 35% to 50% female participation, which is remarkable for the roleplaying industry.
- The growing gender integration has brought new challenges, particularly around romance and attraction.

### The Problem of Romance

- Roleplaying can become intensely emotional, and the line between character feelings and player feelings can blur.
- Character conflicts can spill into real relationships, or real-life issues can disrupt games.
- Breakups between players can split entire games, forcing Storytellers to run multiple separate chronicles.

### Communication Challenges

- Determining attraction in LARP is extremely difficult due to the character/player distinction.
- Simple greetings are often misinterpreted as romantic interest.
- Players must learn to distinguish between in-character and out-of-character interactions.

### Guidelines for Healthy Romance in LARP

- **For Men**: Understand that "Hi" means "hi" - don't read more into simple greetings. Wait for multi-syllabic words indicating actual interest.
- **For Women**: Be clear about boundaries. If you're not interested, say so directly rather than hoping hints will be understood.
- **For Everyone**: Keep in-character and out-of-character relationships separate. Remember that intense roleplay doesn't necessarily mean real attraction.

### Benefits of LARP Relationships

- Shared interests and hobbies from the start
- No need to explain the roleplaying hobby
- Inexpensive dates (game nights)
- Less conflict over spending money on books

### Warnings

- Players must maintain boundaries and respect the "No Touching" rule
- In-character behavior should never excuse offensive out-of-character actions
- Storytellers should monitor for inappropriate behavior and address it immediately

## Purpose and Value

The Journal serves as a bridge between official publications, providing:
- Material that couldn't fit in core rulebooks
- Community-driven content and advice
- Rules clarifications and updates
- Fiction and setting material
- Practical guidance for Storytellers and players

This issue particularly emphasizes the social aspects of LARPing, recognizing that live-action games involve real people with real emotions, and providing guidance for navigating those complexities while maintaining a healthy gaming environment.
