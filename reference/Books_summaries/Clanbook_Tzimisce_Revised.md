# Clanbook: Tzimisce (Revised)

This supplement for Vampire: The Masquerade provides comprehensive information on Clan Tzimisce, the "Fiends" known for their mastery of Vicissitude (the Discipline of flesh-shaping) and their role as spiritual leaders of the Sabbat. The book explores the clan's history from the anarchs who forged the Sabbat to their modern incarnation as degenerate monsters bound by the callous whims of the Sabbat. The book examines how the Tzimisce struggle to survive and escape their grim destiny to once again rise to prominence.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book provides detailed information on Clan Tzimisce for players and Storytellers.
- It explores the clan's history and their fall from masters of hoary estates to Sabbat degenerates.
- Examines the clan's practices, including Vicissitude and their relationship with the land.
- Provides character creation options, new Disciplines, and information on Tzimisce culture.

## Introduction: Winter Fiend

The book opens with a story set during World War II, illustrating the Tzimisce's methods and their relationship with the land. A German soldier encounters a Tzimisce in the Russian winter, experiencing the clan's power and their connection to the earth. The story demonstrates the Tzimisce's ancient nature and their ability to survive in the harshest conditions.

## Chapter One: A Rabble of Fiends

### The History of the Tzimisce

The Tzimisce trace their lineage to the Tzimisce Antediluvian, one of the Third Generation. The clan has always been associated with the land, with territory, and with mastery over their domains. They were once masters of vast estates, ruling over mortals and Kindred alike from their ancestral lands.

### The Fall

The modern nights have caught up with the Tzimisce, who have fallen from their place as masters of hoary estates to degenerates bound by the callous whims of the Sabbat. The clan's history shows a fall from power and prestige to their current state as spiritual leaders of a sect that often uses them as tools.

### The Land Bond

The Tzimisce have a unique connection to the land. They are bound to their ancestral territories, and leaving these lands causes them great pain and weakness. This bond has shaped the clan's culture and their approach to power.

### Vicissitude

Vicissitude is the Tzimisce's greatest asset and their greatest curse. This Discipline allows them to shape flesh and bone, creating works of art or instruments of terror. It also reflects their nature as masters of transformation and change.

## Chapter Two: Ego and Evolution

### Clan Organization

The Tzimisce have a complex organization based on family ties, territory, and spiritual leadership. They are divided into various families, each with its own traditions and practices. The clan's organization reflects their connection to the land and their family structure.

### The Sabbat

The Tzimisce are one of the founding clans of the Sabbat, and they serve as its spiritual leaders. However, their role in the sect is complex - they are both leaders and tools, both respected and used. The Sabbat's callous whims often conflict with the Tzimisce's ancient traditions.

### The Old Clan

Some Tzimisce reject the Sabbat and maintain the old ways. These "Old Clan" Tzimisce are rare but powerful, maintaining the clan's ancient traditions and refusing to be bound by the Sabbat's whims.

### Vicissitude and Transformation

Vicissitude is central to Tzimisce identity. It allows them to transform themselves and others, creating works of art or instruments of terror. The Discipline reflects the clan's nature as masters of change and transformation.

### Relationships with Other Clans

The Tzimisce have complex relationships with other clans:
- They are feared for their Vicissitude and their methods
- They are respected as spiritual leaders of the Sabbat
- They have enemies among clans they have wronged (particularly the Tremere)
- They maintain their power through their connection to the land and their mastery of flesh

## Key Themes

- **The Land Bond**: The Tzimisce's unique connection to their ancestral territories.
- **Vicissitude**: The clan's mastery of flesh-shaping and transformation.
- **The Fall**: The clan's fall from masters of estates to Sabbat tools.
- **Spiritual Leadership**: The Tzimisce's role as spiritual leaders of the Sabbat.
- **Transformation**: The clan's mastery of change and transformation.
- **The Old Ways**: The tension between traditional Tzimisce and those bound to the Sabbat.
