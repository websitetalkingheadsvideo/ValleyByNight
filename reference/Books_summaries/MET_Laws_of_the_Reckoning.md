# MET Laws of the Reckoning

This book is the core rulebook for Mind's Eye Theatre Hunter: The Reckoning, providing all the material that players and Storytellers need for creating, playing, and Storytelling those mortals who have become Imbued. The book presents ordinary humans who have been chosen by mysterious entities known as the Heralds to combat supernatural creatures, thrust into a conflict they barely understand and armed only with their courage, determination, and mysterious powers called Edges.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book contains all the information needed to play Mind's Eye Theatre Hunter: The Reckoning.
- It is based on the tabletop game Hunter: The Reckoning, adapted for live-action play.
- Focuses on ordinary people who have been chosen by the Heralds to fight supernatural creatures.
- Provides rules for character creation, Edges (hunter powers), and Storytelling.

## Chapter One: Introduction and Setting

### What is Storytelling?

The book emphasizes storytelling as one of the oldest forms of human communication, from cave paintings to modern roleplaying games. Live-action roleplaying (LARP) is presented as a return to the oldest form of participatory storytelling.

### What is Mind's Eye Theatre?

Playing Mind's Eye Theatre is like being in a movie. You and your friends portray the main characters, but there is no script - your decisions drive the adventure. The Storyteller creates the framework, then turns players loose to see what happens. Most scenes are played in real time, and always in character.

### Characters

When you play Laws of the Reckoning, you take on the persona of a hunter, an ordinary person gifted (or cursed) with the ability to see supernatural creatures all around her and charged with the duty of battling them. Anyone can be chosen by the Heralds - the only limit on your character concept is your imagination.

Creating a character is easy and only takes a few moments. However, your character should be more than just Traits and numbers - she should be a living, breathing personality with a past, motives, drives, likes, and dislikes.

### Narrators

Narrators are the people who help the Storyteller present adventures. They are impartial judges who describe scenes and events that cannot be staged, adjudicate rules, and occasionally play the roles of antagonists. Generally, enlisting the aid of one Narrator for every 10 players makes for a good ratio.

### Storyteller

Every game must have a Storyteller, who serves as the ultimate authority and final judge. The Storyteller creates the basic elements of the plot and makes sure that the story unfolds well. Storytelling is demanding but rewarding, as the Storyteller creates the framework upon which players build their experiences.

### The Rules of Safety

Critical safety rules that must always be followed:
- **It's Only a Game** - Don't take things too seriously.
- **No Touching** - Never actually have physical contact with other players.
- **No Stunts** - Never climb, jump, run, leap or swing during a game.
- **No Weapons** - Fake or real weapons of any sort are absolutely forbidden.
- **No Drugs or Drinking** - Impaired players are a danger to others and the game.
- **Be Mindful of Others** - Not everyone around you is playing the game.
- **The Rules are Flexible** - Feel free to ignore or adjust rules if it makes your game better (The Golden Rule).
- **Have Fun** - The goal is to have fun, not to achieve superiority over others.

### The World of Darkness

On the surface, the World of Darkness is similar to our own. People are born, grow up, work and die every day. Just beneath this veneer, however, lurk things dark and terrible - an atmosphere of fear, despair and violence. This is the world that hunters call home.

**Gothic-Punk** is the term used to describe the attitude of the World of Darkness. The "Gothic" aspect is that of the sinister, looming shadow that permeates life. The "Punk" aspect is what many citizens have adopted to give their lives meaning - rebellion, crime, and a rejection of the status quo.

### Laws of the Reckoning vs. Laws of the Hunt

White Wolf has two live-action games about humans who hunt supernatural creatures. **Laws of the Hunt** is about mortals who wage their war for personal reasons or on behalf of existing organizations. By contrast, the **imbued of Laws of the Reckoning** are humans with absolutely no supernatural lineage or training, who have been chosen by the Heralds. They have powers called Edges but remain ordinary folks in every other way. The dividing line is that the Heralds have chosen the imbued - all other groups do not have this sanction.

## Chapter Two: The Creeds

The book details the various Creeds that hunters follow, each representing a different philosophy and approach to the hunt. The Creeds include:

- **Avenger**: A hunter who provokes confrontation with monsters, using himself as bait.
- **Defender**: A hunter who strives to protect certain people as much as he hunts monsters.
- **Hermit**: A hunter who works in isolation, apparently suffering great pain from the presence of monsters or other imbued.
- **Innocent**: A hunter who maintains faith in humanity and seeks to redeem monsters when possible.
- **Judge**: A hunter who develops a philosophy regarding the hunt and has few doubts about the Heralds, monsters, or the purpose of the hunt.
- **Martyr**: A hunter willing to sacrifice everything for the mission.
- **Redeemer**: A hunter who seeks out monsters to save rather than destroy them on sight.
- **Visionary**: A hunter who accesses the Internet and researches the supernatural but avoids direct involvement in the mission.
- **Wayward**: A hunter who pursues her mission to extremes, accepting human losses and making tremendous sacrifices.

## Lexicon

Extensive glossary of terms used in Hunter: The Reckoning, including:
- **bystander**: A person who witnesses the supernatural but does not act against it.
- **chosen, the**: A term some hunters call themselves in reference to the imbuing and the Heralds.
- **defenseless, the**: Normal, unimbued humans who have no idea that monsters exist.
- **edge**: Any of the strange, mysterious powers granted to a hunter upon her imbuing.
- **Heralds**: The name given to the mysterious forces which empower normal humans to see the Supernatural and grant them strange powers.
- **imbued**: Humans who have been chosen by the Heralds and granted the ability to see monsters.
- **monsters**: Supernatural creatures that stalk the world. Subcategories include bloodsuckers, rots, skinchangers, ghosts, vampires, werewolves, zombies, witches, etc.
- **Most Dangerous Game (MDG)**: The hunt; also the monsters being hunted.
- **second sight**: Also called the sight, the ability to see monsters even when they disguise or hide themselves.
- **Word, the**: Hunters' language of signs and icons used to communicate simple messages.

## Key Themes

- **Ordinary People in Extraordinary Circumstances**: Hunters are completely normal humans thrust into a supernatural war.
- **The Mystery of the Heralds**: No one knows who or what the Heralds are or why they chose specific people.
- **The Imbuing**: The moment when ordinary people are granted the ability to see monsters and receive their first Edge.
- **The Mission**: The compulsion to act against monsters, though different Creeds interpret this differently.
- **Fear and Hope**: The tension between the horror of discovering monsters are real and the hope that humanity can fight back.
- **Isolation and Community**: Hunters often feel isolated but can find strength in working together.

## Purpose and Value

MET Laws of the Reckoning serves as:
- The complete rulebook for playing Hunter: The Reckoning in live-action format
- A guide to the world of the Imbued and their war against supernatural creatures
- Information on character creation, Edges, and systems for MET Hunter
- Advice for both players and Storytellers on running successful games

This book is essential for anyone wanting to play or run live-action Hunter: The Reckoning games, providing all the tools needed to tell stories of ordinary people chosen to fight monsters in a world where ignorance is no longer an option.
