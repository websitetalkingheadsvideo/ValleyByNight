# MET Mind's Eye Theatre Book of the Wyrm

This supplement for Mind's Eye Theatre Werewolf: The Apocalypse presents the material needed to bring the Apocalypse sharply into focus for your chronicle. From the corrupted fomori to the Hive, Black Spiral Dancers to the many arms of the Pentex corporation, and the very halls of Malfeas, this book lays out what the Garou face in their war against the Wyrm. On what may be the eve of Apocalypse, it will take more than just strength and courage to survive what's coming.

## Purpose and Scope

- This book is a supplement for Laws of the Wild, providing antagonists and threats for Werewolf: The Apocalypse games.
- It details the various minions and manifestations of the Wyrm that the Garou must face.
- Focuses on the enemies of the Garou and the corruption they represent.
- Provides Storyteller resources for creating compelling antagonists and threats.

## Chapter One: Introduction

An overview of the Wyrm and its role in the World of Darkness, setting the stage for the various threats detailed in the book.

## Chapter Two: Cosmology

Detailed information on the cosmology of the World of Darkness as it relates to the Wyrm, including:
- The Triat (Wyrm, Weaver, Wyld)
- How the Wyrm became corrupted
- The relationship between the Wyrm, Weaver, and Wyld
- The spiritual and physical manifestations of corruption

## Chapter Three: A Map of Hell

Information on Malfeas, the spiritual realm where the Wyrm resides, including:
- The geography and nature of Malfeas
- The various layers and regions of the Wyrm's domain
- How to access Malfeas
- The dangers of venturing into the Wyrm's realm

## Chapter Four: The Black Spiral Dancers

Complete information on the Black Spiral Dancers, the fallen tribe of Garou who serve the Wyrm:
- Their history and fall from grace
- Their culture and society
- Their goals and methods
- How to use them as antagonists
- Character creation for Black Spiral Dancers (if Storytellers allow them as player characters)

## Chapter Five: The Fallen and the Wretched

Information on various corrupted beings and creatures that serve the Wyrm:
- Fomori (humans corrupted by the Wyrm)
- Banes (corrupted spirits)
- Other Wyrm-tainted creatures
- How these creatures are created
- Their role in the Wyrm's plans

## Chapter Six: Pentex and Subsidiaries

Detailed information on Pentex, the massive corporation that serves as the Wyrm's primary tool in the physical world:
- The history and structure of Pentex
- Its various subsidiaries and their roles
- How Pentex spreads corruption
- Pentex's goals and methods
- Using Pentex as an antagonist in games

## Appendix: The Wyrm's Arsenal

A collection of tools, weapons, and resources that the Wyrm and its minions use, including:
- Corrupted items and artifacts
- Wyrm-tainted technology
- Resources available to the Wyrm's servants
- How to incorporate these elements into games

## Key Themes

- **Corruption and Decay**: The Wyrm represents corruption, decay, and the destruction of all that is natural and good.
- **The Fallen**: The Black Spiral Dancers represent what happens when Garou fall to corruption.
- **Corporate Evil**: Pentex represents how the Wyrm works through modern institutions to spread corruption.
- **The Price of Power**: Many of the Wyrm's servants gain power but lose their humanity in the process.
- **The War for Gaia**: The Garou's struggle against the Wyrm is a war for the very survival of the Earth.

## Purpose and Value

MET Book of the Wyrm serves as:
- A comprehensive guide to the enemies and threats facing the Garou
- Detailed information on the Wyrm, its minions, and its methods
- Storyteller resources for creating compelling antagonists
- Information on using the Wyrm's forces in games
- A darker look at the World of Darkness from the perspective of corruption and evil

This book is essential for Storytellers running Werewolf: The Apocalypse games who want to create compelling antagonists and threats, providing detailed information on the various forces of corruption that the Garou must face in their war to save Gaia.
