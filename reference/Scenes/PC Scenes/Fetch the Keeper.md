# Fetch the Keeper

**Location:** Hawthorne Estate — Gallery Level — First Night

---

The murmur of Elysium never fully reaches silence here. Voices are lowered, footsteps measured, the air heavy with the shared understanding that this is not a place for mistakes.

A door opens at the far end of the gallery.

Not loudly.  
Not ceremoniously.  
Just enough to be noticed by those who know to watch.

Roland steps out of the Prince's office.

He does not look rushed. He never does. His expression is composed, professional, the sort of calm that suggests everything is under control—or very carefully being kept that way.

His eyes move once across the gallery.

They stop on you.

For a brief moment, there is nothing. Then his gaze sharpens—not hostile, not welcoming. Assessing. As if confirming a detail he already knew but wanted to verify in person.

He raises one hand slightly. Two fingers, curling inward.

A clear summons.

When you approach, he does not lower his voice, but he does angle his body just enough to signal this is instruction, not conversation.

> "Go find the Keeper of Elysium."

A pause. Brief. Deliberate.

> "Tell them the Prince requires their presence. Now."

His eyes hold yours for a heartbeat longer than necessary. Not threatening—measuring.

> "Don't improvise."

With that, he turns back toward the office door, already disengaging, already assuming compliance.

The gallery noise resumes around you, but it feels different now.  
Sharper.  
More attentive.

Several Kindred have noticed the exchange.

This is the first time tonight that the court has used you.

## Design Notes

*Note: Implicit, not shown to player*

### Scene Mechanics

- This scene always occurs

### PC is Being Tested For:

- **Composure**
- **Obedience vs Initiative**
- **Understanding of court dynamics**

### Scene Function

- No relationship scores exist yet
- This scene creates multiple first impressions at once:
  - Roland
  - The Keeper (later)
  - Observers in the gallery

### Why This Scene Works

- The PC cannot refuse without consequence
- Compliance can still be colored (demeanor, speed, attitude)
- It quietly establishes court hierarchy
- It sets the tone for how the PC navigates authority
