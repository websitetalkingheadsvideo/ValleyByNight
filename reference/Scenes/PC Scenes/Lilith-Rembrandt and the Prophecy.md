### 🎬 *Opening Cutscene: “Prophecy, With Notes”*

**[INT. LILITH NIGHTSHADE’S HAVEN – CENTRAL PHOENIX – NIGHT]**

Candlelight trembles across velvet drapes and antique mirrors. The room feels *listened to*. Shadows linger too long in corners, as if waiting their turn to speak.

At the center of it all sits **Lilith Nightshade**, immaculate in lavender silk and black lace. Her parasol rests closed across her knees like a ceremonial blade.

Across from her, perched on the edge of a fainting couch that has *never* fainted in its life, sits **Rembrandt Jones**. He holds a disposable paper cup of blood like it’s bad coffee.

A clock ticks somewhere. It does not keep time correctly.

Lilith’s eyes unfocus. Candle flames bend inward.

> **Lilith:**
> “The blood will divide into many hands.
> Brothers without breath will stitch themselves together.
> They come on borrowed veins, laughing with shared mouths.”

The temperature drops. The mirrors briefly show *too many reflections*.

Rembrandt nods, earnest.

> **Rembrandt:**
> “Oh. Like a collective.”

Lilith’s head snaps toward him.

> **Lilith:**
> “They are a wound pretending to be a family.”

A distant *heartbeat* echoes through the room. Not his. Not hers.

Rembrandt considers this carefully.

> **Rembrandt:**
> “You know, I *did* once try a collaborative sculpture. Six artists, one vision. Absolute nightmare. Nobody agreed on the wiring.”

Lilith rises slowly, parasol opening by itself. Her voice deepens—layered, echoing.

> **Lilith:**
> “They will move into the forgotten places.
> Storm drains. Warehouses. Rooms no one counts anymore.
> They will bleed the city sideways.”

A vision flashes—flesh bound with ritual scars, many arms moving as one.

Rembrandt squints.

> **Rembrandt:**
> “Storm drains have *great* acoustics. Real natural reverb.”

Beat.

Lilith blinks. The vision ends. She straightens, composed once more.

> **Lilith:**
> “…You are not afraid.”

> **Rembrandt:**
> “Oh no, I am. I’m just very used to it coming packaged like this.”

He gestures vaguely at *everything*.

He takes another sip from the cup, winces.

> **Rembrandt:**
> “This one’s a little coppery. Is that intentional or—”

Lilith closes the parasol with a sharp *click*.

> **Lilith:**
> “The Blood Brothers are coming to Phoenix, Rembrandt Jones.”

Rembrandt nods solemnly.

> **Rembrandt:**
> “I’ll let the Primogen know.”

Pause.

> **Rembrandt:**
> “Do you want that… ominously? Or just conversational?”

For the first time, Lilith hesitates.

A candle flickers out.

> **Lilith:**
> “…Conversational will be fine.”

Rembrandt smiles, relieved.

> **Rembrandt:**
> “Great. Oh—before I forget—can I photograph the drapes? The way they’re absorbing the shadows is *phenomenal*.”

Lilith watches him stand, already pulling out a camera, entirely unbothered by prophecy or doom.

**[CUT TO BLACK]**
