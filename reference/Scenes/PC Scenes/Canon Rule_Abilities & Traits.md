# Canon Rule: Abilities & Traits Modify the Option Pool — They Do Not Grow It

Abilities and Traits never add more than 1 option total to a scene, and often add none.

**Their job is to:**
- Replace weaker options
- Refine wording
- Unlock precision, not bravado

---

## Step 1: Establish the Base Option Pool (5–7 candidates)

From narrative + expression profile you generate:

~6–8 candidate options internally

**These include:**
- Compliant
- Cautious
- Awkward
- Assertive
- Risky

> **Note:** This pool is never shown directly.

---

## Step 2: Ability / Trait Relevance Check (Fast, Binary)

The Abilities & Traits Agent runs a quick relevance scan:

> "Is there an Ability or Trait that meaningfully changes how the PC would handle this exact moment?"

**For this scene, relevant examples might be:**
- Etiquette / Protocol / Status Lore
- Leadership
- Subterfuge
- Common Sense (trait/merit)
- Calm Heart / Iron Will–type traits

**Important:**
- No rolls
- No deep reasoning
- Just present or not

---

## Step 3: If Relevant → Inject or Upgrade ONE Option

### Option A: Upgrade an Existing Option (Preferred)

Replace generic phrasing with sharper, more confident wording.

**Example:**

**Base:**
> "Immediately."

**Upgraded (Etiquette / Protocol):**
> "At once. I'll inform the Keeper discreetly."

Same intent. Same tone.  
Just cleaner.

### Option B: Swap in One New Option (Rare)

Only if the ability enables something no baseline neonate would think to do.

**Example:**

**Unlocked by Etiquette / Court Knowledge:**
> "Understood. Shall I escort them directly, or announce them at the threshold?"

This replaces a weaker option (usually awkward or redundant).

---

## Step 4: Re-run Option Curation (Hard Cap Enforced)

After ability/trait adjustments:

1. Re-score options against expression profile
2. Enforce:
   - **Min:** 3
   - **Max:** 5

If adding an option would exceed 5:
- The lowest-fit option is dropped automatically
- **No exceptions.**

---

## Step 5: What Abilities & Traits NEVER Do Here

**They do not:**
- Override hierarchy
- Allow refusal
- Unlock dominance
- Change plot flow
- Force NPC reactions

**They only improve:**
- Clarity
- Confidence
- Social competence

---

## Concrete Example (Roland Scene)

**PC Profile:**
- Polite
- Cautious
- Has Etiquette and Common Sense

**Final Visible Options (5):**
1. "Of course."
2. "Immediately."
3. (Silent nod)
4. "The Keeper, now — understood."
5. **[Etiquette]** "At once. I'll inform the Keeper discreetly."

**Options like:**
- "Right now?"
- "I'll make sure they understand it's urgent."

Are suppressed.

---

## Canon Principle (Put This in Docs)

> **Abilities don't give you more things to say.**  
> **They give you better ways to say them.**

That principle keeps:
- UI clean
- Writing manageable
- Roleplay coherent
- System extensible
