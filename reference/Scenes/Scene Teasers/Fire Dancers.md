EXT. DESERT HILLS – SOUTH OF PHOENIX – NIGHT

The desert rolls low and uneven, scrub brush and black rock catching just enough moonlight to cast broken shadows. The city glow is distant here — Phoenix reduced to a dull amber smear on the horizon.

Barry Horowitz crouches behind a rise of stone, the earth still warm beneath his palm.

He isn’t shaking anymore.

Beside him, Naomi Blackbird kneels effortlessly, her presence grounded, alert. Gangrel stillness — predator calm. Her eyes never stop moving.

Below them, in a shallow basin carved by runoff, fire burns.

Not campfire fire.

Ritual fire.

A small Sabbat pack circles the flames — four figures, maybe five. Bare chests smeared with ash and blood. One of them dances, jerking and twisting as the flames leap unnaturally high, responding to rhythm instead of wind.

Drums.
Bone on bone.
A chant that scrapes instead of carries.

Barry exhales slowly through his nose.

The shadows around them stretch farther than they should.

They obey him now.

Not eagerly.
Not eagerly like before.

But willingly.

The darkness between the rocks thickens, swallowing their silhouettes without effort. Barry doesn’t look at it directly. He feels it settle, like a coat slipping onto his shoulders.

Naomi glances sideways at him — just once.

Low voice. Controlled.

NAOMI
(whisper)
“You’re holding it.”

Barry nods.

BARRY
“Yeah. Took a while to stop fighting it.”

Below, the fire dancer screams — not in pain. In ecstasy. The flames coil up his arms without burning skin, licking at his throat. Another Sabbat steps forward, slicing his palm and letting blood drip into the fire.

The fire answers.

Naomi’s jaw tightens.

NAOMI
“That’s not a scouting party.”

Barry watches the shadows cast by the dancers.

They don’t line up.

One shadow twitches independently, lagging behind its owner by half a second. Another splits briefly, two silhouettes where there should be one.

Barry swallows.

BARRY
“They’re not just passing through.”

A Sabbat priest — older, heavier presence — raises both arms. The chant shifts. The fire drops suddenly, compact, violent, as if being crushed inward.

Naomi’s hand tightens around the hilt of her blade.

NAOMI
“We could take two. Maybe three. Loudly.”

Barry doesn’t answer right away.

He studies the ritual.

Counts breaths.
Counts bodies.
Feels the pull of the shadows downhill — how easily he could let them spill, how quickly panic would break the circle.

And how fast it would turn into a slaughter.

His voice is steadier than he expects.

BARRY
“If we hit them now, they scatter.”
(beat)
“And they come back meaner.”

Naomi looks at him fully now.

This isn’t fear talking.
This is judgment.

Below, the dancer collapses into the fire. Someone drags him out laughing. The priest marks the ground with a symbol that hurts to look at even from this distance.

Naomi exhales slowly.

NAOMI
“So we watch.”

Barry nods.

BARRY
“And we remember everything.”

The fire surges once more — then dies unnaturally fast, leaving only embers and smoke that sinks instead of rising.

The pack disperses, melting into the hills with practiced efficiency.

No celebration.
No hesitation.

Barry releases the shadows.

They retract reluctantly, sliding back into the shape the world expects them to be.

For a moment after, everything feels too bright.

Naomi rises to her feet.

NAOMI
“I’ll tell the Defenders.”

Barry stays crouched, eyes still on the basin.

BARRY
“They weren’t just calling something.”

He finally stands.

BARRY (CONT’D)
“They were answering it.”

Naomi studies him, measuring what he’s becoming.

NAOMI
“You sure you’re ready for this fight?”

Barry looks back toward the city lights.

Toward Phoenix.

Toward everything he lost, and everything that might burn if no one stops this.

BARRY
“No.”
(quiet)
“But they are.”

The wind picks up, carrying the last smell of smoke away.

The desert goes still again — as if nothing happened.

But the shadows linger just a fraction longer than they should.

CUT TO BLACK.
