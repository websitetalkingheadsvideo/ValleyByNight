The lighting is a study in Chiaroscuro, where Muted Gold (#B89B64) desk lamps fight against the encroaching Noir Blue-Black (#0D0E10) of the corners.

Victoria Sterling enters with her signature measured, precise movements, her dark bob perfectly in place. She wears a charcoal-gray tailored suit that is unnaturally immaculate, reflecting her Perfectionist nature even in the face of a midnight summons. She stops three paces from the heavy mahogany desk.

Roland Cross stands by the floor-to-ceiling window, his silhouette cutting a jagged line against the Phoenix city lights. He does not turn around.

Roland: “The hierarchy has a vacuum, Victoria. The Prince was assassinated less than an hour ago.”

Victoria’s piercing blue eyes do not widen; instead, they narrow, immediately beginning to catalog the implications with predatory stillness. Her posture remains ramrod straight, her expression an unreadable mask of corporate composure.

[VO – Victoria]: “A vacuum is not just an absence of power. It is a systemic failure. The others will smell blood and call it opportunity. I see a ledger that has been shredded, and I am the only one who knows where the backups are kept.”

Victoria’s hand moves to her side, her long, disciplined fingers twitching once—not in fear, but in the calculated anticipation of a strategic thinker ready to restore order.

Victoria: “Then we move to contingency protocol Sigma. I will have the financial assets frozen and the heralds briefed by dawn. We do not mourn, Roland. We stabilize.”

She turns to leave, her mind already processing information faster than most can follow, her Natural Ventrue authority radiating through the room.
