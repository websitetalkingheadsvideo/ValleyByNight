🎞️ Opening Cutscene: “What We Carry With Us”

[INT. – ART GALLERY – NIGHT]

The trio moves slowly through the gallery now — not admiring, not lingering.
The overhead lights are dimmed to a soft security glow, turning polished floors into mirrors.

Their footsteps echo too loudly.

Behind them, the private room door is closed.
Whatever they saw in there stays there — but not really.

Sarah Hansen: “Should we tell the Sheriff?”

She doesn’t stop walking when she says it.
Her eyes are on a blank stretch of wall — where a painting should be.

Julien Roche: “Do you think he’d believe us?”

Julien runs a hand through his hair, forcing a laugh that never quite forms.

Julien: “Or do you think he’d believe why we were there in the first place?”

They pass a sculpture — abstract, elegant, meaningless right now.

Mr. Harold Ashby: “The Sheriff believes what keeps order.”

Ashby slows, finally stopping near the center of the room.
The emergency exit sign casts red light across his face.

Ashby: “And right now, order says this never involved us.”

A beat.
The gallery hums quietly — HVAC, electricity, the city breathing outside.

Sarah: “What about the Prince? If we could get in to see him—”

Julien: “We don’t get in to see the Prince.”
(sharper than he intended)
“We get invited. Or we get summoned.”

Ashby exhales slowly.

Ashby: “And walking in unannounced with… this?”
(a small, careful gesture back toward the private room)
“That’s not an invitation. That’s a confession.”

They resume walking.

The front doors are visible now — tall glass, locked, reflecting the three of them back in fragments.

Sarah: “So what do we do?”

Ashby stops again. This time, he turns to face them.

Ashby: “We decide what version of tonight survives.”

Silence stretches.

Ashby: “Because someone will be blamed for this.”
(quiet)
“And I don’t intend for it to be us.”

From somewhere deeper in the building, a door clicks — distant, final.
Jennifer leaving. The clock moving forward.

The trio stands among priceless art, holding something far more dangerous than beauty.
