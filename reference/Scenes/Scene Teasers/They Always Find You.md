🎞️ Opening Cutscene: “They Always Find You”

[INT. – SARAH’S CLUB – NIGHT]

A single volumetric spotlight cuts through the smoke, isolating the stage from the rest of the room.

Sarah Hansen sings into the dark.
Low. Controlled. Intimate.

The audience is invisible — a black void beyond the lights.

[VO – SARAH]:
“Sometimes even vampires know fear.”

She opens her eyes.

[POV SHOT – SARAH]

The crowd resolves just enough to suggest bodies.

All of them indistinct.

Except one.

A woman in black sits near the back, perfectly still. Gold jewelry glints faintly — deliberate, ritualistic. Her eyes shine unnaturally bright.

Roxanne Murthy.

She stands.

She begins to walk toward the stage.

Each step echoes too clearly in the hush between notes.

Sarah keeps singing.

Roxanne walks faster.

The shadows around her stretch.

Her silhouette warps — spine lengthening, shoulders drawing inward. Her shadow rises before she does.

Gold flashes.
Eyes burn.

Her body unfolds into a massive serpent, scales black and burnished gold, coiling through the aisle as if the room itself is making space.

The snake lifts its head.

It looks directly at Sarah.

The spotlight flickers.

Sarah’s song ends in silence.

The serpent keeps coming.
