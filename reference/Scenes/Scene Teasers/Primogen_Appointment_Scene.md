# The Primogen Appointments
## A Pre-Game Scene: Phoenix, Late 1993

**[INT. ELYSIUM - PHOENIX - NIGHT]**

*The air in Phoenix's Elysium carries the weight of unspoken tensions. Soft amber light from chandeliers casts long shadows across marble floors. The gathering is smaller than usual—only the most influential Kindred have been summoned. Prince Solomon Reaves stands at the head of the chamber, his presence commanding but brittle, like glass under pressure.*

*Cordelia Fairchild, the Harpy, observes from her position near the grand staircase, her expression unreadable. She has held her position for over thirty years, and tonight she watches the political landscape shift with the careful attention of a predator.*

---

### **SCENE ONE: THE GATHERING**

**PRINCE SOLOMON REAVES**  
*(His voice carries authority, but there's an edge of exhaustion)*

"Ladies and gentlemen of the Camarilla. We gather tonight to address vacancies that have weakened our council. The Toreador and Malkavian clans have been without Primogen representation for too long. This cannot continue."

*Murmurs ripple through the assembled Kindred. Some exchange glances. Others maintain perfect composure.*

**PRINCE SOLOMON REAVES** (CONT'D)

"Étienne Duvalier. Step forward."

*Étienne Duvalier glides forward with the effortless grace that defines his two centuries of unlife. He wears a tailored suit that seems to catch and reflect the chandelier light, making him appear almost luminous. His silver walking stick—a work of art in itself—taps softly against the marble floor. He stops at a precise distance from the Prince, positioning himself so the light frames him perfectly.*

**ÉTIENNE DUVALIER**  
*(His voice is hypnotic, each word carefully sculpted)*

"Your Excellency."

*He doesn't bow. He doesn't need to. His presence commands attention through aesthetic perfection rather than submission.*

**PRINCE SOLOMON REAVES**

"Étienne, you have cultivated influence in Phoenix for decades. Your gallery salons have become the center of Kindred social life. Your childer—Sebastian and Sabine—show promise. The Toreador clan needs leadership. Will you accept the position of Primogen?"

*Before Étienne can respond, a voice cuts through the silence.*

**UNKNOWN VENTRUE ELDER**  
*(Standing among the assembled Primogen, his tone skeptical)*

"With respect, Your Excellency—Étienne is an artist, not a politician. The Toreador need someone who understands the practical necessities of Camarilla rule, not someone who rearranges furniture for 'symmetry.'"

*Étienne's expression doesn't change, but his eyes sharpen. He turns his head slightly, as if examining a flawed sculpture.*

**ÉTIENNE DUVALIER**  
*(His voice drops, becoming more intimate, more dangerous)*

"Ah. You mistake aesthetics for frivolity. A common error."

*He shifts his weight, and the movement is so perfectly calculated that every eye follows him. His Presence Discipline activates subtly—not overwhelming, but undeniable.*

**ÉTIENNE DUVALIER** (CONT'D)

"The Prince commands the city through law and force. I compose it through culture and connection. Tell me—when was the last time a Ventrue elder convinced a mortal gallery owner to host an Elysium gathering? When did a Brujah's strength persuade a city council member to vote against rezoning that would have exposed our haven?"

*He pauses, allowing the silence to stretch. His gaze sweeps across the assembled Kindred.*

**ÉTIENNE DUVALIER** (CONT'D)

"I have spent two centuries learning that power flows through beauty, through social architecture, through the invisible threads that connect us all. The Toreador clan understands this better than any other. We don't need a politician—we need someone who can shape the very fabric of Kindred society."

*Cordelia Fairchild, from her position on the staircase, gives an almost imperceptible nod. The Harpy's approval is noted by those who know how to read such signals.*

**PRINCE SOLOMON REAVES**  
*(His expression tightens slightly—he recognizes the challenge in Étienne's words, but also their truth)*

"Étienne Duvalier, I appoint you Primogen of Clan Toreador. May you serve the Camarilla with the same precision you bring to your art."

*Étienne inclines his head—not a bow, but an acknowledgment. The gesture is elegant, calculated, perfect.*

**ÉTIENNE DUVALIER**

"Thank you, Your Excellency. I shall compose a council worthy of Phoenix."

*He steps back, but doesn't retreat. He remains visible, a living work of art positioned to observe what comes next.*

---

### **SCENE TWO: THE JESTER'S TURN**

**PRINCE SOLOMON REAVES**

"Misfortune. Step forward."

*The Malkavian emerges from the shadows near the edge of the gathering. He wears a mismatched suit—pinstripe jacket, plaid trousers—that should look ridiculous but somehow conveys an unsettling authority. Tiny bells, nearly inaudible, chime softly as he moves. His eyes sparkle with mischief, but there's calculation beneath the chaos.*

**MISFORTUNE**  
*(His voice carries a singsong quality, but the words are precise)*

"Step forward, step back, step sideways into the light—but which step leads where, and who holds the map? Your Excellency calls, and the fool answers."

*He stops at an angle to the Prince, positioning himself so he can observe both the Prince and the assembled Kindred. His movements are fluid, theatrical—every gesture part of a performance that may or may not be real.*

**PRINCE SOLOMON REAVES**

"Misfortune, the Malkavian clan has been without Primogen representation. The position requires someone who can manage the clan's... unique perspectives while maintaining Camarilla stability. Will you accept?"

*Before Misfortune can respond, the same Ventrue elder speaks again.*

**UNKNOWN VENTRUE ELDER**

"Your Excellency, with all due respect—appointing a Malkavian as Primogen is asking for chaos. Their madness makes them unpredictable. How can we trust someone who speaks in riddles to represent their clan in council?"

**MISFORTUNE**  
*(He tilts his head, and the bells in his cap jingle softly)*

"Ah, but riddles are simply questions that haven't found their answers yet. And chaos, my dear elder, is simply order waiting to be recognized."

*He turns to face the Ventrue directly, and his expression shifts—the theatrical mask drops for just a moment, revealing something sharp and dangerous beneath.*

**MISFORTUNE** (CONT'D)

"You speak of unpredictability, but I see patterns you cannot. Last month, when the Anarchs attempted to breach the Masquerade near 24th Street, who warned the Sheriff before the incident occurred? When the Giovanni made their move in Scottsdale, who had already positioned allies to counter them?"

*He takes a step forward, and his voice drops, losing the singsong quality.*

**MISFORTUNE** (CONT'D)

"I see connections. I see debts. I see the web of favors and obligations that holds this city together—or tears it apart. The Malkavian clan doesn't need a politician. We need someone who understands that information is currency, and secrets are power."

*He glances toward Cordelia Fairchild, and there's a moment of understanding between them—the Harpy and the would-be Primogen, both masters of social architecture.*

**MISFORTUNE** (CONT'D)

"And besides—who else would want the position? The Malkavian clan is not known for its eagerness to take on responsibility. I am willing. I am capable. And I am already here."

*The Ventrue elder opens his mouth to protest, but Prince Solomon Reaves raises a hand.*

**PRINCE SOLOMON REAVES**

"Enough. Misfortune, your point is valid. The position has been vacant because no one else has stepped forward. And your... insights... have proven valuable to the domain."

*He pauses, considering. The weight of the decision hangs in the air.*

**PRINCE SOLOMON REAVES** (CONT'D)

"Misfortune, I appoint you Primogen of Clan Malkavian. May you serve with the clarity that lies beneath your riddles."

**MISFORTUNE**  
*(He performs an elaborate, theatrical bow—but it's perfectly executed, and somehow doesn't look ridiculous)*

"Thank you, Your Excellency. The fool accepts the crown—or is it the crown that accepts the fool? Either way, the joke is only funny when you owe me something."

*He straightens, and his eyes meet Étienne's across the room. There's a moment of mutual recognition—two social architects, two masters of the invisible game, acknowledging each other.*

---

### **SCENE THREE: THE AFTERMATH**

*The formal appointments concluded, the gathering begins to disperse. Kindred move through the chamber in careful patterns, positioning themselves for private conversations, strategic alliances, subtle threats.*

*Étienne Duvalier stands near a marble column, observing the social flow. He adjusts the position of a small sculpture on a nearby pedestal—moving it two inches to the left, creating perfect symmetry with the column's shadow.*

**CORDELIA FAIRCHILD**  
*(Approaching with the measured grace of a predator)*

"Congratulations, Étienne. The Toreador clan is in capable hands."

**ÉTIENNE DUVALIER**  
*(Without looking away from the sculpture)*

"Thank you, Cordelia. Though I suspect you had a hand in this appointment."

**CORDELIA FAIRCHILD**

"Perhaps. The Harpy's influence is subtle, but it exists. And you have proven yourself valuable to the domain's social architecture."

*She turns her attention to Misfortune, who has positioned himself near the exit, observing the departing Kindred with the intensity of a collector cataloging specimens.*

**CORDELIA FAIRCHILD** (CONT'D)

"Misfortune. I trust you understand the responsibilities that come with your new position."

**MISFORTUNE**  
*(His voice returns to its singsong quality, but his eyes remain sharp)*

"Oh, I understand. Responsibilities, debts, favors—they're all the same currency, just different denominations. And I am, after all, a collector."

*He produces a small notebook from his jacket pocket—bound in dark leather, filled with what appears to be a ledger of names and dates.*

**MISFORTUNE** (CONT'D)

"Every Primogen owes the Harpy support. Every Primogen owes the Prince loyalty. And tonight, the Prince owes me a favor for accepting a position no one else wanted."

*He closes the notebook with a soft click.*

**MISFORTUNE** (CONT'D)

"The joke is only funny when you owe me something. And now, my dear Harpy, everyone owes me something."

*Cordelia's expression doesn't change, but there's a flicker of something—respect? Wariness?—in her eyes.*

**CORDELIA FAIRCHILD**

"Be careful, Misfortune. Debts can be called in from both directions."

**MISFORTUNE**

"Oh, I know. That's what makes it interesting."

*He slips the notebook back into his pocket and moves toward the exit, his bells jingling softly with each step.*

*Étienne watches him go, then turns back to Cordelia.*

**ÉTIENNE DUVALIER**

"An interesting choice, the Malkavian. Unpredictable, but potentially valuable."

**CORDELIA FAIRCHILD**

"Phoenix needs stability, but it also needs vision. Misfortune sees things others cannot. And you, Étienne, understand that power flows through beauty and connection."

*She pauses, and her voice drops slightly.*

**CORDELIA FAIRCHILD** (CONT'D)

"The Prince's position is not as secure as it appears. These appointments are meant to strengthen the council, but they may also create new power centers. Be careful how you use your influence."

**ÉTIENNE DUVALIER**  
*(His expression remains perfectly composed, but his eyes sharpen)*

"The Prince commands the city. I compose it. There is a difference, and I understand it perfectly."

*He adjusts the sculpture one more time—a final, precise movement.*

**ÉTIENNE DUVALIER** (CONT'D)

"Thank you for the warning, Cordelia. But I have been playing this game for two centuries. I know how to position my pieces."

*She nods, acknowledging the truth in his words, and moves away to attend to other matters.*

*Étienne remains, observing the chamber, the social flow, the political landscape. He has achieved his goal—Primogen status, official recognition of his power. But he knows this is only the beginning. The real game, the long-term strategy that will eventually position him and his childer for influence in New York, has just entered a new phase.*

*Across the room, Prince Solomon Reaves watches the new Primogen, his expression unreadable. He has made the appointments, strengthened the council, but he knows that every action in Kindred politics has consequences. These two—the artist and the jester, the composer and the collector—will shape Phoenix's future in ways he cannot yet predict.*

*And in the shadows, Misfortune pauses at the exit, his notebook already open, already recording the debts and favors of the night. The fool who knows everyone's secrets. The collector who holds everyone's obligations. The Primogen who sees the patterns others miss.*

*The joke, as he said, is only funny when you owe him something.*

*And now, everyone does.*

### **GM NOTES:**

**Timeline Context:** This scene takes place in late 1993, approximately 6-12 months before the game begins in 1994. At this point, Prince Solomon Reaves is still in power. The scene establishes how Étienne Duvalier and Misfortune came to hold their Primogen positions, setting up the political landscape that will exist when the chronicle begins.

**Key Relationships Established:**
- Étienne's barely concealed contempt for Prince Solomon Reaves begins here, as the Prince "commands" while Étienne "composes"
- Misfortune's relationship with Cordelia Fairchild is strengthened through their mutual understanding of social architecture
- Both new Primogen recognize each other as fellow masters of the invisible game

**Foreshadowing:**
- The Prince's position is noted as "not as secure as it appears"
- Misfortune's collection of debts and favors begins in earnest
- Étienne's long-term strategy for New York influence is already in motion

**Tone:** Elegant, subtle, political, and ominous—exactly as the prompt requested. The scene maintains perfect consistency with established VbN canon while showing the political maneuvering that led to these appointments.
