# Valley by Night — Flashback Scenes Collection (v2)
## Cinematic System & Flashback Philosophy Integration

This document consolidates all flashback and cinematic scenes, rewritten to align with the Art Bible Cinematic System and Flashback Philosophy.  
**Tone reference:** *Angel* / *Forever Knight*.  
All scenes are observer-driven, consequence-focused, and designed to reinforce internal tensions and unlockable character logic.

**Cinematic Specifications:**
- Duration: 30-60 seconds (ideal: 45 seconds)
- Shot count: 7-9 shots maximum
- VO lines: 5-7
- Camera: 35mm, 50mm, 85mm lenses
- Lighting: Warm gold practicals, sodium amber, teal moonlight, crimson neon

## Flashback — "The Long Way In"
### Trigger: Internal tension — mortal endangerment / leadership fracture

**[EXT. DESERT ACCESS ROAD – NIGHT – MID-1970s]**

**SHOT 1 — ESTABLISHING (35mm, wide)**
A mid-'70s high-end pickup truck sits at an angle on a dirt turnout. Oversized hood, chrome dulled by dust. Engine idling. AM radio murmurs faintly through the open window, nearly swallowed by wind. Teal moonlight washes the desert flats. Sodium amber from distant highway lights creates warm pools in the darkness.

**SHOT 2 — CHARACTER REVEAL (50mm, MCU)**
Rey Gonzalez behind the wheel. Leather jacket. Hands tight on the steering wheel. Key light from dashboard glow (warm gold practical) at 40° angle. Shadows retain detail in his face. Eyes visible, emotional anchor.

**SHOT 3 — SECONDARY CHARACTER (85mm, MCU)**
Roland Cross stands twenty feet away in the desert like he belongs there. Trench coat that seems to have grown in around him — timeless, perfectly tailored, flowing easily in the wind, untouched by grit despite the dust. Large cowboy hat with wide brim casts shadows that always hide his eyes. Teal moonlight rim light from upper-right. Predatory stillness.

**SHOT 4 — INSERT (50mm, close)**
Rey's hand reaches for the radio dial. Dashboard light catches chrome. Slow, deliberate movement.

**SHOT 5 — EMOTIONAL BEAT (50mm, MCU)**
Rey kills the radio. Silence expands. Wind takes over.

> **Rey (VO):** "That's as far as the truck goes."

**SHOT 6 — REACTION (85mm, MCU)**
Roland doesn't turn. Large cowboy hat with wide brim casts shadows that always hide his eyes. Trench coat flows easily, never catching on anything. Timeless, perfectly tailored — seems to have grown in around him. Controlled gesture — slight head tilt.

> **Roland (VO):** "You said that already."

**SHOT 7 — INSERT (50mm, close)**
Rey's knuckles white on the steering wheel.

> **Rey (VO):** "Because it keeps being true. Sabbat trails one way. Garou the other. My people don't come back from places like this."

**SHOT 8 — WIDE (35mm)**
Naomi Blackbird moves slowly along a ridge, half-seen in moonlight. Further out. Watching.

> **Naomi (VO):** "They've been watching since sundown."

**SHOT 9 — CLOSING (50mm, MCU)**
Roland rests a hand on the truck door. Trench coat flows easily, never catching on the door's edge. Timeless, perfectly tailored — seems to have grown in around him. Large cowboy hat with wide brim casts shadows that always hide his eyes. Warm gold from interior light catches his hand and the coat's fabric. Rey's face in shadow.

> **Roland (VO):** "If we pull back, the Sabbat fortifies."

> **Rey (VO):** "And if I go forward, the ground fills with crosses."

Silence. A distant howl rolls across the flats — not an animal.

> **Rey (VO):** "Half supplies. Smaller team. No repeat runs."

Roland hesitates — just enough to be seen.

> **Roland (VO):** "Very well."

**SHOT 10 — FADE OUT (35mm, wide)**
The truck pulls away, headlights carving through dust. Teal moonlight and sodium amber mix. Soft vignette closes in.

> **Naomi (VO):** "He won't forget."

> **Roland (VO):** "Neither will I."

**[FADE TO BLACK]**

**GM Note:** This flashback surfaces when Rey refuses a plan that endangers mortals. It personalizes the abstract risk — "exposure" becomes faces, deaths, crosses. Creates future constraint: Rey will not negotiate on mortal safety. The unresolved silence echoes forward — Roland's "Very well" is acceptance, not agreement.

## Flashback — "Why I Stay"
### Trigger: Internal tension — leadership fracture / ideological conflict

**SHOT 5 — EMOTIONAL BEAT (50mm, MCU)**

> **Rey (VO):** "You push until someone else pays."

**SHOT 6 — INSERT (50mm, close)**

**SHOT 7 — CHARACTER REVEAL (50mm, MCU)**

**SHOT 9 — EMOTIONAL BEAT (50mm, MCU)**

**GM Note:** Forever Knight model — conditioning, not trauma. This flashback shows the moment a compromise became habit. Rey's line — "you know what you're doing when you're wrong" — establishes a hardened boundary. This is where he stopped negotiating. The flashback doesn't resolve; it explains why Rey no longer debates certain issues. The weight comes later, in the present.

## Technical Notes

**Lighting Setup:**
- Warm gold practicals (candlelight, lamps, chandeliers) at 35-45° key light angles
- Sodium amber street lights for exterior scenes
- Teal moonlight for rim lights and fill
- Crimson neon accents for supernatural moments
- Marble candlelight for Giovanni aesthetic (Elysium scenes)

**Camera Movement:**
- Slow, deliberate
- Never handheld or chaotic
- Depth of field: shallow to medium
- Lenses: 35mm (establishing), 50mm (character/emotional), 85mm (intimate/close)

**Color Grade:**
- Soft vignette on all shots
- Slight film grain
- Enhanced noir contrast
- Warm/cool split tones (warm gold practicals + teal moonlight)
- Subtle haze

**Audio Design:**
- Ambient: wind, AC hum, city noise, murmurs
- Music: slow jazz, low strings, soft synth pads
- SFX: subtle; footsteps, glass, cloth
- Silence used as dramatic tension

**Discipline Representation (Subtle Only):**
- Obfuscate: edge blur, light distortion (see "Close Enough to Bleed")
- Presence: warm bloom (see "The Offer")
- No overt supernatural effects
