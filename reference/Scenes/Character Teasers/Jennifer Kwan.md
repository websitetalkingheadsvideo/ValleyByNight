## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Watchful Eye*

**Setting:**
The Hole nightclub, second basement. Late night. The air is thick with the scent of ozone from security equipment, old concrete, and something else: the faint chill of hidden surveillance. Jennifer's domain is a space between worlds—the public face of the nightclub and the private reality of the Setite temple beneath.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The security office feels *sterile*—not just professionally clean, but *functionally* clean. Like a military command center or a police precinct. Jennifer Kwan moves through it with the precise, economical motions of someone who's spent their entire unlife preparing for threats. Her tactical gear never quite looks out of place, even here, because it's part of who she is.

Monitors cover the walls, showing surveillance feeds from every level of The Hole—the public dance floor, the VIP areas, the first basement where corruption happens, and the hidden second basement where Roxanne conducts her rituals. But there are other feeds, too—cameras Roxanne doesn't know about. Jennifer's own surveillance network, her private contingency plans.

This isn't disloyalty. It's *redundancy*. Jennifer believes in worst-case planning, and she knows that when the Masquerade breaks or the Giovanni come calling, someone has to be ready. She's that someone.

Her haven is minimalist and functional—weapons, communications equipment, escape routes. She's prepared for the day when The Hole's true nature is discovered, when faith isn't enough, when structure and preparation become the only things that matter.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Jennifer's tactical capabilities—likely Potence for physical strength, Fortitude for endurance, and Presence for commanding authority. Her military background makes her a formidable combatant and strategist.

* **Tone:** Pragmatic, professional, prepared. Jennifer doesn't share Roxanne's religious fervor, but she respects the structure and purpose it provides. She's loyal but not blind—she's prepared for the day when loyalty might not be enough.

* **First impression:** Players see a Setite who's both loyal and independent—a security director who protects the temple not out of faith, but out of professional duty and pragmatic necessity. Her hidden surveillance suggests she's prepared for everything, including betrayal.

* **Hooks:**
  * Jennifer's hidden surveillance is discovered—by Roxanne, by the Giovanni, by PCs. This creates a crisis of trust that could shatter The Hole's stability.
  * She must choose between loyalty and survival when the Masquerade threatens to break or the Giovanni infiltrate successfully.
  * Her contingency plans are needed when The Hole faces a real threat—but using them might reveal her lack of faith.
  * She discovers something about The Hole's true purpose that changes everything—perhaps the blasphemy-shrine serves a purpose beyond Set's return.
  * PCs could exploit her pragmatic nature, convincing her to share information or switch allegiances if the situation demands it.

* **Plot foreshadowing:** "Faith is a luxury. Preparation is a necessity. And Jennifer has been preparing for the day when faith isn't enough."
