## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Art of Corruption*

**Setting:**
The Hole nightclub, VIP lounge and first basement. Late night. The air is thick with the scent of expensive liquor, subtle cologne, and something else: the slow, methodical erosion of moral boundaries. Marcus's domain is the space between public nightclub and private temple—where corruption happens gradually, elegantly, professionally.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The VIP lounge feels *expensive*—not just in terms of money, but in the way it's designed to make people feel important, powerful, *above* the rules. Marcus Webb moves through it like a predator, smooth and confident, his tailored suit and expensive watch marking him as someone who's never been told no.

But there's another space, hidden beneath the public face of The Hole: the first basement. Here, Marcus's work becomes art. High-value targets are guided into this space—politicians, executives, socialites—where their vices are cultivated, their weaknesses exploited, their souls slowly turned toward Set.

Marcus keeps detailed files on every mark, tracking their descent into addiction, blackmail, or worse. He doesn't do it out of religious fervor—he does it because it's *profitable*. Corruption is a business, and Marcus is a master businessman.

His relationship with Roxanne is professional but respectful. He doesn't share her religious fervor, but he appreciates the structure and purpose it provides. The Hole is both a business and a weapon, and Marcus is the one who makes sure it's profitable while Roxanne focuses on her rituals.

But when the Giovanni discover the blasphemy-shrine and Marcus's files are at risk, his pragmatic nature will be tested. Will he choose profit or faith? Business or loyalty? The answer will determine whether The Hole survives—or burns.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Marcus's social manipulation abilities—likely Presence for seduction and social control, Dominate for subtle manipulation, and Serpentis for physical capability. His business acumen makes him a master of corruption and social engineering.

* **Tone:** Smooth, professional, predatory. Marcus approaches corruption like a business transaction—elegant, methodical, profitable. He doesn't see himself as evil; he sees himself as a businessman who happens to trade in souls.

* **First impression:** Players see a Setite who's both dangerous and approachable—a club manager who's also a master of corruption. His polished exterior masks deep ruthlessness, and his professional demeanor makes him seem harmless until it's too late.

* **Hooks:**
  * A corruption target becomes dangerous—perhaps a politician who discovers The Hole's true nature or a business executive who turns the tables on Marcus.
  * The Giovanni discover Marcus's files and use them to blackmail him or turn him against the Setites.
  * He must choose between profit and faith when Roxanne's religious practices conflict with The Hole's business operations.
  * His past catches up with him—perhaps a former target seeking revenge or a business partner from his Las Vegas days.
  * PCs could exploit his pragmatic nature, convincing him to share information or switch allegiances if it serves his business interests.

* **Plot foreshadowing:** "Marcus understands corruption better than anyone. He's made an art of it. But when faith and profit collide, even masters can fall."
