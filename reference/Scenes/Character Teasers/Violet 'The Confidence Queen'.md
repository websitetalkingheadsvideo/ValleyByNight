## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Mask and the Truth*

**Setting:**
The Nosferatu Warren—an old, large military bunker beneath Phoenix. Then Elysium, Cordelia Fairchild's Scottsdale estate.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

In the depths of the Nosferatu Warren, Violet 'The Confidence Queen' sits before a bank of monitors, her true face revealed—the deformed features of her clan, the curse that marks her as one of the hidden. She's watching her information network, screens showing street corners, alleys, and the faces of her informants. A rat messenger scurries up her arm, and she whispers instructions to it, her voice low and streetwise.

Then she rises, adjusting her patched leather jacket and sequined hoodie. Her fingerless gloves trace the air, and for a moment, her features *shimmer*—Obfuscate's Mask of a Thousand Faces wrapping around her like a second skin, transforming her deformed Nosferatu features into a perfect, beautiful human face.

She climbs the stairs from Elysium's basement, the same clothes, the same swagger, but a different mask—one that won't offend the elders above. When she emerges into the grand hall, her violet-tipped hair catches the chandelier light, her mirrored shades reflecting the elegant crowd. Her face is flawless—a lie that lets her walk among the beautiful undead.

She moves through the crowd, already cataloging faces, alliances, and secrets. To the Kindred of Phoenix, she's a neon-soaked gossip broker whose knowledge can crown or ruin princes—if you can afford her price. But in the warren below, she's something else entirely: the truth behind the mask, the Nosferatu who controls the flow of information, and the survivor who knows that in Phoenix, knowledge is power.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Violet's *Obfuscate* (Mask of a Thousand Faces) is active when she's in public, hiding her Nosferatu features. Her *Animalism* allows her to communicate with and command rats.
* **Tone:** Streetwise, confident, with an underlying vulnerability. Violet knows she's playing a dangerous game, but she's too good at it to stop.
* **First impression:** Players see a street-smart information broker who seems perfectly at home in Elysium, but those who know her true face understand the mask she wears—and the power she wields from the shadows.
* **Hooks:**
  * Her rat messenger "Mr. Buttons" goes missing after seeing something in Elysium—she needs help finding him.
  * She owes a pawnshop debt and needs help paying it off, or she'll lose access to valuable information sources.
  * She needs escorting through Elysium without offending elders—her Obfuscate is good, but not perfect, and some Kindred can see through it.
  * Rivals are poaching her informants, and she needs help stopping them before her network collapses.
  * She tries to sell a flash drive of secret Nosferatu surveillance footage—unaware it's hexed, and PCs might discover the trap.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "In Phoenix, everyone wears a mask. But Violet's the only one who remembers which face is real."
