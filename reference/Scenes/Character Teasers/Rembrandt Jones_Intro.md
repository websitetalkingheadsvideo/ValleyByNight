Opening Cutscene: “The Wrong Kind of Beautiful”

[INT. SCOTTSDALE ART GALLERY – NIGHT]

Soft amber light spills across polished concrete floors. Abstract paintings line pristine white walls. Well-dressed patrons murmur politely, glasses clinking, laughter restrained.

Rembrandt Jones stands slightly apart from the crowd, impeccably dressed, smiling easily as he listens. He nods, gestures with practiced grace — charming, present, effortless.

[CUT TO:]

A flicker of neon reflected in glass. Not from the gallery — from outside. A cheap motel sign buzzes faintly across the street.

Rembrandt’s smile falters.

[CLOSE ON:] his eyes, suddenly focused. The hum of fluorescent lighting swells subtly in the sound mix.

[VO – REMBRANDT]:
“They always think beauty announces itself.”

He drifts toward the window, leaving the conversation mid-sentence. Outside, the neon sign flickers — uneven, imperfect, alive.

[CUT TO:]

Rembrandt photographing the sign with meticulous care. Every angle precise. Every detail reverent.

[VO – REMBRANDT]:
“But the best things rarely ask to be understood.”

The camera pulls back. Behind him, the gallery patrons watch, unsure whether they’re witnessing eccentricity… or performance art.

Rembrandt lowers the camera, satisfied. He smiles — genuinely.

[FADE TO BLACK]
