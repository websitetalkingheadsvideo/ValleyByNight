# 💋 *Opening Cutscene: “The Velvet Stage”*

**[INT. THE VELVET DOOR – MAIN ROOM – NIGHT]**

Soft amber light paints the stage in slow gradients.  
Velvet curtains breathe faintly with the pulse of ceiling fans.  
A single microphone — chrome, ribbed, old-fashioned — waits center stage.

**Sarah Hansen** stands before it, still and perfect, a silhouette of satin and firelight.  
The last note of her song lingers in the air like perfume — a sound that refuses to die.

She doesn’t move.  
Head bowed. Eyes closed. Hands trembling just enough to betray the effort of control.

**[VO – SARAH]:** *“They call it passion. But passion’s just hunger with better lighting.”*

The camera drifts slowly across her face, catching the shimmer of stage tears she’ll never admit to.  
Beyond the spotlight, the club is empty — chairs stacked, ashtrays cold, ghosts of applause still echoing off the walls.

**[VO – SARAH]:** *“Beauty isn’t the lie. It’s the promise that someone’s still watching.”*

---

**[CAMERA MOVEMENT]**  
Pull back.  
Past the stage.  
Past the tables, glasses, and faint trails of cigarette smoke still curling in mid-air.  
The only sound now is the soft hum of the neon outside.

At the bar, **the bartender** counts the till, glances once toward the stage, then turns off the last light.  
He walks to the front door, pulls it closed, and turns the key.

**[EXT. THE VELVET DOOR – CONTINUOUS]**

The neon sign buzzes red against the night: **THE VELVET DOOR**.  
Its reflection ripples across a rain-slick sidewalk.  
Camera keeps pulling back until the sign’s glow becomes just another heartbeat in Mesa’s sleepless dark.

**[VO – SARAH]:** *“They only remember the song… never the singer, once the lights go out.”*

**[CUT TO BLACK]**

**VALLEY BY NIGHT – CHAPTER TWO: THE VELVET STAGE**

## 🎭 *Scene Card: “The Velvet Stage”*

**Setting:**  
Late night inside *The Velvet Door*, Mesa. The club is closed. The last song has ended.

**Description (for players):**  
The room feels like it’s still breathing. Tables half-cleared, mirrors fogged from the heat of too many bodies.  
On the stage, **Sarah Hansen** stands motionless before an antique microphone — head bowed, eyes shut.  
The silence feels deliberate, like she’s savoring the absence of applause.  
When the bartender locks the door, the neon outside hums to life, its red light painting her shadow across the curtain.  
It’s not loneliness. It’s ritual.

---

## **GM Notes**

- **Disciplines:** *Presence* (always active), *Auspex 1* (heightened awareness of audience emotion).  
- **Tone:** Controlled melancholy. Every frame is composition and restraint.  
- **Lighting:** Warm amber spotlight on Sarah; club bathed in desaturated reds. Contrast between inner glow and outer neon.  
- **Sound:** Solo piano fades into silence → faint hum of neon → key turning in lock.  
- **Player Hooks:**  
  - Toreador or Setite characters may feel a supernatural pull — her resonance lingers.  
  - Eddy Valiant or Kerry could later recall this imagery in dialogue as shared memory.  
  - Investigating the club reveals each performance ends exactly at midnight — no exceptions.  
- **Mood:**  
  Elegy for beauty, isolation as art. The performer alone with her illusion.
