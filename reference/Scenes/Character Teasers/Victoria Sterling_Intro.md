The only light comes from a single Muted Gold (#B89B64) desk lamp and the soft, rhythmic blink of a corporate laptop’s power light. On the desk, a Montblanc pen and a stack of administrative documents are aligned with obsessive, surgical symmetry.

Victoria Sterling sits perfectly upright in an ergonomic leather chair. She is the picture of Fortune 500 excellence: a charcoal-gray tailored suit, not a single wrinkle, and a dark bob styled with such precision it seems carved from obsidian.

She doesn’t move. She doesn't even seem to breathe. She simply watches a ghouled assistant—a man in a sharp suit who is visibly sweating despite the air conditioning—as he places a final ledger on her desk.

Victoria: “Precision is not a suggestion, David. It is the floor. Without it, the entire structure collapses.”

She opens the ledger. Her piercing blue eyes—analytical and predatory—flicker in the amber lamplight.

Victoria looks up. She doesn't raise her voice, but the air in the room grows heavy, thick with the weight of her Presence. The assistant freezes, his eyes widening as he finds himself unable to look away from her gaze.

Victoria: “Go. Ensure the Primogen’s tithe is processed by midnight. Do not make me find a 'systemic error' in your performance.”

The assistant nods frantically and exits. Victoria reaches for her pen, her movements economical and deliberate. Outside, a cloud passes over the moon, casting the office into Noir Blue-Black (#0D0E10) shadows, leaving only her cold, blue eyes illuminated.
