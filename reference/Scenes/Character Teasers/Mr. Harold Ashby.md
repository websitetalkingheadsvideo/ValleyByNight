## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Pattern in Death*

**Setting:**
Mr. Ashby's haven in Sun City, late night. The space is immaculate, organized with obsessive precision—every object placed deliberately, every photograph arranged like a gallery. The air is thick with the scent of old paper, developing chemicals, and something else: the chill of calculated preservation.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The haven feels *wrong*—not just in the way a serial killer's home feels wrong, but in the way a museum feels wrong when it displays something that shouldn't be displayed. Mr. Harold Ashby moves through it like a curator, his calm, teacherly demeanor masking the horror of what he does.

Photographs cover the walls—victims posed at peace, their grief captured in perfect stillness. Each one is marked with the same eerie serenity, the same disturbing composure. Ashby studies them like a scholar examining artifacts, seeing patterns in death that others cannot perceive.

His methods are precise, ritualized, almost reverent. He studies his victims before acting, learning their grief, understanding their loss, then staging their deaths as the final act in a story only he can read. The photographs he takes are his masterpieces, each one capturing what he believes is the perfect moment of release from mortal suffering.

But beneath the calm exterior lies something deeply unsettling—the way he speaks of murder as preservation, of death as art, of ritualized killing as sacred documentation. His ghoul Tor assists him, believing he's documenting something sacred, unaware that he's helping a serial killer stage ritualized murders.

When the PCs encounter Ashby, they'll find a man who seems reasonable, even kind—a teacherly figure who sees patterns in death that drive others to madness. But his calm composure is more disturbing than rage, his ritualized murders more horrific than frenzy.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Ashby's Malkavian abilities—likely Auspex for reading emotions and detecting grief, Dementation for subtle manipulation, and Obfuscate for maintaining his cover. His calm, teacherly demeanor makes him seem harmless, which is part of his danger.

* **Tone:** Calm, serene, deeply unsettling. Ashby speaks of murder as preservation, death as art, ritualized killing as sacred documentation. His teacherly composure is more disturbing than rage, his ritualized murders more horrific than frenzy.

* **First impression:** Players see a Malkavian who seems reasonable, even kind—a teacherly figure who sees patterns in death that others cannot perceive. But his calm composure masks something deeply unsettling, and his ritualized murders are more horrific than frenzy.

* **Hooks:**
  * Tor realizes the truth about Ashby—his ghoul is starting to suspect that this isn't preservation, it's murder. This could lead to rebellion or confession.
  * One of Barry Washington's parents becomes a victim—forcing Barry to confront his faked death and lost mortal connections, creating a personal stake in stopping Ashby.
  * PCs must stop the killings—the ritualized murders are escalating, and Ashby is becoming bolder in his "preservation" work.
  * Ashby decides to "preserve" someone the PCs care about—making the serial killer a personal threat that must be stopped.
  * The pattern of deaths reveals a deeper conspiracy—perhaps Ashby is working with others, or his "divine pattern" connects to something larger.

* **Plot foreshadowing:** "Ashby sees patterns in death that drive others to madness. But when the pattern leads to someone you love, madness becomes motivation—and preservation becomes war."
