# ROLAND CROSS - CHARACTER TEASER
## "The Sheriff"

---

### FADE IN:

**EXT. PHOENIX STREETS - NIGHT**

The desert sky stretches overhead, infinite and cold. Stars pierce the darkness like bullet holes in black velvet. The city sprawls below, neon and shadow, but up here on this empty overlook, there's only silence and wind.

A figure stands at the edge, motionless as stone.

ROLAND CROSS. Tall, lean, timeless. Wide-brimmed hat casts his face in shadow. The duster coat hangs from his shoulders like a shroud, moving slightly in the night breeze. At his hips, two revolvers with sandalwood grips catch the starlight.

He doesn't move. Doesn't need to. He's already seen what he came to see.

Behind him, the wind picks up. The duster begins to flow, fabric snapping like a flag. Still, he stands. Watching. Waiting. Patient as death itself.

Finally, almost reluctantly, he turns.

His boots crunch on gravel. Each step measured, economical. No wasted motion. The duster trails behind him like wings of shadow, flowing with each stride. He walks with the certainty of a man who knows exactly where every bullet will land.

---

**INT. ELYSIUM - GRAND HALL - CONTINUOUS**

The gathering is in full swing. Kindred society at play - conversations, schemes, the endless dance of politics and power. Toreador artists discuss their latest works. Ventrue executives negotiate territory. Brujah activists argue ideology. The room hums with undead energy.

The doors open.

Conversation doesn't stop immediately. First, it's just those nearest the entrance. Their words trail off mid-sentence. Heads turn. Then the ones beyond them notice. Then more. Like ripples spreading across still water.

Within seconds, the entire room has fallen silent.

ROLAND CROSS stands in the doorway.

He doesn't stride in dramatically. Doesn't make an entrance. He simply steps across the threshold and pauses, letting his eyes sweep the room. Cold blue eyes that miss nothing. That see everything. That remember every face, every weakness, every threat.

Some Kindred meet his gaze and hold it - the brave ones, the foolish ones, or those secure enough in their power not to flinch. Most look away. Not from shame, but from simple survival instinct. You don't stare down a predator.

A young Brujah - too new, too confident - starts to say something. His companion, older and wiser, puts a hand on his arm. Shakes his head once. The Brujah falls silent.

Roland's hand never moves toward his guns. Doesn't need to. Everyone in this room knows what those sandalwood grips mean. Knows the reputation. Knows the stories.

The Sheriff is here.

A Ventrue elder - one of the few who didn't turn to look - speaks without glancing up from his drink. His voice carries in the silence.

**VENTRUE ELDER**  
*"Good evening, Sheriff. The Prince is expecting you in the west gallery."*

Roland's response is economical as everything else about him. A single nod. 

He moves through the crowd, which parts before him like water before the prow of a ship. His boots echo on the marble floor - the only sound in the vast hall besides the nervous shuffle of the crowd repositioning itself in his wake.

As he passes, a young Toreador whispers to her companion, awestruck:

**TOREADOR**  
*"Did you see his coat? The way it moves? Like death itself is following him..."*

Her companion, an older Toreador who understands, corrects her quietly:

**OLDER TOREADOR**  
*"No, my dear. Death doesn't follow him. It walks beside him. As an equal."*

Roland continues forward, unhurried. Inevitable.

He reaches the doorway to the west gallery. Pauses. Turns slightly, and his gaze sweeps the room one final time. Not a threat. Just... awareness. Recording. Cataloging.

Then he steps through.

The door closes behind him with a soft, final click.

The sound echoes in the silence like a gunshot.

---

**EXT. PHOENIX STREETS - LATER**

Roland stands on a rooftop, looking out over the city. The duster moves in the wind. The stars wheel overhead, infinite and uncaring.

His hand rests on the grip of his revolver. Not drawing. Just... resting. A gunfighter's habit, old as the frontier itself.

Somewhere in the city below, someone is breaking the Masquerade. Or planning to. Or thinking about it.

Roland knows. He always knows.

His fingers drum once on the sandalwood grip. A decision made.

He turns and walks toward the edge of the roof. The duster flows behind him. And then he's gone, dropping into shadow with the casual certainty of a man who's faced death so many times it no longer frightens him.

Because death doesn't frighten the Sheriff.

Death works for him.

### FADE TO BLACK.

**TITLE CARD:**
*"I don't miss."*
— Roland Cross, Sheriff of Phoenix
