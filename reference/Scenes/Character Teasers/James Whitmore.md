## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Truth in His Palm*

**Setting:**
Elysium, Cordelia Fairchild's Scottsdale estate. A Camarilla gathering in full swing. Soft jazz, golden chandelier light, the murmur of Kindred politics.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

James Whitmore moves through Elysium like he owns the room—which, in a way, he's trying to. The Tremere Regent has the polished confidence of a 1960s Madison Avenue executive, every gesture calculated, every word measured. His bespoke suit never wrinkles, his pocket square is folded to surgical precision, and his eyes constantly assess the room like a hostile takeover target.

He approaches a Ventrue elder, extending his hand in greeting. The handshake is firm, professional, *perfect*. But if you're watching closely—and you are—you notice something: a smooth marble rests in his palm, hidden between their grip. It looks like nothing more than a worry stone, a nervous habit.

The Ventrue speaks: "The Tremere have been... *cooperative* since your arrival, Regent."

And the marble *flares*—a brief pulse of deep crimson light that only James can feel, a heartbeat of truth that reveals the lie. His expression never changes. The smile remains, the eyes still calculating, but something shifts—a subtle tightening of his jaw, the faintest narrowing of his gaze. He's cataloging the lie, filing it away, building his dossier on every Kindred in Phoenix.

He releases the handshake, the marble already palmed and hidden, and continues through the crowd with the same polished confidence. But now you know: James Whitmore is always collecting information, always building his case, and the Ball of Truth is his most subtle weapon.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** James's Thaumaturgy is always active, subtly enhancing his perception and strategic thinking. The Ball of Truth is an artifact, not a Discipline, but it functions similarly.
* **Tone:** Polished, confident, strategic. James never shows his hand, never reveals what he knows. He's always playing the long game.
* **First impression:** Players see a charismatic Regent who seems perfectly composed, but those who notice the Ball of Truth realize he's always gathering intelligence, always building his case.
* **Hooks:**
  * The Ball of Truth reveals lies, but James must be subtle about using it. If discovered, it could be seen as a violation of Elysium's trust.
  * He's building dossiers on every Kindred faction, treating Phoenix like a hostile takeover target. PCs might become part of his intelligence network—or targets of it.
  * The failed coup that exiled him to Phoenix is a sensitive topic. He can't run for Prince due to clan hatred, but he's using the Prince's death as an opportunity to rebuild Tremere influence.
  * He positions his apprentices (like Andrei) like marketing assets. PCs might be recruited or manipulated into serving his agenda.
  * The Ball of Truth could be stolen, or someone might discover its use and use it against him.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "In Phoenix, truth is a commodity—and James Whitmore is the only one who knows the real price."
