## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Extraction That Never Came*

**Setting:**
Layla's safe house in Phoenix, late night. The space is functional, professional—tactical maps and surveillance equipment organized with military precision. The air is thick with the scent of old coffee, gunpowder, and something else: the weight of a mission gone wrong.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The safe house feels *wrong*—not just in the way a place of violence feels wrong, but in the way a place of abandonment feels wrong. Layla al-Sahr moves through it like a ghost, her elegant Malkavian cover flawless, her true nature hidden beneath layers of deception.

Her assignment was simple: extract the primary assassin after the hit. Ensure the contract ended cleanly with no trace. But when the Prince fell, the first assassin—codename 'Anwar'—never appeared at the rendezvous point. Layla found only faint Quietus residue and signs of a struggle.

Now she's stranded in Phoenix with her handler missing and extraction impossible, maintaining a Malkavian cover under increasing suspicion while covertly searching for her lost comrade. Her predicament intertwines with the city's chaos: Misfortune's surprise 'childe,' Tremere investigation, and Harpy gossip all circle her presence.

She's a professional, trained to adapt and survive. She's using her Malkavian cover to blend into Phoenix's Kindred society, gathering information while searching for Anwar. But every night she remains increases the risk of discovery. Whether she will vanish quietly, hunt for Anwar, or accept a new role within Phoenix's deadly politics remains uncertain.

Her skills make her valuable to those who recognize them—she's an expert in assassination, extraction, and maintaining cover. But her true identity, if discovered, would mark her as an enemy of the Camarilla and a threat to everyone who knows the truth about the Prince's death.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Layla's Assamite abilities (hidden beneath Malkavian cover)—likely Quietus for assassination techniques, Obfuscate for maintaining her cover, and Celerity for combat. Her training makes her a formidable assassin, but her stranded situation makes her vulnerable.

* **Tone:** Professional, tense, trapped. Layla is a master assassin who's lost her extraction, stranded in enemy territory, searching for a lost comrade while maintaining a cover that could shatter at any moment. Her calm exterior masks deep tension and desperation.

* **First impression:** Players see a Malkavian who seems elegant and sophisticated, but something feels *off*—her calm is too controlled, her movements too precise, her cover too perfect. Those who look closely might notice the tension beneath the disguise.

* **Hooks:**
  * Layla's cover is discovered—perhaps by Tremere investigation, Misfortune's suspicions, or Harpy gossip, forcing her to flee or fight.
  * She finds Anwar—but he's been captured, killed, or turned, creating a complex situation that requires delicate handling.
  * She must choose between mission and survival—completing her extraction mission versus protecting her cover and staying alive in enemy territory.
  * PCs could help Layla find Anwar—offering assistance in exchange for information about the Prince's death or protection from her assassination skills.
  * Layla decides to accept a new role—abandoning her Assamite mission to integrate into Phoenix's Kindred society, creating an opportunity for PCs to recruit her.

* **Plot foreshadowing:** "Layla's assignment was to extract the assassin. But when the assassin disappears, the extractor becomes the target—and in Phoenix, every target is someone else's opportunity."
