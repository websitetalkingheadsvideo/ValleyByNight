## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Desert's Thirst*

**Setting:**
The Phoenix Tremere chantry, late night. Andrei Radulescu's private ritual chamber. The air is thick with the scent of ozone, desert dust, and old blood.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The ritual chamber feels *wrong*—not just the Thaumaturgy, but the way the air itself seems to *wither*. Andrei Radulescu moves with nervous precision, his hands trembling slightly as he traces sigils that shouldn't exist. The experimental Dehydrate path glows with deep crimson light, and you can *feel* the moisture being pulled from everything—your skin, your clothes, the very stone.

This is forbidden research. The kind that gets a 12th generation Tremere killed if the Pyramid discovers it. But Andrei doesn't stop—he *can't* stop. This is his ticket to respect, his proof that he deserved the Embrace despite his blood.

The ritual reaches its crescendo, and for a moment, the entire chantry feels like a tomb—dry, dead, *hungry*.

Then, from the desert beyond Phoenix, you hear it: the howl of wolves. Not ordinary wolves. *Garou*. They've sensed the corruption, the unnatural theft of the desert's essence. And they're coming.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Andrei's experimental Dehydrate Thaumaturgy path—a custom ritual that draws moisture from the environment. This is *dangerous* research that could get him killed if discovered.
* **Tone:** Nervous, desperate, obsessive. Andrei knows he's playing with forces that could destroy him, but he can't stop. This research is his only path to validation.
* **First impression:** Players see a Tremere who's both brilliant and terrified—a refugee scholar who's weaponizing the desert itself to prove his worth.
* **Hooks:**
  * The Garou have sensed the ritual. They may come investigating, or this could be the start of a larger conflict.
  * If James Whitmore discovers this research, Andrei's protection disappears—the Regent can't shield him from Pyramid punishment.
  * Dr. Margaret Ashford might recognize the ritual's power and either try to steal it or report it to the Pyramid.
  * The experimental path could be useful to PCs—if they can convince Andrei to share it, or if they discover it after his death.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "The desert remembers what you steal from it—and the Garou are its memory."
