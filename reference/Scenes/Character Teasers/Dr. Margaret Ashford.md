## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Enchanted Scholar*

**Setting:**
Elysium, Cordelia Fairchild's Scottsdale estate. A Camarilla gathering in full swing. Soft jazz, golden light, the scent of old money and rose perfume.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

Dr. Margaret Ashford stands at the edge of the grand hall, impeccably groomed in her Victorian traveling suit, every detail arranged with obsessive symmetry. She's documenting the evening—cataloging alliances, measuring debts, applying the same scholarly precision she uses for her warding research.

But something's *wrong*. Her hand rises to her forehead, touching a spot that makes no sense in this context. Her eyes—cold blue behind gilt spectacles—hold a distant look, as if she's seeing something else entirely.

For a moment, you catch a glimpse of what she sees: a horse in a moonlit stable, a desert ride at night, the brush of her gloved hand against a flank. The images are vivid, *real*, but they don't belong here. They feel like memories bleeding through, or dreams intruding on waking, or something else entirely.

When she blinks, the vision fades. She's back at Elysium, surrounded by Kindred who don't notice the brief absence in her eyes. But you saw it. You saw the question she cannot answer: *Which is the dream, and which the waking?*

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Margaret's protective Thaumaturgy is always active, subtly warding her personal space. Her Auspex might be picking up on the fae enchantment, but she doesn't recognize it.
* **Tone:** Elegant, controlled, but with an underlying unease. The fae enchantment creates moments of dissociation where reality and dream blur.
* **First impression:** Players see a Victorian scholar who seems perfectly composed, but there's something *off*—a gesture that doesn't fit, a look that suggests she's seeing things others cannot.
* **Hooks:**
  * The fae enchantment through Aonbharr is slowly twisting her dreams and possibly her perceptions. This could escalate.
  * If PCs have Auspex or knowledge of fae magic, they might sense something wrong with her.
  * Her protective warding research could be compromised by the enchantment—wards that look perfect but have subtle flaws.
  * She might seek help if she realizes something is wrong, or PCs could discover the enchantment and use it as leverage.
  * Her correspondence with her Philadelphia sire might reveal clues about when the enchantment began.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "The fae don't steal—they *borrow*. And they always return what they've taken, changed."
