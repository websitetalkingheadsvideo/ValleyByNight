## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Neutral Ground*

**Setting:**
The Bali Hai tiki bar on Camelback Road, late night. Warm amber light from tiki torches, the scent of rum and coconut, surf rock playing softly. Anarchs and Camarilla courtiers share the same space, kept separate by unspoken rules and Bob's watchful eye.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The Bali Hai shouldn't exist in Phoenix—a tiki bar in the middle of the Sonoran Desert, complete with torches, blowfish lamps, and the faint scent of the ocean. But here it is, and Bayside Bob runs it like a master.

He moves behind the bar with the ease of someone who's done this for thirteen years, his laid-back island aesthetic masking something sharper. He's friendly, hospitable, the kind of bartender who remembers your drink and asks about your night. But if you watch closely, you'll see it: the way his eyes track every conversation, the way he catalogs every alliance, the way he maintains the delicate balance between Anarchs and Camarilla.

This is neutral ground. Bob's neutral ground. And he'll do whatever it takes to keep it that way.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Bob's *Presence* is subtle but effective—he uses it to maintain the bar's atmosphere, to keep tensions low, to make everyone feel welcome. His *Auspex* might help him read the room, sensing alliances and threats before they become problems.
* **Tone:** Friendly, hospitable, but with an underlying sharpness. Bob is a survivor who's learned to play both sides, and he's very good at it. The laid-back island aesthetic masks a strategic mind that's always calculating.
* **First impression:** Players see a friendly Toreador bartender who seems perfectly relaxed, but there's something *off*—the way he watches everyone, the way he maintains perfect organization behind the scenes, the way he always has an escape plan.
* **Hooks:**
  * The Bali Hai faces a zoning crackdown—Bob needs help defending his neutral ground, or the Anarchs and Camarilla will lose their meeting place.
  * A truce needs to be brokered on Bob's patio—he'll facilitate, but PCs might need to help negotiate or provide security.
  * Bob's missing surfboard reliquary from his mortal past has been stolen—it's sentimental, but it might also contain something valuable or dangerous.
  * Someone is trying to break the neutrality of the Bali Hai, forcing Bob to choose sides or lose everything.
  * Bob's intelligence network is threatened—someone is targeting his informants, and he needs help protecting them.
  * The Coast Guard connection from his past resurfaces, forcing him to confront his mortal life and potentially expose his Kindred nature.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "In Phoenix, neutral ground is a luxury—and Bob is the only one who can maintain it. But even the best bartender can't keep the peace forever."
