🎞️ Prelude Scene — “Before It’s Named”

[INT. STORAGE WORKSHOP – NIGHT]

Close. Too close.

A hand brushes marble dust from a shoulder, from a jaw that never quite became a face. Julien exhales softly and blows—a pale cloud lifts, catching the light, then settles again.

He doesn’t smile.

He just nods, once.

JULIEN (V.O.)
Sometimes you don’t know when something ends.
(beat)
Or when it’s about to begin.

He steps back. The sculpture stands unfinished—deliberately so. Honest. Waiting.

[INT. CAR – MOVING – NIGHT]

Julien drives north.

The city is muted tonight. Streetlights blur. The sky is wrong—no stars. The moon is enormous, low and oppressive, bleaching the world in silver and shadow.

Julien squints up at it through the windshield, unsettled.

JULIEN (V.O.)
Sometimes a beginning doesn’t announce itself.
It just… shows up.

The car continues on.

[EXT. HAWTHORN ESTATE – SIDE ENTRANCE – NIGHT]

The car stops where guests aren’t meant to arrive.

Julien steps out. Opens the trunk.

The crate waits.

He grips it carefully, more reverent than strong, and lifts. The night feels heavier here—older.

The door looms.

Cut.

[INT. PRIVATE ART GALLERY – NIGHT]

The crate is gone.

Broken slats lean against a marble pedestal. Nails lie abandoned. A few dark strands of Julien’s hair cling to the stone where he brushed past.

The sculpture now stands fully revealed.

Julien steps back, breath caught—not awe, not pride. Something closer to relief.

A polite, deliberate clap breaks the silence.

ADRIAN LECLAIR stands nearby, smiling faintly.

ADRIAN LECLAIR
“Very good, Julien. You knew when to stop.”

Julien nods, unsure what to do with the approval.

Adrian inclines his head and moves away, already forgetting the moment.

[INT. PRIVATE ART GALLERY – LATER]

The room has changed.

Lights dim. Conversations drift elsewhere. A single spotlight isolates the sculpture—stone suspended in meaning.

JULIEN (V.O.)
Sometimes a new beginning…

The camera shifts—now from the sculpture’s point of view.

Across the gallery, a door opens quietly.

Beyond it: the Prince’s office.

Voices, sharp. Tension. A sudden movement—

JULIEN (V.O.)
…is being a witness to murder.

The door slams shut.

The sculpture does not move.

It only watches.

[CUT TO BLACK]
