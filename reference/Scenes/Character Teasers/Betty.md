## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Digital Nerve Center*

**Setting:**
The Phoenix Nosferatu haven, late night. An R&D lab filled with modem banks, dual-platform servers, surveillance rigs, and the constant hum of digital infrastructure. The air smells of ozone, hot electronics, and the faint scent of the Nosferatu curse made manifest.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

Betty's haven is an R&D lab, a space where technology and the Nosferatu curse have merged into something *other*. Modem banks hum with digital life, servers stack against walls, and surveillance rigs feed data streams into the nascent Shrecknet—the digital network that will connect Nosferatu across the continent.

But Betty herself is the most striking feature. Her skin flickers with scan lines like a CRT monitor, her hair sprouts fiber-optic filaments that glow with the pulse of data, and her fingertips are hardened into connector pins that can jack directly into machines. She wears oversized engineer coveralls layered with circuit-board jewelry, and a portable keyboard is slung at her hip.

She's building the future, one line at a time. Network architecture. Predictive analytics. The digital nerve center that will make Phoenix the heart of the Nosferatu information network. Politics bore her; she cares about the technology, the connections, the network itself.

But she's already infiltrated both sides. The Anarchs don't realize she can trace their pay phones. The Camarilla doesn't yet grasp that she's already copied their ledgers. In Betty's world, everyone is just another node to be mapped, another connection to be exploited.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Betty's curse has manifested as living hardware, allowing her to interface directly with machines. Her technical expertise with network architecture and hacking makes her invaluable for digital operations. Her *Obfuscate* might help her remain unseen, while her technical skills allow her to infiltrate systems without being detected.
* **Tone:** Obsessive, focused, with an underlying sense of isolation. Betty is a visionary who's lost herself in the network, and she's very good at what she does. The physical manifestation of her curse creates an uncanny, almost unsettling quality that separates her from both mortals and other Kindred.
* **First impression:** Players see a Nosferatu who seems perfectly at home in the digital world, a tech prodigy who's built her own network. But there's something *off*—the way her curse has become part of her, the way she's already infiltrated both sides, the way she doesn't care about the politics, only the network.
* **Hooks:**
  * Secure a prototype router shipment—Betty needs help acquiring cutting-edge hardware for her Shrecknet expansion, but the shipment is being tracked by rivals.
  * Purge a virus planted by Sabbat saboteurs—someone has infected Betty's network, and she needs help identifying and removing the threat before it spreads.
  * Help test a risky Shrecknet patch before it goes live—Betty has developed a new protocol that could revolutionize the network, but it needs field testing and could be dangerous.
  * Someone discovers Betty's surveillance of Anarch and Camarilla communications—she needs help covering her tracks or negotiating with those who've discovered her infiltration.
  * A hardware failure threatens to destroy Betty's entire network—she needs help recovering data or rebuilding the infrastructure before everything is lost.
  * Betty's obsession with the network is causing her to neglect other responsibilities—PCs might need to intervene or help her balance her work with other needs.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "In Phoenix, every connection is a weapon, and every node is a path—but even the most secure networks can be compromised when someone knows where to look."
