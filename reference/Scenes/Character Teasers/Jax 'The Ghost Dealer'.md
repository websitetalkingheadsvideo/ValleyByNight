## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Phantom's Price*

**Setting:**
A street corner in Phoenix, late night. Jax's tarot tent—a patchwork structure of bright fabrics, neon lights, and hidden escape routes. The air smells of incense, cheap cigarettes, and something *wrong*.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The tarot tent feels *off*—not just the street vendor aesthetic, but the way the air itself seems to *shimmer* at the edges, as if reality is thin here. Jax moves with practiced confidence, their hands tracing patterns in the smoke, their voice smooth and convincing. They're selling a "ghost removal," and the mortal is buying it.

But then something *slips*. The illusion becomes too real—a phantom figure writhing in the smoke, a mirage that shouldn't exist. Jax's smile falters, their eyes darting toward the tent's entrance. They know this is wrong. They know their Chimerstry is slipping, that their illusions are becoming *real*.

When they emerge from the tent, you see it: a stray afterimage following them, a phantom of their own making that's become something else entirely. They run, but the ghost follows—a reminder that in Phoenix, even the lies you tell yourself can become real.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Jax's *Chimerstry* is active, creating illusions for their "ghost removal" scam. But the discipline is slipping—their illusions are becoming too real, leaving stray afterimages that stalk them. This is dangerous, both for Jax and anyone nearby.
* **Tone:** Chaotic, charming, but with an underlying terror. Jax is a street grifter who's losing control of their own power, and they know it. The neon-soaked aesthetic masks a deeper fear: that their illusions will become real enough to hurt someone.
* **First impression:** Players see a charming Ravnos con artist who seems perfectly in control, but there's something *wrong*—the way their illusions shimmer too brightly, the way they check escape routes, the way they run from their own creations.
* **Hooks:**
  * A rogue illusion escapes Jax's control and starts causing real problems—maybe it's stalking someone, or it's become visible to mortals, threatening the Masquerade.
  * Jax sold a "cursed talisman" that's actually causing real harm—they need help repossessing it before someone dies.
  * A short-tempered client discovers Jax's scam and wants revenge—PCs must negotiate a truce or help Jax escape.
  * Jax's afterimages are becoming more persistent, more real. They might need help finding a way to control their Chimerstry before it destroys them.
  * Someone is hunting Jax, believing their "ghost removals" are real and wanting to use them for darker purposes.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "In Phoenix, even the lies you tell yourself can become real—and Jax is running out of places to hide from their own illusions."
