## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Weight of Guilt*

**Setting:**
Resistance cell safe house in Phoenix, late night. Core's workspace is functional, militaristic—tactical maps, security schematics, weapons within easy reach. The air is thick with the scent of old coffee, gunpowder, and something else: the weight of crushing guilt.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The safe house feels *stark*—not just in the way military facilities feel stark, but in the way a hospital feels stark. Cold, fluorescent light bleaches everything gray. Core stands before a wall of photographs—mortals she helped surveil, systems she helped build, victims of corporate security she once served.

Her fingers, calloused and steady, trace invisible lines connecting them like threads in a web of her own making. This is her burden: the knowledge that she built the systems that watch them, trained the people who hurt them, made the choices that led to their suffering.

But guilt isn't paralyzing her anymore. It's *fueling* her. Core is using her insider knowledge to dismantle what she helped build, one system at a time, until the people she hurt are free.

Her expertise in tactical planning, corporate security, and strategic operations makes her a formidable leader, but her tendency to shoulder all responsibility alone and difficulty trusting fully even allies creates tension within her resistance cell. Despite her flaws, she inspires loyalty through her competence and dedication.

Her relationship with Phreak is complex—built on mutual respect and complementary skills, with underlying romantic tension that neither fully acknowledges. Together, they're a force: Core's tactical knowledge and Phreak's digital skills creating a partnership that could dismantle the surveillance state.

But Core's past is catching up with her. Her former colleagues are hunting her, the systems she built are being used against her, and the guilt she carries threatens to consume her. When the choice comes between mission and team safety, between idealism and pragmatism, Core will have to decide what matters more: atonement or survival.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Core is listed as Nosferatu, but her biography suggests she's human—a former corporate security specialist turned resistance leader. For this teaser, focus on her tactical abilities: hand-to-hand combat, tactical firearms, strategic planning, and intimate knowledge of corporate defenses and vulnerabilities.

* **Tone:** Determined, guilty, pragmatic. Core carries the weight of her past choices, using that guilt as fuel to dismantle what she helped create. She's a leader who inspires loyalty but struggles with trust, balancing idealism and pragmatism in a war against powerful enemies.

* **First impression:** Players see a resistance leader who's both competent and haunted—a former corporate security specialist using her insider knowledge to fight the systems she built. Her guilt drives her, but it also makes her vulnerable to manipulation and burnout.

* **Hooks:**
  * Core's past catches up with her—former colleagues hunting her, systems she built being used against her, creating a race against time.
  * She must choose between mission and team safety—idealism versus pragmatism when the resistance cell faces impossible odds.
  * Her relationship with Phreak becomes complicated—romantic tension threatens to disrupt the partnership, or external pressure forces difficult choices.
  * Discover that Core's former colleagues are hunting her—corporate security specialists who know her methods, her weaknesses, her vulnerabilities.
  * Use Core's tactical knowledge to infiltrate corporate facilities—her insider knowledge of security systems makes her invaluable, but using it risks discovery.

* **Plot foreshadowing:** "Core built the systems that watch them. Now she'll tear them down. But when the past catches up, guilt becomes a weapon—and she'll have to choose between atonement and survival."
