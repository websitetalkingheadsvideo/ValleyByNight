## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Living Chisel*

**Setting:**
Étienne Duvalier's private theatre, late night. A sculpture studio filled with marble dust, chisels, and works in progress. Warm amber light from vintage lamps, the scent of stone and old wood. Then a hidden alcove in Elysium, where Lucien keeps his private sketches.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

Lucien Marchand is a ghost-pale ghoul sculptor who has served Étienne Duvalier for nearly seventy years. He moves with statue-like stillness, his hands perpetually dusted with marble powder, his intense blue eyes focused on invisible symmetry. He barely speaks unless asked, and when he does, it's in quiet, measured tones.

But when he works, his hands move with Celerity-enhanced precision, executing Étienne's vision with flawless accuracy. He's the Primogen's living chisel, the one who shapes marble, bone, or Kindred fate as instructed. He believes his sire's art is divine, and his purpose is to serve that vision.

Yet there's something hidden—private sketches tucked away in Elysium alcoves, drawings that reveal his own artistic desires, sculptures he longs to create. And when he returns to those sketches, he finds them subtly corrected, improved, refined by Étienne's hand. It's a wordless collaboration, a silent acknowledgment that Lucien's art exists, even if it's never fully his own.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Lucien is a ghoul, so he has access to Celerity through Étienne's blood. This allows him to work with superhuman speed and precision, executing complex sculptures in ways that would be impossible for a mortal. His knowledge of the Primogen's theatre includes every hidden passage, every secret entrance, every alcove where art might be stored.
* **Tone:** Devoted, quiet, with an underlying tension between service and personal desire. Lucien is a perfectionist who's found his purpose in serving Étienne's vision, but his private sketches reveal a longing for his own artistic expression. The wordless collaboration with Étienne creates a complex relationship that's both supportive and constraining.
* **First impression:** Players see a ghoul who seems perfectly devoted, a living tool who executes his sire's vision without question. But there's something *off*—the way he hides his sketches, the way he trembles when he looks at them, the way Étienne silently corrects them without ever acknowledging their existence.
* **Hooks:**
  * Discover who is stealing Lucien's hidden statues—someone is taking his private sculptures from the Elysium alcoves, and he needs help finding the thief.
  * Smuggle clay so Lucien can sculpt secretly—he wants to create something of his own, but he needs materials that won't be traced back to Étienne's studio.
  * Help protect Étienne's latest masterpiece from Sabbat vandals—Lucien knows every hidden passage in the theatre, and he can guide PCs to defend the art.
  * Petition Lucien for art restoration—he has the skills to repair damaged pieces, but he'll need permission from Étienne or a compelling reason to work outside his normal duties.
  * Gain illicit security access inside the Primogen's theatre—Lucien knows every hidden passage, every secret entrance, and he might be willing to share that knowledge for the right price.
  * Lucien's private sketches are discovered by someone dangerous—PCs might need to help him protect his secret art, or they could use the knowledge as leverage.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "In Phoenix, every sculpture tells a story, and every chisel stroke is a choice—but even the most devoted servant has dreams they keep hidden in the shadows."
