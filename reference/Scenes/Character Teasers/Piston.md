## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Mark of the Beast*

**Setting:**
An abandoned warehouse in Surprise, Arizona, late night. Desert dust, motorcycle parts, stacks of cash on a table. Then the desert itself—vast, empty, the kind of place where secrets can be buried.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

Piston is an imposing Brujah biker built like a diesel engine—broad shoulders, scarred knuckles, and a permanent scowl etched into weathered features. He wears a worn leather cut with faded Hell's Angels patches over a dark denim shirt, chain wallet, and heavy boots caked with desert dust. His black hair is kept short and greased back, and his eyes burn with the intensity of someone who's seen too much violence.

But those with Auspex can see something else—the telltale black veins spiderwebbing beneath his skin, the mark of diablerie that follows him like a curse. He committed the act in LA during the Anarch faction wars, frenzied in combat with a Gangrel enforcer, the Beast taking over. He gained Fortitude but acquired the mark, and LA became too dangerous. He fled to Phoenix, established in Surprise, and embraced two former Hell's Angels—Butch Reed and Basher—to rebuild the brotherhood he lost.

Now he's running criminal operations to fund the acquisition of a bar, building his own territory, making Phoenix his home. But the mark remains, a constant reminder of what he did, what he became, and what he's trying to leave behind.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Piston's *Fortitude* was gained through the diablerie, making him incredibly tough. His *Potence* and *Celerity* are typical Brujah disciplines, enhanced by his combat experience. The mark of diablerie is visible to those with Auspex, creating a constant vulnerability that he must manage.
* **Tone:** Aggressive, violent, but with an underlying determination to rebuild. Piston is a survivor who's adapted to his circumstances, but the mark of diablerie creates a constant tension—he's marked, hunted, and must always be careful. His relationship with his childer shows a softer side, a desire to rebuild the brotherhood he lost.
* **First impression:** Players see a Brujah biker who seems like pure violence, a thug who's marked by diablerie. But there's something else—the way he's building something with his childer, the way he's determined to make Phoenix his own, the way he's trying to leave LA behind.
* **Hooks:**
  * Help Piston acquire a bar for his coterie—PCs might need to provide funding, negotiate with owners, or help with the criminal operations that fund the purchase.
  * Gangrel from LA track Piston to Phoenix—the revenge he fled is catching up, and he needs help defending himself and his childer.
  * Someone discovers Piston's diablerie mark—PCs might need to help him cover it up, or they could use the knowledge as leverage.
  * Piston's criminal operations conflict with other Anarch interests—PCs might need to mediate, or help him navigate the politics of the Anarch movement.
  * One of Piston's childer (Butch or Basher) is in danger—he'll do anything to protect them, creating opportunities for alliances or conflicts.
  * Piston's past catches up with him in unexpected ways—maybe someone from LA recognizes him, or the Gangrel he consumed has left a spiritual mark that's causing problems.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "In Phoenix, every scar tells a story, and every mark is a reminder—but even the most violent past can be overcome when you have something to fight for."
