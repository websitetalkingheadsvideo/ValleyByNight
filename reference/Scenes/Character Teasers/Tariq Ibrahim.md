## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The VIP Experience*

**Setting:**
Tariq's premier nightclub in Phoenix, late night. The space is sophisticated, elegant, expensive—velvet ropes and exclusive access lists creating an atmosphere of exclusivity and forbidden access. The air is thick with the scent of expensive perfume, old money, and something else: the slow, methodical erosion of moral boundaries.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The nightclub feels *exclusive*—not just in the way expensive venues feel exclusive, but in the way secret societies feel exclusive. Tariq Ibrahim moves through it like a predator, smooth and elegant, his tailored suit and expensive watch marking him as someone who deals in currency beyond money.

His domain is the hidden VIP chamber—a space that exists between public pleasure and private corruption, where Setite rites masquerade as immersive theater, forbidden experiences traded for influence and secrets. Here, Tariq's work becomes art: subtle manipulation, gradual erosion, souls slowly turned toward Set.

He keeps detailed files on every mark—socialites, executives, politicians—tracking their descent into corruption, their willingness to trade influence and secrets for forbidden experiences. His approach is sophisticated, patient, *professional*—making them feel special, chosen, *exclusive*.

But beneath the polished exterior lies a master of corruption who trades forbidden experiences for souls, influence for secrets, entertainment for damnation. His Setite rites are disguised as immersive theater, his corruption masquerading as exclusive access, his manipulation presented as choice.

When PCs encounter Tariq, they'll find a sophisticated operator who offers them access, influence, and experiences that money can't buy. But the price is higher than they realize—and Tariq always collects.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Tariq's Setite abilities—likely Presence for seduction and social control, Serpentis for physical manipulation, and possibly Obfuscate for maintaining his cover. His sophistication makes him a master of subtle corruption.

* **Tone:** Sophisticated, elegant, predatory. Tariq approaches corruption like an art form—subtle, patient, professional. He doesn't see himself as evil; he sees himself as an impresario who trades in forbidden experiences and exclusive access.

* **First impression:** Players see a Setite who's both cultured and dangerous—a nightclub impresario who offers exclusive experiences, forbidden access, and opportunities that money can't buy. His sophistication makes him seem harmless, but his corruption is deep and methodical.

* **Hooks:**
  * A corruption target becomes dangerous—perhaps a socialite who discovers Tariq's true nature, or an executive who turns the tables on his manipulation.
  * The Giovanni discover Tariq's Setite rites—his hidden chamber threatens the Masquerade, creating a crisis that requires careful handling.
  * He must choose between profit and faith—when the Setites demand more from his operation, Tariq's business interests conflict with religious obligations.
  * His past catches up with him—perhaps a former target seeking revenge, or someone who knows the truth about his "immersive theater."
  * PCs could exploit Tariq's need for exclusivity—using his desire for high-value targets against him, or offering him something he wants more than their souls.

* **Plot foreshadowing:** "Tariq understands corruption better than anyone. He's made an art of it. But when faith and profit collide, even masters can fall—and the VIP experience becomes a death sentence."
