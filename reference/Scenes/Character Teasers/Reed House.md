### 🎬 *Opening Cutscene: "Reed House"*

**[EXT. UNNAMED RESIDENTIAL STREET – PHOENIX – NIGHTFALL]**

The sun dies behind jagged rooftops. A flicker of sodium streetlight buzzes to life, casting *pale amber* over a block of tired, low-slung homes. Most are quiet. One dares you to look closer.

**[CUT TO: SLOW DOLLY FORWARD]**

Camera moves toward a fence-wrapped house of bare walls and boarded windows. Gravel crunches underfoot — no footsteps, just pressure. The air stinks of *oil and heat baked into concrete*.

**TITLE CARD FADES IN:** REED HOUSE

**SFX:** A distant, muffled growl. Almost a voice.

**[TRACKING SHOT THROUGH CHAIN-LINK FENCE]**

Camera tracks through the chain-link fence, following the sound. In the yard, cracked dirt forms ragged paths worn by a creature that never stops pacing.

**[ANGLE – LOW TO GROUND, PUSHING FORWARD]**

We creep toward the front gate. Beyond the steel mesh, a shape waits in the *half-dark* — still, massive, breathing.

**REVEAL:** THE DOG.

A rottweiler, but wrong. Its eyes shine with *unnatural alertness*. Patches of hide stretch too tight over its frame. A scarred metal tag dangles from its collar — unreadable, but it knows who you are.

It doesn't bark.

It steps forward.

**[CUT TO: GROUND-LEVEL SHOT – CAMERA LOOKING UP AT THE DOG]**

From the dirt, we see it loom. It fills the frame like a statue, gaze locked on the lens. Behind it, the house is silent, windowless, watching through the dog.

**[DOG POV – INTO THE STREET]**

The dog looks past us, toward something unseen. We hear the faint hum of a garage across the street. Tools. Torch sputter. Then silence again.

**[CLOSE-UP – THE DOG'S EYES]**

They don't blink.

They don't need to.

They've already memorized your scent.

**[SMASH CUT TO BLACK]**

**VALLEY BY NIGHT – REED HOUSE**

## Scene Card (GM / LARP Narration)

You stand at the edge of a working-class Phoenix neighborhood as night falls. The streetlights flicker on, casting amber pools across cracked asphalt. Most houses are quiet, their windows dark. But one house draws your attention — a low-slung structure wrapped in chain-link fencing, its windows boarded, its walls bare and weathered.

As you approach, gravel crunches under your feet. The air carries the scent of motor oil and heat radiating from concrete. From somewhere in the yard, you hear a low, muffled growl — almost like a voice trying to form words.

Through the fence, you see cracked dirt forming paths worn by something that never stops moving. And then you see it: a rottweiler, massive and still, watching you from the shadows. Its eyes shine with an alertness that feels wrong. Patches of hide stretch tight over its frame. A scarred metal tag hangs from its collar.

The dog doesn't bark. It doesn't need to. It steps forward, and you realize it's already memorized your scent.
