## 🎞️ Scene Card: *Rage Against the Machine*

**Setting:**
The abandoned skate shop on the Mesa/Chandler border, late night. Pete's haven is functional, militant—a rebel's command center hidden in plain sight. The air is thick with the scent of gunpowder, leather, and old blood.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The skate shop feels *alive*—not in the way a normal building feels alive, but in the way a war zone feels alive. Broken ramps serve as barricades, empty shelves as weapon storage, dust-covered skateboards as improvised shields. Pistol Pete moves through it like a predator, his revolver never far from his hand.

Maps of Phoenix cover the walls, marked with Camarilla territory in red, Anarch territory in blue. Photographs of elders are pinned to corkboard like wanted posters, each one a target, each one a promise. One photograph stands out: a Camarilla elder, face circled in red marker. A single word written beneath: **HUMILIATION**.

Pete's goal is clear: capture and publicly humiliate a Camarilla elder to prove the establishment's weakness. Not with subtlety. Not with politics. With *proof* that they're vulnerable, that their power is an illusion, that the street can win.

He can feel the whispers of diablerie on newcomers, taste the corruption in the air. The city needs cleansing. The Anarchs need a victory. And Pete is ready to deliver both.

But beneath the gruff exterior lies a rebel who protects neonates and mortals caught between sect crossfire. Mental discipline isn't his game—pure instinct is—but his loyalty to the Anarch cause and his willingness to fight make him a valuable ally or a dangerous enemy.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Pete's Brujah abilities—likely Celerity for speed and reflexes, Potence for physical strength, and Presence for rallying allies. His street-level experience makes him a formidable brawler and enforcer.

* **Tone:** Aggressive, rebellious, determined. Pete channels his Brujah blood into fists, gunplay, and rallying fellow rebels. He doesn't do subtlety—he does results. But beneath the violence lies a deep loyalty to the Anarch cause.

* **First impression:** Players see a Brujah who's both dangerous and loyal—a street enforcer who'll fight for the Anarchs and against the Camarilla with equal ferocity. His goal of capturing and humiliating a Camarilla elder makes him a potential ally or a threat, depending on PC allegiances.

* **Hooks:**
  * Help Pete expose a rumored diablerist—perhaps a Camarilla elder who's been consuming Kindred to increase power, or a newcomer marked by the crime.
  * Mediate a turf dispute before Pete escalates—Anarch territory is sacred, and Camarilla encroachment triggers immediate violence.
  * Ride shotgun during a retaliatory strike against Camarilla hit squads—Pete needs backup for his raids, and PCs could provide tactical support.
  * Protect Pete's haven from discovery—the abandoned skate shop is his sanctuary, and if the Camarilla finds it, the Anarchs lose a valuable asset.
  * Convince Pete that subtlety might be more effective than brute force—a difficult task, but one that could prevent unnecessary casualties.

* **Plot foreshadowing:** "The establishment thinks they're untouchable. Pete thinks they're vulnerable. One of them is about to be proven wrong."
