🎬 CINEMATIC INTRO — “REED HOUSE”

EXT. UNNAMED RESIDENTIAL STREET — PHOENIX — NIGHTFALL

The sun dies behind jagged rooftops. A flicker of sodium streetlight buzzes to life, casting pale amber over a block of tired, low-slung homes. Most are quiet. One dares you to look closer.

CAMERA MOVES — slow dolly forward toward a fence-wrapped house of bare walls and boarded windows. Gravel crunches underfoot — no footsteps, just pressure. The air stinks of oil and heat baked into concrete.

TITLE CARD FADES IN: REED HOUSE

SFX: A distant, muffled growl. Almost a voice.

CAMERA TRACKS through the chain-link fence, following the sound. In the yard, cracked dirt forms ragged paths worn by a creature that never stops pacing.

ANGLE — LOW TO GROUND, PUSHING FORWARD

We creep toward the front gate. Beyond the steel mesh, a shape waits in the half-dark — still, massive, breathing.

REVEAL: THE DOG.

A rottweiler, but wrong. Its eyes shine with unnatural alertness. Patches of hide stretch too tight over its frame. A scarred metal tag dangles from its collar — unreadable, but it knows who you are.

It doesn’t bark.

It steps forward.

CUT TO: GROUND-LEVEL SHOT — CAMERA LOOKING UP AT THE DOG

From the dirt, we see it loom. It fills the frame like a statue, gaze locked on the lens. Behind it, the house is silent, windowless, watching through the dog.

DOG POV — INTO THE STREET

The dog looks past us, tense.

Then—

LIGHT FADES UP slightly, soft orange rising on the horizon. Footsteps echo down the sidewalk — slow, deliberate.

CAMERA SWINGS TO THE RIGHT — NEW ANGLE:

BUTCH REED, broad-shouldered, grease on his boots, a metal key ring jingling at his hip, walks calmly up the sidewalk. His silhouette casts long shadows across the cracked cement.

He approaches the gate.

The dog doesn’t move.

Butch opens the gate with a practiced click.

BUTCH
(quiet, gravel voice)
Good boy, Brutis... Good boy.

He crouches beside the creature. The beast lowers its head. Butch’s hand ruffles the thick, scarred fur. The growl fades into a low, rhythmic pant.

BUTCH
(gently, as if to a child)
You kept it safe, didn’t you?

The dog licks his hand.

CAMERA HOLDS on the two — man and monster — haloed by the glow of the streetlamp, framed by the rusted gate and the dying light.

BUTCH
(softly)
Good boy, Brutis... Good boy.

He opens the gate and crouches. The dog lets out a low huff, then presses into his hand. For a moment, the world is still.

VO (Voice-Over):
Because in the Valley… even a vampire can have a best friend.

FADE TO BLACK.
