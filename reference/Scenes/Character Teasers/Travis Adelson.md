### 🩸 *Opening Cutscene: “Between Hammer and Blood”*

**[INT. ABANDONED JOB SITE OFFICE – NIGHT]**

A single desk lamp burns in the dark, its warm amber light pooling over scattered papers, old blueprints, and an open, leather-bound book whose pages are too old, too deliberate, to belong here. Dust hangs in the air, visible only when a truck rumbles past somewhere far off, shaking the windows.

**Travis Adelson** sits hunched over the desk.

His shoulders are broad, solid—built for lifting, for strain. The sleeves of his faded flannel are rolled up, revealing forearms marked by old scars and fresh calluses. One hand rests flat on the page, steadying it. The other traces a symbol in the margin, rough fingertip moving with surprising care.

His eyes move constantly.

They flick from line to line, then up—listening—then back down again. Sharp. Alert. Never still.

A faint *growl* of wind slips through a cracked window. Somewhere outside, a coyote calls. Travis freezes, head tilting just slightly, instincts waking before thought. A beat passes. Nothing.

He exhales. Slowly.

> **[VO – TRAVIS]:** *“I used to think the world made sense. Measure twice. Cut once. Follow the plan, and the building stands.”*

He turns the page.

The symbols don’t make sense—and yet they do. His brow furrows with the same focus he once gave to blueprints and load calculations. The book *responds* to his attention, the ink seeming darker where his gaze lingers.

> **[VO – TRAVIS]:** *“Then I found the books they said were just stories.”*

A faint shimmer of candlelight flickers across his eyes—too subtle to be obvious, too real to ignore. His pupils narrow. Something inside him stirs.

He flexes his fingers. The calluses are still there. Proof of a life that required sweat, daylight, and honest exhaustion.

He closes the book carefully.

The room feels suddenly smaller.

**[CUT TO:]**

**[EXT. DESERT EDGE – NIGHT]**

Travis stands alone at the edge of the city, Scottsdale’s sodium lights glowing low behind him. The desert stretches out ahead—dark, vast, and silent. Wind tugs at his shirt, carries the smell of dust and distant rain.

His eyes scan the horizon, feral and calculating.

A low, involuntary smile threatens—quickly suppressed.

> **[VO – TRAVIS]:** *“Now the rules are different. The work’s the same.”*

He steps forward, boots crunching on gravel.

> **[VO – TRAVIS]:** *“Learn fast. Build smart. Don’t let it see you bleed.”*

The moonlight catches his face—human resolve edged with something older, wilder.

**[FADE TO BLACK]**
