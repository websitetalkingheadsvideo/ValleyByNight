## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Perfect Shot*

**Setting:**
Phoenix streets, late night. The air is thick with the scent of desert dust, old gunpowder, and something else: the cold precision of professional violence. Roland's domain is the city itself—every shadow, every rooftop, every dark corner where Kindred law must be enforced.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The warehouse feels *still*—not just quiet, but deliberately, professionally quiet. Roland Cross moves through it like a ghost, his long duster coat flowing behind him, his footsteps silent on the concrete floor. Three fledglings lie dead, each one killed with a single, precise shot. Not messy. Not frenzied. Not personal.

*Perfect*.

Roland holsters his revolvers without looking, his movements practiced, economical, beautiful in their lethal precision. He doesn't speak unless necessary. He doesn't waste bullets. He doesn't waste time. He's the Sheriff, and his job is simple: protect the domain, eliminate threats, serve the Prince, perfect the art.

His workshop is a shrine to his craft—tools organized with obsessive precision, ammunition hand-crafted for maximum accuracy, photographs of past kills studied like reference material. Every shot is analyzed. Every kill is considered. Every death is *improved*.

This isn't just violence. This is *art*. And Roland has spent over a century perfecting it. He can identify firearms by sound alone. He crafts his own ammunition. He studies ballistics, anatomy, and the psychology of violence the way other Toreador study painting or sculpture.

But Roland pays the price all Toreador pay. Sometimes he becomes lost in the beauty of a perfect shot, transfixed by the memory of a particularly flawless execution. Other times, he's overwhelmed by botched killings—sloppy work offends him on a visceral level that other vampires find disturbing.

He has no political ambitions. No desire for power beyond what he needs to do his work. He's the Sheriff because someone must be, and he does it better than anyone else.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Roland's Toreador abilities—likely Celerity for inhuman speed, Auspex for threat detection, and Presence for commanding authority. His century of experience makes him a master marksman and tactician.

* **Tone:** Cold, precise, professional. Roland approaches killing like art—methodical, beautiful, perfect. He doesn't do it for power or status; he does it because someone must, and no one does it better.

* **First impression:** Players see a Sheriff who's both lethal and honorable—a Toreador who treats killing as high art, enforcing Kindred law with surgical precision. His professionalism makes him seem cold, but his dedication to perfection makes him dangerous.

* **Hooks:**
  * Roland must investigate the Prince's death—his death was messy, unprofessional, and that offends Roland's aesthetic sensibilities. He'll want to find whoever did it and show them what a *proper* killing looks like.
  * A botched kill offends his aesthetic sensibilities—perhaps a fledgling kills sloppily, or someone murders in a way that's crude and unprofessional. Roland will want to correct this.
  * His sire, Catherine Barlow, returns from Mexico—she was the one who Embraced him, recognizing his art, and her return could complicate his loyalties.
  * He must choose between duty and art when his aesthetic sensibilities conflict with his role as Sheriff.
  * PCs could hire Roland for sanctioned kills, but they'll need to prove their targets deserve it and that the killing can be done professionally.

* **Plot foreshadowing:** "Roland understands killing better than anyone. It's his art. His craft. His obsession. And when someone kills unprofessionally, he takes it personally."
