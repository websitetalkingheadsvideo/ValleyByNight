🎞️ Opening Cutscene: “The Cost of Being Seen”

[INT. – ART STUDIO – NIGHT]

A warehouse studio, converted badly and expensively.
Concrete floors. Exposed beams. The smell of plaster and metal dust.

A single work light burns over an unfinished sculpture — sharp angles, raw surfaces, something almost human.

Julien Roche circles it slowly, sleeves rolled up, hands chalked white.

He stops.
Steps back.
Shakes his head.

Julien: “No. That’s lying.”

He takes a chisel and strikes — once.
The sound echoes too loudly in the empty space.

The sculpture fractures, just enough to ruin symmetry.

Julien exhales, satisfied… then uneasy.

[CUT TO:]

[INT. – GALLERY OPENING – NIGHT – MEMORY]

Laughter. Wine glasses. Soft applause.
Julien stands beside a different piece — finished, perfect, praised.

A patron leans in, whispering admiration.
Julien smiles automatically.

But his eyes drift — past the crowd — toward a mirrored wall.

For just a moment, his reflection doesn’t move when he does.

[CUT BACK TO:]

[INT. – STUDIO – NIGHT]

Julien wipes his hands on a rag that will never come clean.

His phone buzzes on a nearby table.
A message. No name. Just a location. A time.

He stares at it longer than necessary.

[VO – JULIEN]: “They tell you art is how you leave something behind.”
“No one tells you what it costs to be noticed by the wrong people.”

He turns off the work light.

Darkness claims the sculpture — unfinished, unresolved.

[CUT TO BLACK]
