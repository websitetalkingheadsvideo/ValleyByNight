# Warner Jefferson & Barry Washington - Cinematic Introduction

**Campaign**: Vampire the Masquerade - Phoenix by Night (1994)
**Characters**: Warner Jefferson (Sire) & Barry Washington (Childe)
**Purpose**: Introduce both Ventrue as a unit, establish their dynamic, and set up PC hook

---

## "The Protégé's Report"

### Scene 1: The Penthouse

**INT. WARNER'S PENTHOUSE - NIGHT**

Floor-to-ceiling windows. Downtown Phoenix glitters below like a circuit board. 

WARNER JEFFERSON stands before the glass, hands clasped behind his back. Predator's stillness. Impeccable charcoal suit. The city lights reflect in his calculating eyes.

The elevator CHIMES.

BARRY WASHINGTON enters with a leather portfolio. Energetic, stylish, genuine smile.

**BARRY**  
Warner, the Toreador salon went—

Warner raises one hand slightly. Barry stops.

**WARNER**  
Show me.

Barry spreads documents across the mahogany desk.

**BARRY**  
Madame Sinclair is concerned about the art gallery rezoning. I positioned us as preservation allies. She invited us to her private collection viewing next month.

Warner picks up a note, reads it.

**WARNER**  
This observation about the Nosferatu's reaction. Good work.

Barry straightens, hungry for the approval.

**BARRY**  
I remembered you said they control infrastructure near the galleries—

**WARNER**  
Everyone wants something, Barry. The skill is identifying what they want before they realize it themselves.

He marks the notes with precise annotations.

**WARNER** (CONT'D)  
You attended four gatherings this week. Made seventeen contacts. Positioned us with two factions while managing two companies.  
(pause)  
You did well.

Barry's face lights up.

**BARRY**  
I won't let you down.

**WARNER**  
I know. I don't make bad investments.

Barry doesn't catch the coldness. Warner hands him a folder.

**WARNER** (CONT'D)  
New Kindred. Embraced recently. No patron, no connections. They'll be introduced tonight.

Barry opens it.

**BARRY**  
You want me to assess them?

**WARNER**  
Observe. Make contact if it's natural. See if they're an asset worth cultivating or a problem worth monitoring.

**BARRY**  
What if they're neither?

Warner's cold smile.

**WARNER**  
Everyone is something, Barry. We just need to recognize what before someone else does.

He checks his watch.

**WARNER** (CONT'D)  
We should leave. Arriving fifteen minutes late suggests confidence.

**BARRY**  
(grinning)  
And arriving with you suggests I'm worth attention.

**WARNER**  
You're learning.

---

### Scene 2: The Garage

**INT. UNDERGROUND GARAGE - CONTINUOUS**

Concrete pillars. Dim lighting. A black limousine waits, engine running.

Barry and Warner approach. The driver opens the door.

**BARRY**  
Do you think Victoria will be pleased about the Toreador connection?

**WARNER**  
She'll be pleased. I'll include it in my report to her.

Barry slides in. Warner pauses, looks back at the elevator—his empire above. Then gets in.

---

### Scene 3: The Limousine

**INT. LIMOUSINE - NIGHT**

City lights slide across the windows. Barry reviews the folder. Warner watches Phoenix pass by.

**BARRY**  
Says the sire fled immediately after the Embrace.

**WARNER**  
Reckless. Either panic or deliberate chaos.

**BARRY**  
Should I offer to help them?

**WARNER**  
Not tonight. Be friendly. Approachable. People remember who helped them when they were vulnerable.  
(beat)  
That memory becomes leverage later.

Barry studies Warner's profile.

**BARRY**  
Is that what you did for me?

Warner meets his eyes. Weight in the look.

**WARNER**  
I saw potential others overlooked. Provided opportunities for it to flourish. Everything you achieved, you earned.

**BARRY**  
(earnestly)  
I wouldn't be here without you.

**WARNER**  
And I wouldn't have the connections I need without you. Your success is my success.

Barry smiles. Warner turns back to the window. In the reflection—calculating coldness.

**BARRY**  
(closing folder)  
Do you think they'll survive?

**WARNER**  
Depends on whether they're smart enough to recognize help when it's offered.  
(beat)  
And whether they're useful.

**BARRY**  
And if they're not?

**WARNER**  
Then we ensure they're not a problem.

The limo continues into the Phoenix night. Barry's face open and eager. Warner's controlled and calculating.

Two vampires heading to a gathering. One believes his success was earned. One orchestrated it for a decade.

**FADE OUT.**

## What This Scene Establishes

### Warner Jefferson
- Puppet master who calculates every move
- Views people as assets or problems to manage
- Patient predator building an empire
- Genuine investment in Barry's success (because Barry is his tool)
- Already aware of PC and planning approach

### Barry Washington
- Natural charisma and social skills
- Eager for Warner's approval and validation
- Genuinely likable and warm (not fake)
- Doesn't realize Warner engineered his entire life
- Believes his success was earned through merit
- Learning Kindred politics but still naive

### Their Dynamic
- **Surface**: Mentor and devoted student
- **Reality**: Tool and user
- Complementary strengths make them formidable together
- Warner provides strategy and resources
- Barry provides social skills and public presence
- Genuine affection mixed with utilitarian purpose

### PC Hook
- Warner is already investigating the PC
- Barry will make first contact as "the friendly face"
- Creates immediate contrast: PC struggles alone while Barry has guidance
- Sets up potential alliance, rivalry, or complication
- Shows Warner's method: identify, assess, position
- PC better be useful, or they become a problem to solve

### Core Themes
- Nothing is random in Warner's world
- Success is engineered, not accidental (Barry doesn't know this)
- Every interaction serves multiple purposes
- Social grace as weapon and tool
- Power hidden behind pleasant faces
- The price of guidance is control

## Storyteller Notes

**Pacing**: This scene should take 5-7 minutes to read/perform. Quick, punchy, efficient.

**Tone**: Corporate thriller meets vampire intrigue. Professional veneer over predatory calculation.

**Key Moments**:
1. Barry's hunger for approval (shows vulnerability)
2. "I don't make bad investments" (reveals Warner's true view)
3. The new Kindred discussion (introduces PC hook)
4. "Is that what you did for me?" (Barry starting to question, but not yet)
5. "And if they're not useful?" (the threat beneath civility)

**Visual Motifs**:
- Height = power (penthouse looking down on city)
- Descent = moving from planning to action (elevator to garage)
- Motion = heading toward targets (limo into the night)
- Reflection = duality (Warner's face in window)
- Light and shadow = public charm vs. private calculation

**For Players**:
This scene happens before PC introduction. They don't witness it, but they'll FEEL its effects when Barry approaches them with that warm smile and helpful manner. Everything they see from Barry and Warner has been calculated in scenes like this.

**Foreshadowing**:
- Barry's brief questioning ("Is that what you did for me?") - will he realize the truth?
- Warner's utilitarian language ("investments," "assets," "problems")
- The folder on the PC - they're being studied before they even meet
- Barry wanting direct Victoria contact - seeking independence?
- Warner controlling all information flow - maintaining dependence

## Follow-Up Scenes

After this introduction, the story can proceed to:

1. **The Gathering**: Where Barry makes contact with the PC
2. **Barry's Report**: Following up on his assessment of the PC
3. **Warner's Decision**: What to do about the PC based on Barry's report
4. **The Approach**: Warner's calculated offer of "help" to the PC

This scene sets up the entire Warner/Barry/PC dynamic for the chronicle.
