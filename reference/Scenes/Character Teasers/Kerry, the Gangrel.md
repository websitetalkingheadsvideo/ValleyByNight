## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Witness in the Desert*

**Setting:**
The Superstition Mountains, outside Phoenix. Late night. The air is thick with the scent of desert dust, creosote, and something else: the faint chill of old graves and older secrets. Kerry's haven is hidden deep in an abandoned mine shaft, accessible only through winding tunnels and hidden passages.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The desert feels *alive*—not just in the way the wind moves through it, but in the way it seems to watch, to *remember*. Kerry, the Gangrel, moves through it with the furtive grace of someone who's been hunted for decades. His haven is a shrine to a secret he shouldn't have witnessed—the staking of a Prince in 1973.

His walls are covered in evidence: newspaper clippings, photographs, maps. The faces of those who committed the crime. The location of the staked Prince's body. The secret that could destroy powerful Kindred if it ever got out.

Kerry has been running for over twenty years—hunted by those who want to silence him, protected by those who want to use him, marked by Garou who sense the corruption of that night clinging to him like grave dust. He knows where the Prince lies, hidden in the Superstitions, staked and preserved.

But knowing is a curse. And carrying this secret for so long has made Kerry into something between a man and a ghost—a witness who can't forget, a survivor who can't trust, a secret that could bring down princes or get him killed.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Kerry's Gangrel abilities—likely Protean for shapeshifting and desert survival, Fortitude for endurance, and Animalism for communication with desert predators. His decades in the desert have made him an expert tracker and survivor.

* **Tone:** Furtive, haunted, desperate. Kerry carries the weight of a secret he shouldn't have witnessed, and decades of running have made him paranoid and isolated. He's a ghost in the desert, a witness who can't forget.

* **First impression:** Players see a Gangrel who's both dangerous and vulnerable—a witness to a crime that could destroy Kindred society, hunted and haunted by what he knows. His isolation makes him suspicious, but his knowledge makes him invaluable.

* **Hooks:**
  * Kerry's finally tracked down by those who want him dead—perhaps the perpetrators of the Prince's staking or someone who wants to use his knowledge for their own purposes.
  * He decides to reveal the secret, leading PCs to the staked Prince's location and forcing them to decide what to do with this knowledge.
  * The Garou hunt intensifies—the corruption of that night has attracted their attention, and Kerry's haven is no longer safe.
  * He leads PCs to the staked Prince's location, but getting there requires navigating Garou territory, Kindred hunters, and the secrets of the Superstition Mountains.
  * His isolation makes him suspicious of PCs, but his desperation might make him willing to trust them if they prove themselves.

* **Plot foreshadowing:** "The desert remembers what happened in 1973. Kerry remembers. And when he finally speaks, the truth will bring down princes."
