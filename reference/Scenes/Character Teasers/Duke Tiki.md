## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Torch Passed Forward*

**Setting:**
Duke Tiki's studio, late night. A workspace that spans decades—Vegas tiki boom artifacts, neon signs in progress, sketches and carvings everywhere. Warm amber light from vintage tiki lamps, the scent of coconut oil and sawdust.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

Duke Tiki's studio is a time capsule of mid-century escapism—carved idols from the Vegas boom, neon signs in various stages of completion, sketches pinned to corkboard that show the evolution of his art. He works at a workbench, his hands moving with the precision of someone who's carved beauty for half a century.

But this isn't just about the past. Duke is creating something new—art that bridges the gap between the tiki boom of the 1960s and the desert nights of modern Phoenix. His work mixes sincerity and kitsch, and he takes pride in teaching younger artists to see beauty in unexpected places.

When he's not in his studio, he's at the Bali Hai, quietly mentoring Bayside Bob, teaching him to read the souls of patrons rather than their wallets. Duke believes beauty is a torch that every generation must pass forward—and he's determined to keep that flame burning, even as the desert replaces surf and the tiki boom becomes history.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Duke's *Presence* is subtle but powerful—he uses it to inspire, to mentor, to make others see beauty where they might not have looked. His *Auspex* helps him read people, understanding their souls and motivations in ways that make him an excellent teacher and diplomat.
* **Tone:** Warm, mentor-like, but with an underlying perfectionism. Duke is a Toreador elder who's found his purpose in passing beauty forward, and he takes that responsibility seriously. The contrast between his Vegas past and Phoenix present creates a sense of nostalgia mixed with forward-looking hope.
* **First impression:** Players see a Toreador elder who seems perfectly at peace—a mentor, an artist, someone who's found his place in the world. But there's depth beneath the surface: the perfectionism that drives him, the nostalgia for the tiki boom, the determination to keep beauty alive.
* **Hooks:**
  * A stolen tiki idol imbued with Presence has been taken—Duke needs help recovering it, and the idol's power could be dangerous in the wrong hands.
  * Duke has a feud with a modernist Toreador critic who dismisses his work as "kitsch"—PCs might need to mediate, or the feud could escalate into something more dangerous.
  * Duke wants to host a clandestine luau for uneasy factions (Anarchs and Camarilla)—PCs could help organize, provide security, or navigate the delicate politics.
  * Someone is vandalizing Duke's art installations around Phoenix—he needs help finding the culprit and protecting his work.
  * A young artist Duke is mentoring is in danger—PCs might need to protect them, or help Duke understand when to let go.
  * Duke's connection to the Bali Hai is threatened—if the bar closes, he loses his sanctuary and his ability to mentor Bob.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "Beauty is a torch that every generation must pass forward—but even the brightest flames can be extinguished by those who don't understand their value."
