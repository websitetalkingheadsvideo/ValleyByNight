## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Numbers Behind the Beauty*

**Setting:**
Sebastian's private study, late night. A space of perfect organization, ledgers and files arranged with mathematical precision. Then Elysium, where he stands beside Sabine, the two moving in perfect synchronization.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

Sebastian is the mirror image of Sabine in masculine tailoring—impeccable charcoal suits, silk pocket squares, platinum hair slicked back to match his twin. He moves in lockstep with her, gestures precise and almost mechanical, eyes coldly cataloging every social cue in the room.

But where Sabine curates experience, Sebastian weaponizes data. He handles the numbers behind the beauty—tracking boons, scheduling salons, running predictive models on Valley alliances. His ledgers are encoded in a system only he can read, filled with boon trajectories, guest lists, and whispered scandals that fuel Étienne Duvalier's long-term scheme.

He rarely speaks without his sister present, yet he has cultivated discreet relationships with Harpies across North America, building the data network that will reshape Kindred society. In Sebastian's world, every number tells a story, and every ledger is a weapon.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Sebastian's *Auspex* is constantly active, reading social cues and cataloging information. His *Presence* is subtle but effective, used to maintain the perfect synchronization with Sabine. The twin bond creates a psychic connection that allows them to move in perfect lockstep, sharing information without words.
* **Tone:** Precise, mechanical, with an underlying coldness. Sebastian is a perfectionist who sees people as data points, relationships as equations, beauty as a variable to be calculated. The mirrored movements with Sabine create an uncanny, almost unsettling quality that hints at the depth of their bond.
* **First impression:** Players see a Toreador who seems perfectly composed, precise, and in control. But there's something *off*—the way he mirrors Sabine, the cold calculation in his eyes, the way he catalogs everything as data rather than people.
* **Hooks:**
  * A data breach threatens Sebastian's ledgers—rival clans are trying to steal his files, and he needs help protecting them before the information falls into the wrong hands.
  * Sebastian needs to make a reconnaissance trip to New York—PCs could accompany him, providing security or helping navigate the politics of the city.
  * The twins are forced apart, creating a rare moment of vulnerability—PCs might need to protect Sebastian when he's separated from Sabine, or exploit the opportunity to access his ledgers.
  * Someone plants false data in Sebastian's ledgers—he needs help identifying and correcting the misinformation before it corrupts his predictive models.
  * PCs need to barter for access to Sebastian's ledgers—this could be a social challenge, a test of loyalty, or a way to gain valuable information about Kindred politics.
  * Sebastian's predictive models reveal something dangerous about the future—he must decide whether to reveal it or protect the Primogen's plans.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "In Phoenix, every number tells a story, and every ledger is a weapon—but even the most precise calculations can be disrupted by variables you didn't account for."
