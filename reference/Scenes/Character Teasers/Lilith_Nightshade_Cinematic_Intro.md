# Cinematic Intro: Lilith Nightshade – *“Porcelain Visions”*

## [INT. ELYSIUM – MAIN SALON – NIGHT]

Warm desert amber light spills across marble and velvet. Kindred mingle beneath crystal chandeliers, their murmurs a low, predatory hush. At the top of the stairs, a figure appears: a woman in a lavender 1890s gown, black lace parasol folded at her side, eyes reflecting the room like glass.

The crowd parts almost unconsciously as **LILITH NIGHTSHADE** begins to descend.

> **Whispered Kindred (VO, overlapping):** “Don’t look at her… just don’t meet her eyes when she’s like that…”

As she reaches the floor, the sound dulls, as if someone put a blanket over the world. The music fades to a faint, warped echo.

---

## [INT. ELYSIUM – SALON FLOOR – CONTINUOUS]

Close on her gloved hand brushing the bannister: delicate, controlled, trembling just enough to betray strain. The violet ghost of Guerlain’s “Jicky” hangs in the air as she moves through the salon.

She smiles politely, inclines her head to a Ventrue elder, but her gaze is *elsewhere*, sliding past him, through him, eyes focusing on a corner of the room where no one stands.

**[VO – LILITH]:** *“You are all so loud when you think you’re being quiet.”*

Her pupils dilate suddenly. The sound rushes back — but distorted.

---

## [FLASH-CUT – VISION – UNKNOWN POV]

A pair of hands, not hers, tighten on a crystal goblet. Blood swirls inside like storm clouds. A voice screams, distant, muffled. The viewpoint jerks toward a shadowed figure across a ballroom, hand slipping into a jacket.

Glass shatters. Red everywhere.

---

## [INT. ELYSIUM – SALON FLOOR – NIGHT]

Back in the salon, Lilith staggers as if struck. Conversations freeze. She straightens like a marionette pulled taut and **fixes her eyes on someone in the crowd** — a young Kindred who suddenly realizes she is looking at them the way a surgeon looks at an X-ray.

> **Lilith:** “I’ve seen through your eyes already. You just haven’t gotten there yet.”

The neon city beyond the windows flickers, once, in eerie unison.

She glides past frightened onlookers, moving toward her unseen target, the room bending around her like she is the center of gravity. No one dares step in her path.

## [CUT TO:]

## [INT. SHADOWED CORRIDOR – ELYSIUM – LATER]

Lilith stands alone beneath a dead light fixture, lavender gown painted in stripes of shadow. She closes her eyes, exhaling slowly.

**[VO – LILITH]:** *“The future is rude. It never knocks.”*

A faint chorus of whispers rises — overlapping, unintelligible, like distant radio stations bleeding together. For a heartbeat, her face cracks with terror.

Then she smooths her expression back into porcelain calm, opens her parasol, and steps into the dark.

## [FADE TO BLACK]

**VALLEY BY NIGHT – THE PORCELAIN ORACLE**

## Scene Card (RPG / Tabletop)

Lilith Nightshade arrives at Elysium, dressed in her immaculate lavender Victorian gown and carrying her lace parasol. The crowd instinctively parts for her. At first she is the picture of calm Primogen etiquette, but a sudden vision overtakes her — she freezes, then moves with frightening purpose toward a specific Kindred, locking eyes with them and murmuring that she has already seen through their perspective in the future. The Court goes silent. Later, alone in a dark corridor, she quietly admits in voice-over that the future never asks permission before forcing itself upon her.

---

## GM Notes

- **Purpose:** Introduce Lilith as both **social powerhouse** and **terrifying oracle**.  
- Emphasize her contrast: elegant calm vs. predatory focus during visions.  
- The **vision POV** can foreshadow a major chronicle event.  
- PCs caught by her gaze should feel “story-marked,” for good or ill.
