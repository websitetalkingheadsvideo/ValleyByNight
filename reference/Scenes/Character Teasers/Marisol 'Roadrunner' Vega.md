## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Safe Trails*

**Setting:**
Marisol's haven in an abandoned maintenance shed off I-17, late night. The space is functional, survival-focused—weapons, maps, cached supplies organized with the precision of someone who's learned to live in dangerous territory. The air is thick with the scent of desert dust, old blood, and something else: the chill of supernatural awareness.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The haven feels *alive*—not in the way a normal building feels alive, but in the way a survivalist's bunker feels alive. Marisol moves through it like a predator, her lean frame and hawk-bright eyes marking her as someone who's learned to read terrain like scripture, to track danger like prey, to map safe trails that only she can see.

She was a long-haul driver on the Vegas–Phoenix route before her Embrace—someone who knew the desert roads, who understood the isolation, who learned to read signs that others missed. When she was Embraced at a rest stop by an independent Gangrel, that knowledge became supernatural.

Now she serves as tracker and hunter for the Valley Watch coterie, mapping the supernatural "safe trails" around the Valley and keeping the coterie alive. She can cross the wastelands around Phoenix without vehicles, reads sign like scripture, and knows where the things that hunt Kindred won't find them.

Her goal is clear: map the safe trails, read the sign, keep the Valley Watch alive. She knows Garou patrol patterns anecdotally, leaves coterie sigils on mile markers, maintains alliances with Rafael "Switchback" Ortiz (Brujah sparring partner) and Tahlia Vance (Toreador coterie-mate who bankrolls gear).

But beneath the survivalist exterior lies someone who's lost everything—her mortal life, her route, her normalcy—and found purpose in keeping others alive. Her haven is her sanctuary, her maps are her weapon, and her knowledge of the desert is what keeps the Valley Watch from becoming just another group of corpses in the wasteland.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Marisol's Gangrel abilities—likely Protean for shapeshifting and desert survival, Fortitude for endurance, and Animalism for communication with desert predators. Her experience as a long-haul driver and tracker makes her an expert at reading terrain and mapping safe routes.

* **Tone:** Practical, survival-focused, protective. Marisol approaches her role as tracker with the precision of someone who's learned to live in dangerous territory. She doesn't see herself as a hero; she sees herself as someone who keeps the Valley Watch alive.

* **First impression:** Players see a Gangrel who's both practical and protective—a desert tracker who maps safe trails, reads sign like scripture, and keeps her coterie alive in dangerous territory. Her survivalist focus makes her valuable, but her isolation makes her vulnerable.

* **Hooks:**
  * Garou patrol intensifies—the safe trails are becoming dangerous, forcing Marisol to find new routes or confront the Garou directly.
  * A coterie member goes missing—Marisol must track them through dangerous territory, using her knowledge of the desert and supernatural dangers.
  * Her haven is discovered—perhaps by Garou, Sabbat, or other threats, forcing the Valley Watch to relocate and Marisol to map new safe trails.
  * She leads PCs through dangerous territory—using her knowledge of safe trails, Garou patrols, and supernatural dangers to guide them across the wasteland.
  * Her past catches up with her—perhaps her sire returns, or someone from her mortal life discovers what she's become.

* **Plot foreshadowing:** "Marisol knows every safe trail around the Valley. But when the trails become dangerous and the things that hunt us start hunting *us*, even the best tracker becomes prey."
