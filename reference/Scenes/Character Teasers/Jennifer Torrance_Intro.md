🎞️ Opening Cutscene: “The Woman Who Keeps the Lights On”

[INT. – CORPORATE OFFICE – NIGHT]

The office is quiet in the way only after-hours buildings are — too quiet.
Fluorescent lights hum softly overhead. Rows of empty desks glow with reflected amber and gray.

Jennifer Torrance sits alone at her workstation.

Her blazer is folded neatly over the back of her chair. Sleeves rolled up.
A yellow legal pad rests beside her keyboard, filled with tight, precise handwriting.

She types.
Stops.
Cross-checks a number.
Makes a single correction.

The clock on the wall clicks to 11:47 PM.

She doesn’t look tired.

A folder sits open on her desk — expense reports, property deeds, a handwritten note clipped inside.
One name appears more than once.

She closes the folder carefully.

Jennifer: “Everything balances. It always does.”

She gathers the papers into a slim leather portfolio, aligns the edges with practiced precision, and turns off her desk lamp.

The hallway outside is long, dim, impersonal. Her heels echo once… twice… then stop.

At the end of the hall, an office door stands open. Inside: shadows, expensive furniture, and the faint scent of old cologne.

She pauses at the threshold — respectful, composed.

[A beat.]

She steps inside.

[CUT TO:]

[INT. – PARKING GARAGE – NIGHT]

Jennifer’s car door closes with a soft, final click.
The engine turns over smoothly.

She checks her rearview mirror — not out of fear, but habit.

Her reflection looks calm. Controlled.

The garage lights flicker once as she pulls away.

[VOICE OVER – JENNIFER]:
“Some people think power is loud. Messy. Violent.”
“They never notice the people who make sure the doors stay open… and the bodies stay hidden.”
