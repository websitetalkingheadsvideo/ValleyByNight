# ?? *Opening Cutscene: “The Detective Walks In”*

**[EXT. HAWTHORN ESTATE – SIDE ENTRANCE – NIGHT]**

The moon hangs over Phoenix like a tired bulb in a motel lot.  
Crickets, wind, and the hum of a distant highway are the soundtrack of the desert’s insomnia.  
A side door opens — just a slice of gold spilling into shadow.  

**Eddy Valiant** steps through, fedora first, trench coat hanging heavy in the heat. The kind of man who brings rain to a dry town — metaphorically, at least.  
He shuts the door behind him and lets the party’s murmur crawl down the hallway toward him.

**[VO – EDDY]:** *“I slip in the side door like a thief in the night. But I ain’t here for silverware — I’m here for answers.”*

He moves down the corridor — portraits stare back from the walls, all judging eyes and dead smiles. A servant passes, balancing a tray of crystal glasses. Eddy tips his hat, and the man looks away, the way people do when they see something that doesn’t fit the picture.

**[VO – EDDY]:** *“These blueblood soirées always smell the same — money, perfume, and a dash of old blood. Some call it refinement. I call it camouflage.”*

---

**[INT. HAWTHORN ESTATE – MAIN HALL – CONTINUOUS]**

The ballroom opens like a cathedral of indulgence — chandeliers dripping with light, velvet shadows clinging to the edges.  
Music swells: slow jazz, too smooth to be honest.

Eddy steps in, adjusting his tie that’s never quite straight.  
He’s out of place — and he knows it. That’s the point.

**[VO – EDDY]:** *“They say the city never sleeps. They don’t mention it’s because of the monsters.”*

Across the room, **Sarah Hansen** glides under a spotlight — crimson satin, microphone in hand, voice cutting through the hush.  
Her gaze meets his mid-song.  
He gives a lazy salute with two fingers. She answers with a smirk that could buy or sell your soul.

**[VO – EDDY]:** *“The songbird looks sharp tonight. Probably a dagger somewhere under all that silk. Gotta respect a dame who smiles while she plots.”*

He moves toward the bar, brushing past a Brujah in a tailored jacket who offers a cigarette. Eddy accepts, lights it with a practiced flick, and takes a drag he doesn’t need.

**[VO – EDDY]:** *“You can always count on the Brujah for fire. Never could tell if it’s passion or just the urge to burn something.”*

He exhales smoke, eyes scanning the room until they land on the **Seneschal** — cold, poised, unreadable.  
Eddy gives a single, respectful nod.

**[VO – EDDY]:** *“The help runs this town. They just don’t print it on the invitations.”*

He reaches the bar, leans on it, eyes the reflection of the crowd in the mirror.  
The music drops to a single note, stretched and lonely.

**[VO – EDDY]:** *“The night was young, the blood was fresh, and the city was already guilty. Somebody just hadn’t confessed yet.”*

**[CUT TO BLACK]**

**VALLEY BY NIGHT – CHAPTER ONE: THE PRINCE IS DEAD**

## 🎭 *Scene Card: “The Detective Walks In”*

**Setting:**  
Hawthorn Estate, the night of the Prince’s gathering.  
The Camarilla elite murmur under golden light. Smoke, silk, and tension fill the air.

**Description (for players):**  
A side door opens. A heavyset man in a wrinkled tan suit and fedora slips inside like a shadow pretending to be polite.  
He moves with the tired ease of someone who’s seen too much — and expects worse.  
When he looks your way, his eyes don’t linger; they *weigh* you.  

There’s a faint, impossible echo — like narration bleeding into the room: *“I slip in the side door like a thief in the night…”*  
Then it’s gone, leaving only cigarette smoke and the faint sound of jazz.

He nods to Sarah Hansen, pockets a cigarette from one of the Brujah, and acknowledges the Seneschal with a detective’s quiet respect.  
It’s not grand entrance — it’s a statement:  
someone in this room still remembers what *truth* feels like.

---

## **GM Notes**

- **Disciplines:**  
  *Auspex* (reading the room), *Obfuscate* (presence that feels smaller than it is), *Dementation* (insight as madness).  
- **Tone:**  
  Noir, introspective, coolly self-aware. Every word he speaks sounds half like a confession, half like narration.  
- **Player Hooks:**  
  - PCs who approach Eddy can roll **Perception + Empathy** to notice he sometimes mutters lines that sound *too relevant* to be coincidence.  
  - Toreador or Ventrue may recall old rumors — a detective hired once by the court, whose mind “sees the script before the actors.”  
- **Mood:**  
  Isolation and intuition in equal measure. He’s not the hero — he’s the narrator who might outlive them all.
