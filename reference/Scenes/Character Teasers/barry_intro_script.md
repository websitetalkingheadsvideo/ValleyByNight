# "Shadow Play" - Barry Horowitz Introduction Scene
## Full Cinematic Script

---

**FADE IN:**

**EXT. PHOENIX RESIDENTIAL STREET - NIGHT - 2:47 AM**

*Establishing shot: Quiet neighborhood west of Camelback Mountain. Modest single-story homes, older construction, working-class area. Streetlights cast pools of amber light at irregular intervals. Mountains visible as dark silhouettes against slightly lighter night sky.*

*One streetlight flickers briefly, then steadies.*

**SOUND**: Distant traffic from I-17. A dog barking three blocks over. The hum of the streetlight's ballast.

---

**ANGLE ON: BARRY HOROWITZ**

*Walking down the sidewalk toward camera. Mid-to-late 30s, thin build, wearing khaki pants and a slightly wrinkled polo shirt. Carries worn leather briefcase. He looks tired but relaxed - the most at ease we'll ever see him.*

*He's muttering to himself, practicing. Not crazy-muttering - studying-muttering.*

**BARRY**  
(quiet, to himself)  
"Heightened Senses, that's the first level... Rembrandt said focus on one sense at a time, don't try to amp everything at once..."

*He stops under a streetlight, sets down his briefcase. Closes his eyes. Concentrates.*

**BARRY** *(CONT'D)*  
(whisper)  
"Okay... hearing first..."

*SOUND DESIGN: The ambient noise grows louder, more distinct. We hear what Barry hears - the dog barking becomes crystal clear, individual cars distinguishable on the freeway, a television playing inside a nearby house, someone's refrigerator humming, a cat walking across gravel two houses down.*

*Barry's eyes snap open. He smiles - small victory.*

**BARRY** *(CONT'D)*  
(pleased with himself)  
"Getting better."

*He picks up his briefcase, continues walking. More confident now.*

**BARRY** *(CONT'D)*  
(practicing introduction)  
"Hi, I'm Barry Horowitz, Rembrandt's student. No, too formal... Hey, Barry Horowitz. I'm... I'm working with Rembrandt Jones..."

*He shakes his head, dissatisfied with the phrasing.*

**BARRY** *(CONT'D)*  
(softer, honest)  
"I'm just grateful to be here."

*Beat. He believes that.*

---

**ANGLE: SHADOWS BEHIND HIM**

*Movement in the darkness between streetlights. Two figures, staying just out of the light. Following.*

**SOUND**: Footsteps matching Barry's pace. He doesn't notice yet.

---

**BACK TO BARRY**

*He's approaching his apartment building - a small, 1970s-era two-story complex. Needs paint. He's digging for his keys.*

**VOICE (O.S.)**  
(sharp, hostile)  
"Hey. Caitiff."

*Barry freezes. His whole body tenses. He turns slowly.*

---

**REVEAL: TWO KINDRED**

*Both male, early 20s apparent age, leather jackets, aggressive posture. One tall and angular (DEREK), one stockier with a shaved head (MARCUS). They step into the streetlight's edge, still mostly in shadow.*

*CRITICAL DETAIL: They're not Sabbat - their clothing and demeanor suggests Anarch or just asshole neonates looking for someone weaker. This is bullying, not assassination.*

**DEREK**  
(mocking)  
"That's you, right? The shovelhead the Prince kept like a stray dog?"

**BARRY**  
(careful, non-threatening)  
"I don't know you. I don't want trouble."

*He takes a small step back, calculating distances. His briefcase held slightly in front of him - minimal protection, symbolic barrier.*

**MARCUS**  
(stepping closer, circling)  
"Trouble? Nah, man. We're just curious. What makes you so special? Why'd Mansfield waste mercy on clanless trash?"

**BARRY**  
(swallows hard, trying diplomacy)  
"Look, I'm just trying to get home. We don't have to—"

**DEREK**  
(cuts him off, aggressive)  
"You don't get it, do you? You shouldn't BE here. No clan, no sire, no lineage. You're nobody."

*Derek shoves Barry. Not hard enough to knock him down, but degrading. Testing. Barry stumbles, catches himself.*

**BARRY**  
(voice shaking slightly)  
"Please. I don't—"

**MARCUS**  
(laughing, cruel)  
"Listen to him. 'Please.' Like begging helps."

*Marcus shoves him harder. Barry's briefcase falls. Papers scatter - industrial equipment catalogs, notes in Rembrandt's handwriting about Auspex techniques.*

---

**TIGHT ON: BARRY'S FACE**

*Fear. But also calculation. His salesman brain working: These aren't professionals. They're bullies. Bullies escalate when they get a reaction. De-escalate. Find common ground. Make them see you as human—*

*Derek kicks the briefcase. Papers flutter.*

**DEREK**  
"What's this shit? You still playing human? Pretending you're a traveling salesman?"

*Something shifts in Barry's expression. That hit different. That was his life. His identity.*

**BARRY**  
(quieter, harder edge)  
"Give me my briefcase."

**MARCUS**  
(mock surprise)  
"Oh! He's got some spine! Did you hear that, Derek? The clanless nobody wants his briefcase."

*Marcus picks it up, examines it.*

**MARCUS** *(CONT'D)*  
(theatrical)  
"This thing's a piece of shit. When'd you get this, 1982?"

*He tosses it to Derek. Keep-away game. Barry's jaw clenches.*

---

**WIDER ANGLE**

*Barry is now backed against the stucco wall of his apartment building. The two Kindred flanking him, playing with his briefcase. Classic intimidation setup.*

**BARRY**  
(controlled, but strain showing)  
"Just... give it back. Walk away. Nobody has to know about this."

**DEREK**  
(stepping very close, invading space)  
"Nobody has to know? You gonna tell teacher? Gonna run crying to your Toreador babysitter?"

*He pokes Barry's chest. Hard.*

**DEREK** *(CONT'D)*  
"You're pathetic. The Prince should've done you a favor and—"

*He shoves Barry hard against the wall. Barry's head hits the stucco. Not enough to injure a vampire, but painful, humiliating.*

---

**EXTREME CLOSE-UP: BARRY'S EYES**

*Fear. Anger. Humiliation. And then... something else. Something darker sliding across his expression. A decision being made.*

**SOUND DESIGN**: The ambient noise drops away except for heartbeats (his attackers' stolen blood), breathing, the electrical hum of the streetlight above.

---

**BARRY'S POV: THE SHADOWS**

*Everything has shadows under the streetlight. Long, angular shadows cast by the Kindred, by the building, by a parked car. And Barry can FEEL them. Like invisible strings attached to his fingers. Like they're waiting for permission to move.*

---

**BACK TO SCENE**

*Marcus is saying something - we don't hear it clearly. Barry isn't listening anymore. His hands are shaking, but not entirely from fear now.*

**BARRY**  
(so quiet, almost to himself)  
"I said... I don't want trouble."

*Marcus laughs, doesn't notice the change in Barry's tone.*

**MARCUS**  
"Then you should've stayed buried, shovel—"

---

**THE MOMENT**

*Barry's eyes go completely black. Not red frenzy - BLACK. Ink-dark. Void-dark.*

*The shadows move.*

*It's subtle at first - the shadow of the streetlight pole stretches. Wrong direction. Against the light source. It reaches across the pavement like a living tendril.*

*Derek notices first. He's looking down.*

**DEREK**  
(confused)  
"What the—"

*His shadow is moving independent of his body. Stretching, darkening, pooling around his feet like oil. Marcus's shadow does the same. Both shadows begin crawling up their legs.*

---

**ANGLE: MARCUS**

*He looks down. Sees his own shadow wrapping around his ankle like a serpent. Tries to step back. Can't. It's holding him.*

**MARCUS**  
(genuine fear now)  
"What the FUCK—"

---

**ANGLE: BARRY**

*Still pressed against the wall, but he's not cowering anymore. His hands are slightly raised, fingers trembling. The shadows respond to those tremors - twitching, writhing. His eyes are solid black pools.*

*The shadow under the streetlight itself begins to darken impossibly, becoming a pool of absolute darkness. Unnatural. Wrong. It spreads toward the two Kindred like spilled ink.*

**BARRY**  
(voice different - hollow, echoing slightly)  
"I. Don't. Want. Trouble."

*The shadows crawl up Marcus and Derek's legs to their knees. Cold. Invasive. They can FEEL it - like ice water, like drowning in darkness.*

---

**DEREK**  
(panicking, backing away)  
"Jesus Christ, what IS that?!"

*He breaks free, stumbling backward out of the light pool. Marcus follows, both of them scrambling away from Barry, from the writhing shadows.*

**MARCUS**  
(pointing, backing away)  
"You— you've got— that's fucking LASOMBRA shit! You're Sabbat!"

**BARRY**  
(automatic denial, shadows still moving)  
"I'm not—"

**DEREK**  
(to Marcus, terrified)  
"Fuck this, man, let's go!"

*They run. Actual running, fear overriding vampire pride. Their footsteps echo, then fade.*

---

**SILENCE**

*Barry stands alone under the streetlight. The shadows still writhe around his feet, up the wall behind him, pooling in corners. His briefcase lies on the ground, papers scattered.*

*His hands are still raised, trembling.*

**SOUND**: Just the streetlight hum. His ragged breathing. A car passing two streets over.

---

**SLOW PUSH IN: BARRY'S FACE**

*The black fades from his eyes slowly, like ink dissolving in water. Brown returns. Human-looking returns. But his expression is complex - fear, shock, and something else. Awe? Fascination?*

*He lowers his hands carefully. The shadows respond, slowly settling back to normal. But they move reluctantly, like they don't want to return to just being shadows.*

**BARRY**  
(whisper, shaking)  
"Shit. Shit. Shit."

*He spins around, checking windows. Did anyone see? Are there witnesses? His whole body is tense with panic now.*

---

**CAMERA PULLS BACK**

*Barry drops to his knees, gathering the scattered papers frantically. Shoving them back in the briefcase. His hands won't stop shaking.*

**BARRY** *(CONT'D)*  
(to himself, frantic)  
"Nobody can know. Nobody can— Christ, what if someone saw—"

*He gets the papers gathered, stands with the briefcase clutched to his chest. Looks around one more time.*

*The shadows under the streetlight are normal now. Just regular shadows. Almost.*

---

**TIGHT ON: THE STREETLIGHT SHADOW**

*VERY subtle - barely perceptible - the shadow of the light pole shifts slightly. Independent. Just for a second. Then still.*

---

**BARRY**

*Backs toward his apartment door, fumbling for keys with shaking hands. Gets the door open. Steps inside.*

*Before he closes the door, he looks back at the street one more time. His face: guilt, fear, relief he survived.*

*Door closes.*

---

**INT. BARRY'S APARTMENT - CONTINUOUS**

*Small studio. Modest furniture - Ikea-level, nothing fancy. A few boxes still unpacked from moving in. Rembrandt's LED sculpture sits on a table (questionable artistic merit but technically impressive - pulsing colors). Photos on the wall: highway scenes, desert sunsets, roadside diners - his old life.*

*Barry leans against the door, slides down to sitting. Briefcase in lap. He's coming down from adrenaline, starting to process what just happened.*

**BARRY**  
(whisper, overwhelmed)  
"Holy shit. Holy shit, that actually worked."

*Beat. He looks at his hands. Turns them over, examining them like they belong to someone else.*

---

**ANGLE: HIS HANDS IN LAMPLIGHT**

*Normal hands. Thin fingers, calluses from fifteen years gripping steering wheels and carrying equipment cases. Nothing special about them.*

*Barry flexes his fingers experimentally.*

*The lamp's shadow on the wall behind him stretches. Just slightly. Just for a moment. Like it's testing, seeing if he'll call it again.*

*Barry notices. His breath catches.*

---

**CLOSE-UP: BARRY'S FACE**

*Fear wars with something else. Curiosity? Wonder? He's staring at the shadow on the wall like it's the most fascinating and terrifying thing he's ever seen.*

**BARRY** *(CONT'D)*  
(barely audible)  
"What am I?"

*The shadow on the wall shifts - not much, just enough to be unnatural. Like it's responding to the question. Like it knows something he doesn't.*

*Barry's expression: fear, fascination, confusion. A man who just discovered something powerful and dangerous inside himself. Something he doesn't understand. Something he can't tell anyone about.*

---

**PUSH IN SLOWLY ON HIS EYES**

*Brown eyes reflecting the lamplight. And in that reflection, just for a moment, darkness swirls. Then it's gone. Was it ever there?*

**BARRY** *(CONT'D)*  
(whisper)  
"Rembrandt can't know. Nobody can know."

*Beat.*

**BARRY** *(CONT'D)*  
(softer, almost to the shadows themselves)  
"But you're part of me, aren't you? You kept me alive."

*The shadow on the wall doesn't move. But the quality of the darkness seems to deepen, just perceptibly. Agreement? Understanding?*

*Barry closes his eyes, leans his head back against the door.*

**SOUND**: His breathing steadying. The hum of the refrigerator. Traffic outside. Normal sounds. Safe sounds.

*But underneath, almost subliminal: a whisper. Like wind through leaves. Like darkness breathing.*

---

**FADE TO BLACK**

*Hold the black screen for three seconds. Total silence.*

*Then, barely visible in the darkness: a tendril of deeper black moves across the frame. Shadow within shadow.*

*It curls, retreats, disappears.*

---

**TITLE CARD APPEARS**

**"BARRY HOROWITZ"**

*White text on black background. Clean, simple. The darkness around the letters seems slightly animate, but it could be a trick of the eye.*

**FADE OUT**

---

## END SCENE

## TECHNICAL NOTES

### LIGHTING
- Streetlight should be sodium vapor (amber/orange) for that classic Phoenix night look
- Interior lamplight warm tungsten to contrast with cold streetlight
- Shadow effects practical where possible (actual moving shadows via off-camera manipulation) supplemented with VFX
- Barry's eyes going black should be contact lenses + subtle VFX for the transition

### SOUND DESIGN
- Heavy use of ambient city sounds (Phoenix-specific: distant freeway, occasional police helicopter)
- Heightened Senses sequence should layer sounds, building complexity
- When shadows activate: low frequency rumble (felt more than heard), slight audio distortion
- The "darkness breathing" at end should be subliminal - barely at threshold of hearing

### PERFORMANCE NOTES FOR BARRY
- **Start**: Tired but content. Small victories (practicing Auspex). This is him at peace.
- **Confrontation**: Initial fear, attempting de-escalation. Salesman mode failing.
- **Transition**: The moment he decides to use Obtenebration should be visible - a choice, not loss of control
- **Power activation**: Physical stillness, but intensity in eyes and micro-expressions
- **Aftermath**: Adrenaline crash, paranoia, then creeping fascination with what he can do
- **Final moment**: Complex - fear, wonder, isolation, acceptance

### THEMATIC ELEMENTS
- Barry starts powerless, ends powerful but more isolated (power has costs)
- The briefcase represents his old identity (kicked around, disrespected)
- Shadows respond to emotion/will (extension of self, not separate entity)
- "What am I?" is the core question - identity, transformation, belonging
- Shows that even starting characters have agency and power when pushed

### PACING
- **Total runtime**: 4-5 minutes
- **Build slow** (walking, practicing) - 90 seconds
- **Confrontation escalates** quickly - 60 seconds
- **Shadow activation** - 45 seconds (let it breathe, be creepy)
- **Aftermath paranoia** - 30 seconds
- **Interior revelation** - 45 seconds
- **Denouement** - 30 seconds

---

## SCENE GOALS

This introduction establishes Barry as:
- Vulnerable yet dangerous
- Grateful yet powerful
- Human yet monstrous
- All the contradictions that make him compelling

It demonstrates to players that even starting characters with basic disciplines can be formidable when circumstances demand it, while also showing the political and personal costs of using dangerous powers.
