## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Price of Souls*

**Setting:**
The Giovanni financial office, downtown Phoenix. Late night. The air is thick with the scent of old paper, ink, and grave dust. Alessandro's workspace is immaculate, organized with obsessive precision—every document catalogued, every soul accounted for.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

The office feels *wrong*—not just the professional veneer of a financial liaison, but the way the air itself seems to hold the chill of graveyards. Alessandro Vescari moves with the precise, brittle grace of someone who's spent too long in death's company. His suit is immaculate, tailored, expensive—the uniform of a successful investment liaison. But his eyes hold something else: the obsessive focus of a predator who's finally found his prey.

Photographs cover the walls, each one a mortal who's signed contracts with the Giovanni. Each one marked with a red X—souls collected, debts paid, lives ended. But there's another wall, smaller, hidden behind a false panel: **Setites**. Roxanne Murphy. Marcus Webb. Jennifer Kwan. The entire hierarchy of The Hole, mapped out like a financial portfolio.

This isn't just business. This is *hunting*. Alessandro has spent months infiltrating Setite territory, using his investment liaison credentials as cover. He's tracked their corruption, documented their blasphemy-shrine, catalogued every soul they've touched. And now, he's ready to collect.

The Giovanni don't just trade in death—they *own* it. And Alessandro intends to show the Setites what happens when you traffic in souls without understanding the true price.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Alessandro's Necromancy abilities—likely Dominate for information gathering, Necromancy for communing with the dead, and Potence for physical capability despite his Fragile flaw. His obsessive research gives him extensive knowledge of Setite operations.

* **Tone:** Obsessive, arrogant, fragile. Alessandro believes he's superior to the Setites—more disciplined, more powerful, more *professional* in his necromancy. But his arrogance masks deep insecurity, and his obsessive focus makes him brittle and vulnerable.

* **First impression:** Players see a Giovanni who's both dangerous and fragile—an investment liaison using business as a cover for necromantic infiltration. His obsession with the Setites has consumed him, making him both brilliant and blind.

* **Hooks:**
  * Alessandro's infiltration of The Hole is about to come to a head. He needs allies—or he needs to be stopped before he reveals too much.
  * His fragile nature means he's vulnerable to manipulation. PCs could exploit his arrogance or his obsession.
  * If the Setites discover his infiltration, war erupts between the clans in Phoenix—something Alessandro might be too obsessed to care about.
  * The dead souls he's collected could become allies or enemies for PCs, depending on how they interact with Alessandro.
  * His necromantic knowledge could be invaluable—if PCs can convince him to share it, or if they can use his obsession against him.

* **Plot foreshadowing:** "The Giovanni understand death better than anyone. The Setites understand corruption. When these two forces collide, the price is paid in souls—and Alessandro has come to collect."
