## 🎞️ Scene Card: *The Gallery Master*

**Setting:**
Étienne Duvalier's private theatre/haven, late night. A space that serves as both artistic sanctuary and political staging ground. Sculptures spanning centuries, warm amber light, the scent of marble dust and old wood. Then Elysium, where he moves through the grand hall with perfect elegance.

---

### **Scene Description (for players):**

Étienne Duvalier is an immaculate French aristocrat frozen in timeless elegance—tailored midnight-black suits with subtle silk accents, silver cufflinks, and a pocket watch that hasn't ticked since the 19th century. His features are sculpted perfection, pale skin like polished marble, dark eyes that appraise art and Kindred with equal intensity.

He moves with the controlled grace of a master sculptor, every gesture deliberate and precise. His hands, though immaculate, bear the faintest traces of marble dust—a reminder that he still creates, still shapes beauty from raw material. In Elysium, he commands attention without raising his voice; in his private theatre, he sheds the mask to reveal the artist who sees mortals and Kindred alike as clay waiting for his vision.

He's been building his power base for centuries, positioning his childer and ghouls like chess pieces across the board. Sebastian and Sabine serve as his talons to Harpy Cordelia Fairchild, learning the intricate dance of Kindred politics while he builds alliances that will one day serve his greater vision. To outsiders, he is the refined Primogen who curates beauty; to those who know him, he is the strategist who treats the entire Camarilla as his gallery.

---

### **GM Notes:**

* **Disciplines in play:** Étienne's *Presence* is legendary, allowing him to command attention and shape social dynamics with subtle precision. His *Auspex* lets him read people like artworks, understanding their motivations and potential. His centuries of experience have made him a master of the social game, and his artistic vision extends to every aspect of his unlife.
* **Tone:** Elegant, refined, but with an underlying ruthlessness. Étienne is a perfectionist who sees the world as his gallery, and he's willing to do whatever it takes to create the masterpiece he envisions. His long-term scheme to place cultured power in New York shows his ambition extends far beyond Phoenix.
* **First impression:** Players see a Toreador Primogen who seems perfectly refined, a patron of the arts who supports beauty and culture. But there's something *off*—the way he appraises people like artworks, the way he positions his childer and ghouls like chess pieces, the way his private theatre reveals the true nature of his vision.
* **Hooks:**
  * Commission a sculpture that becomes a boon trap—Étienne might offer to create a piece for PCs, but the commission comes with strings attached that create obligations.
  * Uncover Étienne's true plans for New York—PCs might discover the full scope of his long-term scheme, revealing ambitions that go far beyond Phoenix.
  * Protect one of Étienne's "artworks" from rivals—Sebastian, Sabine, Lucien, or Sofia might be targeted, and Étienne needs help defending his collection.
  * Navigate the delicate politics of Étienne's curated salons—PCs might need to attend one of his events, requiring careful social maneuvering to avoid becoming pawns.
  * Seek patronage from Étienne—PCs might want his support for their own projects, but they'll need to understand what he expects in return.
  * Exploit Étienne's rivalry with other Primogen—his political conflicts create opportunities for PCs to gain leverage or choose sides.
* **Plot foreshadowing:** "In Phoenix, every piece has its place, and every masterpiece serves a purpose—but even the most skilled artist can't control every brushstroke in a painting that spans centuries."
