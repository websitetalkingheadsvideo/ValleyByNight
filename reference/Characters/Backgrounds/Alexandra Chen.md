# Alexandra Chen — History

Alexandra Chen entered the Kindred world the way the Ventrue prefer their mortals: competent, disciplined, and never asking the wrong questions.

In life, she had already proven herself in private security and corporate protection work. She understood discretion, loyalty, and the quiet violence of power exercised behind closed doors. When a high-ranking Ventrue in Phoenix hired her for private security, she performed so well that ghouling felt less like corruption and more like promotion.

The blood made her better. **Stronger. Sharper. Faster.**

She was never told why.

## The First Domitor — Ventrue

Her first regnant trained her to operate around Kindred politics without ever explaining them. Alexandra learned schedules, routes, etiquette, and enforcement. She knew when Elysium was held, where it took place, and how to move around it without being seen. She knew which downtown buildings fronted Ventrue havens, which entrances mattered, and which doors were never to be touched. She understood clan hierarchies the way a soldier understands rank — functionally, not philosophically.

From this Ventrue, she learned **Dominate** — the power to command with a word, to enforce obedience through presence alone. It felt like natural authority, earned through competence.

What she was never told was that her loyalty wasn't entirely her own.

The blood bond was hidden from her deliberately. As far as Alexandra knew, the vitae was a tool — a reward for competence. She believed her devotion was earned, not enforced.

## The Second Domitor — Brujah

Her first master fell to a rival's machinations. Alexandra was too valuable to discard, so she was transferred to a Brujah enforcer who appreciated her tactical mind and physical capabilities. This one was more direct, less subtle. Under their tutelage, she learned **Potence** — raw physical strength that made her a true force in close quarters.

The transition was seamless. She told herself she was adapting, not betraying. The blood bond made such rationalizations easy.

## The Third Domitor — Nosferatu

The Brujah's violent end came during a territory dispute. Alexandra found herself claimed by a Nosferatu information broker who recognized her value as both muscle and someone who could move in daylight. From this shadowy master, she learned **Obfuscate** — the art of moving unseen, of becoming invisible to mortal and Kindred eyes alike.

By now, she had served three masters. She had learned three different ways to exert power. She told herself she was accumulating skills, building independence. She did not yet understand that each bond layered upon the last, making her less free, not more.

## The Fourth Domitor — Gangrel

Her fourth and final regnant was a Gangrel, a survivor who operated on the fringes of Phoenix's Kindred society. This one was different from the others — less interested in politics, more focused on survival. They valued her accumulated experience, her knowledge of multiple clans' methods, her ability to operate across different social strata. From this master, she learned **Fortitude** — the resilience to endure, to survive what would break others, to weather storms both physical and social.

This Gangrel was different. They explained things. They showed her the edges of Kindred society, the cracks in the Masquerade, the places where ghouls could operate with relative autonomy. For the first time, Alexandra began to understand that she might have options beyond servitude.

Then everything collapsed in a single night.

## The Collapse

Her fourth domitor was destroyed in a coordinated hunter strike. Alexandra was incapacitated, captured alive, and interrogated. The hunters knew exactly what she was. They knew about ghouls, vitae, and leverage. They gave her a simple choice: cooperate, or die alongside the monsters she served.

She chose to live.

For a time, she worked with them — daylight logistics, surveillance, cleanup. She told herself she was adapting, not betraying. But as weeks passed, something gnawed at her. The strength she had relied on was fading. The clarity dulled. The world felt smaller. She realized she did not fear vampires as much as she feared becoming powerless again.

So she waited.

She learned how the hunters operated. Where they stored their prizes. How they restrained Kindred. When they were careless. And when the moment came, Alexandra turned on them with the same calm precision she had once used to protect her masters.

She kept what mattered.

Hidden away are staked vampires the hunters had captured — once a steady source of vitae, now dangerously close to running dry. Alexandra does not understand blood bonds, addiction, or enslavement. She only knows that without the blood, she will lose everything she worked to become: her strength, her speed, her invisibility, her resilience.

## The Crossroads

Now, around Day 10 of the chronicle, the clock is against her.

She refuses to return to the Ventrue. She knows too well what they do to useful people. Even the weakest among them, Warner Jefferson, represents a return to ownership — and if she targets him, it will be as prey, not patron.

She is watching. Measuring. Listening.

She knows the rhythms of Elysium. She knows who arrives alone, who is careless, who is new. And as the second Elysium of the chronicle approaches, Alexandra Chen stands at a crossroads:

- disappear a weak Kindred and survive
- gamble on an untested neonate as an equal
- or let the blood fade and face what comes after

She believes she is still choosing freely.

That belief may be the most dangerous thing about her.
