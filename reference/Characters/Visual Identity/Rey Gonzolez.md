Rey Gonzalez — Visual Identity (Canon)

Rey Gonzalez is depicted as a real, grounded presence, not a stylized character portrait. He should always read as a working-class man in his mid-40s, solid and heavy-set with functional strength rather than athletic definition. His body language conveys weight, stability, and patience — the kind that comes from years of responsibility, organizing, and knowing when to stand firm.

His face is lived-in, not idealized. Subtle fatigue, faint lines, and natural skin texture are essential. He should never look glamorous, youthful, or heroic in a traditional sense. His expression is typically calm, contained, and thoughtful, suggesting internal focus rather than reaction. Rey is rarely caught mid-emotion; intensity exists beneath the surface and is implied, not performed.

Rey is almost always shown in practical, everyday clothing — muted earth tones, polos, workwear, or casual professional attire worn out of habit. Nothing fashionable, nothing flashy. Clothing should look functional, slightly worn, and appropriate to a working environment.

Lighting is naturalistic and motivated, almost always nighttime interiors lit by practical sources (desk lamps, shop lights). One side of the face may fall into deeper shadow. Lighting prioritizes mood and realism over clarity; not all facial detail needs to be visible. Avoid evenly balanced portrait lighting or studio aesthetics.

Camera framing should feel observational, as if the viewer is standing quietly in the room. Eye-level or slightly imperfect framing is preferred. Rey should not feel posed or aware of the camera. He exists in the space whether the viewer is there or not.

Overall, Rey Gonzalez should feel like a real man carrying quiet authority, grounded in the physical world, with Brujah intensity present but restrained — a leader who doesn’t need to raise his voice to be dangerous.
