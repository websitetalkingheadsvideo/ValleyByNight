11111 Foreword
“We are the music-makers... And we are the dreamer of the dreams.”                           – Arthur O’Shaughnessy




















This book has been compiled from the
non-game info in the Daughter’s of
Cacophony Clanbook by White Wolf
Games.  The purpose of this book is to
enable interested players to be able to
play the less antagonistic (using the
term in its literal sense) Daughters.

This book is in no way intended to
replace any of White Wolves books, and
contains no game or story info.  It
does contain the history, organisation,
and general overview of the Bloodline
with a view to allowing players to
understand the Daughters,  Maybe even
roleplay them.  Any and all game
information that appears in the White
Wolf books stayed with the books, I
have compiled this book purely for my
own game, and have now decided to
place it Online where others can
access it.

The Daughters of Cacophony are the
property of White Wolf games.

Any and all legal dealings relating
to the Daughters of Cacophony should
be directed to them.








This book follows directly from the
Clanbook Daughters of Cacophony, and,
uncompressed comes in at slightly less
than half the size of the original
Clanbook.  Whilst efforts have been
made to correct spelling /
grammatical errors, they have not been
extensive or vital.  Some errors still
exist in the work, and overwhellming
changes have not been made.  The text
follows as faithfully as possible the
original work from which it was taken,
and, for that reason, this work should
not be considered flawless.

Comments can be directed to the
author of the original Clan  book, 
White Wolf Games,  or the author of
this book (who sadly did not create
this wonderful bloodline, but simply
appreaciates them).

Chapter One
The score of the Bloodline

“We are the music-makers... And we are
the dreamer of the dreams.”
Arthur O’Shaughnessy

We are the Daughters.  The sound of a
harp in the night, and the girl who
sings in the moonlight.
We are the Daughters.  The guitar, the
piano, the oboe and the voice, we play
them all, and more besides.
We are the Daughters.  The harmony to
your discord, the discord to your
harmony and the laughter behind your
face.
Call us the Sirens, for our song can
lure men to their deaths, Lilith for
our beauty, and the delight we take in
pain.  Call us the Muses, for we
inspire, call us the Voices of Heaven.
Call us Daughters, call us Saughters,
call us mad.  But never call us
silent.

Considering their relative youth, the
Daughters of Cacophony have a
significant presence in the world.
Unlike the Ventrue, or the Toreador,
they do not command the mortal world
through rulership, money, influence,
or sex.  Instead, they choose the
ephemeral ways to demonstrate their
existence.  For Daughters, most times
their first priority is finding a new
instrument, a new music to play, to
listen to, a new song to sing. 
Shortly followed by devoting their
undeath to music in all of it’s
forms, discovering ways to ensure that
music survives, and that music in some
form continues to be made somewhere on
this earth.
Some Daughters have never fully
accepted that they are Kindred.  There
are some who are indifferent to their
nightly condition, while others rail
against the Kindred state.  These last
could easily fall prey to the ravages
of their Beast even as they despise it.
But they don’t see themselves as
Kindred.  They view themselves as
something other than vampires.  They
know they don’t belong with Kindred
society, but are so obsessed with
their music that they can’t face
ending their existence.

Interesting as the Daughters are, they
are regarded by most of the other
Kindred with suspicion.  Some hold
other opinions, see them as little
more than a novelty, a curiosity; one
that might be of use when it comes to
their ritual magic and unusual
organization.  It is true that some
Daughters call for fear because of
their Devotions, but others are merely
lost souls who don’t really have
anywhere they belong.  Some belong,
however, and they should never be
forgotten, nor their relative strengths
because of their status, and no
neonate should be lulled into a false
sense of security by the sweetness of
their voices.

No one really knows where the Daughters
of Cacophony came from, although there
seems to be an element amongst them
who delightfully encourage the
theories.  Even their fellow Daughters
will find themselves confused at times
by seemingly contradictory statements
from other Daughters.  The result of
this is that the Daughters have
developed an aura of mystery and
confusion about them.  No one, it
seems, knows the truth, and those who
do know aren’t letting on.

The Mothers, for their part, haven’t
said.  Even to their own Daughters,
they give little information,
preferring instead to see how their
Childer react to confusion, and not a
little chaos.  Should a Daughter react
well to this lack of knowledge, she
will find that she will be given more
and more knowledge of the Daughters, 
and perhaps eventually, the Mothers
will tell her the truth of the
bloodline’s origins, or at least,
their version of it.

The Daughters themselves have long
decided that it is far more amusing
to let the stories circulate, and to
add to them themselves where possible.
This is more the childer and ancille of
the Bloodline than the Mothers
themselves.  But even the Mothers have
been known to add to the confusion.

Even in music circles, Daughters are
hardly known.  Most of the mortal
world sees them as simply another
minor girl band, or solo female
artist, or the occassional male who
doesn’t want to admit that he is
singing songs written by a Daughter.
For this reason, the Daughters tend to
use the male-fronts to spill their
songs into the world.

The Daughters themselves, as a general
rule (if any general rule can be said
to apply to them), are quirky,
mysterious, individualistic and
passionate.  Sometimes over passionate,
in fact.  Like the Toreador, they care
about their art.  Unlike the Toreador,
they care more about the emotional
content of their music than the
fineness and technical perfection of
their music.  In some ways they’re more
like the Brujah in this respect, but
they, again, differ from this clan
because they don’t have the same
desire for political change, they
would rather see emotional change,
see the world react in some way to
their music.

The Daughters, unlike some Kindred
clans and Bloodlines, see no problem
with breaking the Masquerade when it
comes to music.  They don’t do it
deliberately, and they don’t openly
announce that they are vampires.  They
do, however, tend to write music that
reflects their vampiric nature.  Since
most of the mortal populace doesn’t
believe in vampires, or simply sees
them as a sexual release of some kind,
the Daughters don’t really see
anything wrong in this.  After all,
they aren’t telling anyone that they
are vampires, they’re telling a story.
No one will believe it anyhow.

Mortals and the Daughters

“There is nothing wrong with being a
mortal.  Some of my best friends are
mortal.  The only real problem with
them is that they never do what you
want of them...”
Alexander, Lover of Music

The Daughters needs mortals.  They
know this, and they, on some level,
accept this.  Many amongst them prefer
not to break the Sixth Tradition, of
course, but there are still several of
young Daughters who refuse to accept
that they are vampires, and that they
need to feed.  These young Daughters
see the need to feed as something
dirty, wrong.  Many-recent Daughters
are  almost anorexic when it comes to
their feeding, taking as little blood
as possible, and going to some unusual
lengths to deceive themselves about
their nature.  It isn’t surprising
that a lot of these drop into Diaspora
quite early in their unlife.  Those
who have accepted their nature see
feeding as a necessary evil, yet see
no reason that they should smother
themselves in blood.

The Daughters are becoming more and
more inclined to ghouling their lovers.
It gives them some permanence to their
relationships, and they feel less
inclined to go into the black despair
of losing someone they know and care
about.  Sometimes the reverse is true,
and they must end up going into torpor
because the lover ends up with her,
unlife because of his Obeisance to his
mistress.
This is a pain they never choose to
experience, and sometimes, yet, it
happens.  This is especially the case
amongst those young Daughters who
believe they are still alive and human.
They rarely take the risks that others
do in terms of risking their ghouls /
lovers, and the punishment for them
taking the risk is a punishment for
the whole Bloodline.
There are some male Daughters. (In
fact, about 25% are male), and they
have to deal, as with all the kindred,
with the issue of gender and mortal
love.  Sometimes, a male Daughter will
ghoul his lover.  Sometimes he will
choose simply not to.
But where once his lover was his equal,
now there are strange power differentials
– how do you deal with knowing that,
for the most part, the person you love
comes to you because of his addiction ?
How do you deal with knowing that he
can never leave you ?

The Daughters are fully aware of the
issue of love and power.  They know
that they have power over their ghouls.
They know they have a different kind
of power over their herd.  They also
know that sometimes lovers, mortal or
Kindred, can act stupidly, and without
thought.  This can cause all kinds of
problems.  And in the end, the Daughter
has to deal with how she feels about
what they’ve done.  Often this causes
some young Daughters to simply refuse
to involve themselves in anything more
complicated than a one night stand.

Other Kindred and the Daughters

“They’re just another clan.  All right,
they’re a Bloodline.  It still amounts
to being pointless and er...”
Samuel
“You’d say that if you were stoned.
Which I’m not.”
Samuel (a little later)

The Daughters are rarely considered a
threat to anyone.  There are none of
the Daughters who can really deal with
all the horrors of their world, and
yet, they don’t make enemies as a
rule.  They make a lot of friends and
neutrals, but very few enemies. Those
that they do make are usually dangerous
enemies, but as a general rule, the
Daughters don’t go out of their way
to make enemies.

Because the Daughters are so few, most
clans don’t see them as anything
important.  They are considered instead
to be a curiosity, or a potential tool.
The Toreador and the Brujah, being
more closely tied to music than the
others, have more contact with the
Daughters than many of the other
clans.  The Tremere and the Giovanni
see them as potentially useful, while
the Setites are convinced that the
Daughters have some secret they could
use.  The Assamites aren’t sure about
the Daughters, and the Nosferatu and
Malkavians, being their odd little
selves, often see the Daughters as
strange, but not necessarily something
that they can’t use.

The Toreador, more so than any other
clan, are fascinated by the Daughters.
They see the Daughters at once as both
rivals and as allies.  The rivalry
comes because they both care deeply
about art and beauty, but they
approach it from such different angles.
The Toreador approach art with an eye
to technical perfection, and a desire
to hold on to beauty forever.  The
Daughters approach art, in this case
music, with an eye to how it makes the
listener feel.  They want to see their
audience weep, laugh, dance, scream
with rage.  They don’t care if the
music is technically perfect, as long
as it does something.
This difference in approach causes no
small amount of tension, but it also
gives the two groups common ground.
They both care about art.  They both
care deeply about how art affects the
world.  As a result, there are Toreador
and Daughters who form strong bonds,
for good or ill.

The Brujah see the Daughters as allies
in their struggle for change.  Music
has always been a powerful force, and
the Brujah revolutionaries are only
too well aware of this.  For them, the
Daughters are potentially powerful
allies, able to deliver the messages
of change and revolution in a way that
the Brujah themselves cannot always
manage.  Many Daughters are less than
interested in political revolution,
however, and the Brujah can get
frustrated with them for this reason.
Some Daughters, however, do become
heavily involved in the Brujah causes,
and become powerful voices for change.

The Tremere and the Giovanni are
interested in the Daughters for
different reasons.  The Tremere are
interested in their Devotions, and in
the way that their blood magic works.
They suspect that the Daughters have
some form of Blood Sorcery, or that
their Devotions are related to Thaumaturgy
in some way.  The Daughters themselves
are often less than interested in
sharing their secrets with the Tremere,
and see them as stuffy, overly
controlling, and lacking in passion.

The Giovanni, on the other hand, are
interested in the Daughters because
they see a potential for controlling
or influencing large numbers of mortals
through their music.  The Daughters,
being passionate about their music and
about not compromising it, often
resent this, and relationships between
the two groups can be strained.

The Setites, the Followers of Set, are
convinced that the Daughters are
hiding something.  They are certain
that the Daughters have some secret
that they could use, and they will go
to great lengths to try and uncover
it.  The Daughters, for their part,
are amused by this.  They enjoy teasing
the Setites with half truths, riddles,
and nonsense.

The Assamites are wary of the Daughters.
They aren’t sure if they’re a threat
or not, and they are aware that the
Daughters could, if they chose, be
dangerous.  They tend to keep an eye
on them, but they don’t, as a rule,
go out of their way to involve
themselves with the Daughters unless
there is a contract involved.

The Nosferatu and Malkavians are
perhaps the clans that the Daughters
understand the least, and vice versa.
The Nosferatu are, for the most part,
content to stay in the shadows, and
they don’t see much point in music.
Some of them do, however, appreciate
the emotional content of music, and
they can form strange alliances with
the Daughters.  The Malkavians, being
mad in their own ways, can sometimes
form odd friendships with the Daughters.
Sometimes they get along, sometimes
they don’t.  It all depends on the
individuals involved.

Chapter Two
The Music of the Night

“Sing, my angel of music...”
The Phantom of the Opera

The Daughters have a strange and
unusual relationship with music.  For
them, music is not just art, or
entertainment.  It is life.  It is
everything.  Without music, there is
nothing.  This is not an exaggeration
for most of the Daughters.  They mean
it.

Every Daughter has some form of music
in her background.  She might have
been a singer, a musician, a composer,
a conductor, or even simply someone
who loved music beyond all reason.
Whatever her mortal background, music
was at the core of who she was, and
that did not change when she was
Embraced.

The Embrace, for a Daughter, is often
wrapped up in music.  Sometimes she is
Embraced in the middle of a concert,
sometimes in a small smoky club,
sometimes in a private rehearsal room.
The circumstances vary, but the music
is always there.

Once she has been Embraced, the
Daughter finds that her relationship
with music has changed.  In some ways,
it is stronger.  She can hear more, 
feel more, understand more.  In other
ways, it is more painful.  Music can
remind her of the life she has lost.
It can drag up emotions that she would
rather not deal with.

But she cannot escape it.  Music is
what she is.

The Daughters are drawn to music in
all its forms.  Classical, rock,
folk, jazz, blues, industrial, metal,
electronic, ambient, opera, pop, even
country.  There is no genre of music
that at least one Daughter does not
love.  They can be snobs about music,
of course.  Some Daughters consider
certain genres beneath them.  Others
revel in the most obscure and
unappreciated forms of music they can
find.

The one thing they all agree on is
that music must be honest.  It must
come from the heart.  It must do
something to the listener.  If it
doesn’t, it’s just noise.

The Daughters are constantly seeking
new music.  They haunt record stores,
clubs, concerts, street performers.
They listen to buskers and symphonies,
garage bands and choirs.  They are
always listening, always searching for
that next sound, that next song that
will move them.

Some Daughters are performers.  They
take to the stage, the studio, the
street.  They sing, they play, they
write, they produce.  Others are
behind the scenes, writing songs for
others, producing records, managing
bands, or running clubs.  Some are
simply obsessive fans, following
bands, collecting records, tapes, CDs,
bootlegs, rare recordings, anything
they can find.

The Daughters often form little
subcultures within the Bloodline,
based on musical taste.  There are
the opera lovers, the goths, the punks,
the ravers, the jazz aficionados, the
folkies, the metalheads, the ambient
drifters, and so on.  These groups
can be tight-knit, and they can
squabble like any group of fans.
Arguments over bands, songs, genres,
and scenes can last for nights.

There are, however, some things that
unite the Daughters.  One of these is
the belief that music matters.  It’s
not just background noise.  It’s not
just something to dance to.  It’s
important.

Another is the belief that music can
change things.  It can change people,
change moods, change minds.  It can
even change the world, if enough
people listen.

The Daughters, as a result, often
become involved in using music to
influence mortals.  They write songs
that carry messages, sometimes subtle,
sometimes not.  They use lyrics to
sneak ideas into people’s heads.  They
use rhythm and melody to stir emotions.
They use concerts and clubs as places
to gather people together, to create a
sense of community.

Their Devotions, too, are tied up with
music.  They can use songs to inspire,
to command, to soothe, to terrify.
They can sing someone into a frenzy,
or into sleep.  They can use their
voices, their instruments, their
rhythms, their melodies, to work
wonders and horrors.

For the Daughters, every night is a
performance.  Even when they’re not on
stage, they are still aware of the
soundtrack of their lives.  The hum of
the city, the whispers of the wind,
the drip of water, the thud of bass
through a wall.  All of it is music,
if you listen the right way.

Chapter Three
Playing a Daughter

“So you want to be a Daughter of
Cacophony ?  Can you sing ?”
Cassandra, to a potential recruit

Playing a Daughter of Cacophony
requires a particular mindset.  You
are playing someone for whom music is
everything.  This is the first and
most important thing to remember.

Your character might be a former
opera singer, a punk guitarist, a
folk singer, a choir girl, a DJ, a
producer, a composer, a busker, or
just a desperate fan who lived and
breathed music.  Whatever her
background, music was at the core of
who she was.

After the Embrace, this did not
change.  If anything, it became more
intense.

When you are creating a Daughter, you
should think about:

* What kind of music does she love ?
* What kind of music does she hate ?
* Does she perform ?  If so, what ?
* Does she write music ?
* Does she play an instrument ?
* How does she feel when she can’t
  listen to music ?
* What was the most important song in
  her life ?
* Who are her musical heroes ?
* Does she still follow mortal music
  scenes ?  Which ones ?

You should also think about how she
feels about being a vampire.  Does she
accept it ?  Does she hate it ?  Does
she deny it ?  How does this affect
her music ?

Does she write songs about being a
vampire ?  Does she hide it in her
lyrics ?  Does she pretend she’s still
human ?  Does she sing about death,
about blood, about hunger ?  Or does
she sing about love, joy, sorrow,
anger, hope ?

Think about how she relates to other
Kindred.  Does she get along with the
Toreador ?  Does she sneer at them ?
Does she admire the Brujah ?  Does she
think the Tremere are boring ?  Does
she play for the Giovanni’s parties ?
Does she mock the Setites ?  Does she
secretly hang out with the Malkavians ?
Does she have a Nosferatu friend who
likes her music ?

Think about her mortal ties.  Does she
still have mortal friends ?  Lovers ?
Family ?  Fans ?  Does she keep in
touch with them ?  Does she use them ?
Does she protect them ?  Does she
ghoul anyone ?  Does she regret it ?

Daughters, as a rule, are passionate,
intense, and sometimes a little
unbalanced.  They can be funny, witty,
sarcastic, cruel, kind, gentle, harsh,
bitter, hopeful, cynical, romantic,
idealistic, pragmatic, or any
combination of these.  They are
individuals.  The one thing they all
have in common is their love of music.

When you play a Daughter, you should
try to bring music into your roleplay.
Quote lyrics.  Hum.  Tap rhythms.
Talk about bands.  Make playlists for
your character.  Think about what song
is playing in the background of each
scene.

Remember that, for your character,
there is always a soundtrack.  Even if
no one else can hear it.
