# Clanbook: Assamite in Phoenix

*Valley by Night — Phoenix, Arizona, 1994*

---

## Overview (Phoenix Lens)

Phoenix Assamites don't live in the city—they *work* in it. While other Kindred establish territories and build power bases, Assamites move through Phoenix like professionals on assignment, their presence temporary, their purpose clear, their methods precise. They don't seek to rule or control—they seek to judge, to execute, to fulfill contracts that serve their codes of justice and honor.

The desert city provides perfect anonymity. Phoenix's sprawl, its transient population, its isolation from major Kindred centers—these create conditions where Assamites can operate without drawing attention. The city's newness means there are no ancient grudges, no established surveillance networks, no accumulated knowledge of Assamite patterns. Every assignment feels like a fresh start, every contract like a clean slate.

The Prince's murder has changed everything and nothing. Assamites don't care who sits in the Prince's chair—they care about justice, about contracts, about fulfilling their role as judges and executioners of the Damned. But the murder has created demand for their services. Every faction wants someone eliminated, every clan wants someone judged, every power player wants someone removed. The contracts flow, and Assamites evaluate each one according to their codes.

## Territory & Feeding

### Primary Territories

**Various Safehouses (Temporary)**: Assamites maintain temporary safehouses throughout Phoenix, never staying in one location long enough to establish patterns. These safehouses serve as staging areas for contracts, places to prepare and plan, locations where they can operate without drawing attention. Feeding is carefully managed—each target selected with precision, each feeding executed with discipline.

**Desert Peripheries**: The desert surrounding Phoenix provides isolation that suits Assamite operations. Remote locations offer space for training, preparation, and discrete operations away from city surveillance. The harsh environment filters out casual observers, creating privacy for work that requires secrecy.

**Urban Transient Areas**: Areas with high transient populations—motels, truck stops, areas near highways—provide temporary bases where Assamites can blend in with other travelers. These spaces offer anonymity and access to prey who won't be missed.

**Contract Locations (Mobile)**: Assamites operate wherever contracts take them, never claiming permanent territory. They move through the city as needed, establishing temporary positions near targets, preparing for operations, then moving on when contracts are fulfilled.

### Feeding Risks & Advantages

**Advantages**:
- Transient nature makes feeding patterns difficult to track
- Desert areas provide isolation for discrete feeding
- Contract work provides access to specific targets who may serve as feeding opportunities
- Anonymity enables feeding without establishing recognizable patterns

**Risks**:
- Transient nature makes establishing secure feeding arrangements difficult
- Contract work requires careful feeding to avoid compromising operations
- Desert areas can be dangerous even for Assamites, with extreme isolation
- Anonymity requires constant movement, preventing establishment of reliable feeding sources

## Local Power Web

### Allies & Rivals

**Allies**:
- **Independent Clans**: Assamites maintain relationships with independent clans who don't require sect allegiance, creating flexible alliances when contracts align.
- **Nosferatu**: Mutual respect for competence and independence. Both clans operate on margins and value professional relationships.

**Rivals**:
- **Tremere**: Historical conflicts and curses create mutual hostility. Tremere fear Assamite assassins; Assamites resent Tremere thaumaturgical power and past betrayals.
- **Ventrue**: Conflicts arise when Ventrue attempt to control or restrict Assamite operations. Assamites view Ventrue authority as illegitimate; Ventrue view Assamites as dangerous threats.
- **Camarilla (Formal)**: Tense relationships due to historical treaties and restrictions. Camarilla tolerates Assamites but doesn't trust them; Assamites honor treaties but maintain independence.

**Institutions & Mortal Influence**:
- **Contract Networks**: Connections to those who need elimination services—Kindred and mortal
- **Safehouses**: Temporary locations that provide anonymity and operational security
- **Desert Territories**: Remote areas that offer isolation and privacy for training and operations

## Themes & Touchstones

### Phoenix-Specific Themes

**Judgment and Execution in a New World**: Phoenix is a young city without the centuries of established grudges and historical conflicts that define Assamite work in older domains. Every contract feels like a fresh evaluation, every judgment like a new precedent.

**The Desert as Sanctuary**: The desert surrounding Phoenix provides isolation that suits Assamite operations. Remote locations offer space for training, preparation, and discrete operations away from city surveillance. The harsh environment creates privacy for work that requires secrecy.

**Anonymity Through Transience**: Phoenix's transient population and sprawling geography enable Assamites to operate without establishing recognizable patterns. Movement becomes both operational necessity and protective strategy.

**Contracts in Chaos**: The Prince's murder has created demand for Assamite services, but evaluating contracts becomes more difficult when political structures are unstable. Assamites must navigate between fulfilling contracts and maintaining their codes of justice.

### Touchstones

- **Temporary Safehouses**: Staging areas for contracts, never permanent
- **Desert Peripheries**: Isolation and privacy for operations
- **Contract Documents**: Agreements that define work and obligations
- **Ritual Blades**: Tools cleaned with care, symbols of precision and honor
- **Ledgers of Names**: Records of contracts, debts, and judgments

## Chronicle Hooks (6-10)

### 1. The Contract Offer
PCs are approached with a contract offer—someone wants another Kindred eliminated, and Assamites are considering the job. The contract might be legitimate (target has violated codes), questionable (political assassination), or clearly wrong (innocent target). PCs must decide whether to help evaluate the contract, prevent the assassination, or become involved in other ways.

### 2. The Code Violation
An Assamite violates their codes—possibly killing without contract, breaking treaty restrictions, or committing an act that violates clan honor. Other Assamites must judge and potentially execute their clanmate, but the violation reveals deeper problems. PCs might be witnesses, might be asked to help judge, or might become involved in the fallout.

### 3. The Tremere Conflict
Tremere attempt to eliminate or restrict Assamite operations in Phoenix, using thaumaturgy and political pressure to drive Assamites out. Assamites respond with targeted eliminations, escalating the conflict. PCs must navigate between Tremere authority and Assamite independence.

### 4. The Treaty Question
Someone challenges the Treaty of Tyre or other restrictions on Assamite operations, arguing that current circumstances justify violating treaty terms. Assamites are divided—some want to honor treaties, others want to break restrictions. PCs must navigate the conflict or choose sides.

### 5. The Innocent Target
A contract targets someone who appears innocent or undeserving of death. Assamites evaluate the contract but disagree on whether the target deserves judgment. PCs might be asked to investigate, might be the target, or might need to prevent an unjust execution.

### 6. The Clan Judgment
Assamites judge a Kindred for violating codes that Assamites enforce—possibly violating the Masquerade, committing diablerie, or breaking other Kindred laws. The judgment requires execution, but other clans object. PCs must navigate between Assamite codes and political consequences.

### 7. The Desert Training
PCs encounter Assamites training in the desert, witnessing methods and techniques that reveal clan capabilities. The encounter might be accidental, might be intentional (recruitment attempt), or might lead to conflicts over secrecy. PCs must decide how to handle knowledge of Assamite operations.

### 8. The Contract War
Multiple contracts conflict, creating situations where fulfilling one contract means violating another. Assamites must choose which contracts to honor, which to refuse, and how to resolve conflicts without violating their codes. PCs might be involved in the contracts or might need to navigate the conflict.

## NPC Hooks & Seeds (at least 6)

### NPC Seed 1: The Executioner
- **Concept/Role**: Warrior caste assassin, contract fulfiller, clan enforcer
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Various temporary safehouses, mobile
- **Clan tie-in**: Assamite Warrior who fulfills contracts and enforces clan codes
- **Motivation**: Fulfill contracts honorably, enforce justice through elimination, maintain clan reputation for precision and honor
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether contracts serve justice or just power—worried that accepting contracts without proper evaluation compromises clan honor
- **Secret**: Has been secretly refusing contracts that violate his personal code of justice, but this violates clan discipline—if discovered, would face judgment
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides contract services but evaluates each contract carefully. Can offer elimination services but only for legitimate targets. Represents Assamite commitment to justice

### NPC Seed 2: The Jurist
- **Concept/Role**: Sorcerer caste ritualist, code enforcer, mystical law specialist
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Temporary safehouses, various locations
- **Clan tie-in**: Assamite Sorcerer who enforces codes through ritual and judgment
- **Motivation**: Enforce clan codes, maintain mystical law, provide judgment through ritual and curse
- **Conflict**: Torn between enforcing codes strictly and recognizing that circumstances sometimes require flexibility—worried that rigid enforcement causes injustice
- **Secret**: Has been secretly modifying judgments to account for circumstances, violating strict code enforcement—if discovered, would face clan discipline
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides ritual services and code enforcement. Can offer judgment and curse but evaluates each case carefully. Represents Assamite mystical law

### NPC Seed 3: The Advisor
- **Concept/Role**: Vizier caste diplomat, contract negotiator, strategic consultant
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Various locations, mobile, appears where contracts require negotiation
- **Clan tie-in**: Assamite Vizier who handles contracts and negotiations
- **Motivation**: Evaluate contracts, negotiate terms, ensure that clan work serves justice and honor
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether contract negotiation serves justice or just business—worried that accepting questionable contracts compromises clan principles
- **Secret**: Has been secretly rejecting contracts that don't serve justice, but this limits clan income—if discovered, would face questions about judgment
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides contract evaluation and negotiation. Can facilitate Assamite services but only for legitimate purposes. Represents Assamite commitment to honor

### NPC Seed 4: The Penitent
- **Concept/Role**: Warrior who questions contracts, code examiner, justice seeker
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Various locations, mobile, often in desert areas
- **Clan tie-in**: Assamite Warrior who struggles with clan codes and contract ethics
- **Motivation**: Fulfill contracts while maintaining personal honor, question whether contracts serve justice, seek to improve clan codes
- **Conflict**: Torn between clan loyalty and personal conscience—worried that following orders violates justice, that questioning orders violates loyalty
- **Secret**: Has been secretly investigating contracts before fulfilling them, trying to ensure targets deserve death—if discovered, would face accusations of disobedience
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides contract services but questions each assignment. Can offer elimination but only after careful evaluation. Represents tension between loyalty and conscience

### NPC Seed 5: The Traditionalist
- **Concept/Role**: Code enforcer, treaty maintainer, clan traditionalist
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Various locations, mobile, maintains clan traditions
- **Clan tie-in**: Assamite who strictly adheres to clan codes and treaties
- **Motivation**: Maintain clan traditions, enforce codes without exception, honor treaties and restrictions
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether strict enforcement serves justice—worried that inflexibility causes injustice, that tradition prevents necessary adaptation
- **Secret**: Has been secretly questioning whether strict code enforcement serves justice or just tradition—if discovered, would face accusations of heresy
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides strict code enforcement and treaty maintenance. Can offer services but only within strict limits. Represents Assamite traditionalism

### NPC Seed 6: The Reformer
- **Concept/Role**: Code reformer, contract evaluator, justice improver
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Various locations, mobile, seeks to improve clan codes
- **Clan tie-in**: Assamite who wants to reform clan codes and contract evaluation
- **Motivation**: Improve clan codes, reform contract evaluation, ensure that clan work serves justice more effectively
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether reform serves justice or just change—worried that challenging tradition violates clan loyalty, that maintaining tradition prevents improvement
- **Secret**: Has been secretly working with other clans to reform contract evaluation, but this violates clan independence—if discovered, would face accusations of betrayal
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides contract services but advocates for code reform. Can offer elimination but questions current evaluation methods. Represents possibility of clan improvement
