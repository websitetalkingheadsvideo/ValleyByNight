# Clanbook: Baali in Phoenix

*Valley by Night — Phoenix, Arizona, 1994*

---

## Overview (Phoenix Lens)

Phoenix Baali don't live in the city—they *corrupt* it from within. While other clans fight for visible power, Baali work through secrecy, infiltration, and the careful cultivation of dependency on forbidden knowledge and dark services. They don't need to hold titles or claim territories—they need to control corruption, to shape decay, to ensure that the city's vices serve entities greater than themselves. Phoenix is a young city without the centuries of established corruption networks that Baali navigate in older domains, but that newness creates opportunity. In a city where everything is still being built, Baali can establish foundations of corruption rather than inheriting existing structures.

The desert provides perfect isolation. Phoenix's sprawl, its isolation, its connection to primordial forces—these create conditions where Baali can operate without notice, where dark rituals can be performed, where corruption can flourish. The city's newness means there are no ancient patterns, no established knowledge, no accumulated understanding of Baali presence. Every operation feels like a fresh start, every corruption like a new opportunity.

The Prince's murder has changed everything. What was chaos for others is opportunity for Baali. Every faction needs forbidden services, dark knowledge, and corrupt influence. Baali provide all three, becoming essential to the city's dark functioning even as they remain completely hidden. Their corruption ensures their survival, but their secrecy ensures they'll never be exposed—or so they believe.

## Territory & Feeding

### Primary Territories

**Hidden Temples (Various Locations)**: Baali maintain hidden temples where they conduct rituals, perform sacrifices, and serve the sleeping entities they call the Children or Ba'al. These temples are disguised as legitimate businesses, private clubs, or abandoned spaces. They provide sanctuary, ritual space, and feeding opportunities among the initiated.

**Corruption Networks (Mobile)**: Baali operate through corruption networks that span the city, using mortal followers and corrupted Kindred to extend influence and provide cover. These networks offer access to vulnerable individuals, feeding opportunities, and resources that support Baali operations.

**Respectable Fronts (Various)**: Baali maintain respectable fronts—businesses, organizations, institutions—that provide cover and access to targets. These fronts appear legitimate but serve Baali purposes, providing feeding opportunities among those who trust the front.

**Sacred Spaces (Hidden)**: Baali maintain sacred spaces where they commune with sleeping entities, perform dark rituals, and conduct initiations. These spaces are carefully hidden, protected, and unknown to all but the initiated.

### Feeding Risks & Advantages

**Advantages**:
- Hidden temples provide access to the initiated and corrupted
- Corruption networks offer feeding opportunities among those who have been corrupted
- Respectable fronts provide access to targets who trust the front
- Sacred spaces offer discrete feeding among worshippers

**Risks**:
- Hidden operations must remain completely secret to avoid destruction
- Corruption networks create dependencies that can be exploited
- Respectable fronts require careful management to avoid exposure
- Sacred spaces must be protected from discovery

## Themes & Touchstones

### Phoenix-Specific Themes

**Corruption Through Secrecy in a New World**: Phoenix is a young city where Baali can establish foundations of corruption rather than inheriting existing structures. Dark networks can be built from scratch, cults can be founded, corruption can be cultivated without existing patterns to follow.

**The Desert as Sacred Space**: Phoenix's desert environment provides isolation where dark rituals can be performed without notice. The desert's connection to primordial forces resonates with Baali service to sleeping entities.

**Service to Greater Entities**: Baali believe they serve entities greater than themselves—the Children, Ba'al, sleeping horrors that predate humanity. In Phoenix, this service creates purpose and power, but also dependency and corruption.

**Respectability as Mask**: Baali maintain respectable fronts, appearing as priests, scholars, or nobles while secretly serving dark entities. In Phoenix, this respectability provides cover and access to targets who trust the front.

### Touchstones

- **Hidden Temples**: Sanctuaries where Baali worship and perform dark rituals
- **Corruption Networks**: Organizations that extend Baali influence
- **Respectable Fronts**: Legitimate-seeming businesses that provide cover
- **Sacred Spaces**: Locations where Baali commune with sleeping entities
- **The Children**: The sleeping entities Baali serve

## Chronicle Hooks (6-10)

### 1. The Corruption's Discovery
PCs discover Baali corruption within an organization or individual they trust. The discovery reveals Baali presence and influence, but exposing it requires evidence and careful planning. PCs must decide whether to expose the corruption or whether doing so creates greater risks.

### 2. The Ritual's Awakening
A Baali ritual threatens to awaken a sleeping entity or create other catastrophic consequences. The ritual must be stopped, but doing so requires understanding Baali methods and the entities they serve. PCs must prevent the awakening while dealing with Baali resistance.

### 3. The Front's Exposure
A Baali respectable front is exposed, revealing corruption hidden behind legitimate appearance. The exposure threatens to break the Masquerade or create other problems, but preventing exposure requires careful management. PCs must decide whether to help contain the exposure or whether the front is too dangerous.

### 4. The Cult's Influence
A Baali cult gains influence over important individuals or organizations in Phoenix. The cult's control threatens to corrupt city politics or break the Masquerade, but exposing it requires evidence that Baali have carefully hidden. PCs must discover and stop the corruption.

### 5. The Temptation's Offer
A Baali offers PCs forbidden knowledge, power, or services that create dependency and corruption. The offer is genuine, but accepting it creates obligations to dark entities. PCs must decide whether the temptation is worth the cost.

### 6. The Entity's Demand
A sleeping entity Baali serve makes demands that threaten to break the Masquerade or create other catastrophic consequences. The demands must be fulfilled or resisted, but either choice creates risks. PCs must navigate between serving dark entities and preventing catastrophe.

### 7. The Infiltration Revealed
PCs discover that Baali have infiltrated Kindred structures, using positions of power to advance Baali goals and serve dark entities. The infiltration threatens to corrupt city politics, but exposing it requires evidence and careful planning. PCs must discover and stop the infiltration.

### 8. The Corruption's Price
Someone corrupted by Baali influence faces transformation that threatens to break the Masquerade or create other problems. The corruption might be spiritual, physical, or both. PCs must decide whether to help the corrupted individual or whether the corruption is too far gone.
