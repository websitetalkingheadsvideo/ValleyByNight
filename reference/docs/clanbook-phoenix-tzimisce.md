# Clanbook: Tzimisce in Phoenix

*Valley by Night — Phoenix, Arizona, 1994*

---

## Overview (Phoenix Lens)

Phoenix Tzimisce don't live in the city—they *transform* it. While other Kindred establish territories and build power bases, Tzimisce reshape the world around them, using Vicissitude to mold flesh and bone, Koldunic Sorcery to command the land, and territorial dominance to claim ownership of everything within their domain. Phoenix is a young city without the centuries of established domains that Tzimisce claim in older regions, but that newness creates opportunity. In a city where everything is still being built, Tzimisce can shape foundations rather than rebuilding existing structures.

The desert provides unique challenges and opportunities. The harsh environment, the isolation, the connection to the land—these resonate with Tzimisce who practice Koldunic Sorcery. The desert's ancient power, its elemental spirits, its connection to primordial forces—these provide resources for Tzimisce who know how to commune with the land. But the desert is also hostile, unforgiving, and alien to Tzimisce who come from forested domains and mountain strongholds.

The Prince's murder has changed everything. What was once a stable domain is now chaos, and Tzimisce see opportunity in that chaos. While other clans fight for political control, Tzimisce seek territorial dominance—not to rule the city, but to own it, to reshape it, to transform it into something that serves their vision. They don't need titles or positions—they need land, territory, and the power to reshape everything within their domain.

## Territory & Feeding

### Primary Territories

**Desert Peripheries (Territorial Claims)**: Tzimisce claim desert territories on Phoenix's periphery, using Koldunic Sorcery to commune with the land and establish domains. These territories are harsh and isolated, but they provide space where Tzimisce can operate without interference. Feeding targets include desert dwellers, travelers, and those who venture into Tzimisce territory.

**Abandoned Developments (Transformed Spaces)**: Phoenix's abandoned developments provide spaces that Tzimisce can claim and transform. Using Vicissitude, Tzimisce reshape these spaces into works of art and horror, creating domains that reflect their vision. Feeding targets include squatters, transients, and those who enter Tzimisce territory.

**Underground Spaces (Hidden Domains)**: Tzimisce create underground domains—basements, tunnels, hidden spaces—where they can operate without notice. These spaces serve as laboratories, workshops, and sanctuaries where Tzimisce practice Vicissitude and pursue transformation. Feeding is carefully managed within these hidden domains.

**Territorial Markers (Claimed Areas)**: Tzimisce mark territory using Vicissitude-created guardians, Koldunic Sorcery-empowered landmarks, and transformed creatures that serve as warnings. These markers define Tzimisce domains and deter intruders.

### Feeding Risks & Advantages

**Advantages**:
- Territorial control enables feeding within claimed domains without competition
- Desert territories provide isolation that makes feeding discrete
- Hidden domains offer privacy for feeding and transformation work
- Territorial markers deter intruders and protect feeding arrangements

**Risks**:
- Desert territories are harsh and require careful management
- Territorial claims can be challenged by other Kindred or Garou
- Hidden domains require maintenance and protection
- Territorial markers can attract attention from those who understand their meaning

## Local Power Web

### Allies & Rivals

**Allies**:
- **Sabbat (If Present)**: Tzimisce are the spiritual heart of the Sabbat, creating and maintaining the sect's rituals. They guide the sect through the Paths of Enlightenment and serve as pack priests.
- **Lasombra (If Present)**: Fellow Sabbat founders and strategic partners. Tzimisce respect Lasombra power and work with them to guide the sect.
- **Gangrel**: Mutual respect for wild nature and territorial instincts. Both clans understand the connection to land and the importance of territory.

**Rivals**:
- **Tremere**: Ancient enemies due to Tremere betrayal. Tzimisce resent Tremere use of Gangrel in creating Gargoyles and Tremere sorcerous power.
- **Ventrue**: Conflicts over territorial control and authority. Tzimisce view Ventrue as weak and decadent; Ventrue view Tzimisce as monsters.
- **Camarilla (Formal)**: Open enemies. Tzimisce view the Camarilla as oppressors who reject transformation and territorial dominance.

**Institutions & Mortal Influence**:
- **Territorial Control**: Domains claimed through Koldunic Sorcery and Vicissitude
- **Revenant Families**: Ghoul servants and revenant lines that serve Tzimisce
- **Elemental Spirits**: Connections to land spirits through Koldunic Sorcery
- **Transformation Works**: Creations of flesh and bone that serve as guardians and art

## Themes & Touchstones

### Phoenix-Specific Themes

**Territorial Transformation in a New World**: Phoenix is a young city where territorial claims can still be established. Tzimisce can shape domains rather than inheriting them, creating opportunities for transformation that wouldn't be possible in older cities.

**The Desert as Alien Domain**: The desert surrounding Phoenix is alien to Tzimisce from forested or mountain domains, but its elemental power provides resources for Koldunic Sorcery. Tzimisce must adapt their territorial practices to desert environments.

**Transformation Through Vicissitude**: Tzimisce use Vicissitude to reshape themselves, others, and the world around them. In Phoenix, this transformation creates domains of flesh and bone, works of art and horror that define Tzimisce territory.

**The Paths of Enlightenment**: Tzimisce pursue the Paths of Enlightenment, seeking to transcend humanity and become something greater. In Phoenix, this pursuit creates spiritual authority that guides Sabbat packs and challenges Camarilla structures.

### Touchstones

- **Territorial Domains**: Areas claimed and transformed through Koldunic Sorcery and Vicissitude
- **Desert Territories**: Harsh environments that provide isolation and elemental power
- **Vicissitude Works**: Creations of flesh and bone that serve as art and guardians
- **Koldunic Sorcery**: Elemental magic that communes with land spirits
- **The Paths of Enlightenment**: Spiritual quests that transcend humanity

## Chronicle Hooks (6-10)

### 1. The Territorial Claim
A Tzimisce claims territory in Phoenix, using Koldunic Sorcery and Vicissitude to establish a domain. The claim conflicts with other Kindred interests or Garou territories, creating territorial disputes. PCs must navigate between Tzimisce territorial dominance and other interests.

### 2. The Transformation's Horror
A Tzimisce's Vicissitude work creates something that threatens the Masquerade or draws unwanted attention. The transformation might be art, guardian, or experiment, but it becomes dangerous or exposed. PCs must decide whether to help contain the threat or whether the Tzimisce is too dangerous to assist.

### 3. The Path's Challenge
A Tzimisce following a Path of Enlightenment creates challenges for PCs or city politics. The Path's requirements conflict with practical needs or ethical considerations, creating moral dilemmas. PCs must navigate between spiritual authority and practical consequences.

### 4. The Revenant Network
A Tzimisce's revenant family or ghoul servants become involved in city events, creating connections between Tzimisce operations and city politics. The network provides resources and influence, but it also creates vulnerabilities. PCs must navigate between Tzimisce power and the consequences of revenant involvement.

### 5. The Desert Communion
A Tzimisce uses Koldunic Sorcery to commune with desert spirits, awakening something ancient or powerful. The communion provides power but also creates risks—the spirits might demand service or the communion might attract unwanted attention. PCs must navigate between Tzimisce power and the consequences of awakening desert forces.

### 6. The Sabbat Pack
A Tzimisce leads or guides a Sabbat pack, providing spiritual authority and maintaining sect rituals. The pack's activities conflict with city interests or draw attention to Sabbat presence. PCs must navigate between Sabbat goals and city stability.

### 7. The Flesh Art
A Tzimisce's Vicissitude creations become objects of desire or fear for other Kindred. The art might be beautiful, horrifying, or both, but it draws attention and creates conflicts. PCs must navigate between artistic expression and practical consequences.

### 8. The Old Clan vs. Modern
A conflict between Old Clan Tzimisce (who reject Vicissitude) and modern Tzimisce (who embrace transformation) creates division within the clan. The conflict spills into city politics, forcing PCs to choose sides or navigate between traditional and modern Tzimisce factions.

## NPC Hooks & Seeds (at least 6)

### NPC Seed 1: The Voivode
- **Concept/Role**: Territorial lord, domain master, feudal ruler
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Claimed desert territory or transformed urban space
- **Clan tie-in**: Tzimisce who rules a domain with absolute authority
- **Motivation**: Maintain territorial dominance, reshape domain according to vision, command revenant families and ghoul servants
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether territorial control serves vision or just power—worried that claiming territory means responsibility for everything within it
- **Secret**: Has been secretly allowing some transformation within domain to proceed naturally, violating Tzimisce philosophy of control—if discovered, would face questions about commitment
- **Connection Prompt**: Controls specific territory that PCs might need access to. Can provide resources but demands respect for territorial authority. Represents Tzimisce territorial dominance

### NPC Seed 2: The Metamorphosist
- **Concept/Role**: Flesh-shaper, transformation artist, Vicissitude master
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Hidden workshop or transformed space
- **Clan tie-in**: Tzimisce who pursues transformation through Vicissitude
- **Motivation**: Reshape flesh and bone into works of art and horror, pursue transformation as spiritual practice, transcend limitations of form
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether transformation serves enlightenment or just aesthetics—worried that reshaping everything prevents natural development
- **Secret**: Has been secretly questioning whether Vicissitude transformation serves the Path or just creates dependency on power—if discovered, would face accusations of heresy
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides Vicissitude services and transformation work. Can reshape bodies but requires understanding of consequences. Represents Tzimisce transformation

### NPC Seed 3: The Koldun
- **Concept/Role**: Elemental sorcerer, land spirit communer, territory enchanter
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Desert territory or land-connected space
- **Clan tie-in**: Tzimisce who practices Koldunic Sorcery
- **Motivation**: Commune with land spirits, use elemental magic to control territory, serve as bridge between Tzimisce and the land
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether communing with spirits serves Tzimisce goals or creates dependency on elemental forces—worried that serving spirits means serving masters
- **Secret**: Has been secretly negotiating with desert spirits for power, but the negotiations require services that violate Tzimisce autonomy—if discovered, would face questions about loyalty
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides Koldunic Sorcery and elemental magic. Can control territory through land connection but requires understanding of spirit relationships. Represents Tzimisce land connection

### NPC Seed 4: The Pack Priest
- **Concept/Role**: Sabbat spiritual guide, Path teacher, ritual master
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Sabbat pack territory or mobile with pack
- **Clan tie-in**: Tzimisce who guides Sabbat packs through the Paths of Enlightenment
- **Motivation**: Teach the Paths, maintain Sabbat rituals, guide packs toward enlightenment
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether teaching serves enlightenment or just creates dependency—worried that providing guidance prevents individual discovery
- **Secret**: Has been secretly questioning whether Sabbat Paths serve enlightenment or just create sect loyalty—if discovered, would face accusations of heresy
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides spiritual guidance and Sabbat connections. Can offer Path teachings but requires commitment. Represents Tzimisce spiritual authority

### NPC Seed 5: The Revenant Master
- **Concept/Role**: Ghoul commander, revenant controller, servant master
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Domain with revenant families or ghoul networks
- **Clan tie-in**: Tzimisce who commands revenant families and ghoul servants
- **Motivation**: Control revenant families, maintain ghoul networks, use servants to extend territorial influence
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether controlling servants serves vision or just creates dependency—worried that relying on servants prevents self-sufficiency
- **Secret**: Has been secretly developing genuine care for some revenants, creating emotional connections that violate Tzimisce philosophy of ownership—if discovered, would face questions about control
- **Connection Prompt**: Controls revenant and ghoul networks. Can provide services but requires understanding of servant relationships. Represents Tzimisce command structure

### NPC Seed 6: The Old Clan Traditionalist
- **Concept/Role**: Vicissitude rejector, tradition maintainer, Old Clan member
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Traditional domain or independent territory
- **Clan tie-in**: Old Clan Tzimisce who rejects Vicissitude and Koldunic Sorcery
- **Motivation**: Maintain traditional Tzimisce practices, reject Vicissitude transformation, preserve clan traditions
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether rejecting transformation serves tradition or just limits power—worried that maintaining old ways prevents adaptation
- **Secret**: Has been secretly questioning whether Old Clan rejection serves clan or just fear—if discovered, would face accusations of cowardice
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides traditional Tzimisce perspective. Can offer guidance but requires respect for traditions. Represents Tzimisce traditionalism
