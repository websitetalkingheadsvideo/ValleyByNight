# Clanbook: Followers of Set in Phoenix

*Valley by Night — Phoenix, Arizona, 1994*

---

## Overview (Phoenix Lens)

Phoenix Setites don't rule the city—they *corrupt* it. While other clans fight for visible power, Setites work through temptation, vice, and the careful cultivation of dependency. They don't need to hold titles or claim territories—they need to control desires, to shape wants, to ensure that the city's vices flow through channels they control. Phoenix is a young city without the centuries of established vice networks that Setites navigate in older domains, but that newness creates opportunity. In a city where everything is still being built, Setites can establish foundations of corruption rather than inheriting existing structures.

The desert resonates with Setite mythology. Phoenix's heat, its isolation, its connection to primordial forces—these echo the desert origins of Set himself. The city's sprawl creates spaces where Setites can operate without notice, where vice can flourish, where corruption can grow. The desert's harshness mirrors Setite philosophy: liberation through destruction, enlightenment through corruption, freedom through breaking chains.

The Prince's murder has changed everything. What was chaos for others is opportunity for Setites. Every faction needs vice, information, and forbidden services. Setites provide all three, becoming essential to the city's functioning even as they remain hidden. Their corruption ensures their survival, but their secrecy ensures they'll never be exposed.

## Territory & Feeding

### Primary Territories

**Vice Districts (Various Locations)**: Setites control vice districts throughout Phoenix—nightclubs, strip clubs, drug markets, gambling operations. These locations serve as both business operations and feeding grounds, providing access to those who seek vices and those who provide them. Feeding targets include addicts, thrill-seekers, and those who have been corrupted by Setite influence.

**Temples (Hidden Locations)**: Setites maintain hidden temples where they conduct rituals, perform initiations, and worship Set. These temples are disguised as legitimate businesses, private clubs, or abandoned spaces. They provide sanctuary, ritual space, and feeding opportunities among the initiated.

**Cult Networks (Mobile)**: Setites operate through cult networks that span the city, using mortal followers to extend influence and provide cover. These networks offer access to vulnerable individuals, feeding opportunities, and resources that support Setite operations.

**Underground Markets (Various)**: Setites control underground markets for drugs, weapons, forbidden knowledge, and other vices. These markets provide income, influence, and feeding opportunities among those who participate in illegal activities.

### Feeding Risks & Advantages

**Advantages**:
- Vice districts provide access to vulnerable individuals who seek corruption
- Cult networks offer feeding opportunities among the initiated and controlled
- Underground markets provide access to those who participate in illegal activities
- Temple locations offer discrete feeding among worshippers

**Risks**:
- Vice operations attract attention from authorities and other Kindred
- Cult networks create dependencies that can be exploited
- Underground markets require careful management to avoid exposure
- Temple locations must remain hidden to avoid discovery

## Local Power Web

### Allies & Rivals

**Allies**:
- **Independent Clans**: Setites maintain relationships with independent clans who don't require sect allegiance, creating flexible alliances when interests align.
- **Criminal Networks**: Connections to mortal criminal organizations that provide cover and resources.

**Rivals**:
- **Ventrue**: Conflicts arise when Ventrue attempt to control vice operations or restrict Setite influence. Setites view Ventrue authority as hypocrisy; Ventrue view Setites as corruption.
- **Tremere**: Conflicts over occult knowledge and influence. Setites respect Tremere power but see them as rivals in knowledge markets.
- **Camarilla (Formal)**: Tense relationships. Camarilla tolerates Setites when useful but doesn't trust them; Setites infiltrate Camarilla while maintaining independence.

**Institutions & Mortal Influence**:
- **Vice Operations**: Control of nightclubs, strip clubs, drug markets, gambling operations
- **Cult Networks**: Networks of mortal followers who worship Set or serve Setite purposes
- **Underground Markets**: Control of illegal markets for drugs, weapons, knowledge
- **Religious Fronts**: Connections to religious organizations that provide cover and access

## Themes & Touchstones

### Phoenix-Specific Themes

**Corruption Through Vice in a New World**: Phoenix is a young city where Setites can establish foundations of corruption rather than inheriting existing structures. Vice networks can be built from scratch, cults can be founded, corruption can be cultivated.

**The Desert as Sacred Space**: Phoenix's desert environment resonates with Setite mythology—the heat, the isolation, the connection to primordial forces. The desert becomes sacred space where Setites can conduct rituals and commune with their god.

**Liberation Through Destruction**: Setite philosophy teaches that liberation comes through breaking chains—destroying illusions, corrupting institutions, freeing individuals from false constraints. In Phoenix, this philosophy finds fertile ground in a city where everything is still being built.

**The Path of Typhon**: Setites follow the Path of Typhon, seeking enlightenment through corruption and degradation. In Phoenix, this path creates spiritual authority that guides initiates toward Set's vision of liberation.

### Touchstones

- **Vice Districts**: Areas where corruption flourishes and Setites operate
- **Hidden Temples**: Sanctuaries where Setites worship and perform rituals
- **Cult Networks**: Organizations of mortal followers who serve Setite purposes
- **Underground Markets**: Illegal operations that provide income and influence
- **Serpents and Venom**: Symbols of Set's power and the cleansing force of corruption

## Chronicle Hooks (6-10)

### 1. The Temptation Offer
A Setite offers PCs exactly what they secretly crave—power, knowledge, pleasure, or something else. The offer is genuine, but accepting it creates dependency and corruption. PCs must decide whether the temptation is worth the cost.

### 2. The Cult's Influence
A Setite cult gains influence over important individuals or organizations in Phoenix. The cult's control threatens to corrupt city politics or break the Masquerade, but exposing it requires evidence that Setites have carefully hidden. PCs must discover and stop the corruption.

### 3. The Vice Network
Setites use vice operations to corrupt individuals and create dependencies. The network provides income and influence, but it also creates victims who suffer from Setite corruption. PCs must decide whether to oppose the network, work with it, or navigate around it.

### 4. The Temple's Secret
PCs discover a Setite temple hidden in plain sight, disguised as a legitimate business or private club. The discovery reveals Setite presence and operations, but exposing it requires careful planning. PCs must decide whether to expose the temple or use knowledge of it for their own purposes.

### 5. The Corruption's Price
Someone corrupted by Setite influence faces consequences that threaten to break the Masquerade or create other problems. The corruption might be addiction, obsession, or deeper transformation. PCs must decide whether to help the corrupted individual or whether the corruption is too far gone.

### 6. The Liberation's Cost
A Setite offers liberation through corruption, promising to free PCs from constraints they find limiting. The liberation is real, but the corruption is also real—freedom comes at the price of becoming something else. PCs must decide whether liberation is worth transformation.

### 7. The Serpent's Wisdom
A Setite offers knowledge or wisdom that PCs need, but the knowledge comes with strings attached—accepting it means accepting Setite influence or philosophy. The knowledge is valuable, but the cost is high. PCs must decide whether knowledge is worth corruption.

### 8. The Infiltration Revealed
PCs discover that Setites have infiltrated Camarilla or other structures, using positions of power to advance Setite goals. The infiltration threatens to give Setites control over city politics, but exposing it requires evidence and careful planning. PCs must discover and stop the infiltration.

## NPC Hooks & Seeds (at least 6)

### NPC Seed 1: The Vice Merchant
- **Concept/Role**: Club owner, drug dealer, vice operator
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Vice districts, nightclubs, strip clubs, drug markets
- **Clan tie-in**: Setite who runs vice operations that provide income and corruption
- **Motivation**: Control vice networks, corrupt individuals through addiction and dependency, provide income and influence for Setite operations
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether corruption serves Set's vision or just creates suffering—worried that exploiting vice violates Setite philosophy of liberation
- **Secret**: Has been secretly helping some addicts recover, violating Setite philosophy of corruption—if discovered, would face accusations of heresy
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides vice services and access to corrupted networks. Can offer drugs, pleasure, or other vices but creates dependency. Represents Setite vice operations

### NPC Seed 2: The Cult Leader
- **Concept/Role**: Charismatic preacher, cult organizer, Setite prophet
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Hidden temples, cult meeting places, various locations
- **Clan tie-in**: Setite who builds and leads cults that worship Set
- **Motivation**: Build cult networks, corrupt individuals through religious devotion, advance Setite goals through mortal followers
- **Conflict**: Torn between genuine faith and pragmatic manipulation—worried that using faith for power violates Setite spirituality
- **Secret**: Has been secretly questioning whether cult manipulation serves Set's vision or just creates dependency—if discovered, would face questions about commitment
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides cult connections and religious influence. Can offer spiritual guidance but requires devotion. Represents Setite religious operations

### NPC Seed 3: The Occult Scholar
- **Concept/Role**: Forbidden knowledge collector, heretic researcher, secret historian
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Libraries, archives, research facilities, hidden collections
- **Clan tie-in**: Setite who collects and hoards forbidden knowledge
- **Motivation**: Gather occult knowledge, preserve heretical texts, use information as both sacrament and weapon
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether hoarding knowledge serves Set's vision or just creates dependency on information—worried that keeping secrets prevents liberation
- **Secret**: Has been secretly sharing some knowledge with those who need it, violating Setite philosophy of hoarding—if discovered, would face questions about loyalty
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides access to forbidden knowledge but requires services or devotion. Can offer information but creates obligations. Represents Setite knowledge operations

### NPC Seed 4: The Shadow Fixer
- **Concept/Role**: Problem solver, corruption facilitator, behind-the-scenes operator
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Various locations, mobile, operates wherever corruption is needed
- **Clan tie-in**: Setite who facilitates corruption and solves problems through vice
- **Motivation**: Facilitate corruption, solve problems through temptation and vice, maintain networks that provide Setite influence
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether facilitating corruption serves liberation or just creates suffering—worried that causing harm violates Setite philosophy
- **Secret**: Has been secretly helping some individuals resist corruption, violating Setite philosophy—if discovered, would face accusations of betrayal
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides problem-solving services through vice and corruption. Can offer solutions but creates dependencies. Represents Setite facilitation operations

### NPC Seed 5: The Desert Oracle
- **Concept/Role**: Traditional Setite, ritual specialist, desert mystic
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Desert territories, hidden temples, isolated locations
- **Clan tie-in**: Setite who maintains traditional Setite practices and rituals
- **Motivation**: Preserve Setite traditions, perform rituals that serve Set, maintain connection to desert origins
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether traditional practices serve Set's vision or just create dependency on old ways—worried that maintaining traditions prevents adaptation
- **Secret**: Has been secretly modernizing some practices to adapt to Phoenix, violating traditional Setite philosophy—if discovered, would face accusations of heresy
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides traditional Setite services and ritual guidance. Can offer spiritual connection but requires devotion. Represents Setite traditionalism

### NPC Seed 6: The Infiltrator
- **Concept/Role**: Camarilla infiltrator, sect spy, hidden agent
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Camarilla territories, operates within sect structures
- **Clan tie-in**: Setite who infiltrates Camarilla to advance Setite goals
- **Motivation**: Infiltrate Camarilla structures, gain positions of power, advance Setite interests from within
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether infiltration serves Set's vision or just creates dependency on Camarilla—worried that serving Camarilla violates Setite independence
- **Secret**: Has been secretly developing genuine loyalty to some Camarilla allies, creating divided loyalty—if discovered, would face accusations of betrayal from both sides
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides insider information about Camarilla operations. Can offer access to sect structures but requires discretion. Represents Setite infiltration capabilities
