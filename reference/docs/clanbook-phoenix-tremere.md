# Clanbook: Tremere in Phoenix

*Valley by Night — Phoenix, Arizona, 1994*

---

## Overview (Phoenix Lens)

Phoenix Tremere don't rule the city—they *understand* it. While other clans scramble for dominance in the wake of the Prince's murder, the Tremere continue their work: researching, analyzing, and preparing. Their power doesn't come from territory or political position—it comes from knowledge, from the understanding that information is the ultimate weapon in a world where power structures are collapsing.

The Pyramid provides structure in chaos. While other clans fragment, the Tremere maintain their hierarchical organization, their chantries, their network of blood bonds and obligations. The Prince's death has changed everything for others, but for the Tremere, it's simply another variable in the equation—another piece of data to analyze, another pattern to understand, another opportunity to strengthen their position through knowledge rather than force.

Phoenix is a young city, and the Tremere are a clan that values tradition. The city's newness works against them—no ancient chantries, no centuries of accumulated knowledge, no established networks of influence. But the city's rapid growth, its technological infrastructure, its isolation from older power centers—these create opportunities. The Tremere adapt, as they always have, finding new ways to maintain their position in a world that's changing faster than tradition can account for.

## Territory & Feeding

### Primary Territories

**Chantries (Various Locations)**: Tremere maintain fortified havens and laboratories throughout Phoenix, often hidden in plain sight—university buildings, research facilities, corporate offices. These chantries serve as both sanctuary and power base, providing space for research, ritual work, and training. Feeding is carefully managed through connections to academic institutions, research facilities, and professional networks.

**University Areas (ASU, Community Colleges)**: Tremere gravitate toward educational institutions, using academic connections to maintain feeding opportunities and research access. Students, faculty, and researchers provide both prey and cover for thaumaturgical research. Feeding targets are selected from transient academic populations who won't be missed immediately.

**Downtown Phoenix (Near Elysium)**: Some Tremere maintain positions near Elysium, providing thaumaturgical services and advice to Camarilla leadership. These positions offer access to political power but require careful navigation of social games that Tremere find distasteful.

**Corporate Research Facilities**: Phoenix's growing technology sector provides opportunities for Tremere to establish legitimate business operations that serve as both cover and feeding grounds. Research companies, laboratories, and technology firms offer access to educated professionals.

### Feeding Risks & Advantages

**Advantages**:
- Academic environments provide access to educated prey that Tremere prefer
- Research facilities offer legitimate cover for thaumaturgical activities
- Corporate connections enable discrete feeding through professional networks
- Pyramid structure provides support network for feeding arrangements

**Risks**:
- Academic environments require careful feeding to avoid exposure
- Research facilities attract attention from mortal authorities
- Corporate connections create dependencies that can be exploited
- Pyramid hierarchy means feeding mistakes can be reported up the chain

## Local Power Web

### Allies & Rivals

**Allies**:
- **Ventrue**: Shared commitment to Camarilla structure and hierarchical organization. Both clans value stability and order, though they pursue it through different means.
- **Toreador (Traditionalist)**: Shared appreciation for tradition and cultural institutions. Tremere provide thaumaturgical services; Toreador provide social access.
- **Camarilla Structure**: Tremere are one of the seven pillars of the Camarilla, providing thaumaturgical services and support to maintain sect stability.

**Rivals**:
- **Gangrel**: Ancient grudges and historical conflicts create ongoing tension. Gangrel resent Tremere for past betrayals and use of Gangrel in creating Gargoyles.
- **Assamites**: Historical conflicts and curses create mutual hostility. Tremere fear Assamite assassins; Assamites resent Tremere thaumaturgical power.
- **Sabbat**: Open enemies, especially followers of Goratrix who broke with the Pyramid. Tremere view Sabbat as existential threat to their power structure.

**Institutions & Mortal Influence**:
- **Academic Institutions**: Connections to universities, research facilities, and educational networks
- **Research Facilities**: Laboratories, technology companies, and scientific institutions
- **Corporate Networks**: Business connections that provide cover and resources
- **Occult Communities**: Connections to mortal occultists and mystics who provide information and resources

## Themes & Touchstones

### Phoenix-Specific Themes

**Knowledge as Power in a New World**: Phoenix is a young city without the centuries of tradition that support Tremere power in older domains. The Tremere must adapt, finding new ways to maintain their position through knowledge and understanding rather than inherited influence.

**The Pyramid in Isolation**: Phoenix's isolation from major Kindred centers means the local chantry operates with more autonomy than usual. This creates opportunities for ambitious Tremere but also risks of fragmentation and loss of Pyramid discipline.

**Adaptation Through Technology**: Phoenix's growing technology sector provides opportunities for Tremere to adapt their traditions to modern methods, combining ancient thaumaturgy with contemporary research techniques.

**The Price of Knowledge**: The Prince's murder creates demand for information that only Tremere can provide, but sharing knowledge creates dependencies and vulnerabilities that the Pyramid must carefully manage.

### Touchstones

- **Chantries**: Fortified havens and laboratories, symbols of Tremere knowledge and power
- **University Buildings**: Academic connections that provide cover and feeding opportunities
- **Research Facilities**: Laboratories where thaumaturgy and science meet
- **Ritual Spaces**: Chambers where blood magic is performed and knowledge is preserved
- **Blood Bonds**: The network of obligations that binds the Pyramid together

## Chronicle Hooks (6-10)

### 1. The Research Project
A Tremere research project requires resources or access that PCs can provide, but the research involves dangerous thaumaturgical work or violates Kindred law. The Tremere need PCs' help but can't fully explain what they're researching. PCs must decide whether to trust Tremere intentions or refuse involvement.

### 2. The Blood Bond Network
PCs discover that a Tremere has been creating blood bonds throughout the city, building a network of obligation that gives them influence over multiple Kindred. The network threatens to give Tremere control over city politics, but exposing it risks breaking the Masquerade.

### 3. The Ritual Gone Wrong
A Tremere ritual goes wrong, creating supernatural consequences that threaten the city. The Tremere need help containing the damage, but admitting failure risks exposing Pyramid vulnerabilities. PCs must help resolve the crisis while dealing with Tremere attempts to cover up their mistake.

### 4. The Rogue Thaumaturge
A Tremere has broken with the Pyramid, using their knowledge independently or in service to enemies. The Pyramid needs the rogue eliminated, but doing so requires skills or access that PCs can provide. The rogue might be innocent, might be justified, or might be dangerous.

### 5. The Knowledge Trade
Tremere offer valuable information in exchange for services that compromise PC principles or create dangerous obligations. The information is crucial—possibly about the Prince's murder—but the price is high. PCs must decide whether knowledge is worth the cost.

### 6. The Chantry Under Siege
A Tremere chantry comes under attack from enemies—possibly Sabbat, Assamites, or internal rivals. The Tremere need help defending their stronghold, but the attack reveals secrets that the Pyramid wants kept hidden. PCs must choose between helping Tremere and learning the truth.

### 7. The Modern Innovation
A Tremere attempts to modernize thaumaturgical practices, combining ancient blood magic with contemporary technology. Traditionalists oppose the innovation, creating internal conflict that spills into city politics. PCs must navigate between traditional and modern Tremere factions.

### 8. The Pyramid's Secret
PCs discover a secret about the Phoenix Tremere chantry that the Pyramid wants kept hidden—possibly involving illegal research, forbidden rituals, or violations of Camarilla law. The secret threatens to destroy Tremere credibility, but exposing it creates enemies.

## NPC Hooks & Seeds (at least 6)

### NPC Seed 1: The Regent
- **Concept/Role**: Chantry regent, Pyramid administrator, local Tremere leader
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Primary chantry location, various Phoenix areas
- **Clan tie-in**: Tremere regent who oversees Phoenix chantry operations
- **Motivation**: Maintain Pyramid structure, advance through hierarchy, preserve Tremere knowledge and power
- **Conflict**: Struggles with balancing Pyramid loyalty and personal ambition—worried that following orders too closely limits advancement, that challenging superiors risks punishment
- **Secret**: Has been secretly researching forbidden thaumaturgical practices, combining ancient blood magic with modern technology—if discovered, would face Pyramid discipline
- **Connection Prompt**: Primary point of contact for Tremere operations. Provides access to thaumaturgical services and Pyramid resources. Represents Tremere hierarchical structure

### NPC Seed 2: The Researcher
- **Concept/Role**: Cloistered scholar, thaumaturgical researcher, knowledge seeker
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Chantry laboratories, university connections, research facilities
- **Clan tie-in**: Tremere who focuses on research and knowledge accumulation
- **Motivation**: Pursue knowledge for its own sake, expand understanding of thaumaturgy, preserve and document Tremere traditions
- **Conflict**: Torn between pursuing knowledge freely and adhering to Pyramid restrictions—worried that hierarchical constraints limit research possibilities
- **Secret**: Has discovered information about the Prince's murder through research, but sharing it would require revealing forbidden research methods
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides research capabilities and thaumaturgical knowledge. Can offer information but may require services in return. Represents Tremere pursuit of knowledge

### NPC Seed 3: The Advisor
- **Concept/Role**: Political advisor, Camarilla consultant, Pyramid diplomat
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Near Elysium, various Camarilla territories
- **Clan tie-in**: Tremere who provides services to Camarilla leadership
- **Motivation**: Maintain Tremere influence in Camarilla, provide valuable services that create obligations, navigate political structures
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether serving Camarilla serves Tremere interests—worried that dependency on Camarilla compromises Pyramid independence
- **Secret**: Has been providing information to multiple factions, trying to maintain balance—if discovered, would face accusations of divided loyalty
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides political advice and Camarilla connections. Can facilitate access to Camarilla resources. Represents Tremere role as Camarilla pillar

### NPC Seed 4: The Security Specialist
- **Concept/Role**: Chantry defender, ward specialist, protection consultant
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Chantries, various secured locations
- **Clan tie-in**: Tremere who specializes in protection and defense
- **Motivation**: Protect Tremere chantries and operations, maintain security networks, defend against threats
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether security means isolation—worried that defensive measures create barriers that prevent necessary connections
- **Secret**: Has discovered vulnerabilities in chantry defenses but hasn't reported them, trying to fix them independently—if discovered, would face discipline for failing to report
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides security services and protection. Can help defend against threats but may require assistance. Represents Tremere defensive capabilities

### NPC Seed 5: The Apprentice
- **Concept/Role**: Neonate Tremere, Pyramid student, knowledge seeker
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Chantries, university areas, under supervision
- **Clan tie-in**: Tremere neonate learning Pyramid structure and thaumaturgy
- **Motivation**: Learn thaumaturgy, advance through Pyramid ranks, prove worthiness to superiors
- **Conflict**: Torn between loyalty to Pyramid and personal ambition—worried that following orders prevents individual growth, that questioning authority risks punishment
- **Secret**: Has been secretly learning thaumaturgy beyond authorized levels, using forbidden sources—if discovered, would face severe Pyramid discipline
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides access to Pyramid operations from lower level. Can offer information about internal dynamics. Represents Tremere hierarchy from bottom

### NPC Seed 6: The Rogue
- **Concept/Role**: Former Tremere, independent thaumaturge, Pyramid exile
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Various hidden locations, mobile
- **Clan tie-in**: Tremere who has broken with the Pyramid
- **Motivation**: Operate independently, use thaumaturgical knowledge freely, survive Pyramid retribution
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether independence is worth the cost—worried that breaking with Pyramid means losing access to resources and protection
- **Secret**: Has information about Pyramid secrets that could destroy Tremere credibility, but sharing it would require revealing their own violations—doesn't know if exposing truth serves justice or just revenge
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides independent thaumaturgical services but at risk. Can offer knowledge about Pyramid operations. Represents consequences of breaking with hierarchy
