# Clanbook: Samedi in Phoenix

*Valley by Night — Phoenix, Arizona, 1994*

---

## Overview (Phoenix Lens)

Phoenix Samedi don't belong to the city—they *survive* in it. While other Kindred establish territories and build power bases, Samedi operate as information brokers, assassins, and bodyguards, selling their services to whoever can pay. They don't seek to rule or control—they seek to be useful, to survive, to maintain their existence through utility. Phoenix is a young city without the established networks that Samedi navigate in older domains, but that newness creates opportunity. In a city where connections are still being built, Samedi can establish themselves as essential services rather than tolerated outcasts.

The desert provides perfect isolation. Phoenix's sprawl, its transient population, its isolation from major Kindred centers—these create conditions where Samedi can operate without drawing attention. The city's newness means there are no ancient grudges, no established patterns, no accumulated knowledge of Samedi presence. Every assignment feels like a fresh start, every contract like an opportunity to prove value.

The Prince's murder has changed everything. What was chaos for others is opportunity for Samedi. Every faction needs information, elimination services, and protection. Samedi provide all three, becoming essential to the city's functioning even as they remain outcasts. Their utility ensures their survival, but their condition ensures they'll never be accepted.

## Territory & Feeding

### Primary Territories

**Various Locations (Temporary)**: Samedi don't claim permanent territory—they maintain temporary safehouses, graveyards, mausoleums, and hidden spaces that serve as havens. These locations provide sanctuary where Samedi can operate without notice. Feeding is carefully managed—targeting those who won't be missed, using their appearance and skills to their advantage, operating in ways that leave no trace.

**Graveyards and Mausoleums**: Samedi gravitate toward graveyards and mausoleums, finding comfort and cover among the dead. These locations provide sanctuary, feeding opportunities among mourners and cemetery workers, and connections to the spirit world through voodoo. Feeding targets include those who visit graves, cemetery workers, and those drawn to death.

**Information Networks (Mobile)**: Samedi operate through information networks that span the city, using contacts, connections, and intelligence gathering to maintain their position. These networks provide both income and protection—information is currency, and Samedi are its brokers.

**Temporary Safehouses**: Various locations throughout Phoenix serve as temporary safehouses where Samedi can rest, plan, and prepare for assignments. These locations are never permanent, always changing, always temporary.

### Feeding Risks & Advantages

**Advantages**:
- Appearance and skills enable discrete feeding among those drawn to death
- Graveyard and mausoleum locations provide cover and feeding opportunities
- Information networks provide access to vulnerable individuals
- Utility ensures that Samedi presence is tolerated despite appearance

**Risks**:
- Appearance makes social interactions challenging and potentially dangerous
- Temporary nature makes establishing secure feeding arrangements difficult
- Utility creates dependencies that can be exploited
- Appearance ensures Samedi will never be fully accepted

**Allies**:

## Themes & Touchstones

### Phoenix-Specific Themes

**Survival Through Utility in a New World**: Phoenix is a young city where Samedi can establish themselves as essential services rather than tolerated outcasts. Their utility ensures their survival, but their condition ensures they'll never be accepted.

**The Visible Reality of Death**: Samedi embody death and decay visibly, reminding all Kindred of their true nature. In Phoenix, this visible reality creates both fear and utility—Samedi are needed precisely because they represent what others hide.

**Information as Currency**: In the chaos following the Prince's murder, information becomes more valuable than ever. Samedi are information brokers, collecting secrets and selling them to the highest bidder. Their utility comes from knowledge others don't have.

**Voodoo and the Spirit World**: Samedi maintain connections to voodoo, spirit guides, and the world of the dead. In Phoenix, these connections provide power and protection, access to information from beyond, and resources that other Kindred don't have.

### Touchstones

- **Graveyards and Mausoleums**: Sanctuaries among the dead
- **Fine Clothing on Rotting Bodies**: The contrast between appearance and reality
- **Information Networks**: Connections that provide currency and protection
- **Voodoo Symbols**: Connections to the spirit world
- **Decay as Visible Reality**: The embodiment of death that all Kindred hide

## Chronicle Hooks (6-10)

### 1. The Information Contract
PCs need information that only a Samedi can provide, but the price is high—requiring services, favors, or knowledge that creates dangerous obligations. The information is crucial, but the cost is significant. PCs must decide whether the information is worth the price.

### 2. The Elimination Request
Someone contracts a Samedi to eliminate a target—possibly a PC, a PC ally, or someone important to city politics. The contract is legitimate from Samedi perspective, but PCs must decide whether to prevent the elimination, negotiate with the Samedi, or become involved in other ways.

### 3. The Voodoo Connection
A Samedi's voodoo connections reveal information about the Prince's murder or other city events. The information comes from the spirit world, but verifying it is difficult. PCs must decide whether to trust spirit-given information or whether it's deception.

### 4. The Giovanni Tension
Giovanni attempts to restrict or eliminate Samedi presence in Phoenix, violating the arrangement that keeps Samedi away. The tension threatens to expose the connection between the bloodlines, creating risks for both. PCs must navigate between Giovanni power and Samedi utility.

### 5. The Appearance's Consequences
A Samedi's appearance creates problems when they must operate in situations where their condition is exposed. The exposure threatens to break the Masquerade or create panic, but preventing exposure requires careful planning. PCs might need to help the Samedi or might be caught in the consequences.

### 6. The Utility's Price
A Samedi's utility creates dependency that other Kindred try to exploit. The dependency gives Samedi power, but it also creates vulnerability. PCs must navigate between Samedi utility and the dangers of dependency.

### 7. The Spirit's Warning
A Samedi receives a warning from the spirit world about impending danger or important events. The warning is cryptic and difficult to interpret, but it might be crucial. PCs must decide whether to trust the warning and how to act on it.

### 8. The Survival's Cost
A Samedi faces a situation where survival requires compromising principles or violating codes. The compromise might involve harming innocents, breaking contracts, or abandoning responsibilities. PCs must decide whether to help the Samedi or whether the cost is too high.

## NPC Hooks & Seeds (at least 6)

### NPC Seed 1: The Information Broker
- **Concept/Role**: Secret collector, intelligence trader, knowledge merchant
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Various locations, operates through information networks
- **Clan tie-in**: Samedi who collects and sells information
- **Motivation**: Gather intelligence, sell information to highest bidder, maintain information networks that ensure survival
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether selling information serves survival or just creates dependency—worried that becoming essential makes Samedi vulnerable
- **Secret**: Has information about the Prince's murder but hasn't sold it, holding it in reserve for maximum leverage—if discovered, would face pressure from all sides
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides information services but charges high prices. Can offer intelligence but requires services or favors in return. Represents Samedi information brokering

### NPC Seed 2: The Assassin
- **Concept/Role**: Elimination specialist, contract killer, problem solver
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Various locations, mobile, appears where contracts require
- **Clan tie-in**: Samedi who uses skills and appearance for elimination work
- **Motivation**: Fulfill contracts, eliminate targets efficiently, maintain reputation for precision
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether elimination serves survival or just creates enemies—worried that killing creates risks that outweigh benefits
- **Secret**: Has been secretly refusing contracts that target innocents, violating Samedi philosophy of survival through utility—if discovered, would face questions about commitment
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides elimination services but evaluates each contract carefully. Can offer protection through threat elimination. Represents Samedi capacity for violence

### NPC Seed 3: The Bodyguard
- **Concept/Role**: Protection specialist, security consultant, voodoo guardian
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Various locations, mobile, protects clients
- **Clan tie-in**: Samedi who uses voodoo and skills to protect employers
- **Motivation**: Protect clients, maintain reputation for effectiveness, ensure survival through utility
- **Conflict**: Torn between protecting clients and maintaining Samedi independence—worried that dependency on clients creates vulnerability
- **Secret**: Has been secretly using voodoo to protect clients beyond contract terms, creating spiritual connections that violate professional boundaries—if discovered, would face questions about motives
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides protection services using voodoo and skills. Can offer security but requires understanding of Samedi methods. Represents Samedi protective capabilities

### NPC Seed 4: The Voodoo Practitioner
- **Concept/Role**: Spirit worker, ritual specialist, voodoo priest
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Graveyards, mausoleums, voodoo-connected spaces
- **Clan tie-in**: Samedi who maintains deep voodoo connections
- **Motivation**: Maintain voodoo traditions, commune with spirit world, use spiritual connections for power and protection
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether voodoo connections serve Samedi or create dependency on spirits—worried that serving spirits means serving masters
- **Secret**: Has been secretly negotiating with spirits for power, but the negotiations require services that violate Samedi autonomy—if discovered, would face questions about loyalty
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides voodoo services and spirit connections. Can offer spiritual guidance but requires understanding of voodoo traditions. Represents Samedi spiritual connections

### NPC Seed 5: The Outcast
- **Concept/Role**: Isolated survivor, condition embracer, defiant loner
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Hidden locations, graveyards, isolated spaces
- **Clan tie-in**: Samedi who has learned to embrace their condition
- **Motivation**: Survive through acceptance, use appearance as weapon, maintain independence despite condition
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether accepting condition serves survival or just creates isolation—worried that embracing decay means abandoning connection
- **Secret**: Has been secretly developing connections despite condition, creating relationships that violate Samedi philosophy of isolation—if discovered, would face questions about authenticity
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides perspective on survival and acceptance. Can offer guidance on embracing difficult conditions. Represents Samedi defiance

### NPC Seed 6: The Pragmatist
- **Concept/Role**: Survival specialist, utility maximizer, practical operator
- **Domain/Neighborhood**: Various locations, mobile, operates where utility is needed
- **Clan tie-in**: Samedi who focuses on survival and practical needs
- **Motivation**: Maximize utility, ensure survival through value, maintain position as essential service
- **Conflict**: Struggles with whether utility serves survival or just creates dependency—worried that becoming essential makes Samedi vulnerable
- **Secret**: Has been secretly limiting utility to maintain independence, refusing some contracts to avoid dependency—if discovered, would face questions about commitment to survival
- **Connection Prompt**: Provides practical services and survival knowledge. Can offer assistance but requires understanding of utility relationships. Represents Samedi pragmatism
