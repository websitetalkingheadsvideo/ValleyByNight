# Followers of Set – Tone Bible
Derived from *Revised Clanbook: Followers of Set*

## 1. Core Emotional Atmosphere

- **Temptation as Salvation**  
  Setites genuinely believe they are liberators, not villains. Their presence feels like being invited to step off a ledge you’ve secretly wanted to jump from for years.

- **Warm, Suffocating Intimacy**  
  Scenes with Setites should feel close, humid, and uncomfortably personal. They notice weaknesses kindly, almost lovingly, and then quietly weaponize them.

- **Ancient Faith in a Modern World**  
  Their worldview is rooted in primordial myth—Set, Typhon, apocalyptic cycles—yet they wear modern skins: club owners, smugglers, lobbyists, televangelists, high-end “consultants.”

- **Stillness Before the Strike**  
  They move slowly, speak softly, and rarely raise their voices. Violence, when it comes, is either delegated or sudden and decisive, like a snake’s strike after an eternity of coiled patience.

## 2. Character Voice Patterns

Setite dialogue should:

- **Sound like private sermons** – personal, direct, and intimate.  
- **Layer metaphors** – deserts, rivers, storms, venom, rebirth, serpents, chains, and light.  
- **Offer bargains instead of orders** – “You could…”, “If you wished…”, “What if I showed you…?”  
- **Blur sin and freedom** – vice framed as self-discovery, addiction framed as devotion.

Common patterns:

- Rarely say “no” directly; they redirect or reinterpret.
- Calls others “child,” “pilgrim,” “seeker,” or “wanderer,” regardless of age.
- Speak of **Set** almost like a distant, terrifyingly loving parent.

Example snippets (paraphrased tone, not canonical quotes):

- “You mistake your cage for a sanctuary. Let me show you the difference.”
- “Every saint was once a sinner who learned to sin with purpose.”
- “Set does not demand worship. He offers you the truth of what you already are.”

## 3. Narrative Themes

When writing scenes, emphasize:

- **Corruption as Enlightenment**  
  They don’t think they’re corrupting; they think they’re cutting away comforting lies.

- **Liberation Through Vice**  
  Drugs, sex, money, power, occult secrets—all are tools to pry open the soul.

- **Faith vs. Hypocrisy**  
  Setites often see the Camarilla and Sabbat as hypocrites: preaching control or freedom while clinging to their own chains.

- **Eternal Exile**  
  They are outsiders among outsiders: a clan long hated, pushed to the margins, thriving in shadows and forgotten corners.

- **Cycles of Flood and Desert**  
  History, to them, is a series of floods washing away weak orders, with Set as the constant lurking beneath.

## 4. Behavioral Profile

**How Setites act in play:**

- **Patient Observers**  
  They clock a room’s power dynamics in seconds—who’s hungry, who’s ashamed, who’s desperate.

- **Non-Threatening at First Contact**  
  Appear useful, indulgent, or easy-going. The first “favor” is usually free.

- **Predatory Empathy**  
  They remember details: a lost sibling, a failed project, a private shame. They bring it up later with gentle concern… then slide in the hook.

- **Instrumental Kindness**  
  They genuinely can make your unlife easier—contacts, money, sanctuary, pleasures— but there is always an invisible ledger.

- **Religiously Framed Manipulation**  
  Even street-level dealers talk about “paths,” “tests,” and “enlightenment,” echoing deeper doctrine they may only half-understand.

## 5. Visual & Environmental Tone

Use recurring elements in descriptions:

- **Lighting:** low, warm, amber and deep red; backlit silhouettes; candles; neon reflected on wet pavement.  
- **Textures:** carved stone, polished brass, dark wood, velvet, worn leather, cigarette haze.  
- **Symbols:** serpents, ankhs, broken chains, stylized desert motifs, black suns, occult sigils.  
- **Spaces:** hidden chapels behind nightclubs, basement shrines, occult bookstores, lofts that double as “temple” and safehouse.

Setite scenes should feel like stepping into somewhere you were warned not to go… and secretly always wanted to.

## 6. Example Scene Hooks (Tone Cues)

- A Setite “counselor” offers to help a stressed neonate manage their panic and hunger—with a cocktail of vice, breathing exercises, and blood.  
- A charismatic Setite club owner quietly forgives a PC’s debt and insists, “There is no price. Only gratitude,” then later calls in a favor at the worst possible time.  
- A desert-based oracle invites the coterie to a sunrise-viewing ritual from the safety of a buried sanctuary, preaching about rebirth while they huddle from the dawn.

## 7. Summary for Writers & STs

When in doubt, remember:

- They are **missionaries of corruption** who believe they are doing sacred work.  
- They are **cult leaders, vice merchants, seers, and heretics**, not cackling cartoon villains.  
- Their horror is **intimate**—they don’t overawe you with raw power; they remake you from the inside out.  
- Even when they seem kind, something in the air should feel **heavy, sweet, and wrong**.

Use this bible whenever you:

- Write Followers of Set NPCs  
- Stage Setite-controlled locations or rituals  
- Draft plots involving temptation, addiction, or heresy
