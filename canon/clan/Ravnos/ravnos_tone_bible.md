# Ravnos – Tone Bible
Derived from *Revised – Clanbook Ravnos*

> **Note for VbN:** The Ravnos are the tricksters and wanderers of Kindred society, cursed with an irresistible compulsion to commit crimes. They are individualists who value freedom above all else, and their unique Discipline of Chimerstry allows them to manipulate illusion and perception in ways that blur the line between reality and fantasy.

---

## 1. Core Emotional Atmosphere

- **The Compulsion and the Paradox**  
  Every Ravnos is driven to commit a specific type of crime, a compulsion that gnaws at them constantly. This isn't a choice—it's a curse that defines their existence. Yet they must master this compulsion to find their true purpose (svadharma) through the Path of Paradox. The emotional temperature is one of constant tension between the urge to give in and the need to transcend.

- **Freedom and Isolation**  
  Ravnos value their freedom above all else, often rejecting both Camarilla and Sabbat structures. This independence comes at a cost: they are often alone, wandering from place to place, never truly belonging anywhere. The mood is one of restless movement, of never staying in one place too long.

- **The Week of Nightmares**  
  The recent destruction of most Ravnos elders during the Week of Nightmares has left the clan shattered and scattered. Those who remain are traumatized, paranoid, and struggling to understand what happened. This recent trauma colors every interaction and decision.

- **Illusion and Reality**  
  Ravnos live in a world where nothing is quite what it seems. Their Chimerstry allows them to create illusions so real they can cause physical harm, but this power comes with a price: they may lose the ability to distinguish between what's real and what's not. The line between truth and deception is constantly blurred.

- **The Wanderer's Path**  
  Most Ravnos are nomads, traveling from city to city, never putting down roots. They are the eternal outsiders, the ones who pass through but never stay. This creates a sense of transience, of impermanence, of being always on the move.

## 2. Character Voice Patterns

Ravnos dialogue should feel:

- **Cynical and world-weary** – they've seen too much to be naive, but they maintain a dark sense of humor.  
- **Restless** – conversations often reference travel, movement, and the need to keep moving.  
- **Paradoxical** – they speak in contradictions, using logic that seems to make no sense but somehow does.  
- **Guarded** – they rarely reveal their true intentions, preferring to keep others guessing.

Common speech tendencies:

- Using travel and movement metaphors:  
  - "I'll be gone by morning."  
  - "This town's getting too hot for me."  
  - "Time to hit the road again."  
- References to illusion and deception:  
  - "Nothing is what it seems."  
  - "Reality is just another illusion."  
  - "What you see isn't always what you get."  
- Dark humor about their compulsion:  
  - "I can't help myself—it's in my blood."  
  - "The urge is calling again."  
  - "Just one more time, then I'll stop."  
- Philosophical musings about purpose:  
  - "Everyone has their svadharma—their true purpose."  
  - "The Path of Paradox teaches us to transcend our curse."  
  - "We must find our own way, not follow another's path."

They speak quickly, often changing topics mid-conversation, and may use Chimerstry to create visual aids or distractions while talking.

## 3. Narrative Themes

When writing Ravnos-focused scenes or plots, lean into:

- **The Compulsion vs. Free Will**  
  Every Ravnos is driven to commit crimes, but they must learn to control this urge to find their true purpose. When do they give in? When do they resist? What happens when the compulsion becomes too strong?

- **Illusion vs. Reality**  
  Ravnos can create illusions so real they cause physical harm, but this power can make them lose touch with reality. What happens when a Ravnos can no longer tell what's real and what's not? How do others react to a world where nothing can be trusted?

- **The Week of Nightmares**  
  The recent destruction of most Ravnos elders has left the clan in chaos. Those who remain are traumatized, paranoid, and struggling to understand what happened. How do they cope with this loss? What secrets died with the elders?

- **Freedom vs. Belonging**  
  Ravnos value their freedom above all else, but this often means they're alone. When do they choose to stay? When do they choose to leave? What happens when they find something worth staying for?

- **The Path of Paradox**  
  The Path of Paradox teaches Ravnos to find their true purpose (svadharma) by transcending their curse. But the path is difficult, and few can follow it. What happens when a Ravnos finds their purpose? What happens when they fail?

## 4. Behavioral Profile

How Ravnos behave in play:

- **Wanderers**
  - Never stay in one place too long.  
  - Always have an escape route planned.  
  - Travel light and don't accumulate possessions.  
  - May form temporary alliances but rarely long-term commitments.

- **Tricksters**
  - Use Chimerstry to create illusions and distractions.  
  - Prefer subtle manipulation to direct confrontation.  
  - May use their compulsion to their advantage, turning it into a tool.  
  - Often play multiple roles, never revealing their true self.

- **Survivors**
  - Adapt quickly to new situations.  
  - Use Animalism to gather information and create diversions.  
  - Rely on Fortitude to survive dangerous situations.  
  - May form loose networks with other Ravnos for mutual support.

- **Individualists**
  - Reject authority and structure.  
  - Make their own rules and follow their own path.  
  - May join sects temporarily but rarely commit fully.  
  - Value personal freedom above all else.

Common to all:

- **Restless Movement** – they're always planning their next move, always ready to leave.  
- **Paranoid Security** – multiple escape routes, false identities, and contingency plans are standard.  
- **Dark Humor** – they use humor to cope with their curse and the horrors they've seen.  
- **The Compulsion** – they must constantly resist (or give in to) their urge to commit crimes.

## 5. Visual & Environmental Tone

Use recurring visual cues:

- **Transient spaces**: motels, train stations, bus stops, roadside diners, truck stops, rest areas
- **Illusion and distortion**: things that look real but aren't, visual tricks, impossible perspectives
- **Travel gear**: backpacks, duffel bags, maps, travel guides, fake IDs, burner phones
- **Animal companions**: dogs, birds, or other animals used for scouting and information gathering
- **Paradoxical imagery**: things that shouldn't exist but do, contradictions made manifest, reality bending

Color palette: warm earth tones (browns, tans, rust), vibrant but faded colors (like old postcards), and the colors of twilight and dawn (when travelers are most active).

Lighting: dim, flickering, or artificial—the lighting of transient spaces. Things may look different depending on the angle or perspective, reflecting the illusory nature of Chimerstry.

## 6. Example Scene Hooks

- **The Compulsion Takes Over**  
  A Ravnos's compulsion becomes too strong to resist, and they commit a crime that threatens the Masquerade or draws unwanted attention. The coterie must help cover it up or deal with the consequences.

- **The Treatment**  
  A prince has kicked a Ravnos out of town, and other Ravnos are planning "The Treatment"—a coordinated campaign of harassment and disruption. The coterie must decide whether to help, stop it, or stay out of the way.

- **The Week of Nightmares Survivor**  
  A Ravnos who survived the Week of Nightmares approaches the coterie with information about what really happened, but they're clearly traumatized and may not be entirely reliable. Is their information real, or just another illusion?

- **The Path of Paradox**  
  A Ravnos seeks the coterie's help in finding their svadharma (true purpose), but the path is dangerous and may require them to confront their deepest fears and desires.

- **The Illusion Breaks**  
  A Ravnos's Chimerstry has become so powerful that they can no longer tell what's real and what's not. The coterie must help them regain their grip on reality before they hurt themselves or others.

## 7. Summary for Writers/Storytellers

Ravnos are the tricksters and wanderers of Kindred society, cursed with an irresistible compulsion to commit crimes but gifted with the power to manipulate illusion and perception. They are individualists who value freedom above all else, often rejecting both Camarilla and Sabbat structures in favor of their own path.

When using Ravnos in your chronicle:

- **Respect their compulsion** – every Ravnos is driven to commit a specific type of crime, and this should be a constant source of tension and conflict.
- **Emphasize their isolation** – they're wanderers who never stay in one place too long, always ready to move on.
- **Use Chimerstry creatively** – their illusions can be subtle tools or overwhelming weapons, but they should always serve the story.
- **Remember the Week of Nightmares** – this recent trauma has left the clan shattered, and those who remain are traumatized and paranoid.
- **Explore the Path of Paradox** – the path teaches Ravnos to find their true purpose by transcending their curse, but it's difficult and few can follow it.

Ravnos are tragic figures: cursed with an irresistible compulsion, gifted with the power to create illusions, and doomed to wander forever in search of a purpose they may never find. They are tricksters, survivors, and individualists who refuse to be bound by the rules of others, even as their own curse binds them tighter than any chain.
