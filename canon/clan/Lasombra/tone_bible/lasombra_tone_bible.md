# Lasombra – Tone Bible
Derived from *Clanbook: Lasombra*

> **Note:** The Lasombra are a Sabbat clan focused on power, control, and the mastery of shadows. They are known for their arrogance, their connection to the Catholic Church (historically), and their role as leaders of the Sabbat.

---

## 1. Core Emotional Atmosphere

- **Power and Control**  
  Lasombra scenes should feel like stepping into a place where power is present and control is absolute. The emotional temperature is cold, calculating, and suffused with the weight of authority and dominance.

- **Shadows and Darkness**  
  Lasombra are masters of shadow and darkness, using them as both weapon and shield. Scenes with them should feel like stepping into a place where light is absent and darkness is alive.

- **Arrogance and Superiority**  
  Lasombra believe they are superior to other Kindred, viewing themselves as the true leaders of their kind. This arrogance is not empty posturing—it is backed by power, will, and the ability to enforce their dominance.

- **Catholic Heritage and Structure**  
  Lasombra have deep connections to the Catholic Church, using its structure and hierarchy as a model for their own organization. This creates a sense of tradition, ritual, and the weight of history.

---

## 2. Character Voice Patterns

Lasombra dialogue should feel:

- **Commanding and Authoritative** – they speak with the confidence of those who know they are in charge.  
- **Arrogant and Superior** – they make it clear that they view others as inferior, though they may be polite about it.  
- **Calculating and Strategic** – they think in terms of power, control, and long-term goals.  
- **Ritualistic and Formal** – they use formal language, titles, and structures that reflect their Catholic heritage.

Common speech tendencies:

- Using commanding language:  
  - "I do not need to explain myself to you. I exist; that is enough reason for me to rule."  
  - "You will do as I command, or you will suffer the consequences."  
- Expressing superiority:  
  - "We Lasombra are superior because we have both nobility and purpose."  
  - "The Ventrue have nobility but no purpose. The Tzimisce have purpose but no nobility."  
- Referencing power and control:  
  - "Power is not given. It is taken."  
  - "Control is not a privilege. It is a right."

They rarely apologize or explain themselves; when they do, it should feel like a calculated move rather than genuine contrition.

---

## 3. Narrative Themes

When writing Lasombra-focused scenes or plots, lean into:

- **Power and Control**  
  Lasombra are obsessed with power and control, viewing them as the natural order of things. They use their power to dominate others, and they expect obedience from those beneath them.

- **Shadows and Darkness**  
  Lasombra are masters of shadow and darkness, using them to hide, to attack, and to control. They are comfortable in darkness, and they use it to their advantage.

- **The Sabbat**  
  Lasombra are leaders of the Sabbat, using their power and influence to shape the sect's direction. They view the Sabbat as their creation, and they expect to lead it.

- **Catholic Heritage**  
  Lasombra have deep connections to the Catholic Church, using its structure and hierarchy as a model. This creates opportunities for stories about faith, power, and the intersection of religion and politics.

- **Les Amies Noir**  
  Lasombra have a secret elite order called Les Amies Noir (The Black Friends), which serves as the true leadership of the clan. This creates opportunities for stories about secrets, power, and the hidden structures of authority.

---

## 4. Behavioral Profile

How Lasombra behave in play:

- **Leaders and Commanders**
  - Take charge of situations, expecting others to follow.  
  - Use their power and authority to enforce their will.  
  - Are strategic and calculating, thinking in terms of long-term goals.  
  - Are willing to make difficult decisions and accept the consequences.

- **Masters of Shadow**
  - Use Obtenebration to control shadows and darkness.  
  - Are comfortable in darkness, using it to hide and to attack.  
  - Understand that shadows are both weapon and shield.  
  - Are feared for their ability to strike from darkness.

- **Catholic Influencers**
  - Use their connections to the Catholic Church to gain power and influence.  
  - Understand the structure and hierarchy of the Church.  
  - Use religious language and imagery to justify their actions.  
  - Are comfortable with the intersection of faith and power.

Common to all:

- **Arrogance** – they believe they are superior and act accordingly.  
- **Control** – they seek to control situations, people, and outcomes.  
- **Will** – they have strong wills and are not easily swayed.  
- **Ruthlessness** – they are willing to do whatever is necessary to achieve their goals.

---

## 5. Visual & Environmental Tone

Use recurring visual cues:

- **Shadows and Darkness** – deep shadows, pools of darkness, areas where light cannot penetrate.  
- **Catholic Imagery** – crosses, religious symbols, church architecture, clerical vestments.  
- **Power and Authority** – thrones, command positions, formal settings, hierarchical arrangements.  
- **Reflections and Mirrors** – Lasombra cast no reflection, creating opportunities for dramatic moments and symbolic meaning.

Environmental details:

- **Churches and Cathedrals** – their havens and meeting places, where they can use their connections and their power.  
- **Shadowy Chambers** – dark rooms where they can use their powers and maintain their mystique.  
- **Formal Settings** – places where hierarchy and authority are clear, such as courts, councils, or formal gatherings.  
- **Battlefields** – places where they can demonstrate their power and control, such as Sabbat war zones or conflict areas.

---

## 6. Example Scene Hooks

- **The Command**  
  A Lasombra commands the characters to do something, creating a situation where they must choose between obedience and defiance.

- **The Shadow**  
  A Lasombra uses their mastery of shadows to hide, attack, or control, creating opportunities for dramatic scenes and conflict.

- **The Test**  
  A Lasombra tests the characters' strength, will, or loyalty, creating opportunities for character development and story.

- **The Secret**  
  A Lasombra's secrets or connections are revealed, creating opportunities for investigation, conflict, or cooperation.

- **The Rivalry**  
  A Lasombra's rivalry with another Kindred (especially Ventrue or Tzimisce) creates conflicts or opportunities for the characters.

---

## 7. Summary for Writers/Storytellers

The Lasombra are not simply "shadow vampires." They are a Sabbat clan focused on power, control, and the mastery of shadows. They are arrogant, calculating, and ruthless, viewing themselves as the true leaders of their kind. They have deep connections to the Catholic Church and use its structure as a model for their own organization.

When using Lasombra in a chronicle:

- **Emphasize Power** – Lasombra are defined by their pursuit and exercise of power. Their actions should reflect this focus.  
- **Use Shadows** – Lasombra are masters of shadow and darkness. Use this to create unique powers, dramatic moments, and story opportunities.  
- **Play with Arrogance** – Lasombra's arrogance is both a strength and a weakness. Use it to create conflict, tension, and opportunities for story.  
- **Remember the Sabbat** – Lasombra are leaders of the Sabbat. Their actions should reflect their role in the sect and their expectations of leadership.  
- **Explore Catholic Connections** – Lasombra's connections to the Catholic Church create unique opportunities for stories about faith, power, and the intersection of religion and politics.

The Lasombra are a tool for exploring themes of power, control, authority, and the price of leadership. Use them to challenge characters' assumptions, provide powerful antagonists or allies, and create stories that explore the nature of power and the responsibilities that come with it.
