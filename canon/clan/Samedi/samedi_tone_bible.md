# Samedi – Tone Bible
Derived from *Bloodline Book: Samedi*

> **Note:** The Samedi are a bloodline created by a Giovanni necromantic experiment gone wrong. They are characterized by their horrific, rotting appearance and their connection to death, decay, and voodoo.

---

## 1. Core Emotional Atmosphere

- **Decay and Death**  
  Samedi scenes should feel like stepping into a morgue or a graveyard—places where death is present and decay is visible. The emotional temperature is cold, morbid, and suffused with the reality of death and decomposition.

- **Outcast and Proud**  
  Samedi are outcasts, rejected by most Kindred for their appearance and their nature. They have learned to embrace their condition, finding strength in their isolation and using their horrific appearance as both weapon and shield.

- **Survival Through Utility**  
  Samedi survive by being useful—as information brokers, assassins, bodyguards, and specialists in death and decay. They understand that their continued existence depends on their value to others, and they work hard to maintain that value.

- **Voodoo and the Spirit World**  
  Samedi have a deep connection to voodoo, spirit guides, and the world of the dead. This connection gives them power and knowledge that others lack, but it also marks them as different and dangerous.

---

## 2. Character Voice Patterns

Samedi dialogue should feel:

- **Direct and Unapologetic** – they speak plainly about death, decay, and the reality of their condition. They don't sugarcoat or apologize.  
- **Darkly Humorous** – they use gallows humor and macabre jokes to cope with their condition and to unsettle others.  
- **Practical and Shrewd** – they speak in terms of deals, information, and survival. Everything is a transaction.  
- **Proud and Defiant** – they refuse to be ashamed of what they are, even as they acknowledge the horror of their condition.

Common speech tendencies:

- Using direct, unflinching language:  
  - "We're all dead. Some of us just look the part."  
  - "You want to know what death really looks like? Look in a mirror."  
- Making dark jokes:  
  - "I don't need a coffin—I am one."  
  - "The smell? That's just reality catching up with you."  
- Speaking in terms of survival:  
  - "I survive because I'm useful. When I'm not useful, I'm dead."  
  - "Information is currency. Secrets are power. I deal in both."

They rarely show vulnerability; when they do, it should feel like a rare moment of connection or trust.

## 3. Narrative Themes

When writing Samedi-focused scenes or plots, lean into:

- **The Reality of Death**  
  Samedi force others to confront the reality of death and decay. They are a constant reminder that all Kindred are dead, and that death is not pretty or noble—it is decay and rot.

- **Survival Through Utility**  
  Samedi survive by being useful. They are information brokers, assassins, bodyguards, and specialists. Their continued existence depends on their value to others.

- **Outcast and Proud**  
  Samedi are rejected by most Kindred, but they have learned to embrace their condition. They find strength in their isolation and use their horrific appearance as both weapon and shield.

- **Voodoo and the Spirit World**  
  Samedi have a deep connection to voodoo, spirit guides, and the world of the dead. This connection gives them power and knowledge, but it also marks them as different and dangerous.

- **The Giovanni Connection**  
  Samedi were created by a Giovanni experiment gone wrong. This connection creates tension, conflict, and opportunities for story.

## 4. Behavioral Profile

How Samedi behave in play:

- **Information Brokers**
  - Collect secrets and information obsessively, using it as currency and protection.  
  - Are patient and methodical, willing to wait years for the right information.  
  - Build networks of contacts and sources, always looking for new leads.  
  - Understand that knowledge is power, and they use it to survive.

- **Assassins and Bodyguards**
  - Are practical and efficient, using whatever means necessary to complete a job.  
  - Are not subtle—they use explosives, curses, and direct violence as needed.  
  - Understand that their appearance is a weapon, using it to intimidate and unsettle.  
  - Are loyal to their employers, as long as the pay is good and the job is clear.

- **Voodoo Practitioners**
  - Maintain connections to spirit guides and the world of the dead.  
  - Use voodoo rituals, curses, and geases to protect themselves and their employers.  
  - Understand the power of names, symbols, and ritual.  
  - Are respected (and feared) for their knowledge of the spirit world.

Common to all:

- **Practicality** – they are focused on survival and utility, not ideology or politics.  
- **Independence** – they work alone or in small groups, avoiding large organizations.  
- **Resourcefulness** – they make do with what they have, finding creative solutions to problems.  
- **Defiance** – they refuse to be ashamed or hidden, even as they acknowledge their condition.

---

## 5. Visual & Environmental Tone

Use recurring visual cues:

- **Decay and Rot** – visible decomposition, leaking fluids, brittle hair, receded lips, missing noses, sunken eyes.  
- **Graveyards and Mausoleums** – their preferred havens, where death is present and decay is natural.  
- **Fine Clothing on Rotting Bodies** – the contrast between elegant dress and horrific appearance.  
- **Voodoo Symbols and Rituals** – altars, candles, symbols, and ritual spaces.

Environmental details:

- **Mausoleums and Crypts** – their havens, where they can be themselves without hiding.  
- **Graveyards** – their hunting grounds and meeting places, where death is present and natural.  
- **Abandoned Buildings** – places where they can operate without attracting attention.  
- **Voodoo Ritual Spaces** – chambers designed for ceremonies, where they connect with the spirit world.

## 6. Example Scene Hooks

- **The Information Broker**  
  A Samedi offers to sell information that the characters desperately need, but the price is high and the information comes with dangerous implications.

- **The Bodyguard**  
  A Samedi is hired to protect someone important to the characters, but their methods are disturbing and their appearance creates problems.

- **The Voodoo Ritual**  
  A Samedi performs a voodoo ritual that has unexpected consequences, drawing unwanted attention or creating new problems.

- **The Giovanni Connection**  
  A Samedi's connection to the Giovanni creates conflicts or opportunities, forcing the characters to deal with both bloodlines.

- **The Candomble**  
  The characters are invited (or stumble upon) the annual Samedi gathering, creating opportunities for interaction, conflict, or discovery.

---

## 7. Summary for Writers/Storytellers

The Samedi are not simply "ugly vampires." They are a bloodline created by a Giovanni experiment gone wrong, forced to live with horrific, rotting bodies and the constant reality of death and decay. They survive by being useful—as information brokers, assassins, bodyguards, and specialists—and they have learned to embrace their condition, finding strength in their isolation.

When using Samedi in a chronicle:

- **Emphasize Their Utility** – Samedi survive by being useful. Their actions should reflect this focus on survival and value.  
- **Use Their Appearance** – The Samedi's horrific appearance is both a curse and a weapon. Use it to create tension, conflict, and opportunities for story.  
- **Remember the Voodoo Connection** – Samedi have deep connections to voodoo and the spirit world. Use this to create unique powers, knowledge, and story opportunities.  
- **Play with Contrast** – The gap between their appearance and their capabilities, their isolation and their connections, creates opportunities for story.  
- **Respect Their Defiance** – Samedi refuse to be ashamed or hidden. They are proud of what they are, even as they acknowledge the horror of their condition.

The Samedi are a tool for exploring themes of death, decay, survival, and the reality of unlife. Use them to challenge characters' assumptions, provide information, and create stories that force others to confront the reality of death and decay.
