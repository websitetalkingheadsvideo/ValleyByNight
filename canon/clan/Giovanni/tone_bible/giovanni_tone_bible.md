# Giovanni – Tone Bible
Derived from *Revised – Clanbook Giovanni*

---

## 1. Core Emotional Atmosphere

- **Wealth and Decadence**  
  The Giovanni are defined by their money, their power, and their willingness to do anything to maintain both. Scenes with them should feel opulent, calculated, and morally compromised. They move in circles of old money, corporate boardrooms, and family estates where the price of everything is known—and paid.

- **Family Above All**  
  The Giovanni are a true family, bound by blood (both mortal and Kindred), tradition, and mutual interest. This creates an atmosphere of intense loyalty mixed with suffocating control. Family members are assets, tools, and sometimes threats—but always family.

- **The Painful Kiss**  
  Every Giovanni's Embrace is marked by agony. Their victims feel excruciating pain when fed upon, and this shapes the clan's relationship with mortality. They don't hide what they are—they make their victims suffer for it.

- **Necromantic Power**  
  The Giovanni's connection to death and the Underworld creates an atmosphere of cold calculation and otherworldly knowledge. They speak with the dead, command spirits, and see the world through the lens of mortality's end.

---

## 2. Character Voice Patterns

Giovanni dialogue should feel:

- **Calculating and precise** – every word has purpose, every statement is measured.  
- **Wealthy and cultured** – references to art, history, business, and family legacy.  
- **Morally flexible** – they don't apologize for their actions; they justify them.  
- **Family-focused** – constant references to family, bloodlines, and legacy.

Common speech tendencies:

- Using business and financial metaphors:  
  - "That's a poor investment."  
  - "The return on that isn't worth the risk."  
  - "We need to liquidate that asset."
- Family terminology:  
  - "Cugino" (cousin), "Signore" (sir/master), references to bloodlines and generations.  
  - "For the family" as justification for any action.
- References to death and the dead:  
  - "The dead don't lie."  
  - "Our ancestors have seen this before."  
  - "Death is just another transaction."
- Justification through pragmatism:  
  - "It's business."  
  - "Someone had to do it."  
  - "The ends justify the means."

They rarely express genuine emotion unless it serves a purpose. When they do, it's usually anger, greed, or cold satisfaction.

---

## 3. Narrative Themes

When writing Giovanni-focused scenes or plots, lean into:

- **Family Loyalty vs. Personal Ambition**  
  The tension between serving the family and pursuing individual goals. Every Giovanni must balance their own desires against the family's needs—and the family always wins.

- **Wealth as Power**  
  Money isn't just comfort—it's influence, protection, and leverage. The Giovanni use their wealth to control mortals, buy silence, and secure their position in Kindred society.

- **The Promise of 1528**  
  The Giovanni's isolation from Kindred politics is both a protection and a limitation. They operate outside the Camarilla and Sabbat, but this means they have no allies when trouble comes.

- **Necromantic Knowledge**  
  The Giovanni's connection to the dead gives them information others lack, but it also ties them to the Underworld in ways that can be dangerous. The dead have their own agendas.

- **The Endless Night**  
  The clan's ultimate goal: tearing down the barrier between the living and the dead. This apocalyptic ambition drives many Giovanni actions, even if most don't know the full plan.

- **Incest and Degeneracy**  
  The Giovanni's preference for "single-blooded" childer (those born from incestuous unions) creates a culture of sexual deviancy and family dysfunction that outsiders find repulsive.

---

## 4. Behavioral Profile

How Giovanni behave in play:

- **Calculating Every Move**  
  Giovanni think in terms of long-term strategy, investments, and returns. They rarely act impulsively—every action is weighed against potential consequences and benefits.

- **Family Hierarchy**  
  They respect age, generation, and family position. Younger Giovanni defer to elders, and those outside the main family lines (like the Milliners) are treated as second-class.

- **Business First**  
  Even personal relationships are transactional. Favors are debts, relationships are investments, and emotions are tools to be used.

- **Necromantic Practices**  
  They regularly consult with the dead, use spirits as spies and servants, and see death as a resource to be managed.

- **Isolation from Other Kindred**  
  They keep their distance from Camarilla and Sabbat politics, but they're always watching, always calculating how events might affect their interests.

Common to all:

- **Wealth Display** – expensive clothes, cars, homes, art collections.  
- **Family References** – constant mentions of relatives, ancestors, and family history.  
- **Cold Pragmatism** – they do what's necessary, not what's moral.  
- **Necromantic Knowledge** – they know things others don't because they ask the dead.

## 5. Visual & Environmental Tone

Use recurring visual cues:

- **Lighting:** dim, expensive—candlelight, fireplaces, tasteful lamps. Nothing harsh or fluorescent.  
- **Textures:** silk, velvet, mahogany, marble, polished brass, old leather, fine paper.  
- **Symbols:** family crests, old money, corporate logos, necromantic symbols, death imagery (skulls, bones, funerary art).  
- **Spaces:** mansions, corporate offices, family crypts, private libraries, exclusive clubs, old European estates.

Giovanni scenes should feel **opulent and calculated**—like stepping into a world where everything has a price and everyone knows their place in the hierarchy.

---

## 6. Example Scene Hooks (Tone Cues)

- A Giovanni elder calls in a favor, demanding the coterie help with a "family matter"—which turns out to be eliminating a rival family member who's become a liability.  
- The coterie discovers that a Giovanni has been using necromancy to spy on them through the spirits of their victims. They must decide how to respond without violating the Promise of 1528.  
- A Giovanni neonate approaches the coterie with an offer: help them escape the family's control, and they'll share valuable information about the clan's plans.  
- The coterie needs information that only the dead can provide, and a Giovanni offers to help—for a price that goes beyond money.

---

## 7. Summary for Writers & STs

When in doubt, remember:

- Giovanni are **not just rich vampires**; they are **a family business that happens to be undead**, with all the loyalty, dysfunction, and calculation that implies.  
- Their horror is in **what they're willing to do**—incest, murder, necromancy, and worse—all justified as "for the family."  
- They bring **cold calculation and otherworldly knowledge** to politics: they know things others don't, and they use that knowledge ruthlessly.  
- Use them to **ground the chronicle in wealth, power, and moral compromise**: every deal has a price, and the Giovanni always collect.

Use this bible when:

- Writing Giovanni NPCs (family members, business associates, necromancers, or those who've broken from the family)  
- Creating plots around wealth, family loyalty, necromantic knowledge, or the Promise of 1528  
- Framing conflicts between personal ambition and family duty, or between the Giovanni and other Kindred who resent their isolation and power
