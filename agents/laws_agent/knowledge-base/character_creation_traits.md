# Traits in Laws of the Night Revised - Page References

## Chapter Three: Character Creation and Traits

**Primary Location:** Pages 58-127

### Main Discussion Sections:

#### **Page 58-62** - Introduction to Character Creation
- Quick Character Creation Process overview
- Step Two: Attributes - What are your basic capabilities?

#### **Page 67-68** - Attributes Discussion
- **Physical Attributes** - Measure general physical prowess, strength, speed, coordination, and reflexes
- **Social Attributes** - Determine the force of a character's personality and ability to interact well with others
- **Mental Attributes** - Help in problem-solving, learning, deduction and general alertness

#### **Page 68** - "Choosing Traits" Section
States: "Once you've decided on your character's inherent strengths, you should describe exactly the sorts of exceptional characteristics..."

This is where traits are selected. The book notes that:
- Traits are adjectives used to describe a character's Attributes
- Physical Traits examples: Brawny, Quick, Tough, Graceful, Steady, Rugged
- Social Traits examples: Charismatic, Commanding, Empathetic, Intimidating, Witty, Dignified  
- Mental Traits examples: Alert, Creative, Cunning, Disciplined, Knowledgeable, Observant

#### **Page 71** - Negative Traits
- Negative Traits are discussed on page 71
- Each Negative Trait grants one Free Trait later in character creation
- Maximum of 5 Negative Traits allowed (no more than 3 in any one attribute category)
- Negative Traits must be chosen from the official list (not player-created)

### Key Points from the Rules:

1. **Trait System**: Uses cards/bidding system, NOT dots
2. **Distribution**: Primary (7 Traits), Secondary (5 Traits), Tertiary (3 Traits)
3. **Trait Names**: Each trait is listed by NAME and used for challenge comparisons
4. **Examples Given**: The book provides examples but does not give complete lists - it assumes players will choose appropriate adjectives

### Important Note:
The Laws of the Night Revised rulebook provides **examples** of traits (like "Brawny, Quick, Tough" for Physical) but does **NOT** provide a complete, exhaustive list of all possible traits. The system is designed to be flexible, allowing players to choose appropriate adjectives that describe their character's capabilities.

The complete trait lists used in VbN (from Fandom/TV Tropes sources) are appropriate expansions that align with the spirit of the system, even though they're not explicitly listed in the core rulebook.
