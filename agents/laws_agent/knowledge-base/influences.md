# Influence System - Comprehensive Summary

## Overview

Influence is a Background trait that represents a vampire's sway over the institutions of mortal society. It allows Kindred to manipulate various aspects of human civilization indirectly, from government agencies to criminal organizations, enabling them to strike at enemies, protect assets, and gain information and resources.

**Primary Source:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 370, 581-733

---

## Core Mechanics

### Definition
Influence represents sway over the institutions of mortal society in various areas. It is one of the standard Backgrounds available during character creation.

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 370

### Trait Allocation
- Influence must be allocated separately to each area/category
- If you have Legal x 4, you could still have Police x 3 independently, but you must spend Traits for each
- You cannot manage more total Influence than the sum of your permanent Physical, Social, and Mental Traits combined
- This limit counts against all your total Influence - your combined levels cannot exceed this total

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 583

### Using Influence
- When you exercise Influence, you expend **temporary** Influence Traits
- Performing an action requires a number of Traits equal to the level of the action
- You must use three Traits to perform an action listed at the third level of an Influence chart
- With high levels of Influence, you can perform many small actions, or a few significant ones

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 583

### Money and Equipment from Influence
- Unlike the Resources Background, money and equipment garnered with Influence does not come automatically each month
- If you want a steady income from Influence, you must direct your Influence in that direction continually
- This income does not come with any associated trappings of wealth (you'd have to buy a house and car separately)
- Aides garnered with Influence generally help only for one specific task
- Aides usually only have the equivalent of one level of Ability in their area of skill
- For more competent and readily available help, take Allies

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 583

### City Influence Limits
- Most cities have only a set amount of Influence in various areas
- Example: Atlanta has a great deal of Transportation Influence (hub of travel)
- Hollywood would have a lot of High Society and Media Influence
- A Rust-Belt city where manufacturing has disappeared would have very little to no Industry Influence
- Storytellers should map out the total amount of each type of Influence available in the city
- Once all Influence of a given type is used up, the only way to get more is to:
  1. Use Influence to grow that area of society (making new projects or sponsoring investment)
  2. Destroy someone else's Influence and thus free up those resources
  3. Acquire an adversary's Influence in an area

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 583

## The 15 Types of Influence

### 1. Bureaucracy
**Description:** You can manage various government agencies and bureaus. By dealing with social programs and public servants, you can spin red tape, bypass rules and regulations, or twist bureaucratic regimentation to your advantage. Bureaucracy is useful in operating or shutting down businesses, faking or acquiring permits and identification papers, and manipulating public utilities and facilities.

**Contacts/Allies:** Government clerks at city and county level, utility workers, road crews, surveyors, and other civil servants.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Trace utility bills; Fake a birth certificate or driver's license
- **2 Traits:** Learn about general economic trends
- **3 Traits:** Learn real motivations for many financial actions of others
- **4 Traits:** Disconnect a single small residence's utilities; Close a small road or park; Get public aid ($250); Fake a death certificate, passport, or green card; Close a public school for a single day; Shut down a minor business on a violation
- **5 Traits:** Initiate a phone tap; Fake land deeds; Initiate a department-wide investigation; Start, stop, or alter a city-wide program or policy; Shut down a big business on a violation; Rezone areas; Obliterate records of a person on a city or county level

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 584-590

### 2. Church
**Description:** Though the modern church has arguably less control over temporal society than in the Middle Ages, its policies still exert considerable influence over politics and communities. Knowing the appropriate people allows insight into many mainstream religions (Christianity, Judaism, Islam, Hinduism, Shinto, Buddhism). Fringe or alternative groups (such as Scientology) are considered Occult.

**Contacts/Allies:** Ministers, priests, bishops, Church-sponsored witch-hunters, holy orders, various attendees and assistants.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Identify most secular members of a given faith in the local area; Pass as a member of the clergy
- **2 Traits:** Perform ceremonies (marriage, burial, etc.); Identify higher church members; Track regular church members; Suspend lay members
- **3 Traits:** Open or close a single church; Find the average church-associated hunter; Dip into the collection plate ($250); Access private information and archives of a church
- **4 Traits:** Discredit or suspend high-level church members; Manipulate regional branches of the church
- **5 Traits:** Organize major protests; Access ancient church lore and knowledge

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 591-598

**Clan Bonuses:**
- **Lasombra:** All Lasombra gain one Trait of Church, Political, or Underworld Influence (player's choice)
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 223

### 3. Finance
**Description:** Manipulating markets, stock reports, and investments is a hobby of many Cainites, especially those who use their knowledge to keep hidden wealth. Though your actual available money is a function of Resources, you can use Finance Influence to start or smother businesses, crush or support banking institutions, and alter credit records.

**Contacts/Allies:** CEOs, bankers, stockbrokers, bank tellers, yes-men, financiers, loan agents.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Learn about major transactions and financial events; Raise capital ($1,000)
- **2 Traits:** Learn about general economic trends
- **3 Traits:** Learn real motivations for many financial actions of others
- **4 Traits:** Trace an unsecured small account; Raise capital to purchase a small business (single, small store); Purchase a large business (a few small branches or a single large store or service); Manipulate local banking (delay deposits, some credit rating alterations); Ruin a small business
- **5 Traits:** Control an aspect of city-wide banking (shut off ATMs, arrange a bank 'holiday'); Ruin a large business; Purchase a major company

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 600-612

**Clan Bonuses:**
- **Ventrue:** All Ventrue additionally gain one Trait of Finance, High Society, or Political Influence (player's choice)
- **Giovanni:** Giovanni characters start with an automatic Influence level in Finance or Health (player's choice)
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 217, 243

### 4. Health
**Description:** Some vampires rely on connections in the medical community to acquire blood. Necromancers and practitioners of arcane arts may also require body parts or medical data. Furthermore, maintaining the Masquerade often calls for alteration of medical records or faking diseases. Some Cainites specialize in the study of blood-borne ailments.

**Contacts/Allies:** Coroners, doctors, lab workers, therapists, pharmacists, specialists.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Access a person's health records; Fake vaccination records and the like; Use public functions of health centers at your leisure; Get a single Blood Trait of mortal blood
- **2 Traits:** Access some medical research records; Have minor lab work done; Get a copy of a coroner's report; Instigate minor quarantines
- **3 Traits:** Corrupt results of tests or inspections
- **4 Traits:** Alter medical records; Acquire a body; Completely rewrite medical records; Abuse grants for personal use ($250); Have minor medical research performed on a subject; Institute large-scale quarantines; Shut down businesses for 'health code violations'
- **5 Traits:** Have special research projects performed

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 616-633

**Clan Bonuses:**
- **Giovanni:** Giovanni characters start with an automatic Influence level in Finance or Health (player's choice)
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 243

### 5. High Society
**Description:** The glitterati at the top of society move in circles of wealth and elegance. Many Kindred find such positions alluring and indulge in the passions of the famous and wealthy. Access to famous actors, celebrities, and the idle rich grants sway over fashion trends. Combined with Fame, a modicum of High Society Influence turns a vampire into a debonair darling of the most exclusive social circles.

**Contacts/Allies:** Dilettantes, artists of almost any stripe, old money families, models, rock stars, sports figures, jetsetters.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Learn what is trendy; Obtain hard-to-get tickets for shows; Learn about concerts, shows, or plays well before they are made public
- **2 Traits:** Track most celebrities and luminaries; Be a local voice in the entertainment field; 'Borrow' idle cash from rich friends ($1,000)
- **3 Traits:** Crush promising careers; Hobnob well above your station; Minor celebrity status; Get a brief appearance on a talk show that's not about to be canceled
- **4 Traits:** Ruin a new club, gallery, festival, or other posh gathering
- **5 Traits:** [Not detailed in source material]

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 635-644

**Clan Bonuses:**
- **Ventrue:** All Ventrue additionally gain one Trait of Finance, High Society, or Political Influence (player's choice)
- **Daughters of Cacophony:** New inductees have at least one level of High Society Influence or an additional level of Performance Ability
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 217, 255

### 6. Industry
**Description:** The grinding wheels of labor fuel the economies and markets of the world. Machines, factories, and blue-collar workers line up in endless drudgery, churning out the staples of everyday living. Control over Industry Influence sways the formation of unions, movements of work projects, locations for factories, and the product of manufacturing concerns.

**Contacts/Allies:** Union workers, foremen, engineers, construction workers, manual laborers, all manner of blue-collar workers.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Learn about industrial projects and movements
- **2 Traits:** Have minor projects performed
- **3 Traits:** Organize minor strikes; Appropriate machinery for a short time
- **4 Traits:** Close down a small plant; Revitalize a small plant
- **5 Traits:** Manipulate large local industry

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 645-654

### 7. Legal
**Description:** Since many operations that Cainites undertake are at least marginally illegal, sway over judges and lawyers is indispensable. Those Kindred who dabble in law often pull strings in the courts to ensure questionable practices go unnoticed and unpunished. Legal Influence is also excellent for harassing an enemy's assets.

**Contacts/Allies:** Law schools and firms, lawyers, judges, DAs, clerks, public defenders.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Get free representation for minor cases
- **2 Traits:** Avoid bail for some charge
- **3 Traits:** Have minor charges dropped; Manipulate legal procedures (minor wills and contracts, court dates); Access public or court funds ($250)
- **4 Traits:** Get representation in most court cases; Issue subpoenas; Tie up court cases
- **5 Traits:** Close down all but the most serious investigations; Have deportation proceedings held against someone

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 656-661

### 8. Media
**Description:** Directing media attention away from vampire activities is key to the Masquerade. Putting specific emphasis on certain events can place an enemy in an uncomfortable spotlight or discredit a rival. With Media, you can crush or alter news stories, control operations of news stations and reporters, and sway public opinion.

**Contacts/Allies:** DJs, editors of all varieties, reporters, cameramen, photographers, broadcasters. At Storyteller discretion, Media Influence may also allow access to technical areas of television, radio, or movies.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Learn about breaking stories early
- **2 Traits:** Submit small articles (within reason); Suppress (but not stop) small articles or reports; Get hold of investigative reporting information
- **3 Traits:** Initiate news investigations and reports; Get project funding and waste it ($250); Ground stories and projects
- **4 Traits:** [Not detailed in source material]
- **5 Traits:** Broadcast fake stories (local only); Kill small local articles or reports completely

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 663-673

### 9. Occult
**Description:** The hidden world of the supernatural teems with secrets, conspiracies, and unusual factions. Obviously, a vampire is aware that strange things exist, but hard knowledge is a function of Abilities. By using Occult Influence, you can dig up information to improve knowledge, get inside the occult community, and find rare components for magical rituals. Even parts of the elusive Book of Nod are available to those with the right connections.

**Contacts/Allies:** Cult leaders, alternative religious groups, charlatans, occultists, New Agers, and a few more dangerous elements.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Contact and make use of common occult groups and their practices; Know some of the more visible occult figures
- **2 Traits:** Know and contact some of the more obscure occult figures
- **3 Traits:** Access resources for most rituals and rites; Know the general vicinity of certain supernatural entities and (possibly) contact them; Access vital or rare material components; Milk impressionable wannabes for bucks ($250)
- **4 Traits:** Research an Intermediate ritual from your sect
- **5 Traits:** Access minor magic items; Unearth an Advanced ritual from your sect

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 675-682

**Clan Bonuses:**
- **Tremere:** All Tremere gain one Occult Ability and one Occult Influence automatically
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 213

### 10. Police
**Description:** 'To protect and serve' is the motto of the police, but these days, Kindred and kine alike may wonder who is being protected and served. Police Influence can be very handy to assist with the Masquerade, protect holdings, or raid the assets of another.

**Contacts/Allies:** Police of all ranks, detectives, clerical staff, dispatchers, prison guards, special divisions (SWAT, homicide), local highway patrol.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Learn police procedures
- **2 Traits:** Have license plates checked; Avoid minor violations (first conviction); Get 'inside information'
- **3 Traits:** Get copies of an investigation report; Have police hassle, detain, or harass someone
- **4 Traits:** Find bureau secrets; Access confiscated weapons or contraband; Have some serious charges dropped; Start an investigation; Get money, either from the evidence room or as an appropriation ($1,000)
- **5 Traits:** Institute major investigations; Arrange setups; Instigate bureau investigations; Have officers fired

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 684-712

### 11. Political
**Description:** Deal-making is second nature to most vampires, so they get along very well with politicians. Altering party platforms, controlling local elections, changing appointed offices, and calling in favors all fall under Political Influence. Well-timed blackmail, bribery, spin doctoring, or any sundry tricks are stock in trade.

**Contacts/Allies:** Pollsters, lobbyists, activists, party members, spin doctors, politicians from rural zoning committees to mayors of major cities or Congressional representatives.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Minor lobbying; Identify real platforms of politicians and parties
- **2 Traits:** Be in the know; Meet small-time politicians; Garner inside information on processes, laws, and the like; Use a slush fund or fund-raiser ($1,000)
- **3 Traits:** Sway or alter political projects (local parks, renovations, small construction)
- **4 Traits:** Enact minor legislation
- **5 Traits:** Get your candidate in a minor office; Enact encompassing legislature

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 691-699

**Clan Bonuses:**
- **Ventrue:** All Ventrue additionally gain one Trait of Finance, High Society, or Political Influence (player's choice)
- **Lasombra:** All Lasombra gain one Trait of Church, Political, or Underworld Influence (player's choice)
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 217, 223

### 12. Street
**Description:** Ignored and often spat on by their 'betters,' those in the dark alleys and slums have created their own culture. When calling on Street Influence, you use connections on the underside of the city to find the homeless, gang members, street buskers, petty criminals, prostitutes, residents of slums or barrios, and fringe elements of 'deviant' cultures.

**Contacts/Allies:** Homeless, gang members of all sorts, street buskers, petty criminals, prostitutes, residents of slums or barrios, fringe elements.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Open an ear for the word on the street
- **2 Traits:** Identify most gangs and know their turfs and habits; Live mostly without fear on the underside of society; Keep a contact or two in most aspects of street life; Access small-time contraband
- **3 Traits:** Get insight into other areas of Influence; Arrange some services from street people or gangs; Get pistols or uncommon melee weapons
- **4 Traits:** Mobilize groups of homeless; Panhandle or hold a 'collection' ($250); Get hold of a shotgun, rifle, or SMG; Have a word in almost all aspects of gang operations
- **5 Traits:** Control a single medium-sized gang; Arrange impressive protests by street people

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 701-708

**Clan Bonuses:**
- **Ravnos:** All Ravnos have a level of Street Influence or Transportation Influence
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 245

### 13. Transportation
**Description:** Most Cainites make their havens in defensible parts of cities. Traveling across wilderness is difficult, with problems of daylight and marauding Lupines. Without this Influence, the vampiric world shrinks into islands of 'civilization' with dangerous wastelands in between. Getting access to special supplies and services can also take Transportation Influence.

**Contacts/Allies:** Truckers, harbors, railroads, airports, taxis, border guards, pilots, untold hundreds, as well as mundane aspects like shipping and travel arrangements.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Know what goes where, when, and why
- **2 Traits:** Travel locally quickly and freely; Track an unwary target if he uses public transportation; Arrange passage safe (or at least concealed) from mundane threats (robbery, terrorism, sunlight, etc.)
- **3 Traits:** Seriously hamper an individual's ability to travel; Avoid most supernatural dangers when traveling (such as Lupines)
- **4 Traits:** Shut down one form of transportation (bus lines, ships, planes, trains, etc.) temporarily; Route money your way ($500)
- **5 Traits:** Reroute major modes of travel; Smuggle with impunity

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 714-721

**Clan Bonuses:**
- **Ravnos:** All Ravnos have a level of Street Influence or Transportation Influence
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 245

### 14. Underworld
**Description:** The world of crime offers lucrative possibilities to strong-willed or subtle leaders. Guns, money, drugs, and vice - such delicious pastimes can be led by anyone talented or simply vicious enough to take them. Underworld Influence lets you call on favors for all manner of illegal dealings.

**Contacts/Allies:** Mafia, La Cosa Nostra, drug dealers, bookies, Yakuza, tongs, hitmen, fences, criminal gangs.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Locate minor contraband (knives, small-time drugs, petty gambling, scalped tickets)
- **2 Traits:** Obtain pistols, serious drugs, stolen cars; Hire muscle to rough someone up; Fence stolen loot
- **3 Traits:** Prove that crime pays (and score $1,000); Obtain a rifle, shotgun, or SMG; Arrange a minor 'hit'; Meet someone in 'the Family'
- **4 Traits:** Make white-collar crime connections
- **5 Traits:** Arrange gangland assassinations; Hire a demolition man or firebug; Supply local drug needs

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 723-730

**Clan Bonuses:**
- **Lasombra:** All Lasombra gain one Trait of Church, Political, or Underworld Influence (player's choice)
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 223

### 15. University
**Description:** Institutions of learning and research are the purview of University Influence. Access to halls of learning can help with any number of resources, from ancient languages to research assistance to many impressionable young minds.

**Contacts/Allies:** School boards, students from kindergarten through college, graduate students, professors, teachers, deans, Greek orders, variety of staff.

**Effects by Level:**
- **1 Trait:** Know layout and policy of local schools; Have access to low-level university resources; Get records up to the high school level
- **2 Traits:** Know a contact or two with useful knowledge or Abilities; Have minor access to facilities; Fake high school records; Obtain college records
- **3 Traits:** Call in faculty favors; Cancel a class; Fix grades
- **4 Traits:** Discredit a student; Organize student protests and rallies; Discredit faculty members
- **5 Traits:** Falsify an undergraduate degree

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 732-739

## Relationship to Other Backgrounds

### Contacts
- Contacts allows you to get information as if you possessed a certain amount of Influence
- You can get information of a level equal to however many contacts you use
- If you use Contacts x 3 on Industry, you get information as if digging up dirt with Industry x 3 Influence
- The advantage of Contacts is that contacts can be switched from place to place each game, getting information in different areas at your demand
- Contacts will not generally function as aides or lackeys; that is the purview of Allies and Retainers

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 554

### Fame
- Fame allows you to exercise your connections over a longer distance than usual
- With Fame, you can make a few calls and get your Contacts, Influence, or Resources exerted over a greater range without impediment
- Your total Fame determines your maximum range for unimpeded use of your Contacts, Influence, and Resources
- When you make an effort to exert Contacts, Influence, or Resources over a long range, you lean on your popularity and image, thus expending your Fame Traits until the next game session
- Fame is most often used in downtime between sessions to facilitate long-range plans

**Fame Ranges:**
- Fame x 1: Local scene
- Fame x 2: City
- Fame x 3: State
- Fame x 4: Adjoining states
- Fame x 5: Entire country

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 556-562

### Retainers
- Retainers can be used to manage assets and perform tasks
- Retainers tied up in this fashion allow you to manage more Influence than normal
- They add to the number of Attribute Traits that you possess for purposes of counting your total Influences
- Each retainer directed in this fashion adds one to your maximum Influence Traits
- If retainers are later lost, killed, or reassigned, the excess Influence Traits are lost, starting with the highest levels of Influence held

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 754

### Allies
- Unlike Influence, your allies have special talents that make them better than the average person on the street
- Allies do not show up in play directly; instead, you use them for certain services between play sessions

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 552

### Resources
- Equipment: Your character's Backgrounds of Resources and Influence may grant access to certain pieces of special gear
- Whether you have a leather jacket, a handgun, or a wad of cash, you should talk to your Storyteller to make sure your character's gear is accounted for

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 385

## Clan-Specific Influence Bonuses

### Tremere
- **Bonus:** All Tremere gain one Occult Ability and one Occult Influence automatically
- **Reasoning:** Training in mystical arts and clan contacts with arcane circles
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 213

### Ventrue
- **Bonus:** All Ventrue additionally gain one Trait of Finance, High Society, or Political Influence (player's choice)
- **Reasoning:** Connections to society and social manipulation
- **Note:** Even those Ventrue without such ties themselves may call on their clanmates for appropriate aid, effectively granting the same capabilities
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 217

### Lasombra
- **Bonus:** All Lasombra gain one Trait of Church, Political, or Underworld Influence (player's choice)
- **Reasoning:** Long heritage of influence and manipulation; during Dark Ages, exercised subtle authority within the Church and over secular rulers
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 223

### Giovanni
- **Bonus:** Giovanni characters start with an automatic Influence level in Finance or Health (player's choice)
- **Additional Option:** They can either take a further additional level in one of those Influences or else gain an automatic one Trait Retainer - a wraith
- **Note:** A ghostly Retainer cannot help with Influence or perform other functions in the physical world, but it is capable of spying and exerting some ghostly powers on behalf of its master
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 243

### Ravnos
- **Bonus:** All Ravnos have a level of Street Influence or Transportation Influence
- **Reasoning:** They tend to practice various vices and move from city to city
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 245

### Daughters of Cacophony
- **Bonus:** New inductees have at least one level of High Society Influence or an additional level of Performance Ability
- **Reasoning:** The Sirens often Embrace from talented or up-and-coming performers
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 255

### Assamites
- **Note:** Players of Assamites should not use clan benefits as a replacement for proper Mentor and Influence Backgrounds - the clan doesn't reward those who rest on their laurels at the clan's expense
- **Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 233

## Conversion Rules

### From Tabletop to Live-Action
- The Influence Background runs from one to five dots in the tabletop game
- It is subdivided into several categories for live-action play
- In Mind's Eye Theatre conversions, simply split up any dots of Influence into any desired levels of Influence that total the same amount
- If your character has three dots of Influence, you get to spread around three Traits of Influence in live-action play, among whatever categories desired (or all in one)

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 985

### From Previous Editions
- Influence Backgrounds remain unchanged
- The same categories of Influence still exist, with the same levels
- They are no trouble for converted characters

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 961

## Politics Ability and Influence

The Politics Ability is useful primarily in dealings with mortal society or Influence:
- You may be able to manipulate the outcome of local political actions, with the proper Influence and some well-placed Social Challenges
- Politics Ability ensures that matters proceed in the direction that you desire
- Politics is also used to discern hidden motives and broker deals
- With Politics, you can usually set an appropriate level of debt for a prestation boon
- The hierarchies of power are often obvious to you, granting insight into whether the prince is really in charge, or is simply a puppet of the primogen

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 534

## Elder Vampires and Influence

- Elder vampires may possess truly far-reaching Influence, giving them the power to exert control beyond the levels included in the standard charts
- Storytellers should adjudicate elder-level Influence effects based on the character's age, power, and resources

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 583

## Additional Notes

### Equipment Access
- Your character's Backgrounds of Resources and Influence may grant access to certain pieces of special gear
- Weapons that have Influence values listed under Availability indicate the level and type of Influence required in order to procure the weapon through illicit channels
- A character must have some level of the Finance Ability or money-raising Influence to procure exotic melee weapons and nearly all firearms

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), pages 385, 1608

### City Variations
- Each city may have different reflections of the Influences listed
- A city with a thriving independent film community will have a different picture of Media or High Society than a city where the arts are being starved out due to budget cuts
- Storytellers should consider local flavor when determining available Influence

**Reference:** Laws of the Night (1st Edition), page 583

## Summary

The Influence system is a comprehensive Background that allows vampires to manipulate mortal institutions across 15 distinct categories. It operates on a Trait-based system where temporary Traits are expended to perform actions, with the total Influence limited by a character's combined Physical, Social, and Mental Traits. Various clans receive automatic bonuses to specific Influence types, reflecting their historical and cultural connections. The system integrates with other Backgrounds like Contacts, Fame, and Retainers, and can be used to gain information, resources, money, equipment, and temporary aides. Storytellers must manage city-wide Influence limits to maintain game balance and realism.
