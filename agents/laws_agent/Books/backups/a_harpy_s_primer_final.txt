Theposition of harpy can be a very hard role to play, both for storytellers and players, yet theposition is very im portant to the integrity of the Camarilla. The following is a guide to help those interested in playing a harpy. The history of theposition, what is all included with the power of the harpy, socializing and snubs, and the organization of harpies themselves. This primer is designed to help both new and expe rienced players understand the history of the harpyposition. The harpy can be a very intimidatingposition, but can offer a compelling and intriguing obsta cle for players to overcome. New players can use it to help understand theposition and utilize it to enhance their roleplay. Experienced players can enrich their knowledge and use this as a way to expand their roleplaying. Storytellers are welcome to use what works for their games and adapt what they need to fit their story. Communication and Organization Theposition of harpy has been in place since theformation of the Camarilla. The purpose of the harpy is to be the arbiter for overseeing the currency of boons and to maintain the pecking order of status within the sect. In the bigger pic ture, the harpy is the one that keeps neonates from rising up against their elders, though it is not outwardly the perception. The system is set up specifically to keep elders ahead in the game and give them an unfair advantage, and the po sition of harpy is setup to ensure that advantage is kept. TTHHEE HHAARRPPYY’’SS PPRRIIMMEERR The harpies of the Camarilla are a force to be reckoned with. From social influences, to being the arbiter of boons, the currency of the Camarilla, they can move the Camarilla in whateverdirection they wish. The Harpy Primer is the first of 3 different guides on theposition, and is a introduction to getting off on the right foot as a harpy. Both for players and storytellers, this guide is a base to build up on for theposition. The Harpy Primer includes: • ThePosition within the Camarilla; • In Character introductions to NPC harpies; • The History of thePosition; • How to Socialize; • Rumors surrounding the harpies; • Organization and Communication between harpies.
