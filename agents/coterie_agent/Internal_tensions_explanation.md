# Internal Tensions Explanation

## Key Fields Explained

### `type` (Controlled Vocabulary)

Use intentional categories so the agent can reason about them:

- **`authority`** — leadership legitimacy
- **`trust`** — belief in motives or competence
- **`exposure`** — Masquerade / mortal risk
- **`ideology`** — disagreement on goals or methods
- **`resource`** — control of assets or logistics
- **`clan_pressure`** — external loyalties bleeding inward

### `severity` (0–3 recommended initially)

- **0** — Dormant (exists only as backstory)
- **1** — Subtle (tone shifts, hesitation)
- **2** — Active (decisions affected)
- **3** — Critical (forces a scene or consequence)

> **Important:** Severity should never auto-increase on a timer — only via events.

### `trigger_conditions`

Short tags the agent can watch for:

- `time_pressure`
- `public_failure`
- `masquerade_risk`
- `elder_intervention`
- `character_injury`
- `betrayal_reveal`

When a trigger fires, the agent can:

- Suggest a flashback
- Escalate severity
- Modify a Strength or Gap temporarily

### `flashback_hook`

This is gold.

Every tension must imply a past moment:

- A bad call
- A necessary compromise
- A success that came at a cost

This lets you:

- Generate flashback scenes on demand
- Recontextualize current events
- Make consequences feel earned

## Example Internal Tensions for Defenders

Tailored directly to the roster you showed.

### 1. Tracker vs Leadership (Authority)

> "Results came faster when permission wasn't asked."

- **Severity:** 2
- **Pressure:** Success outside the chain of command
- **Cost:** Leader authority erodes quietly

### 2. Caitiff Visibility Risk (Exposure / Clan Pressure)

> "Some elders don't see a member. They see a liability."

- **Severity:** 1
- **Pressure:** Court politics, Elysium scrutiny
- **Cost:** Defensive decision-making, secrecy

### 3. Logistics & Mortals (Exposure)

> "Every truck, phone, and safehouse leaves a trail."

- **Severity:** 2
- **Pressure:** Surveillance, hunters, subpoenas
- **Cost:** Slower response or bolder risks

### 4. Mission Purity vs Survival (Ideology)

> "Removing the Sabbat is easy. Living afterward isn't."

- **Severity:** 1
- **Pressure:** Brutal choices, collateral damage
- **Cost:** Moral fracture inside the group

## How Internal Tension Interacts with Existing Sections

### Strengths

A tension can temporarily suppress a Strength ("Clear hierarchy" becomes contested).

### Gaps

A tension can explain why a Gap exists, or turn a Gap from abstract → personal.

### Story Hooks

Hooks can target tensions:

- Elders exploiting authority fractures
- Rivals offering protection to one member only

## Flashbacks: The Payoff

**Every internal tension earns at least one flashback scene.**

Flashbacks should:

- Be 30–90 seconds
- Focus on one role
- End with a choice that echoes forward
