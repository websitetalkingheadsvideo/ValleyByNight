# PART VII — MARKETING MATERIALS SYSTEM  
## (Full, Exhaustive Version)

## 1. Overview
The Marketing Materials System defines the entire promotional visual identity for *Valley by Night*:  
- Posters  
- Key Art  
- Character Announcement Cards  
- Social Media Banners  
- Chapter Reveal Graphics  
- Cinematic Promo Stills  
- Web Landing Hero Images  
- Trailer Thumbnails  

Marketing art allows *slightly heightened stylization* while remaining fully consistent with:  
- Portrait System  
- Cinematic System  
- UI Palette  
- Location Aesthetics  
- 1994 Phoenix Tone  

It must always reinforce the Valley by Night brand:  
**gothic–noir • desert modern • psychological tension • cinematic depth**

---

# 2. Stylistic Philosophy

Marketing art is **bolder**, **more dramatic**, and **more stylized** than in-game assets, yet strictly avoids:  
- oversaturation  
- comic-book aesthetics  
- neon cyberpunk looks  
- modern/futuristic lighting  
- clean digital vectors  

The tone should evoke films such as:  
- *Interview with the Vampire* (1994)  
- *Seven* (1995)  
- *Heat* (1995) — night exterior scenes  
- *Lost Highway* (1997)*  

Marketing must feel **prestige noir with supernatural undertones**.

---

# 3. Color & Contrast Rules

## 3.1 Allowed Enhancements
Marketing art may push:
- **10–20% deeper contrast**
- **Richer blood-red (#8B0000)**
- **More visible gold accents (#d4b06d)**
- **Slightly heavier vignette**
- **More pronounced haze/smoke**

## 3.2 Forbidden Enhancements
- Pure white elements  
- Neon blue, pink, green  
- High-saturation movie-poster tropes  
- Comic gradients  
- Modern LED lighting effects  

---

# 4. Composition Philosophy

Marketing imagery follows three principles:

## 4.1 Rule of Thirds
Characters or objects sit on precise thirds lines.

## 4.2 Negative Space for Titles
Large text must fit comfortably without covering key elements.

## 4.3 Depth Layering
Foreground → midground → background must be clear:
- silhouettes  
- atmospheric haze  
- background architecture tied to Phoenix 1994  

---

# 5. Typography Guidelines

## 5.1 Typefaces
- **Titles:** IM Fell English SC, Libre Baskerville, or Trajan-style serif  
- **Subtitles:** small caps serif  
- **Body Copy:** Libre Baskerville or Georgia  
- **Taglines:** parchment-tone serif  

## 5.2 Effects
- Soft gold bevel (subtle)  
- Drop shadow: small, dark red or black  
- Glow: minimal, only in red or gold

- symbolic objects  
- desert moonlit scenes  
- silhouettes in alleys  
- candlelit interiors  

## 6.4 Web Landing Hero Images
- dark composition  
- heavy negative space  
- must integrate with UI header placement  

## 6.5 Social Media Banners
- 1920×1080 landscape  
- or 1080×1920 reels/stories  
- minimal text  

## 6.6 Promotional Thumbnails (YouTube / Website)
- tight framing  
- bold contrast  
- single focal point

# 10. Marketing Pipeline

## 10.1 Steps
1. Concept sketch  
2. Photographer-style lighting plan  
3. 3D/portrait integration (if needed)  
4. Composition blocking  
5. Final illustration / render  
6. Color grading and effects pass  
7. Title & typography layout  
8. Export for multiple aspect ratios  

## 10.2 Export Formats
- PNG (primary)  
- WEBP (web optimization)  
- JPG (social platforms)  

## 10.3 Aspect Ratios
- Landscape 1920×1080  
- Square 1080×1080  
- Vertical 1080×1920  
- Thumbnails 1280×720
